IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 20/2100Z
TO 21/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS VERY LOW. OCCASIONAL SUBFLARES
OCCURRED FROM REGIONS 5942 (S35E28), 5946 (N03W07), AND 5947
(S17E48), EACH OF WHICH GREW GRADUALLY DURING THE PERIOD. NEW
REGIONS 5951 (N21W21) AND 5952 (S03E08) WERE NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW. C-CLASS SUBFLARES ARE EXPECTED FROM REGIONS 5942, 5946,
AND 5947. REGIONS 5942 AND 5947 ALSO PROVIDE A SLIGHT CHANCE
FOR ISOLATED M-CLASS ACTIVITY.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 20/2100Z TO 21/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT MOSTLY UNSETTLED LEVELS FOR
THE PAST 24 HOURS. INTERVALS OF ACTIVE LEVELS OCCURRED AT
HIGH LATITUDES.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE LEVELS. THE ACTIVE
PERIODS ARE EXPECTED IN RESPONSE TO A CORONAL HOLE CURRENTLY
POSITIONED IN THE NORTHWEST QUADRANT.
