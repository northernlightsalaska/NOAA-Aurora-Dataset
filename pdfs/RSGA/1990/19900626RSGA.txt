IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 25/2100Z
TO 26/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 6122 (S23E32),
WHICH REMAINED IN A PHASE OF GRADUAL GROWTH, PRODUCED AN M1/1B
FLARE AT 26/1940Z. THE FLARE WAS ACCOMPANIED BY A MAJOR TYPE II
SWEEP (ESTIMATED SHOCK VELOCITY 3000 KM/SEC) AND A 240 SFU
TENFLARE. A BRIGHT SURGE TO .19RV OCCURRED NEAR S10E90 AT
26/0043Z. NEW REGIONS 6126 (S12E68) AND 6127 (N09E72) WERE
NUMBERED. REGION 6127 PRODUCED SEVERAL SUBFLARES DURING THE LAST
HALF OF THE PERIOD.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. REGION 6122 REMAINS THE PRINCIPAL THREAT FOR
ISOLATED M-FLARE PRODUCTION. C-CLASS FLARES MAY OCCUR FROM
REGIONS 6114 (S09W27) AND 6127.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 25/2100Z TO 26/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO UNSETTLED LEVELS
FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED AT MIDDLE LATITUDES. THE HIGH
LATITUDE FIELD IS EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE. THE
ACTIVE LEVELS ARE EXPECTED IN RESPONSE TO A CORONAL HOLE NOW
LOCATED IN THE NORTHWEST QUADRANT.
