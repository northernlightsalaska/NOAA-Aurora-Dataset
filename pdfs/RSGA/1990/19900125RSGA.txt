FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 025
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 25 JAN 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 24/2100Z
TO 25/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS.
REGION 5900 (S10E23), WHICH CONTAINS A NAKED-EYE SPOT, REMAINS
IMPRESSIVE IN WHITE LIGHT AND H-ALPHA. IT PRODUCED SEVERAL
C-CLASS X-RAY EVENTS. REGION 5892 (S28W21), THE SOURCE OF
YESTERDAY'S M-CLASS ACTIVITY, SIMPLIFIED TODAY. TWO NEW REGIONS
EMERGED ON THE DISK. SURGING WAS REPORTED ON THE WEST LIMB
NEAR N23 AND S27 AS ACTIVE REGIONS ROUNDED THE LIMB.  A
FILAMENT EXTENDING OUT TO .21 RADII COULD BE SEEN ON THE SW
LIMB NEAR S40.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE BASED ON THE FLARE POTENTIAL OF REGION 5900.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 24/2100Z TO 25/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN HIGHLY VARIABLE FOR THE PAST
24 HOURS. PERIODS FROM QUIET THROUGH MINOR STORM HAVE BEEN
EXPERIENCED AT ALL LATITUDES. THESE CONDITIONS WERE EXPECTED
AS INDICATED BY SOLAR FILAMENT DISAPPEARANCES ON 21 AND 22
JANUARY.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE GENERALLY UNSETTLED, WITH PERIODS AT BOTH ACTIVE
AND QUIET LEVELS.  ACTIVE PERIODS ARE EXPECTED TOMORROW IN
RESPONSE TO DISTURBED SOLAR WIND ASSOCIATED WITH A FILAMENT
DISAPPEANCE ON 23 JANUARY.  THEREAFTER, THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD
IS EXPECTED TO PERSIST AT UNSETTLED LEVELS FOR THE REMAINDER OF
THE FORECAST PERIOD.
