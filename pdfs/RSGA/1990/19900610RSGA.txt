FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 161
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 10 JUN 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 09/2100Z
TO 10/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE.  REGION 6089 (N09W19)
PRODUCED TWO M-CLASS EVENTS DURING THE PERIOD.  THE FIRST, AN
M2/2B TENFLARE AT 10/0721Z HAD AN ASSOCIATED TYPE IV SWEEP AND
MODEST RADIO EMISSIONS.  THE SECOND WAS AN M3/1B WHICH
OCCURRED AT 10/1506Z AND HAD A TYPE II SWEEP ASSOCIATED WITH IT.
ADDITIONAL FLARING WAS OBSERVED FROM REGION 6100 (S14E50) AND
REGION 6095 (N23W12) WHICH SHOWED CONSIDERABLE GROWTH DURING THE
PERIOD.  RECENTLY INACTIVE REGION 6090 (S23W65) REEMERGED AS A
SIMPLE SPOT GROUP.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE.  C-CLASS ACTIVITY AND ISOLATED M-CLASS FLARING IS
POSSIBLE FROM REGIONS 6089, 6095, AND 6100.  THE CLOSE PROXIMITY
OF REGIONS 6089 AND 6095 MAKE INTERACTION AND RESULTING MAJOR
FLARING A POSSIBILITY.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 09/2100Z TO 10/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN MOSTLY UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE
THE PAST 24 HOURS.  SEVERAL PERIODS OF MINOR STORMING WERE
OBSERVED AT HIGH LATITUDES.  THE SOURCE OF THIS ACTIVITY IS
POSSIBLY THE CORONAL HOLE IN THE NORTHWEST QUADRANT AND THE
RECENT DISAPPEARING FILAMENTS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE.
