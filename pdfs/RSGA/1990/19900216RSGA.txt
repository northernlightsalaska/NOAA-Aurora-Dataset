IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 15/2100Z
TO 16/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW.  THE LARGEST EVENTS OF THE
PERIOD WERE UNCORRELATED C1 FLARES.  REGIONS 5927 (N20W36) AND
5030 (N12W68), THE TWO LARGEST SPOT GROUPS ON THE DISK, APPEAR
STABLE OR DECLINING.  REGION 5940 (N10E71) AN AXX SPOT GROUP
WAS NUMBERED.  OLD REGION 5900 (S10 L-340) IS DUE BACK ON THE
EAST LIMB BUT THERE HAS BEEN NO EVIDENCE TO INDICATE THAT THE
REGION IS ACTIVE.  BACKGROUND XRAY LEVELS REMAIN LOW.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 15/2100Z TO 16/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT UNSETTLED TO MAJOR STORM
LEVELS.  K'S OF SIX AND SEVEN WERE REPORTED BETWEEN 0000UT
AND 0900UT AND AURORA WAS VISIBLE IN THE CONTINENTAL US.
A CORONAL HOLE NOW NEAR S40W40 MAY BE THE SOURCE OF THIS
DISTURBANCE.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE ACTIVE EARLY IN THE PERIOD, RETURNING TO GEN-
ERALLY UNSETTLED BY THE END OF THE PERIOD.
