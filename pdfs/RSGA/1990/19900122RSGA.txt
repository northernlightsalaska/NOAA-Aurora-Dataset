FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 022
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 22 JAN 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 21/2100Z
TO 22/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE.  M CLASS FLARES
WERE OBSERVED IN REGION 5890 (S10W02), AN M1/1N AT 21/0556Z,
AND IN REGION 5892 (S28E20), AN M1/1N AT 21/1200Z.  BOTH REGIONS
APPEAR TO BE DECAYING SLOWLY.  ADDITIONAL TRAILER SPOTS HAVE
ROTATED INTO VIEW BEHIND REGION 5900 (S08E60).  THIS REGION
MAY HAVE A DELTA CONFIGURATION LOCATED NEAR THE LEADER SPOT
COMPLEX, BUT SO FAR HAS ONLY PRODUCED SMALL SUBFLARES.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE TO HIGH.  THE PRIMARY CANDIDATES FOR MAJOR ACTIVITY
ARE REGIONS 5890, 5892, AND 5900.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 21/2100Z TO 22/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS MOSTLY UNSETTLED WITH ISOLATED
PERIODS OF ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM AT SOME HIGH LATITUDE STATIONS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE.  INCREASED ACTIVITY ON
24 AND 25 JANUARY IS POSSIBLE DUE TO FILAMENT DISRUPTIONS.
