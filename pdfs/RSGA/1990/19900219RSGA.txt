JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 19 FEB 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 18/2100Z
TO 19/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW.  SEVERAL LOW LEVEL C CLASS
FLARES HAVE OCCURRED SINCE YESTERDAY.  MOST OF THESE FLARES
WERE IN REGION 5942 (S35E53).  THIS REGION HAS BEEN FLARE BRIGHT
MOST OF TODAY AND IS EMERGING RAPIDLY IN WHITE LIGHT.  NEW
REGION 5947 (S16E71) ALSO PRODUCED A NUMBER OF SUBFLARES.
THIS REGION APPEARS TO BE A FAIRLY LARGE, COMPACT GROUP OF
MODERATE COMPLEXITY.  REGION 5927 (N21W77) ALSO CONTRIBUTED TO
TODAY'S ACTIVITY WITH A FEW SUBFLARES AND SURGES.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE.  THE LIKELIHOOD OF M CLASS ACTIVITY IS
INCREASING.  AN ISOLATED MAJOR FLARE IS POSSIBLE IN REGIONS
5942 AND 5947.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 18/2100Z TO 19/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT UNSETTLED TO STORM LEVELS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT MOSTLY ACTIVE TO STORM LEVELS AS THE
DISTURBANCE CONTINUES.
