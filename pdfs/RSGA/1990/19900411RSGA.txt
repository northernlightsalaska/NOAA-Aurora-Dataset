JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 11 APR 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 10/2100Z
TO 11/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. REGION 6017 (N22E18)
ENTERED A PERIOD OF SIGNIFICANT GROWTH AND PRODUCED SEVERAL
SUBFLARES. THE LARGEST OF THESE WAS A C2 AT 11/1556Z. REGION
6007 (N22W18) CONTINUED TO DECAY. AN OPTICALLY UNCORRELATED
TYPE II SWEEP OCCURRED AT 11/0749Z; REPORTED SHOCK SPEEDS RANGED
FROM 600-1000 KM/SEC. TWO NEW A-TYPE REGIONS WERE ASSIGNED:
6019 (N33W62) AND 6020 (N04E15).
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW. C-CLASS SUBFLARE ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED FROM REGIONS 6007
AND 6017. REGION 6017 ALSO PROVIDES A SLIGHT CHANCE FOR AN
M-CLASS FLARE.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 10/2100Z TO 11/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT ACTIVE TO SEVERE STORM
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS. A SUDDEN IMPULSE REGISTERING
69 NANOTESLA WAS RECORDED BY THE BOULDER USGS MAGNETOMETER
AT 11/0533Z. THE STORMING MAY BE A CONSEQUENCE OF A 48 DEGREE
FILAMENT WHICH DISAPPEARED FROM THE SOUTHEAST QUADRANT
SOMETIME BETWEEN 5-6 APRIL.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT ACTIVE TO UNSETTLED LEVELS AT MIDDLE
LATITUDES AND AT ACTIVE LEVELS AT HIGHER LATITUDES. PERIODS OF
MINOR STORMING MAY OCCUR DURING THE FIRST HALF OF TOMORROW AS
THE PRESENT DISTURBANCE SUBSIDES.
