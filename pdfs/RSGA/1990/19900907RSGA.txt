JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 250 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 07 SEP 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 06/2100Z
TO 07/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE.  AN UNCORRELATED
M1 AT 2151Z MAY HAVE BEEN FROM REGION 6233 WHICH PASSED
OVER THE WEST LIMB ON 06 SEPTEMBER.  REGION 6241 (S14W47)
PRODUCED A C7/1F WITH A TYPE II AND IV RADIO SWEEP AT 0539Z.
THIS REGION HAS SHOWN GROWTH AND SURGING THROUGHOUT THE DAY.
A SIZABLE FILAMENT DISAPPEARED FROM S19W07 AROUND 1200Z TODAY.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MOSTLY LOW.  REGION 6241 MAY BE ABLE TO PRODUCE AN ISOLATED
M-CLASS FLARE ALTHOUGH ITS NOT LIKELY.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 06/2100Z TO 07/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT MOSTLY UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS.  HIGH LATITUDES EXPERIENCED
SEVERAL PERIODS OF MINOR TO MAJOR STORM CONDITIONS FROM
0900 TO 1800Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO CONTINUE AT UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE CONDITIONS.
A SLIGHT INCREASE IN ACTIVITY IS POSSIBLE STARTING ON DAY TWO
OF THE FORECAST PERIOD DUE TO THE COMBINED EFFECTS OF TODAY'S
C7/1F AND A PROPERLY POSITIONED CORONAL HOLE.
