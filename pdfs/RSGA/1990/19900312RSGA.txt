JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 12 MAR 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 11/2100Z
TO 12/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW.  SEVERAL C CLASS X-RAY
FLARES WERE OBSERVED DURING THE PAST 24 HOURS.  MOST OF THESE
FLARES ORIGINATED IN REGION 5969 (N32E40).  THIS REGION IS A
DEVELOPING D-TYPE SUNSPOT GROUP AT PRESENT.  THE REMAINING
SUNSPOT GROUPS HAVE BEEN QUIET.  NEW REGION 5972 (S17W33) WAS
NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW.  AN ISOLATED M CLASS FLARE IS POSSIBLE IN REGION 5969.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 11/2100Z TO 12/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS QUIET TO UNSETTLED UNTIL 12/1501Z
WHEN A STRONG SUDDEN IMPULSE (24 NANOTESLA AT BOULDER) WAS
OBSERVED AT MIDDLE LATUTUDE STATIONS.  THE FIELD HAS BEEN AT
STORM LEVELS SINCE THAT TIME.  THE ORIGIN OF THIS DISTURBANCE
IS NOT CERTAIN.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT STORM LEVELS FOR THE NEXT 24 HOURS.  MOSTLY
ACTIVE CONDITIONS ARE EXPECTED FOR THE REMAINDER OF THE PERIOD.
