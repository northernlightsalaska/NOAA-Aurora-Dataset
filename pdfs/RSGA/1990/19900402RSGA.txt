FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 092
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 02 APR 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 01/2100Z
TO 02/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 6001 (N21E35)
PRODUCED TODAY'S ONLY M-CLASS EVENT: AN M1/1N AT 1706Z. THE
FLARE WAS ASSOCIATED WITH MODEST DISCRETE RADIO BURSTS. REGION
6001 DOMINATES THE DISK IN SIZE AND COMPLEXITY AND APPEARS TO
BE IN A GRADUAL GROWTH PHASE AT THIS TIME. THE REMAINDER OF
THE DAY WAS CHARACTERIZED BY A FEW SMALL SUBFLARES, AND THE
REMAINDER OF THE SOLAR DISK WAS ESSENTIALLY QUIET AND STABLE.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE. REGION 6001 SHOWS CONTINUED POTENTIAL TO PRODUCE ADD-
ITIONAL ISOLATED M-CLASS EVENTS, WITH  PERHAPS A SLIGHT CHANCE
FOR A MAJOR FLARE. OLD REGION 5969 (N32, L-074), WHICH PRODUCED
1 X-CLASS FLARE AND 2 M-CLASS FLARES IS DUE TO RETURN ON THE
THIRD DAY (05 APRIL) AND MAY INCREASE SOLAR ACTIVITY LEVELS
SOMEWHAT AT THAT TIME.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 01/2100Z TO 02/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS GENERALLY QUIET FROM 01/2100Z
THROUGH ABOUT 02/1030Z. CONDITIONS WERE GENERALLY UNSETTLED
AT MID-LATITUDES AND WERE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE AT HIGHER
LATITUDES FOR THE REMAINDER OF THE PERIOD.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE GENERALLY UNSETTLED FOR THE NEXT THREE DAYS. A
SLIGHT UPWARD TREND IS POSSIBLE ON THE THIRD DAY IN REPONSE TO
THE LONG DURATION M4 FLARE OF 01 APRIL.
