IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 28/2100Z
TO 29/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. REGION 6133 (N18E66)
PRODUCED NUMEROUS SUBFLARES INCLUDING FIVE C-CLASS EVENTS. THE
LARGEST OF THESE WAS A C6/SF AT 29/1319Z. REGION 6126 (S13E27)
DISPLAYED A MODEST INCREASE IN SIZE AND MAGNETIC COMPLEXITY AND
YIELDED SEVERAL SUBFLARES. REGIONS 6125 (N06W00), 6127 (N09E34),
AND 6131 (S20E40) ALSO DISPLAYED MINOR GROWTH. IN CONTRAST,
REGION 6122 (S21W06) CONTINUED A GRADUAL DECLINE IN SIZE AND
MAGNETIC COMPLEXITY. NO NEW REGIONS WERE NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE. REGIONS 6122, 6125, 6126, AND 6133 COULD EACH PRODUCE
AN ISOLATED M-CLASS FLARE.
