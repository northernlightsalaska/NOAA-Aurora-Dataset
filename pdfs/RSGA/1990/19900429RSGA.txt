JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 29 APR 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 28/2100Z
TO 29/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. ONLY A FEW SUBFLARES WERE
OBSERVED DURING THE PERIOD AND MOST OF THESE WERE FROM REGION
6042 (S19W62). TWO NEW REGIONS WERE ASSIGNED: 6046 NEAR S10E54
AND 6047 NEAR N24E27. THE REMAINDER OF THE DISK WAS REMARKABLY
QUIET AND STABLE.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW. REGION 6042 MAY CONTINUE TO PRODUCE OCCASIONAL SUBFLARES.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 28/2100Z TO 29/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS GENERALLY UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE AT
MID-LATITUDES. HIGH LATITUDES WERE AT PREDOMINANTLY ACTIVE
TO MINOR STORM LEVELS. THE DISTURBANCE MAY HAVE BEEN CAUSED BY
EFFECTS FROM A RECURRENT CORONAL HOLE. THE GREATER THAN
10MEV PROTON EVENT WHICH BEGAN AT 28/1005Z ENDED TODAY AT
29/0820Z. THE PEAK FLUX WAS 150 PFU AT 28/1735Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE MOSTLY ACTIVE TOMORROW AS THE CURRENT DISTUBANCE
CONTINUES AND AS EFFECTS RESULT FROM YESTERDAY'S DISAPPEARING
FILAMENT/LONG DURATION X-RAY EVENT. CONDITIONS SHOULD GRADUALLY
SUBSIDE TO UNSETTLED LEVELS OVER THE SECOND AND THIRD DAYS.
