IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 16/2100Z
TO 17/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 6063 (N34E03)
PRODUCED AN M1/SF AT 17/1032Z. THIS REGION MAINTAINED ITS HIGH
DEGREE OF MAGNETIC COMPLEXITY DISPLAYING A DOUBLE DELTA
CONFIGURATION WITHIN ITS MAIN PENUMBRAL MASS, BUT WAS RELATIVELY
STABLE. AN OPTICALLY UNCORRELATED M1 OCCURRED AT 17/0330Z
ASSOCIATED WITH MINOR DISCRETE FREQUENCY BURSTS ACROSS THE
SPECTRUM. REGION 6064 (S15E12) APPEARED TO STABILIZE AFTER
YESTERDAY'S PERIOD OF RAPID GROWTH AND REMAINED MODERATELY
COMPLEX IN MAGNETIC ASPECTS. NEW REGION 6069 (S09E70) WAS
NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE TO HIGH. REGION 6063 MAY PRODUCE ISOLATED MAJOR FLARE
ACTIVITY. REGION 6064 DISPLAYS POTENTIAL FOR M-CLASS ACTIVITY.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 16/2100Z TO 17/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET LEVELS FOR THE PAST
24 HOURS. THE SATELLITE LEVEL PROTON ENHANCEMENT AT GREATER
THAN 10 MEV MENTIONED IN YESTERDAY'S REPORT GRADUALLY
DECLINED TOWARD BACKGROUND LEVELS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE LEVELS. THE 15 MAY X1/3B
FLARE MAY PROVIDE ACTIVE CONDITIONS DURING THE FIRST HALF OF
THE PERIOD.
