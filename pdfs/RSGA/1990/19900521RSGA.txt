FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 141
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 21 MAY 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 20/2100Z
TO 21/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 6063 (N35W42)
PRODUCED TODAY'S LARGEST EVENT: AN M4/1B AT 0132Z WHICH WAS AC-
COMPANIED BY A TYPE II AND A TYPE IV RADIO SWEEP. THE REGION
PRODUCED ADDITIONAL FLARES DURING THE PERIOD INCLUDING AN M1/2N
AT 0008Z. THE GROUP APPEARS TO BE FRAGMENTING SLIGHTLY BUT
CONTINUES TO POSSESS STRONG MAGNETIC FIELDS AND GRADIENTS IN
A COMPLICATED DELTA STRUCTURE. REGION 6064 (S16W38), THE LARGEST
GROUP ON THE DISK (1510 MILLIONTHS), PRODUCED NUMEROUS UPPER
LEVEL C-FLARES DURING THE DAY AS WELL AS AN M1/1N AT 0513Z.
THE REMAINDER OF THE DISK AND LIMBS WERE RELATIVELY CALM,
ALTHOUGH REGIONS 6070 (S19E28) AND 6071 (S12E37) SHOWED SIGNS
OF GROWTH.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE TO HIGH. REGIONS 6063 AND 6064 ARE EXPECTED TO KEEP
ACTIVITY AT MODERATE LEVELS WITH A FAIR CHANCE FOR AN ISOLATED
MAJOR FLARE.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 20/2100Z TO 21/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD RANGED FROM QUIET TO MINOR STORM LEVELS.
CONDITIONS BEGAN AT ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM LEVELS, BECAME QUIET
AROUND 0300Z, BUT WENT BACK TO ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM LEVELS
AFTER A SUDDEN IMPULSE (28 GAMMA IN BOULDER), WHICH OCCURRED
AT 1021UT.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE GENERALLY UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE OVER THE NEXT
THREE DAYS. THERE MAY BE A SLIGHT ENHANCEMENT IN ACTIVITY ON
THE SECOND DAY IN RESPONSE TO TODAY'S M4/1B FLARE.
