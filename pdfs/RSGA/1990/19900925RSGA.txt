JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 268 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 25 SEP 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 24/2100Z
TO 25/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 6280 (N13E05)
PRODUCED AN M1/1B FLARE AT 0228Z. THE GROUP APPEARS TO BE DEV-
ELOPING NEW SPOTS AND HAS A SLIGHTLY COMPLEX BETA-GAMMA MAGNETIC
CONFIGURATION. THE GROUP PRODUCED ADDITIONAL SMALL FLARES DURING
THE PERIOD. REGION 6283 (S12E48) CONTINUES TO BE THE LARGEST
GROUP ON THE DISK BUT COULD ONLY PRODUCE A COUPLE OF SUBFLARES
AND CONTINUES TO BE MAGNETICALLY SIMPLE. THE REMAINING REGIONS
WERE SIMPLE AND QUIET.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
PREDOMINANTLY LOW, BUT WITH A CONTINUED CHANCE FOR AN ISOLATED
M-CLASS FLARE FROM REGION 6280, AND POSSIBLY FROM REGION 6283.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 24/2100Z TO 25/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS QUIET TO UNSETTLED.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED WITH A POSSIBILITY FOR ACTIVE CON-
DITIONS TOMORROW IF EFFECTS FROM THE M2 FLARE OF 23 SEPTEMBER
SHOULD MATERIALIZE. THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS EXPECTED TO BE
GENERALLY UNSETTLED FOR THE REMAINDER OF THE FORECAST PERIOD.
