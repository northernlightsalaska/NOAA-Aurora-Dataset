FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 145
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 25 MAY 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 24/2100Z
TO 25/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW.  THE MOST PRODUCTIVE
REGION WAS A NEW REGION, 6077 (N21E67), PRODUCING SEVERAL
SUBFLARES.  REGION 6064 (S17W89) PRODUCED ADDITIONAL
SUBFLARES INCLUDING TODAY'S LARGEST EVENT, A C6 AT
2343Z.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE TOMORROW AND DROP TO LOW ON DAY 2 AS REGIONS 6063
AND 6064 ROTATE BEYOND THE LIMB.  REGION 6077 IS SHOWING SIGNS
OF ACTIVITY AND GROWTH AND IS EXPECTED TO PRODUCE ADDITIONAL
SMALL FLARES.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 24/2100Z TO 25/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN MOSTLY ACTIVE.  A SUDDEN
IMPULSE WAS OBSERVED AT 0307Z TODAY.  THE ACTIVITY IS
BELIEVED TO HAVE BEEN CAUSED BY THE X5 FLARE THAT OCCURRED
ON 21 MAY.  DUE TO THE X9/1B FLARE THAT OCCURRED 24 MAY,
A PROTON EVENT BEGAN 2125Z ON 24 MAY WITH A MAXIMUM FLUX
OF 180 PFU AT GREATER THAN 10MEV AT 0115Z.  INCREASES
WERE OBSERVED ON HIGH ENERGY PARTICLES: GREATER THAN 100MEV
PARTICLES ATTAINED 19 PFU AT 2155Z ON 24 MAY.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE GENERALLY ACTIVE FOR THE NEXT THREE DAYS.  ACTIVE
CONDITIONS ON THE FIRST DAY ARE EXPECTED DUE TO RECURRENT
CORONAL HOLE EFFECTS.  ACTIVE CONDITIONS ON THE SECOND DAY
AND INTO THE THIRD DAY WILL BE IN RESPONSE TO THE X9/1B FLARE.
