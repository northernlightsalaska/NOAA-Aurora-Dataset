FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 183
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 02 JUL 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 01/2100Z
TO 02/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. THE PERIOD WAS
CHARACTERIZED BY THE OCCURRENCE OF NUMEROUS SUBFLARES OFFSET
BY THE OCCASIONAL C-CLASS EVENT. REGION 6126 (S12W13) DISPLAYED
MINOR GROWTH TODAY WHILE CONTRIBUTING THE MAJORITY OF THE DAY'S
LOW-LEVEL ACTIVITY. REGIONS 6124 (S08W88) AND 6129 (N13W92)
PROVIDED THE SOURCE FOR TWO OF THE DAY'S MORE ENERGETIC EVENTS:
A C7/SF FLARE AT 02/1225Z FROM 6124 AND A C6/SF FLARE AT
02/0457Z FROM 6129 WHICH ALSO INCLUDED AN ERUPTIVE PROMINENCE.
REGIONS 6133 (N18E32) AND 6127 (N08W05) REMAIN THE LARGEST
ON THE DISK IN TERMS OF SPOT AREA HOWEVER THEY HAVE CONTRIBUTED
VERY LITTLE TO ACTIVITY LEVELS. AN UNCORRELATED C9 X-RAY
EVENT, THE LARGEST OF THE PERIOD, WAS NOTED AT 02/1938Z. NO
NEW REGIONS WERE ASSIGNED TODAY.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE. REGIONS 6126 AND 6133 REMAIN THE BEST CANDIDATES
FOR AN ISOLATED M-CLASS EVENT.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 01/2100Z TO 02/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO UNSETTLED
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED.
