IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 29/2100Z
TO 30/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE.  THE DAY'S ONLY M
FLARE OCCURRED AT 1927UT (THE MAX TIME OF THE FLARE).  THIS
EVENT APPEARS TO HAVE ORIGINATED IN REGION 5983 (S31W82).  THE
REMAINING EVENTS OF THE DAY WERE SMALL C FLARES, MOSTLY FROM
DEPARTING REGION 5984 (S14 W93).  REGION 5998 (S16W13) IS
GROWING SLOWLY BUT ACTIVITY HAS BEEN MINOR.  THE SPOTS IN
REGION 5999 (S10E57) APPEAR TO BE OF THE SAME POLARITY, POSSIBLY
THE REMNANTS OF TWO OLD REGIONS.  NEWLY NUMBERED REGION 6001
(N20E71) ALSO APPEARS TO BE A DECAYING REGION.  REGION 6002
(S13E30) WAS ALSO NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE.  THERE IS STILL A SLIGHT CHANCE FOR MORE ENER-
GETIC ACTIVITY FROM THE REGIONS AT OR BEHIND THE WEST LIMB.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 29/2100Z TO 30/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT UNSETTLED TO MAJOR
STORM LEVELS.  THE FIELD BECAME ACTIVE LATE ON THE 29TH AND
WAS AT STORM LEVELS THROUGH MOST OF TODAY, K'S OF 7 WERE
OBSERVED AT MID-LATITUDES AND K'S OF 8 AT HIGH LATITUDES.
THIS STORM IS BEING ATTRIBUTED TO THE M4/2N FLARE FROM REGION
5988 (S06W37 AT 28/0751UT,THE TIME OF THE FLARE).  THE FIELD
IS CURRENTLY RETURNING TO PRE-STORM LEVELS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT MINOR STORM LEVELS EARLY IN THE PERIOD,
DECLINING TO ACTIVE AND UNSETTLED LEVELS LATE IN THE PERIOD.
