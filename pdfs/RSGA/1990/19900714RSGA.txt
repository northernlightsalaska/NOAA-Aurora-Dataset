JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 195 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 14 JUL 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 13/2100Z
TO 14/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW.  THE LARGEST EVENT OF THE
DAY WAS AN UNCORRELATED C1.8 FLARE AT 0254Z.  REGION 6153
(N15W50) WAS THE MOST PRODUCTIVE REGION PRODUCING A C-CLASS
FLARE AND SUBFLARES.  A BRIGHT SURGE ON THE LIMB OCCURRED
AT 2329Z FROM REGION 6138 (N22) WHICH HAS ROTATED OFF THE
DISK.  REGION 6156 (S27E25) WAS NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE.  REGIONS 6150 (N07W45) AND REGION 6153
STILL HAVE POTENTIAL TO PRODUCE AN M-CLASS FLARE.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 13/2100Z TO 14/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN QUIET TO UNSETTLED.  PROTONS
AT GREATER THAN 10MEV WERE SLIGHTLY ENHANCED EARLY IN THE DAY.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE GENERALLY UNSETTLED WITH SOME MINOR STORMING
ON THE THIRD DAY AT THE HIGHER LATITUDES.
