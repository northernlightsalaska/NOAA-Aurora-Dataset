JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 343 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 09 DEC 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 08/2100Z
TO 09/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. AN M2/1N FLARE
ERUPTED FROM NEWLY ASSIGNED REGION 6410 (N05E71) AT 09/0821Z.
THIS EVENT WAS FOLLOWED AT 09/0928Z BY AN M1/1F FLARE FROM
REGION 6397 (N10W21) WHICH REMAINED GENERALLY STABLE THROUGHOUT
MOST OF THE PERIOD. REGION 6398 (N14W43) DISPLAYED MODEST
SPOT GROWTH WHILE CONTRIBUTING THE MAJORITY OF THE DAY'S
C-CLASS FLARE ACTIVITY. REGION 6395 (N18W25) ALSO SHOWED SOME
SPOT GROWTH BUT REMAINED STABLE. BOTH REGIONS 6395 AND 6397
SHOW SOME MAGNETIC COMPLEXITY. NEW REGION 6411 (S23E61) WAS
ALSO ASSIGNED TODAY.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE. THE CLUSTER OF REGIONS IN THE NORTHWEST HEMISPHERE
(6395, 6397, AND 6398) POSE THE BEST THREAT FOR M-FLARE
PRODUCTION.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 08/2100Z TO 09/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO UNSETTLED
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE QUIET TO UNSETTLED.
