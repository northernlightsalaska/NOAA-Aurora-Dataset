FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 017
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 17 JAN 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 16/2100Z
TO 17/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. NEW REGION 5890 (S12E63)
PRODUCED TODAY'S LARGEST EVENT, A C7/SF AT 0724Z. THE REMAINDER
OF THE DAY WAS CHARACTERIZED BY OCCASIONAL C-CLASS SUBFLARES
FROM A VARIETY OF REGIONS. THREE ADDITIONAL NEW REGIONS WERE
ASSIGNED TODAY INCLUDING 5892 (S27E78), THE RETURN OF OLD
REGION 5852 WHICH WAS ACTIVE ON THE PREVIOUS ROTATION BUT
APPEARS TO BE STABLE THIS TIME AROUND. SURGING WAS OBSERVED ON
EAST LIMB AT N21, S08, AND S13.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. THE MAIN THREATS FOR M-CLASS ACTIVITY ARE FROM
REGION 5882 (N21E01), WHICH CONTINUES TO POSSESS FAIRLY STRONG
MAGNETIC FIELDS, AND FROM NEW REGION 5890.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 16/2100Z TO 17/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS QUIET WITH OCCASIONAL UNSETTLED
PERIODS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE GENERALLY UNSETTLED FOR THE NEXT THREE DAYS.
ACTIVE CONDITIONS ARE POSSIBLE AT HIGH LATITUDES ON THE SECOND
DAY IN RESPONSE TO THE FILAMENT WHICH DISAPPEARED ON 15 JANUARY.
