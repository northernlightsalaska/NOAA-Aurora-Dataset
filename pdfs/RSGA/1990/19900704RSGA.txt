FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 185
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 04 JUL 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 03/2100Z
TO 04/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH. THE HIGHLIGHT OF THE
PERIOD WAS A PAIR OF M-CLASS EVENTS ERUPTING IN TANDEM IN
OPPOSITE HEMISPHERES OF THE DISK. REGION 6138 (N23E23)
STARTED OFF THE PROCESSION AT 04/1654Z WITH AN M7/2B FLARE
ACCOMPANIED BY DARK SURGING AND A 670 SOLAR FLUX UNIT TENFLARE.
THIS REGION DISPLAYED SIGNIFICANT GROWTH IN SPOT AREA AND
MAGNETIC COMPLEXITY IN THE PAST 24 HOURS, ESPECIALLY IN THE
LEADING PORTION OF THE REGION WHERE THE MAJOR FLARE LATER
ERUPTED. REGION 6131 (S21W26) PRODUCED THE SECOND M EVENT:
AN M4/2B FLARE AT 04/1743Z. REGIONS 6126 (S11W41) AND 6133
(N18E04) REMAINED GENERALLY STABLE DESPITE THEIR APPARENT
COMPLEXITY.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE WITH REGIONS 6126, 6131, 6133, AND 6138 SHOWING
PROMISE FOR ISOLATED M-CLASS FLARE PRODUCTION.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 03/2100Z TO 04/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO UNSETTLED
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE WITH ACTIVE CONDITIONS
POSSIBLE DUE TO EFFECTS FROM A CORONAL HOLE.
