FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 099
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 09 APR 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 08/2100Z
TO 09/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW.  ONLY A FEW SUBFLARES
WERE OBSERVED SINCE YESTERDAY, ALL OF WHICH HAD MODEST X-RAY
OUTPUT.  REGION 6007 (N24E04) APPEARS TO HAVE SIMPLIFIED.
NEW REGIONS 6015 (N12W31) AND 6016 (S18E34) WERE NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE.  SMALL C CLASS X-RAY FLARES ARE EXPECTED.
AN ISOLATED M CLASS FLARE IS POSSIBLE IN REGION 6001 (N21W53),
6007 (N24E04), OR 6012 (N30E29).
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 08/2100Z TO 09/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS QUIET UNTIL ABOUT 09/0845Z WHEN
THE FIELD GRADUALLY BECAME DISTURBED.  MAJOR STORM CONDITIONS
WERE EXPERIENCED UNTIL 09/1500Z.  MINOR STORM TO UNSETTLED
LEVELS WERE OBSERVED FOR THE REMAINDER OF THE DAY.  A PROTON
EVENT AT GREATER THAN 10 MEV BEGAN AT 07/2240Z, REACHED A PEAK
OF 18 PFU AT 08/1330Z AND ENDED AT 08/1830Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE ACTIVE TO UNSETTLED.
