JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.

IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 12/2100Z
TO 13/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 6063 (N34E53)
HAS ROTATED COMPLETELY INTO VIEW NOW SHOWING A LARGE SPOT
AREA. THE EXTENT OF THE REGIONS MAGNETIC STRUCTURE IS STILL
SOMEWHAT VAGUE, DUE TO IT'S LOCATION NEAR THE LIMB, HOWEVER
PAST FLARE HISTORY SUGGESTS MODEST COMPLEXITY. A C5/SN FLARE
AT 13/1706Z WAS IT'S ONLY SIGNIFICANT EVENT THIS PERIOD.
REGION 6064 (S15E66) A RAPIDLY DEVELOPING AREA, PRODUCED THE
LARGEST EVENT OF THE PERIOD: AN M2/1N FLARE AT 13/1246Z.
NUMEROUS SUBFLARES HAVE ALSO BEEN ATTRIBUTED TO THIS REGION.
REGION 6049 (N18W61) CONTINUED IT'S GRADUAL, QUIET DECAY.
AN UNCORRELATED M1 X-RAY EVENT WAS OBSERVED AT 13/0333Z.
NEW REGION 6066 (S17E30) WAS ASSIGNED TODAY.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE TO HIGH. REGION 6063 REMAINS THE PREDOMINANT CHOICE
FOR MAJOR FLARE PRODUCTION. REGIONS 6049 AND 6064 MAY ADD TO
M-CLASS ACTIVITY LEVELS.
