JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 262 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 19 SEP 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 18/2100Z
TO 19/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. NEW REGION 6281
(N05E81) PRODUCED AN M2/2N FLARE AT 19/0908Z WITH MINOR RADIO
BURSTS AND A BRIGHT SURGE TO 0.17R ABOVE THE LIMB. AT THIS
TIME, ONLY A FEW SMALL SPOTS ARE VISIBLE AT THIS LOCATION. NEW
REGION 6280 (N13E83) SHOWED ITSELF AS A SMALL TO MODERATE
CLASS H SPOT WHICH IS LOCATED IN THE AREA OF OLD REGION 6233.
NON-ENERGETIC SURGING OCCURRED AT THIS LOCATION. REGION 6272
(S13E09) WAS STABLE AND REGION 6279 (S08E32) CONTINUED TO
GROW FOLLOWING ITS EMERGENCE YESTERDAY. SMALL NEW REGION
6282 (N25E56) EMERGED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY SHOULD CONTINUE
AT A PREDOMINANTLY MODERATE LEVEL FOR THE NEXT THREE DAYS.
ISOLATED M-CLASS FLARES ARE POSSIBLE FROM REGIONS 6272, 6280,
6281, AND 6279 IF GROWTH CONTINUES.  A SLIGHT POSSIBILITY
EXISTS FOR A MAJOR FLARE, PRIMARILY FROM REGION 6272.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 18/2100Z TO 19/2100Z:
GEOMAGNETIC FIELD CONDITIONS AT MID LATITUDES WERE QUIET TO
UNSETTLED FOR THE MAJORITY OF THE PERIOD.  HIGH LATITUDES
EXPERIENCED ACTIVE CONDITIONS FOR A LARGE PART OF THE PERIOD
WITH UNSETTLED LEVELS BETWEEN 1200Z-1630Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD
SHOULD BECOME GENERALLY ACTIVE FOR THE NEXT THREE DAYS.
THIS DISTURBANCE SHOULD BEGIN EARLY ON 20 SEP IN RESPONSE
TO THE M5 FLARE FROM REGION 6272 ON 17 SEP AND DIMINISH
THROUGHOUT THE PERIOD.
