IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 30/2100Z
TO 31/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW.  THE LARGEST EVENTS OF THE
DAY WERE A C8 FROM REGION 5988 (S05W87) AT 1109UT AND AN UN-
CORRELATED C7 AT 0916UT.  MOST DISK REGIONS APPEAR STABLE OR
DECLINING.  REGION 6001 (N20E59) IS NOW FULLY ON THE DISK AND
APPEARS TO BE A LARGE, EXTENDED BUT RELATIVELY QUIET REGION.
REGION 5998 (S16W27) HAS HAD AFS REPORTED BUT MAY HAVE REACHED
ITS PEAK DEVELOPMENT.  REGION 6003 (26E72) WAS NUMBERED.  THERE
ARE SEVERAL SMALL CORONAL HOLES IN THE NORTHWEST QUADRANT OF
THE SUN, LOCATED NEAR REGION 6000 (N20W49).  THERE MAY BE OTHER
SMALL CORONAL HOLES NEAR CENTRAL MERIDIAN, JUST SOUTH OF THE
SOLAR EQUATOR.

LOW.  THERE IS STILL A SLIGHT CHANCE FOR ENERGETIC ACTIVITY
FROM THE REGIONS AT OR BEYOND THE WEST LIMB AS WELL AS REGIONS
6001 AND 5998.
