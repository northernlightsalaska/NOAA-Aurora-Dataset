IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 12/2100Z
TO 13/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 5882 (N20E53)
GENERATED AN M3/0N AT 12/2112Z. THIS WAS THE ONLY M-LEVEL EVENT
OF THE INTERVAL AND IT CAME FROM WHAT APPEARS TO BE THE MOST
COMPLEX REGION ON THE DISK. REGION 5871 (S23W74), THE SITE OF
M-CLASS ACTIVITY THE LAST FEW DAYS, GAVE JUST A SMALL SUBFLARE
AS IT DECAYED. SURGING ACCOMPANIED THE WEST LIMB PASSAGE OF
REGION 5873 (N26W94). TWO NEW REGIONS WERE NUMBERED,
5885 (S07W35) AND 5886 (N42E36), MAKING A TOTAL OF 14 SPOT
GROUPS NOW VISIBLE.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY SHOULD BE
MODERATE. REGIONS 5882 AND 5871 ARE STILL PRIME CANDIDATES
FOR AN OCCASIONAL M-CLASS FLARE.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 12/2100Z TO 13/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO UNSETTLED
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO REMAIN AT QUIET TO UNSETTLED CONDITIONS FOR THE
DURATION OF THE FORECAST PERIOD.
