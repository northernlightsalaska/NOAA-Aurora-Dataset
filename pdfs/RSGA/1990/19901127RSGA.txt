JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 331 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 27 NOV 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 26/2100Z
TO 27/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH. REGION 6368 (N17W90)
PRODUCED AN M7/1N FLARE AT 26/2317Z AS IT ROUNDED THE LIMB.
AN UNCORRELATED M1 X-RAY EVENT WAS NOTED AT 27/0512Z AND
WAS MOST LIKELY A PRODUCT OF THIS REGION WHICH. SIGNIFICANT
SURGING WAS NOTED THROUGHOUT MOST OF THE DAY FROM THIS AREA,
OCCASIONALLY BECOMING EVENT LEVEL. REGION 6377 (S05W42)
CONTINUED TO SHOW SLIGHT SPOT GROWTH. THIS AREA PRODUCED ONLY
SPORADIC C-CLASS SUBFLARING. NEW REGIONS 6384 (N10E21), 6385
(S16E73), AND 6386 (N24W04) WERE ASSIGNED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. REGION 6368 MAY PRODUCE AN ISOLATED M-CLASS
FLARE AS IT TRANSITS THE LIMB. REGION 6377 SHOULD CONTINUE TO
CONTRIBUTE OCCASIONAL C-CLASS EVENTS.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 26/2100Z TO 27/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT UNSETTLED TO MAJOR STORM
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS. A SUDDEN IMPULSE OF 21
NANOTESLAS WAS MEASURED ON THE BOULDER MAGNETOMETER AT
26/2332Z. MAGNETOPAUSE CROSSINGS WERE NOTED ON BOTH GOES 6
AND 7 MAGNETOMETERS STARTING AT 27/1555Z. DISAPPEARING
FILAMENTS MAY BE A POSSIBLE SOURCE FOR THIS ACTIVITY.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BEGIN THE PERIOD AT ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM LEVELS
WITH ACTIVE TO UNSETTLED CONDITIONS EXPECTED THE LAST TWO DAYS.
