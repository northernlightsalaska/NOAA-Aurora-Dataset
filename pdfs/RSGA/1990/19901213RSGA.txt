JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 347 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 13 DEC 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 12/2100Z
TO 13/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE.  ONLY TWO M-CLASS
FLARES WERE OBSERVED SINCE YESTERDAY.  AN M1/SF OCCURRED IN
REGION 6410 (N05E18) AT 13/1359Z.  GROWTH IN THIS REGION HAS
SUBSIDED AND SOME SIMPLIFICATION IS EVIDENT IN THE LEADER SPOT
COMPLEX.  REGION 6398 (N13W92) PRODUCED AN M1/SF AT 13/1630Z.
SEVERAL SMALL FLARES WERE OBSERVED SINCE YESTERDAY, MANY OF
WHICH WERE IN REGION 6412 (N18E47).  THE MOST SIGNIFICANT WAS
A 1F FLARE ASSOCIATED WITH A 2695 MHZ RADIO BURST OF 560 SFU
AT 13/1024Z.  THIS REGION IS LARGE BUT APPEARS MAGNETICALLY
SIMPLE.  NEW REGION 6415 (N09E35) WAS NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE.  M-CLASS ACTIVITY IS POSSIBLE IN REGIONS 6410 AND
6412.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 12/2100Z TO 13/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS QUIET TO UNSETTLED FOR MOST OF THE
24 HOUR PERIOD.  ACTIVE TO STORM CONDITIONS WERE OBSERVED FROM
13/0600-0900Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE QUIET TO UNSETTLED FOR THE NEXT 24 HOURS AND
UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE FOR THE REMAINDER OF THE FORECAST PERIOD.
