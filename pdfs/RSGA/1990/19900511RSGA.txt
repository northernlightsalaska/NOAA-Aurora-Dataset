IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 10/2100Z
TO 11/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH. AN X2 X-RAY EVENT WAS
OBSERVED AT 11/0548Z FROM BEHIND THE EAST LIMB NEAR LATITUDE
N34. TWO REGIONS ARE ROTATING ONTO THE DISK NEAR THAT
LOCATION: 6062 (N30E67) AND 6063 (N34E79) BOTH ASSIGNED TODAY.
REGION 6063 APPEARS TO BE THE LIKELY SOURCE FOR THIS ACTIVITY
SO FAR WHICH ALSO INCLUDES AN M2 X-RAY EVENT AT 10/2357Z.
THIS REGION IS EMERGING NEAR THE LATITUDE WHERE A PREVIOUS,
HIGHLY ACTIVE REGION (6022) WAS DUE TO RETURN. REGION 6049
(N18W35) PRODUCED A C8/SB FLARE AT 11/1824Z. GRADUAL DECAY
IN OVERALL COVERAGE WAS NOTED. REGION 6054 (S18E11) DISPLAYED
MINOR GROWTH WHILE CONTRIBUTING A C5/1B FLARE AT 11/1505Z.
THE REMAINING REGIONS WERE CHARACTERIZED BY GENERALLY STABLE
CONDITIONS.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
HIGH. HIGH LEVELS OF ACTIVITY ARE POSSIBLE FROM REGION 6063.
REGIONS 6049 AND 6054 MAY ALSO CONTRIBUTE ISOLATED M-CLASS
ACTIVITY.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 10/2100Z TO 11/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT UNSETTLED TO MAJOR STORM
LEVELS. MAJOR STORMING WAS OBSERVED BRIEFLY BETWEEN
11/0300-0600Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM LEVELS FOR THE NEXT
THREE DAYS DUE TO EFFECTS FROM A CORONAL HOLE COUPLED WITH A
SLIGHT CHANCE OF ENHANCEMENT FROM THE PREVIOUS MAJOR FLARING.
