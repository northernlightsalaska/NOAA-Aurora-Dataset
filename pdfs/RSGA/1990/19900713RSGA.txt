JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 194 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 13 JUL 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 12/2100Z
TO 13/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE.  A LONG DURATION
(APPRX. 2 AND 1/2 HOURS) M3.1 X-RAY EVENT TOOK PLACE AT 1130Z.
THIS EVENT APPEARS TO BE FROM REGION 6138 (N22W89)
WHICH EXHIBITED POST-FLARE LOOPS IN CONJUNCTION WITH THIS
EVENT.  ONLY ONE C-CLASS FLARE AND SOME SUBFLARING HAS
OTHERWISE OCCURRED THOUGHOUT THE DAY.  TWO NEW REGIONS
6154 (N20E28) AND 6155 (N12E79) WERE NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE.  REGIONS 6150 (N07W32) AND 6153 (N16W36) SHOW
SOME GROWTH AND HAVE POTENTIAL TO PRODUCE AN M-CLASS FLARE.
ONLY C-CLASS FLARES ARE EXPECTED FROM THE REST OF THE
REGIONS ON THE DISK.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 12/2100Z TO 13/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN QUIET TO ACTIVE WITH SOME
PERIODS OF MINOR STORMING IN THE HIGHER LATITUDES FROM
03Z TO 09Z.  A PROTON ENHANCEMENT STARTED AT APPROXIMATELY
1700Z AND REACHED A TENTATIVE HIGH OF 1.7 PFU AT GREATER
THAN 10MEV AT 2030Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE GENERALLY UNSETTLED.
