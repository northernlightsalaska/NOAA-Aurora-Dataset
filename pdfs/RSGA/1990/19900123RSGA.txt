FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 023
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 23 JAN 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 22/2100Z
TO 23/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 5900 (S09E47)
PRODUCED AN M2/1N FLARE THAT MAXED AT 0505 UT. REGION 5900 HAS
DEVELOPED IN BOTH H-ALPHA AND WHITE LIGHT AND IS NOW COMPLEX
MAGNETICALLY. REGION 5890 (S11W15) PRODUCED NUMEROUS SUBFLARES
AND CONTINUES TO GROW.  FOUR NEW REGIONS WERE NUMBERED TODAY.
SURGING MARKED THE WEST LIMB PASSAGE OF REGION 5884 (N32W83)
AND SURGING AS WELL AS C-CLASS X-RAY ACTIVITY WAS NOTED FROM
REGION 5882 (N20W80). A LARGE FILAMENT DISAPPEARED BETWEEN
22/2019 AND 23/1500 NEAR S45W60.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE. REGIONS 5900 AND 5890 ARE BOTH CAPABLE OF M-CLASS
X-RAY FLARES, AND REGION 5900 SHOULD BE WATCHED FOR FURTHER
DEVELOPMENT LEADING TOWARD X-CLASS ACTIVITY.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 22/2100Z TO 23/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO ACTIVE LEVELS
FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO CONTINUE AT UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE LEVELS
THROUGHOUT THE FORECAST PERIOD IN RESPONSE TO NORTHERN
CORONAL HOLES NEAR CENTRAL MERIDIAN ON 21 JANUARY AND MAJOR
FILAMENT DISAPPEARANCES OVER THE PAST SEVERAL DAYS.
