IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 08/2100Z
TO 09/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 6314
(S18E62) PRODUCED A LONG DURATION M1/1F FLARE AT 09/0911Z.
THIS REGION HAS BEEN FAIRLY ACTIVE SINCE IT ROTATED INTO
VIEW ON THE 8TH AND IS A LIKELY SOURCE FOR THE UNCORRELATED
M1 X-RAY EVENT OBSERVED EARLIER THAT SAME DAY. REGIONS 6206
(S25E17) AND 6308 (S08E12) DISPLAYED MINOR SPOT GROWTH
WITH OCCASIONAL SUBFLARING NOTED. A FAINT, 30 DEGREE SEGMENT
OF FILAMENT LOCATED NEAR N47W28 DISAPPEARED BETWEEN 08/1556Z
AND 09/0002Z. AN IMPRESSIVE ERUPTIVE PROMINENCE WAS
OBSERVED ON THE SOUTHWEST LIMB AT 09/1821Z. NEW REGIONS
6316 (N17E25) AND 6317 (S11W29) WERE ASSIGNED TODAY.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. REGION 6314 CURRENTLY POSES THE GREATEST
THREAT FOR AN ISOLATED M-CLASS FLARE.
