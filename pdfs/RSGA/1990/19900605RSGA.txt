FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 156
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 05 JUN 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 04/2100Z
TO 05/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. TWO C1/SF FLARES
CHARACTERIZED THE PERIOD. ONE EACH FROM REGION 6089 (N10E50) AND
6080 (N15W46). AN UNCORRELATED 1400 SFU BURST ON 245 MHZ COUPLED
WITH A SIGNIFICANT TYPE IV SWEEP AT 05/1012Z MAY SIGNAL THE
RETURN OF OLD REGIONS 6063 AND 6064, DUE TO RETURN EARLY 8 JUNE.
THE REMAINING REGIONS WERE GENERALLY STABLE. NEW REGIONS
6091 (N01E48) AND 6092 (N18E53) WERE ASSIGNED TODAY.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW WITH THE TREND TOWARDS INCREASED LEVELS. ISOLATED C-CLASS
EVENTS ARE POSSIBLE FROM REGIONS 6088 (S17E33), 6089, AND 6091.
RETURNING REGIONS 6063 (N34) AND 6064 (S14) ARE EXPECTED TO
ENHANCE ACTIVITY LEVELS LATE IN THE PERIOD. THESE REGIONS
PRODUCED NUMEROUS MAJOR EVENTS DURING THEIR LAST TRANSIT.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 04/2100Z TO 05/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT MOSTLY QUIET LEVELS
FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE MOSTLY QUIET TO UNSETTLED WITH A CHANCE OF
ACTIVE CONDITIONS POSSIBLE ON 7 JUNE IN RESPONSE TO A
DISAPPEARING SOLAR FILAMENT.
