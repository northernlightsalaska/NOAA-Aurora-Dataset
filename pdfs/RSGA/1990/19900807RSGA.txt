JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 219 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 07 AUG 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 06/2100Z
TO 07/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW.  REGION 6192 (S17W32) WAS
THE MOST ACTIVE, PRODUCING A C-CLASS FLARE AND FOUR SUBFLARES.
THIS REGION HAS GROWN FROM 90 MILLIONTHS TO 180 MILLIONTHS.
THE BIGGEST EVENT WAS AN UNCORRELATED C2.4 X-RAY EVENT AT
07/0004Z.  ONE NEW REGION WAS NUMBERED: 6195 (S11E63).
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW.  REGION 6192 HAS THE MOST POTENTIAL TO PRODUCE FLARES.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 06/2100Z TO 07/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN QUIET TO UNSETTLED.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED.  ACTIVITY MAY BE SLIGHTLY ENHANCED
THE FIRST TWO DAYS DUE TO A DISAPPEARING FILAMENT ON 03 AUGUST.
