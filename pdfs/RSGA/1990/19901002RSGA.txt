IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 01/2100Z
TO 02/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW.  REGION 6283 (S09W54)
CONTINUED TO PRODUCE C-CLASS FLARES, THE LARGEST BEING A
C5/1N AT 02/0145Z.  THIS REGION CONTINUES TO DECAY SLOWLY.
REGION 6294 (S04W17) IS GROWING BUT AT A REDUCED RATE
COMPARED TO YESTERDAY.  SO FAR THIS REGION HAS ONLY PRODUCED
SMALL FLARES.  NEW REGIONS 6298 (N17W72) AND 6299 (S14W15)
WERE NUMBERED.  REGION 6298 IS EMERGING RAPIDLY AND HAS
PRODUCED SEVERAL SMALL FLARES.  THIS REGION IS DEVELOPING
ADJACENT TO A LARGE BUSHY FILAMENT AND MAY PRECIPITATE ITS
ERUPTION BEFORE IT ROTATES AROUND THE WEST LIMB.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW WITH A SMALL CHANCE OF AN ISOLATED M-CLASS FLARE.
