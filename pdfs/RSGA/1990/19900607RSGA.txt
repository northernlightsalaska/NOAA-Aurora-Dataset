FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 158
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 07 JUN 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 06/2100Z
TO 07/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. AN M4 X-RAY
EVENT OCCURRED AT 07/1930Z. NO OPTICAL FLARE WAS OBSERVED
HOWEVER POST-FLARE LOOPS WERE NOTED ON THE SOUTHEAST LIMB
WHERE RETURNING REGION 6064 SHOULD BECOME VISIBLE BY 8 JUNE.
AN ERUPTIVE PROMINENCE WAS NOTED IN THAT AREA AT 07/1218Z.
A 450 SFU TENFLARE RADIO BURST WAS ASSOCIATED WITH THE
LONG DURATION M4 X-RAY EVENT. REGION 6093 (S18W90) PRODUCED
NUMEROUS C-CLASS SUBFLARES THROUGHOUT THE PERIOD TO INCLUDE
A C8/SF FLARE AT 07/1444Z. THIS REGION IS DUE TO ROTATE
AROUND THE LIMB 8 JUNE. REGION 6089 (N08E21) HAS SHOWN
MODEST SPOT GROWTH TODAY AND SLIGHTLY ENHANCED MAGNETIC
COMPLEXITY. REGIONS 6096 (N26E75) AND 6095 (N24E26) WERE
ASSIGNED TODAY. A C2/SF FLARE AT 07/1752Z WAS OBSERVED FROM
REGION 6096.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE. REGION 6093 (S18W90) MAY PRODUCE C-CLASS FLARING
OR AN ISOLATED M-CLASS EVENT PRIOR TO ROTATING AROUND THE LIMB.
REGIONS 6089 AND 6096 PROVIDE AN ADDITIONAL SOURCE FOR
OCCASIONAL SUBFLARING. OLD REGIONS 6063 (N35, L314) AND
6064 (S16, L308), DUE TO RETURN 8 JUNE, ARE EXPECTED TO
ENHANCE M-CLASS ACTIVITY LEVELS.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 06/2100Z TO 07/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT GENERALLY QUIET TO ACTIVE
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS WITH MINOR STORMING OBSERVED
BRIEFLY BETWEEN 07/0300-0600Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED FOR THE NEXT THREE DAYS.
