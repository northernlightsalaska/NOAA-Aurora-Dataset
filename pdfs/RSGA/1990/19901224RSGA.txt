JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 358 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 24 DEC 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 23/2100Z
TO 24/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH. REGION 6412 (N16W94)
PRODUCED THE MOST ENERGETIC EVENT OF THE PERIOD: AN X1/1B
TENFLARE AT 24/1541Z ACCOMPANIED BY MODEST RADIO BURST
EMISSIONS DISPLAYING A CASTELLI-U SIGNATURE. REGION 6420
(S23E34), PART OF A COMPLEX AREA IN THE SOUTHEAST WHICH
ALSO INCLUDES REGIONS 6422 (S16E46), 6423 (S19E24), AND NEW
REGION 6424 (S08E31), PRODUCED TWO M-CLASS EVENTS: AN M1/SF
FLARE AT 24/0106Z AND AN M2/1F FLARE AT 24/1326Z. REGION
6418 (N19W08) DISPLAYED SIGNIFICANT OVERNIGHT GROWTH IN SPOT
AREA WHILE PRODUCING OCCASIONAL C-CLASS FLARES. REGIONS
ASSIGNED TODAY INCLUDE 6424, NOTED ABOVE, AND 6425 (N09E07).
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE TO HIGH. REGION 6412 MAY PRODUCE MAJOR FLARING AS
IT CONTINUES TO ROUND THE LIMB. THE COMPLEX OF REGIONS IN
THE SOUTHEAST (6420, 6422, 6423, AND 6424) MAY PRODUCE
ISOLATED M-CLASS EVENTS.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 23/2100Z TO 24/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO UNSETTLED
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS. HIGH LATITUDE STATIONS
EXPERIENCED A PERIOD OF MINOR STORMING BETWEEN 12-18Z.
SATELLITE LEVEL PROTONS AT GREATER THAN 10MEV BECAME
ENHANCED AT 24/1850Z REACHING 3 PFU BY 24/2035Z. ACTIVITY
IS IN RESPONSE TO THE X1/1B FLARE.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED WITH A CHANCE OF ACTIVE CONDITIONS
TOWARDS THE END OF THE PERIOD. SATELLITE LEVEL PROTONS MAY
REMAIN AT ENHANCED LEVELS FOR THE NEXT 24 HOURS.
