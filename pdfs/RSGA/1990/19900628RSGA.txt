JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 28 JUN 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 27/2100Z
TO 28/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. THE PERIOD BEGAN WITH
AN OPTICALLY UNCORRELATED M1 X-RAY BURST AT 27/2113Z8. NEW
REGION 6133 (N18E76), THUS FAR REPORTED AS A LARGE ASSYMETRICAL
H-TYPE SPOT WHICH CONTINUED TO ROTATE INTO VIEW, PRODUCED THE
REMAINING TWO M-CLASS EVENTS OF THE DAY: AN M2/SF FLARE AT
28/1409Z AND AN M2/1N AT FLARE 28/2034Z. REGION 6122 (S21E06)
DECLINED SLOWLY IN MAGNETIC ASPECTS AND PRODUCED SEVERAL
SUBFLARES INCLUDING A C5/1N AT 28/0108Z. REGION 6125 (N05E13)
GREW FROM A PLAGE REGION INTO AN INCREASINGLY COMPLEX D-TYPE
SPOT GROUP. REGION 6127 (N08E47) ALSO DISPLAYED SIGNIFICANT
GROWTH AND PRODUCED A 1F FLARE AT 28/0407Z WHICH WAS ACCOMPANIED
BY A LOOP PROMINENCE SYSTEM, BUT NO X-RAY BURST. REGIONS 6134
(N13E30) AND 6135 (S19E34) WERE ALSO NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE. REGIONS 6122 AND 6133 DISPLAY POTENTIAL FOR M-CLASS
FLARE PRODUCTION. REGIONS 6125 AND 6127 ARE EXPECTED TO PRODUCE
C-CLASS SUBFLARES.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 27/2100Z TO 28/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO UNSETTLED LEVELS
FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE QUIET TO UNSETTLED AT MIDDLE LATITUDES. THE
HIGH LATITUDE FIELD IS EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE
TOMORROW FOLLOWED BY TWO DAYS OF UNSETTLED CONDITIONS. THE
ACTIVE LEVELS MAY OCCUR IN RESPONSE TO THE M1/1B FLARE OF
26 JUNE.
