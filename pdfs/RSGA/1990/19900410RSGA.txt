JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 10 APR 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 09/2100Z
TO 10/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 6007 (N24W09)
PRODUCED AN M1/1N FLARE AT 1216Z. A MODERATE TYPE IV SWEEP WAS
ALSO ASSOCIATED WITH THIS EVENT. REGION 6007 CONTINUES TO DECAY
AND MAY BE SPOTLESS BEFORE THE END OF THE ZULU DAY. REGION
6001 (N22W71) CONTINUES TO BE THE MOST COMPLEX SPOT GROUP ON
THE DISK. IT REMAINS QUIET AND IS APPROACHING THE WEST LIMB.
ACTIVE SURGE REGION (ASR) OBSERVED AT SE33. TWO NEW REGIONS
NUMBERED TODAY: 6017 (N21E31) AND 6018 (S32E77).
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. THE POSSIBILITY OF AN ISOLATED M-CLASS FLARE
MAY OCCUR FROM REGIONS 6001 AND 6007.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 09/2100Z TO 10/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT UNSETTLED TO MAJOR
STORM LEVELS. THIS DISTURBANCE COULD BE IN RESPONSE TO THE X1
FLARE ON 03 APRIL IN REGION 6007, HOWEVER, THE CORRESPONDENCE
IS NOT DEFINITE. THE MAJOR STORM CONDITIONS BEGAN AT
APPROXIMATELY 0600Z AND IS STILL IN PROGRESS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT MINOR STORM LEVELS ON 11 APRIL DECREASING
TO ACTIVE LEVELS ON 12/13 APRIL.
