SDF NUMBER 210 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 29 JUL 1990

IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 28/2100Z
TO 29/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS VERY LOW. ONLY ISOLATED
SUBFLARING WAS OBSERVED. REGION 6174 (S12W00) PRODUCED
THE MOST ENERGETIC SUBFLARE OF THE PERIOD AT 28/2125Z.
REGION 6162 (N11W66) CONTINUED TO DECLINE IN SPOT AREA
WHILE REMAINING GENERALLY STABLE. NEW REGION 6183 (S03W18)
WAS ASSIGNED TODAY.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE THE FIRST DAY OF THE PERIOD WITH THE TREND
TOWARDS LOW LEVELS OF ACTIVITY THEREAFTER. INTERACTION
BETWEEN REGIONS 6172 (S23W03), 6177 (S19E07), AND 6174
MAY PRODUCE OCCASIONAL C-CLASS FLARING OR AN ISOLATED
M-CLASS EVENT.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 28/2100Z TO 29/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT MINOR TO MAJOR
STORM LEVELS. HIGH LATITUDE STATIONS OBSERVED PERIODS
OF SEVERE STORMING. FURTHER ANALYSIS INDICATES THAT A
27 DEGREE SEGMENT OF FILAMENT THAT DISAPPEARED 25 JULY
MAY HAVE CONTRIBUTED TO THIS ACTIVITY ALONG WITH THE
LONG DURATION X-RAY EVENT NOTED THE SAME DAY. THE
SATELLITE LEVEL PROTON EVENT (GREATER THAN 10 MEV) ENDED
28/1310Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT MINOR STORM LEVELS THE FIRST DAY OF THE
PERIOD WITH ACTIVE TO UNSETTLED CONDITIONS EXPECTED
THEREAFTER.
