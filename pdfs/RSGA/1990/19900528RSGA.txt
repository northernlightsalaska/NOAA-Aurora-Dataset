JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.

IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 27/2100Z
TO 28/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE.  REGION 6077 (N21E29)
PRODUCED THE ONLY M CLASS FLARE OBSERVED DURING THE PAST 24
HOURS, AN M1/SN AT 28/1510Z.  AN OPTICALLY UNCORRELATED C1 FLARE
AT 28/0433Z WAS ACCOMPANIED BY A DISCRETE RADIO BURST OF
1600 SFU AT 2695 MHZ AND A STRONG TYPE IV RADIO SWEEP.  THE
SOURCE OF THIS FLARE IS PROBABLY REGION 6063 WHICH CROSSED THE
WEST LIMB ON 25 MAY.  OTHER DISK REGIONS WERE QUIET.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE.  REGION 6077 IS A LIKELY SOURCE OF M FLARES.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 27/2100Z TO 28/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS QUIET TO UNSETTLED.  THE PROTON EVENT
THAT BEGAN AT 2125Z ON 24 MAY ENDED AT 27/2115Z.  A NEW PROTON
EVENT BEGAN AT 28/0715Z AS A RESULT OF THE BEHIND-THE-LIMB FLARE
MENTIONED ABOVE.  THE FLUX AT 28/2100Z AT GREATER THAN 10 MEV,
50 MEV, AND 100 MEV WAS 32 PFU, 10 PFU, AND 4 PFU, RESPECTIVELY.
THE PEAK OF THIS EVENT HAS NOT YET BEEN REACHED.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO ACIVE.  THE PROTON EVENT IS EXPECTED
TO REACH A MAXIMUM WITHIN THE NEXT 24 HOURS AND THEN SLOWLY
DECLINE.
