JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 297 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 24 OCT 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 23/2100Z
TO 24/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW.  THE LARGEST EVENT OF THE
PERIOD WAS AN UNCORRELATED C2 AT 24/0211Z.  ADDITIONAL C-CLASS
ACTIVITY WAS OBSERVED FROM REGIONS 6331 (N18W02) AND 6337
(S14E43).  REGION 6337 HAS SHOWN SIGNIFICANT GROWTH THE PAST
24 HOURS, MORE THAN DOUBLING IN SIZE.  A 16 DEGREE FILAMENT
DISAPPEARED FROM N40E43 ABOUT 24/1500Z.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MOSTLY LOW.  THERE IS A SLIGHT CHANCE OF M-CLASS ACTIVITY FROM
REGIONS 6327 (N19W22) AND 6337.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 23/2100Z TO 24/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT MOSTLY ACTIVE CONDITIONS
FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS.  SEVERAL PERIODS OF MINOR STORM
CONDITIONS WERE OBSERVED AT BOTH MID AND HIGH LATITUDES.
THE SOURCE OF THIS ACTIVITY IS MOST LIKELY THE FAVORABLY
POSITIONED CORONAL HOLE AND THE DISAPPEARING FILAMENT OF
19 OCTOBER.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT MOSTLY ACTIVE CONDITIONS WITH ISOLATED
PERIODS OF MINOR STORM CONDITIONS THE NEXT 24 HOURS.  ACTIVITY
LEVELS ARE EXPECTED TO BECOME MOSTLY UNSETTLED ON DAYS TWO AND
THREE OF THE FORECAST PERIOD.
