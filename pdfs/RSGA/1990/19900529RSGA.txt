IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 28/2100Z
TO 29/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW.  REGION 6077 (N20E15)
HAS SIMPLIFIED AND HAS PRODUCED ONLY OCCASSIONAL C CLASS
X-RAY FLARES.  OPTICALLY UNCORRELATED TYPE II/IV SWEEP
ACTIVITY WAS OBSERVED AT 29/1250Z.  THIS ACTIVITY IS BELIEVED
TO HAVE ORIGINATED BEYOND THE WEST LIMB.  NEW REGION
6082 (S05W45) WAS NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW.  THERE IS AN OUTSIDE CHANCE OF AN ISOLATED M CLASS FLARE
IN REGION 6077.

THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS QUIET TO UNSETTLED.  THE PROTON
EVENT CONTINUES IN PROGRESS.  THIS EVENT SLOWLY INCREASED TO
A BROAD MAXIMUM AND IS NOW SLOWLY DECLINING.  PEAK FLUXES
AT GREATER THAN 10 MEV, 50 MEV AND 100 MEV WERE 45 PFU AT
29/0100Z, 11 PFU AT 28/1520Z, AND 4 PFU AT 28/1520Z,
RESPECTIVELY.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE QUIET TO UNSETTLED.  THE PROTON EVENT IS EXPECTED
TO END WITHIN THE NEXT 24 HOURS.
