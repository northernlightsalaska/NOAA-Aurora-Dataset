JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 321 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 17 NOV 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 16/2100Z
TO 17/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE.  REGION 6368 (N19E25)
PRODUCED THREE M CLASS FLARES SINCE YESTERDAY- AN M1/1N AT
16/2351Z, M1/SN AT 17/1848Z, AND M2/SF AT 17/1946Z.  THIS REGION
DOMINATES THE DISK WITH A SUNSPOT AREA OF ABOUT 3000 MILLIONTHS.
COMPLEXITY HAS INCREASED SINCE YESTERDAY WITH CONTINUED SUNSPOT
GROWTH AND TWO DELTA CONFIGURATIONS LOCATED IN THE TRAILER SPOT
COMPLEX.  THIS REGION ALSO PRODUCED SEVERAL C CLASS FLARES
DURING THE PAST 24 HOURS.  NEW REGIONS 6371 (N18W17) AND 6372
(S17E66) WERE NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE TO HIGH.  MAJOR ACTIVITY IS POSSIBLE IN REGION 6368.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 16/2100Z TO 17/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS MOSTLY QUIET AT THE BEGINNING OF
THE PERIOD BECOMING MOSTLY ACTIVE.  STORM CONDITIONS WERE
OBSERVED AT SOME HIGH LATITUDE STATIONS FROM 17/09-18Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE FOR THE NEXT 24 HOURS.
MOSTLY ACTIVE CONDITIONS ARE EXPECTED FOR THE REMAINDER OF THE
FORECAST PERIOD DUE TO A CORONAL HOLE.
