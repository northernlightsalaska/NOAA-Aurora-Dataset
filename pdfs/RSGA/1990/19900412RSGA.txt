IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 11/2100Z
TO 12/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. AN M3 X-RAY BURST
OCCURRED AT 12/0259Z. THERE WAS NO OPTICAL CORRELATION FOR THIS
BURST. REGION 6017 (N21E04) STABILIZED AND PRODUCED ISOLATED
SUBFLARES THE LARGEST OF WHICH WAS A C2/SF AT 12/1830Z. NEW
REGION 6022 (N28E72) ROTATED INTO VIEW. A LOOP PROMINENCE SYSTEM
OCCURRED AT 12/0821Z IN THE VICINITY OF THIS REGION. AN ERUPTIVE
PROMINENCE TO 0.16 RV OCCURRED AT 12/1509Z, ALSO ASSOCIATED WITH
REGION 6022. NEW REGIONS 6021 (S15E76) AND 6023 (N18E10) WERE
ALSO ASSIGNED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW. C-CLASS SUBFLARE ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED FROM REGIONS 6007
(N22W27), 6017, AND 6022. REGION 6017 ALSO PROVIDES A SLIGHT
CHANCE FOR AN ISOLATED M-CLASS FLARE.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 11/2100Z TO 12/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT ACTIVE TO SEVERE STORM
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS. THE STORMING MAY BE A
CONSEQUENCE OF A 48 DEGREE FILAMENT WHICH DISAPPEARED FROM
THE SOUTHEAST QUADRANT SOMETIME BETWEEN 5-6 APRIL. A
SATELLITE LEVEL PROTON EVENT AT GREATER THAN 10 MEV BEGAN AT
11/2120Z, REACHED A MAXIMUM OF 13 PFU AT 11/2130Z, AND ENDED
AT 11/2145Z. THE SOURCE OF THIS EVENT MAY HAVE BEEN AN M1/1N
(WITH AN ASSOCIATED TYPE IV SWEEP) AT 10/1216Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT ACTIVE TO UNSETTLED LEVELS.
