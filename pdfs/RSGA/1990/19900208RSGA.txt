JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 08 FEB 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 07/2100Z
TO 08/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. AN M1 X-RAY FLARE
WAS DETECTED BY GOES AT 1833Z. THIS VERY IMPULSIVE FLARE WAS
NOT SEEN BY ANY OBSERVATORY ON THE SUN AT THAT TIME. VERY FEW
OPTICAL FLARES OCCURRED DURING THE DAY AS THE DISK HAD LITTLE TO
OFFER. THE 10.7 CM. FLUX DROPPED SLIGHTLY FROM YESTERDAY, BUT
IT MAY HAVE BOTTOMED OUT AS THERE ARE NEW AND AS YET UNNUMBERED
SPOT GROUPS COMING INTO VIEW.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW THE NEXT 72 HOURS. ADDITIONAL PHANTOM M-CLASS EVENTS ARE
NOT ANTICIPATED.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 07/2100Z TO 08/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS MOSTLY UNSETTLED ALL PERIOD.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD
SHOULD BE QUIET AT MID-LATITUDES. UNSETTLED CONDITIONS
ARE LIKELY AT HIGH LATITUDES.
