JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 23 JUN 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 22/2100Z
TO 23/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. REGION 6114 (S09E13)
CONTINUES TO DEVELOP WITH THE EMERGENCE OF SEVERAL SMALL, NEW
SPOTS AND THE DEVELOPMENT OF PENUMBRA. NEW REGION 6122 (S21E65)
IS ROTATING AROUND THE EAST LIMB SOMEWHAT MORE ACTIVE THAN THE
CURRENT DISK REGIONS. IT HAS ALREADY PRODUCED SEVERAL OPTICAL
SUBFLARES, OF WHICH A C2/SF AT 23/1216Z WAS ASSOCIATED
WITH A TYPE II SWEEP. THE SUNSPOTS IN 6122 ARE STILL DIFFICULT
TO SEE BUT THEY ARE POSSIBLY LARGER THAN ANYTHING CURRENTLY
ON THE DISK. REGION 6123 (N15W21) WAS ALSO NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. M CLASS ACTIVITY IS BECOMING MORE LIKELY DUE
TO GROWTH IN REGION 6114 AND THE ARRIVAL OF REGION 6122.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 22/2100Z TO 23/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS QUIET TO UNSETTLED.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE MOSTLY UNSETTLED.
