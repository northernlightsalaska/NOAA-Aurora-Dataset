JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 25 APR 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 24/2100Z
TO 25/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW.  REGION 6022 (N34W80)
APPEARS TO HAVE LOST ITS DELTA CONFIGURATION AS IT CONTINUES TO
DECAY.  THE MOST NOTABLE ACTIVITY WAS A C4/SF AT 2256Z FROM
REGION 6022 AND A DISAPPEARING FILAMENT FROM THE VICINITY OF
S24E43.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO
BE LOW TO MODERATE.  REGION 6038 (N15W12) HAS SHOWN
CONSIDERABLE GROWTH AND HAS HAD ARCH FILAMENT SYSTEMS
REPORTED.  REGIONS 6038 AND 6022 APPEAR TO HAVE THE BEST
POSSIBILITY OF PRODUCING AN M-CLASS EVENT.  MOST OTHER
REGIONS HAVE REMAINED STABLE.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 24/2100Z TO 25/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM
LEVELS.  SUBSTORM DISTURBANCES HAVE OCCURRED DURING THE PAST
24 HOURS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE MOSTLY ACTIVE.  OCCASIONAL PERIODS OF SUBSTORM
ACTIVITY ARE LIKELY FOR AT LEAST THE NEXT 24 HOURS.  BEYOND
THIS THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS EXPECTED TO BECOME UNSETTLED
TO ACTIVE.
