FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 144
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 24 MAY 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 23/2100Z
TO 24/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH. REGION 6063 (N35W79) PRO-
DUCED AN X9/1B AT 2051Z. THE EVENT WAS ACCOMPANIED BY LOOPS,
VERY STRONG DISCRETE RADIO BURSTS (INCLUDING A TENFLARE) AND A
STRONG TYPE IV SWEEP. THE REGION APPEARED TO MAINTAIN ITS DELTA
CONFIGURATION ALTHOUGH PROXIMITY TO THE LIMB MADE ANALYSIS DIF-
FICULT. THE ONLY OTHER FLARES OF CLASS M OR GREATER WERE AN
M1/SF AT 0607Z FROM 6064 (S16W78) AND AN M1/1F FROM 6063 AT
1942Z. TWO NEW REGIONS WERE ASSIGNED: REGION 6075 (N22E61) AND
REGION 6076 (N11E52).
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE TO HIGH TOMORROW DUE TO THE CONTINUED THREAT OF
ACTIVITY FROM REGIONS 6063 AND 6064. CONDITIONS SHOULD BECOME
MOSTLY LOW BY DAYS 2 AND 3 AS THOSE REGIONS WILL BE WELL BEYOND
THE LIMB BY THAT TIME.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 23/2100Z TO 24/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS QUIET TO UNSETTLED. MAGNETIC CROCHETS
WERE OBSERVED AT ABOUT 2050Z IN RESPONSE TO TODAY'S X9 FLARE.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE GENERALLY ACTIVE OVER THE NEXT TWO DAYS. ACTIVE
CONDITIONS ARE EXPECTED TOMORROW IN RESPONSE TO THE X5 OF 21
MAY. ACTIVE CONDITIONS SHOULD CONTINUE IN RESPONSE TO RECURRENT
CORONAL HOLE EFFECTS ON THE SECOND DAY. CONDITIONS SHOULD GRAD-
UALLY SUBSIDE BY DAY 3. A GREATER THAN 10 MEV PROTON EVENT IS
EXPECTED TO BEGIN BY 0200Z ON 25 MAY. A PEAK FLUX OF ABOUT 100
PFU IS EXPECTED AT THIS TIME.
