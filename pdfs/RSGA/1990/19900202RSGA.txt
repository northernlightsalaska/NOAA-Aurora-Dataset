JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 02 FEB 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 01/2100Z
TO 02/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. THE HIGHLIGHT OF THE
PERIOD WAS A C4/SF AT 02/2006Z FROM REGION 5900 (S10W83) WHICH
WAS ACCOMPANIED BY A BRIGHT SURGE ON THE LIMB. AN UNCORRELATED
C1 EVENT WAS NOTED AT 02/0535Z. REGIONS 5919 (N08E06) AND
5920 (N15E02) CONTINUE TO MATURE WITH GROWTH IN SPOT AREA AND
OCCASIONAL SUB FLARING NOTED. A POSSIBLE ERUPTIVE PROMINENCE
WAS OBSERVED FROM REGION 5906 (N10 W88) BETWEEN 02/1317-1402Z.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
PREDOMINANTLY LOW. ISOLATED M-CLASS ACTIVITY REMAINS POSSIBLE
HOWEVER FROM REGION 5900 AS IT TRANSITS THE LIMB.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 01/2100Z TO 02/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT UNSETTLED TO MINOR STORM
LEVELS. THE MINOR STORMING WAS MOST EVIDENT AT HIGHER
LATITUDES WITH THE CONDITION OCCURING ONLY BRIEFLY AT MID
LATITUDES. THIS ACTIVITY IS PRESUMED TO BE DUE TO THE EFFECTS
FROM A TYPE II SWEEP OBSERVED ON 29 JANUARY.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE ACTIVE AT THE BEGINING OF THE PERIOD WITH
THE TREND TOWARDS UNSETTLED CONDITIONS.
