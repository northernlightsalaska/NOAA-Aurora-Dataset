JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 05 APR 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 04/2100Z
TO 05/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 6007 (N23E53)
PRODUCED TODAY'S ONLY M-CLASS FLARE: AN M3/1N AT 1346Z WHICH WAS
ACCOMPANIED BY MODERATE DISCRETE RADIO BURSTS. MOST OF THE
DYNAMICS IN THE GROUP APPEARS TO BE ASSOCIATED WITH AN ACTIVE
INVERSION LINE FILAMENT: THE GROUP CONTINUES TO BE A D-TYPE
OF ONLY MODEST SIZE (ABOUT 200 MILLIONTHS) WITHOUT INDICATION
OF RAPID GROWTH OR DECAY. REGION 6006 (N32W24) HAS SHOWN STEADY
GROWTH AND PRODUCED A FEW C-FLARES. REGION 6001 (N21W03) CON-
TINUES AS THE LARGEST GROUP ON THE DISK AND PRODUCED A COUPLE
C-CLASS SUBFLARES. NEW REGION 6009 (S41E72) ROTATED INTO VIEW
AS A SIMPLE C-TYPE GROUP AND WAS ASSOCIATED WITH EAST LIMB
SURGE ACTIVITY. ADDITIONAL SURGING WAS REPORTED ON EAST LIMB AT
ABOUT N33.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE. REGION 6007 IS EXPECTED TO PRODUCE ADDITIONAL M-CLASS
FLARES WITH CONTRIBUTIONS ALSO POSSIBLE FROM REGION 6001. THERE
CONTINUES TO BE A SLIGHT THREAT FOR MAJOR FLARE ACTIVITY FROM
REGION 6007.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 04/2100Z TO 05/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS AT QUIET TO ACTIVE LEVELS. MIDDLE
LATITUDES WERE ACTIVE FROM ABOUT 0600Z-1200Z AND HIGH LAT-
ITUDES WERE ACTIVE FROM APPROXIMATELY 0600-1800Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE GENERALLY UNSETTLED TOMORROW. CONDITIONS ARE
EXPECTED TO BECOME SLIGHTLY MORE ACTIVE OVER THE SECOND AND
THIRD DAYS DUE TO THE COMBINATION OF RECURRENT EFFECTS AND
THE POSSIBILITY OF SOME EFFECTS FROM MAJOR FLARE ACTIVITY
ON THE PREVIOUS DAYS.
