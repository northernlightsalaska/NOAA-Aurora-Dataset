JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 18 JAN 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 17/2100Z
TO 18/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 5890 (S12E50)
PRODUCED  TODAY'S ONLY  M-CLASS EVENT:  AN M1/1F AT 0227Z AT
S14E67. THE REGION IS GROWING AND DEVELOPING AT THIS TIME. IN
ADDITION,  A C9 X-RAY FLARE OCCURRED AT 0427Z WHICH WAS AS-
SOCIATED WITH A MODERATE TYPE II SWEEP. NO OPTICAL CORRELATIONS
COULD BE MADE DURING THE EVENT, SUGGESTING THAT THE FLARE WAS
PRODUCED BY A REGION BEHIND THE LIMB. SURGING WAS OBSERVED ON
EAST LIMB AT S12 AND S20. THE REMAINDER OF THE DISK WAS QUIET.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE. REGION 5890 IS THE MAIN THREAT FOR FLARE ACTIVITY.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 17/2100Z TO 18/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS QUIET UNTIL ABOUT 12Z WHEN CONDITIONS
BECAME UNSETTLED TO SLIGHTLY ACTIVE. THE INCREASE MAY HAVE BEEN
RELATED TO THE FILAMENT DISAPPEARANCE OF 15 JANUARY.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE GENERALLY UNSETTLED. SOME BRIEF, ISOLATED
PERIODS OF ACTIVE CONDITIONS ARE POSSIBLE AT HIGH LATITUDES
DURING THE NEXT 24 HOURS.
