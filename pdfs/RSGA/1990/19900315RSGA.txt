FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 074
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 15 MAR 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 14/2100Z
TO 15/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW.  THE LARGEST EVENT WAS AN
UNCORRELATED LONG DURATION C5.5 PEAKING AT 0623UT.  REGIONS 5969
(N33E02) AND 5974 (S34E53) PRODUCED THE BULK OF THE REMAINING C
FLARES.  REGIONS 5976 (N39E41), 5977 (S17E17), AND 5978 (N17E69)
WERE NUMBERED.  REGION 5976 IS A GROWING HIGH LATITUDE REGION
WITH NORMAL MAGNETIC POLARITIES FOR THIS SOLAR CYCLE.  SEVERAL
SMALL 10830A CORONAL HOLES ARE LOCATED IN THE AREA OF REGION
5976 AND IN THE SOUTHWEST QUADRANT OF THE SUN.  THERE IS STILL
A CORONAL HOLE VISIBLE AT THE SOLAR SOUTH POLE.  A LARGE POLAR
CROWN FILAMENT IS CROSSING THE WEST LIMB NEAR N45.  THE 10.7CM
RADIO FLUX JUMPED 17 SFU TODAY.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE.  THERE IS A SMALL BUT INCREASING CHANCE OF
MORE ENERGETIC ACTIVITY AS THE ACTIVE SIDE OF THE SUN COMES
INTO VIEW.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 14/2100Z TO 15/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO ACTIVE LEVELS
FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE FOR THE NEAR FUTURE.
