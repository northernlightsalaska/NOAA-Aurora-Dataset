FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 139
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 19 MAY 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 18/2100Z
TO 19/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGIONS 6063 (N34W21)
AND 6064 (S16W13) WERE THE PERIODS MOST ACTIVE, EACH PRODUCING
AN M-CLASS FLARE AND SEVERAL SUBFLARES.  AN M1/1F AT 2242Z WAS
OBSERVED FROM REGION 6063 WHILE AN M2/2N AT 1307Z WAS REPORTED
FROM REGION 6064.  THESE REGIONS REMAIN THE LARGEST AND MOST
COMPLEX ON THE DISK, EACH DISPLAYING A BETA-GAMMA-DELTA
MAGNETIC CONFIGURATION.  THE REMAINING REGIONS HAVE REMAINED
MOSTLY STABLE, PRODUCING ONLY MINOR SUBFLARING.  NEW REGION
6072 (N25E79) WAS ASSIGNED TODAY.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE TO HIGH.  REGIONS 6063 AND 6064 CONTINUE TO HAVE
POTENTIAL FOR MAJOR FLARE ACTIVITY.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 18/2100Z TO 19/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN MOSTLY UNSETTLED THE PAST
24 HOURS.  A SATELLITE LEVEL PROTON ENHANCEMENT CONTINUES
ALTHOUGH PROTON LEVELS REMAIN WELL BELOW EVENT LEVELS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE.
