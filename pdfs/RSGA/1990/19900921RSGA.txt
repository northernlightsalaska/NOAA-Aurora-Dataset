JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 264 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 21 SEP 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 20/2100Z
TO 21/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW.  THE LARGEST EVENT OF
THE DAY WAS A C2/SF FROM REGION 6280 (N13W57) AT 1257Z.  REGION
6272 (S12W19) HAS CONTINUED ITS DECAY WHILE REGION 6280
CONTINUED TO GROW, BECOMING THE LARGEST REGION NOW ON THE DISK.
A 20 DEGREE FILAMENT DISAPPEARED FROM S14W21 AROUND 1730Z, THERE
WAS NO X-RAY OR RADIO EMISSION DETECTED WITH IT.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MOSTLY LOW.  REGION 6280 NOW POSSESSES THE BEST POTENTIAL FOR
AN ISOLATED M-CLASS FLARE.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 20/2100Z TO 21/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT MOSTLY UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS.  PERIODS OF STORM CONDITIONS
WERE OBSERVED AT HIGH LATITUDES BETWEEN 0900Z AND 1500Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO REMAIN AT MOSTLY UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE CONDITIONS
THROUGH 23 SEPTEMBER DUE TO THE DISAPPEARING FILAMENTS
REPORTED RECENTLY.
