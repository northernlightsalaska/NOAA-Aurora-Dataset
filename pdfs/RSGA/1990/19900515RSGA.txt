FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 135
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 15 MAY 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 14/2100Z
TO 15/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH. REGION 6063 (N33E28)
PRODUCED AN X1/3B TENFLARE AT 15/1321Z. ASSOCIATED WITH THIS
EVENT WERE MAJOR TYPE II AND TYPE IV SWEEPS, A 710 SFU
TENFLARE, AND AN IMPRESSIVE LOOP PROMINENCE SYSTEM. THIS
REGION CONTINUED TO GROW IN SPOT AREA AND REMAINS MAGNETICALLY
COMPLEX. NUMEROUS SUBFLARES WERE ALSO NOTED FROM THIS REGION.
REGION 6064 (S17E40) HAS SHOWN SIGNIFICANT GROWTH THIS
PERIOD. SEVERAL C-CLASS EVENTS WERE ATTRIBUTED TO THIS AREA
AS WELL AS AN M1/2B FLARE AT 15/0035Z. REGION 6058 (N15W29)
PRODUCED A 1N FLARE AT 15/1400Z WHICH RESULTED IN THE ERUPTION
OF A 14-DEGREE-LONG SEGMENT OF FILAMENT. REGION 6060 (N22E01)
DISPLAYED MINOR GROWTH IN BOTH SPOT AND PLAGE AREA AND SHOWS
SIGNS OF CONTINUED DEVELOPEMENT. REGION 6067 (S16E13) WAS
ASSIGNED TODAY.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
HIGH. REGION 6063 POSES THE GREATEST THREAT FOR CONTINUED
MAJOR FLARE PRODUCTION WITH REGION 6064 SHOWING THE POTENTIAL
FOR OCCASIONAL M-CLASS EVENTS. REGIONS 6058 AND 6060 MAY
CONTRIBUTE ISOLATED ACTIVITY.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 14/2100Z TO 15/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT MOSTLY QUIET LEVELS
FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE. ACTIVE CONDITIONS ARE
POSSIBLE STARTING ON 17 MAY AS A RESULT OF TODAY'S X1/3B
FLARE. SATELLITE LEVEL PROTONS AT GREATER THAN 10MEV MAY
ALSO BECOME ENHANCED STARTING 16 MAY AS A RESULT OF THIS
MAJOR FLARE.
