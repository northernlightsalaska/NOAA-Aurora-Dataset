FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 110
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 20 APR 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 19/2100Z
TO 20/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGIONS 6022 (N31W23)
AND 6021 (S12W20) REMAINED GENERALLY STABLE WITH LITTLE CHANGE
IN SIZE OR COMPLEXITY NOTED. EACH REGION PRODUCED SEVERAL LOW-
LEVELS C-CLASS FLARES DURING THE PERIOD. REGION 6031 (S18E07)
ALSO GENERATED OCCASIONAL SUBFLARING. REGION 6018 PRODUCED
THE PERIOD'S SOLE M-CLASS EVENT: AN M1/2B FLARE AT 20/1825Z.
SPOT AREA GROWTH AND ELONGATION COUPLED WITH INCREASED
COMPLEXITY WAS NOTED. REGION 6032 (S12E01) SHOWED MODEST GROWTH
PRODUCING SEVERAL SUBFLARES EARLY IN THE PERIOD. NEW REGION
6035 (N16W03) WAS ASSIGNED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE. REGIONS 6018, 6021, 6022, AND 6031 ARE THE BEST
CANDIDATES FOR M-CLASS ACTIVITY. REGION 6022 CONTINUES TO
SHOW THE BEST POTENTIAL FOR AN ISOLATED MAJOR FLARE.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 19/2100Z TO 20/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO UNSETTLED
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS. A WEAK SHOCK WAS OBSERVED ON
THE GOES MAGNETOMETER AT 20/1800Z. SOURCE UNDETERMINED AT
THIS TIME. SATELLITE LEVEL PROTONS AT GREATER THAN 10 MEV
REMAIN ENHANCED ABOVE BACKGROUND LEVELS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE GENERALLY ACTIVE THROUGHOUT THE FORECAST
PERIOD.
