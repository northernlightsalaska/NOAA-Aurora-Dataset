IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 23/2100Z
TO 24/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 5983 (S33W04)
DECAYED IN SPOT AREA DURING THE PERIOD BUT STILL MANAGED TO
PRODUCE THE DAY'S MOST ENERGETIC EVENT: AN M1/1N FLARE AT
24/0258Z ACCOMPANIED BY MODEST RADIO EMISSIONS AND A TYPE II
SWEEP. REGION 5988 (S07E13) PRODUCED AN M1/SF FLARE AT
24/0512Z. NUMEROUS SUBFLARES HAVE ALSO BEEN NOTED FROM THIS
REGION WHICH HAS GROWN IN SIZE AND MAGNETIC COMPLEXITY.
REGION 5984 (S12W17) HAS ALSO SHOWN NOTABLE GROWTH WHILE
PRODUCING OCCASIONAL SUBFLARES. REGION 5974 (S34W56)
CONTINUED TO DECLINE. AN EVENT LEVEL BRIGHT SURGE WAS NOTED
ON THE SOUTHEAST LIMB AT 24/1727Z SIGNALING THE EMERGENCE
OF ANOTHER ACTIVE REGION. REGION 5995 (N25E70) WAS ASSIGNED
TODAY.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE. M-CLASS ACTIVITY MAY OCCUR FROM REGIONS 5974, 5983,
5984, OR 5988. REGIONS 5984 AND 5988 ARE ALSO CAPABLE OF
ISOLATED MAJOR FLARE PRODUCTION.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 23/2100Z TO 24/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE WITH ACTIVE CONDITIONS
LIKELY TOWARDS THE END OF THE FORECAST PERIOD DUE TO RETURNING
CORONAL HOLES.
