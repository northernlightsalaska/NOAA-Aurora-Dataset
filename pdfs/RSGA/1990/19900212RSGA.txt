FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 043
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 12 FEB 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 11/2100Z
TO 12/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. ACTIVITY WAS CHARACTERIZED
BY OCCASIONAL C-CLASS SUBFLARES AND FREQUENT RADIO BURSTS AND
SWEEPS. MOST OF THE SUBFLARES CAME FROM REGION 5927 (N18E11),
WHICH PRODUCED TODAY'S LARGEST EVENT: A C3/SN AT 1724Z. THE
GROUP DOMINATES THE DISK AS THE ONLY GROUP WITH AREA EXCEEDING
100 MILLIONTHS.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW. REGION 5927 IS THE MOST LIKELY SOURCE FOR CONTINUED SMALL
C-CLASS EVENTS.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 11/2100Z TO 12/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS MOSTLY QUIET WITH A FEW BRIEF
UNSETTLED PERIODS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE QUIET TO UNSETTLED FOR THE NEXT TWO DAYS, AND
UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE ON THE THIRD DAY. THE INCREASE IS FORECAST
DUE TO POSSIBLE EFFECTS FROM FILAMENTS WHICH DISAPPEARED ON
10 FEBRUARY.
