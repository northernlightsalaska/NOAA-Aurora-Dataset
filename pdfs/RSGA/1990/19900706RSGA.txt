JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 06 JUL 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 05/2100Z
TO 06/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE.  AN M2/2N AT 0552Z
FROM REGION 6133 (N17W24) WAS THE MOST SIGNIFICANT EVENT OF THE
PERIOD.  REGION 6137 (S14W53) HAS GROWN CONSIDERABLY DURING THE
PERIOD, HAS PRODUCED NUMEROUS SUBFLARES AND A C3/SF AT 1829Z.
TWO SIZABLE DISAPPEARING FILAMENTS WERE REPORTED FROM
APPROXIMATELY N04W40 AND N17W25.  REGION 6142 (S16E72) WAS
NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE.  NUMEROUS REGIONS CONTINUE TO POSSESS THE ABILITY TO
PRODUCE AN ISOLATED M-CLASS FLARE.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 05/2100Z TO 06/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO UNSETTLED
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE ACTIVE THE NEXT 48 HOURS IN RESPONSE TO THE
MID-DISK CORONAL HOLE AND THE M7/2B TENFLARE ON 04 JULY.
ACTIVITY ON THE THIRD DAY OF THE FORECAST PERIOD IS EXPECTED
TO BE UNSETTLED.
