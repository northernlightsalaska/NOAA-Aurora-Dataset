IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 15/2100Z
TO 16/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW.  SEVERAL C CLASS SUBFLARES
WERE OBSERVED SINCE YESTERDAY, MOSTLY IN REGIONS 5969 (N33W11)
AND 5974 (S34E43).  REGION 5969 APPEARS TO BE STABILIZING IN
ITS DEVELOPMENT WHILE REGION 5974 IS CONTINUING TO GROW.
THREE NEW REGIONS WERE NUMBERED - 5979 (N29E13), 5980 (S11E73),
AND 5981 (S12E35).
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE.  C CLASS ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED IN REGIONS
5969 AND 5974.  AN ISOLATED M CLASS FLARE IS POSSIBLE IN THESE
REGIONS.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 15/2100Z TO 16/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS QUIET TO UNSETTLED.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE QUIET TO UNSETTLED FOR THE NEXT 24 HOURS.
UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE CONDITIONS ARE POSSIBLE ON 17 AND 18
MARCH DUE TO CORONAL HOLES.
