JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 359 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 25 DEC 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 24/2100Z
TO 25/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 6420
(S24E18) PRODUCED THE SOLE M-FLARE OF THE PERIOD: AN M1/1F
EVENT AT 24/2300Z. AFTER FURTHER ANALYSIS OF THE SPOT STRUCTURE
ASSOCIATED WITH THIS REGION, THE DISTANT LEADING SPOT HAS NOW
BEEN GROUPED WITH REGION 6423 (S17E12). BOTH REGIONS 6420 AND
6423 HAVE BEEN FAIRLY ACTIVE PRODUCING A NUMBER OF SUBFLARES
AND OCCASIONAL C-CLASS EVENTS. REGION 6424 (S11E14) EXHIBITED
SUSTAINED GROWTH EARLY IN THE PERIOD BUT APPEARS TO BE SLOWING
DOWN NOW. ISOLATED SUBFLARING WAS ATTRIBUTED TO THIS AREA TODAY.
NEW REGION 6426 (S13E25) WAS ASSIGNED TODAY.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE. REGIONS 6420 OR 6423 MAY PRODUCE ISOLATED M-CLASS
FLARES.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 24/2100Z TO 25/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO UNSETTLED
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS. SATELLITE LEVEL PROTONS AT
GREATER THAN 10 MEV REMAINED SLIGHTLY ENHANCED ABOVE
BACKGROUND LEVELS IN RESPONSE TO EFFECTS FROM THE 24
DECEMBER X1/1B FLARE.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE IN RESPONSE TO EFFECTS
FROM THE RECENT X-CLASS FLARING AND A CORONAL HOLE.
SATELLITE LEVELS PROTONS SHOULD CONTINUE TO RETURN TO
BACKGROUND LEVELS.
