JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 17 APR 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 16/2100Z
TO 17/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 6022 (N31E13)
PRODUCED THE SOLE M-CLASS FLARE OF THE PERIOD: AN M1/2N EVENT
AT 17/1434Z. THE REGION REMAINS MAGNETICALLY COMPLEX WITH
GROWTH IN THE TRAILING PORTION OF THE REGION NOTED. REGION 6021
(S13E19) ALSO EXHIBITED SLIGHT GROWTH. A C4/SF FLARE WITH AN
ASSOCIATED SIGNIFICANT TYPE II SWEEP WAS OBSERVED AT 17/1629Z.
A DECLINE IN SPOT AREA WAS NOTED WITH REGION 6018 (S32W08).
NONE THE LESS, THIS REGION PRODUCED THE MAJORITY OF THE DAY'S
FLARE ACTIVITY WITH A C5/SF FLARE AT 17/1602Z THE LARGEST.
UNCORRELATED ACTIVITY INCLUDED A C9 X-RAY EVENT AT 17/0322Z
AND A MINOR TYPE II SWEEP AT 17/1708Z. NEW REGIONS 6030
(N22E33) AND 6031 (S20E50) WERE ASSIGNED TODAY.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE TO HIGH. REGIONS 6021 AND 6022 REMAIN THE BEST
CANDIDATES FOR MAJOR FLARE PRODUCTION WITH REGION 6018 A
POSSIBLE SOURCE FOR ISOLATED M-CLASS ACTIVITY.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 16/2100Z TO 17/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO MINOR STORM
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS. A SUDDEN IMPULSE OF 45
NANOTESLAS WAS OBSERVED AT 17/0719Z. THE X1/2B FLARE NOTED
15/0302Z WAS A LIKELY SOURCE. A SATELLITE LEVEL PROTON
EVENT AT GREATER THAN 10 MEV BEGAN 17/0500Z, REACHED A
PEAK OF 12 PROTONS/CM2-S-STER AT 17/0655Z, AND ENDED AT
17/0715Z. ALSO IN RESPONSE TO THE RECENT X-CLASS FLARING.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BECOME INCREASINGLY DISTURBED WITH MINOR TO
MAJOR STORMING EXPECTED THE FIRST DAY. THE REMAINDER OF THE
FORECAST PERIOD SHOULD BE CHARACTERIZED BY ACTIVE TO
MINOR STORM LEVELS. SATELLITE LEVEL PROTONS AT GREATER THAN
10 MEV REMAIN ENHANCED AND MAY EXCEED ALERT CRITERIA
WITHIN THE NEXT 24 HOURS.
