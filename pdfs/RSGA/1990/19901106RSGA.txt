JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 310 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 06 NOV 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 05/2100Z
TO 06/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 6350
(S24W15) STARTED OFF THE PERIOD WITH AN M1/2B FLARE AT
05/2312Z FOLLOWED BY ISOLATED SUBFLARING THEREAFTER.
REGIONS 6345 (S05W47) AND 6347 (S18W20) BOTH CONTINUED
TO DECAY WITH OCCASIONAL C-CLASS FLARING ATTRIBUTED TO
6347. REGION 6358 (N17E46) EXHIBITED MODEST GROWTH WHILE
MAINTAINING AN ENHANCED PLAGE FIELD THROUGHOUT MOST OF THE
PERIOD. A C6/SF FLARE AT 06/1022Z WAS THE LARGEST EVENT
OBSERVED FROM THIS AREA. A 28 DEGREE LONG SEGMENT OF FILAMENT
DISAPPEARED FROM NEAR S48E33 PRIOR TO 0707Z TODAY. AN M3/2B
FLARE FROM REGION 6361 (N07E66) AT 06/2052Z CLOSED OUT THE
PERIOD. THIS NEWLY EMERGED REGION DISPLAYED SIGNIFICANT
OVERNIGHT GROWTH. NEW REGION 6364 (S29E63) WAS ASSIGNED
TODAY.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE WITH THE TREND TOWARDS AN INCREASED CHANCE OF
ISOLATED M-CLASS FLARING.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 05/2100Z TO 06/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET LEVELS FOR
THE PAST 24 HOURS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  GENERALLY ACTIVE
CONDITIONS SHOULD START THE PERIOD IN RESPONSE TO EFFECTS
FROM A RETURNING CORONAL HOLE FOLLOWED BY UNSETTLED LEVELS
OF ACTIVITY.
