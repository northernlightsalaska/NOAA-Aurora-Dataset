JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 236 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 24 AUG 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 23/2100Z
TO 24/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 6223 (S12W07),
PRODUCED THE DAY'S LONE M-CLASS EVENT, AN M1/1N AT 0940Z. THIS
GROUP AND NEARBY REGION 6226 (S21W03) DOMINATE THE CENTER OF
THE DISK. FREQUENT SURGING HAS BEEN SEEN NEAR REGION 6214
(S12W74), ALSO IMPOSING IN H-ALPHA AND WHITE LIGHT. THE BACK-
GROUND X-RAY AND 10 CM. FLUX VALUES REMAIN ELEVATED WITH THE
RADIO FLUX BEING THE HIGHEST RECORDED SINCE JUNE, 1989. A
FILAMENT LOCATED NEAR N60W15 DISAPPEARED IN THE LAST 24 HOURS.
TWO NEW REGIONS, 6234 (N24W03) AND 6235 (S13W19), WERE
ASSIGNED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE. REGIONS 6214, 6223, AND 6226 ALL POSSESS THE ABILITY
TO PRODUCE OCCASIONAL M-CLASS FLARES.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 23/2100Z TO 24/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS AT MINOR TO MAJOR STORM LEVELS
EARLY IN THE INTERVAL. THE DISTURBANCE HAS SINCE WEAKENED,
WITH QUIET TO UNSETTLED CONDITIONS NOW PREVALENT. THE SOURCE
OF THIS ACTIVITY IS THOUGHT TO BE RELATED TO THE FILAMENT
DISAPPEARANCE OF 17-18 AUGUST.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE THE NEXT THREE DAYS. SHORT-
LIVED MINOR STORM CONDITIONS MAY OCCUR DURING LOCAL NIGHTTIMES.
