FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 130
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 10 MAY 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 09/2100Z
TO 10/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH. AN X3 X-RAY BURST WAS
OBSERVED AT 10/1930Z FROM BEHIND THE EAST LIMB AT LATITUDE N28.
A TYPE II SWEEP (EST. SHOCK VEL. 1600 KM/SEC), A 1200 SFU
TENFLARE, AND A VERY BRIGHT LOOP PROMINENCE SYSTEM ACCOMPANIED
THE BURST. AN OPTICALLY UNCORRELATED M3 X-RAY BURST OCCURRED AT
10/1539Z ACCOMPANIED BY MINOR DISCRETE RADIO EMISSIONS. REGION
6054 (S18E24) SHOWED SLIGHT GROWTH AND PRODUCED OCCASIONAL
SUBFLARES THE LARGEST OF WHICH WAS A C2 AT 09/2305Z. REGION 6049
(N18W23) APPEARED TO RELAX IN MAGNETIC ASPECTS AND WAS FAIRLY
STABLE. NEW REGION 6061 (N15E16) WAS NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE TO HIGH. HIGH ACTIVITY MAY COME FROM THE NORTHEAST
LIMB AS THE YET UNSEEN SUNSPOT GROUP ROTATES INTO VIEW. M-CLASS
ACTIVITY MAY OCCUR FROM REGIONS 6049 AND 6054.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 09/2100Z TO 10/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT UNSETTLED TO MAJOR STORM
LEVELS. A SUDDEN IMPULSE OF 53 NANOTESLA WAS OBSERVED AT
BOULDER AT 09/2128Z. THE STORMING OCCURRED FROM 10/0600-1200Z
AND MAY HAVE BEEN IN RESPONSE TO A LARGE FILAMENT WHICH
DEPARTED THE SOUTHWEST QUADRANT BETWEEN 04/2100-05/0300Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE LEVELS AT MIDDLE
LATITUDES. THE HIGH LATITUDE FIELD IS EXPECTED TO BE AT ACTIVE
TO MINOR STORM LEVELS DUE TO LINGERING FILAMENT EFFECTS
COUPLED WITH ANTICIPATED CORONAL HOLE EFFECTS.
