JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 256 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 13 SEP 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 12/2100Z
TO 13/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. REGIONS 6267 (S13E46)
AND 6268 (N23E52) HAVE OBSERVED MODEST SPOT AREA GROWTH.
REGION 6266 (S26E44) PRODUCED A C2/SF FLARE AT 13/1757Z, THE
LARGEST OF THE PERIOD. SEVERAL LOW-LEVEL C-CLASS EVENTS
WERE ATTRIBUTED TO REGION 6263 (N10E38) WHICH HAS REMAINED
GENERALLY STABLE OTHERWISE. AN AREA OF ENHANCED PLAGE WAS
OBSERVED ON THE SOUTHEAST LIMB POSSIBLY HAILING THE RETURN
OF OLD REGION 6223 WHICH IS DUE BACK TODAY. NEW REGION
6270 (S19E65) WAS ASSIGNED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW WITH AN INCREASING CHANCE FOR MODERATE LEVELS AS PREVIOUS
ACTIVE CENTERS RETURN.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 12/2100Z TO 13/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO ACTIVE LEVELS
FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT ACTIVE LEVELS THE FIRST DAY WITH THE
TREND TOWARDS UNSETTLED CONDITIONS THEREAFTER.
