JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 322 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 18 NOV 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 17/2100Z
TO 18/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE.  REGION 6368 (N18E13)
CONTINUED TO PRODUCE SMALL M CLASS FLARES (AN M1/SN AT 18/0022Z
AND AN M1/1N AT 18/1814Z) IN ADDITION TO SEVERAL C CLASS FLARES.
THE REGION REMAINS COMPLEX ALTHOUGH THE RATE OF GROWTH HAS
DECREASED AND SIGNS OF FRAGMENTATION OF THE TRAILER SPOTS IS
EVIDENT.  AT LEAST TWO DELTA CONFIGURATIONS ARE STILL PRESENT
IN THE TRAILER SPOT COMPLEX.  NEW REGIONS NUMBERED INCLUDE
6373 (N10W17), 6374 (N21W13), AND 6375 (N21E34).
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE TO HIGH.  MAJOR ACTIVITY IS POSSIBLE IN REGION 6368.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 17/2100Z TO 18/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS QUIET TO UNSETTLED AT MIDDLE
LATITUDES AND QUIET TO ACTIVE AT HIGH LATITUDES.  PERIODS OF
STORM CONDITIONS WERE OBSERVED AT SOME HIGH LATITUDE STATIONS
FROM 18/09-18Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE.  CORONAL HOLE ACTIVITY
IS POSSIBLE THE FIRST TWO DAYS OF THE FORECAST PERIOD.
