IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 07/2100Z
TO 08/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE.  AN M1/1B X-RAY FLARE
WAS OBSERVED FROM REGION 6131 (S20W79) AT 0050Z.  ADDITIONAL
C-CLASS FLARING WAS REPORTED FROM FROM REGION 6133 (N18W50).
A NUMBER OF SMALL DISAPPEARING FILAMENTS WERE ALSO REPORTED
TODAY.  THE LARGEST OF THESE WAS ABOUT 8 DEGREES FROM
APPROXIMATELY N31E13.  TWO REGIONS WERE NUMBERED, 6145 (N12E63)
AND 6146 (S23E38).
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE.  SEVERAL REGIONS CONTINUE TO MAINTAIN A SLIGHT
POTENTIAL FOR ISOLATED M-CLASS FLARES.  REGION 6137 (S15W80)
APPEARS TO HAVE THE MOST POTENTIAL.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 07/2100Z TO 08/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN QUIET TO UNSETTLED AT
MID LATITUDES.  SEVERAL PERIODS OF ACTIVE AND ONE OF MINOR
STORM CONDITIONS WERE OBSERVED AT HIGH LATITUDES.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE MOSTLY UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE THROUGHOUT THE
FORECAST PERIOD.
