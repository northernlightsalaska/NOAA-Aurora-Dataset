IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 21/2100Z
TO 22/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 5947 (S17E36)
PRODUCED AN M1/1N AT 22/1454Z ASSOCIATED WITH MINOR DISCRETE
RADIO EMISSIONS. THIS REGION DISPLAYED A SIGNIFICANT INCREASE IN
MAGNETIC STRUCTURE DEVELOPING A DELTA CONFIGURATION WITHIN ITS
CENTRAL PENUMBRAL MASS. GRADUAL GROWTH CONTINUED IN REGIONS
5942 (S35E16) AND 5946 (N03W22) AND BEGAN IN REGION 5948
(N16E41). REGION 5948 PRODUCED OCCASIONAL C-CLASS SUBFLARES. NEW
REGIONS 5953 (S11E59) AND 5954 (S14W47) WERE NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE. REGION 5947 IS EXPECTED TO PRODUCE OCCASIONAL
M-CLASS FLARES. THIS REGION PROVIDES A SLIGHT CHANCE FOR MAJOR
FLARE ACTIVITY GIVEN ITS MAGNETIC COMPLEXITY.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 21/2100Z TO 22/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO ACTIVE LEVELS FOR
THE PAST 24 HOURS. THE AURORAL LATITUDES REPORTED MINOR
STORM LEVELS FROM 22/1200-1800Z. THE ORIGIN OF THE STORMING
MAY BE A CORONAL HOLE CURRENTLY LOCATED IN THE NORTHWEST
QUADRANT.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE.
