JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 239 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 27 AUG 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 26/2100Z
TO 27/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 6233 (N14E23),
STILL COMPLEX AND BRIGHT, PRODUCED AN M2/0F AT 1918Z. IT ALSO
GENERATED FREQUENT SUBFLARES THROUGHOUT THE DAY. REGION 6226
(S22W43) YIELDED THE OTHER M-CLASS EVENT OF THE INTERVAL, AN
M1/2F AT 0358Z. THAT REGION'S FLARE OUTPUT DECREASED AS IT
EXHIBITED OVERALL DECAY. NEWLY ASSIGNED REGIONS ARE ALL NEAR
EAST LIMB - 6237 (S17E64), 6238 (S11E81), AND 6239 (N17E84).
**LATE NOTE:  REGION 6233 PRODUCED AN X3 FLARE IN PROGRESS AT
2100Z.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE TO HIGH. REGION 6233 HAS THE BEST POTENTIAL FOR
ENERGETIC ACTIVITY, CLOSELY FOLLOWED BY REGION 6226.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 26/2100Z TO 27/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS AT ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM LEVELS
THROUGH 0600Z. THE DISTURBANCE THEN WEAKENED, GIVING WAY TO
QUIET TO UNSETTLED CONDITIONS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE THE NEXT 48 HOURS. AUGUST
30 SHOULD BE ACTIVE AT ALL LATITUDES DUE TO THE ANTICIPATED
EFFECTS OF A WELL-POSITIONED CORONAL HOLE.
