JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 249 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 06 SEP 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 05/2100Z
TO 06/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW.  ONLY SMALL FLARES WERE
OBSERVED.  MOST REGIONS APPEAR STABLE OR DECLINING.  MINOR
ACTIVITY WAS REPORTED EARLY IN THE DAY ASSOCIATED WITH DEPARTED
REGION 6233 (N14 L032).  REGION 6253 (N07 E68) HAS ROTATED INTO
VIEW BEHIND REGION 6252 (N13 E47).  SOME OF THE MAJOR REGIONS
OF LAST MONTH ARE DUE TO RETURN OVER THE NEXT FEW DAYS, THE
LARGEST, OLD REGION 6214 (S10 L166), IS DUE BACK LATE ON 8 SEP.
SEVERAL LARGE PROMINENCES ARE VISIBLE ON THE WEST LIMB.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW WITH AN OUTSIDE CHANCE FOR AN ISOLATED M FLARE.  ACTIVITY
COULD PICK UP AT THE END OF THE PERIOD IF RETURNING REGIONS
ARE ACTIVE, HOWEVER, THERE IS NO INDICATION OF ANY SIGNIFICANT
LIMB ACTIVITY AT THIS TIME.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 05/2100Z TO 06/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT MOSTLY UNSETTLED
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE QUIET TO UNSETTLED.
