JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 309 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 05 NOV 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 04/2100Z
TO 05/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. REGION 6358 (N15E60)
DISPLAYED MODEST GROWTH WHILE PRODUCING A NUMBER OF C-CLASS
FLARES. THE LARGEST BEING A C4/SF EVENT AT 05/1304Z. THE
PLAGE REMAINED ENHANCED IN THIS AREA THROUGHOUT MOST OF THE
PERIOD. REGIONS 6350 (S25W02) AND 6347 (S18W06) PRODUCED
OCCASIONAL SUBFLARING. 6347 CONTINUED TO DECAY WHILE 6350
GREW SLIGHTLY IN SPOT AREA. SCATTERED PLAGE AREAS DOTTED THE
EASTERN LIMB WITH OCCASIONAL SURGES NOTED BOTH NORTH AND
SOUTH. A 22 DEGREE LONG SEGMENT OF FILAMENT DISAPPEARED FROM
NEAR S24W25 LATE ON THE 4TH. NEW REGION 6359 (S18E63) WAS
ASSIGNED TODAY.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
GENERALLY LOW. INCREASED GROWTH IN REGION 6358 OR INTERACTION
BETWEEN REGIONS 6347 AND 6350 MAY SPAWN AN ISOLATED M-EVENT
HOWEVER.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 04/2100Z TO 05/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET LEVELS FOR
THE PAST 24 HOURS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE GENERALLY ACTIVE IN RESPONSE TO EFFECTS FROM
RETURNING CORONAL HOLES.
