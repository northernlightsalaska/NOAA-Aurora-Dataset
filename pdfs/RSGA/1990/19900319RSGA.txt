JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 19 MAR 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 18/2100Z
TO 19/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH. REGION 5969 (N32W51)
PRODUCED AN X1/2B PARALLEL-RIBBON FLARE AT 19/0508Z. A 1400 SFU
TENFLARE AND TYPES II AND IV RADIO SWEEPS ACCOMPANIED THE FLARE.
THIS REGION ALSO PRODUCED AN M1/2N FLARE AT 19/0338Z. REGION
5969 MAY HAVE ENTERED A PERIOD OF GRADUAL DECAY AFTER THE MAJOR
FLARE. REGION 5987 (S16E66) PRODUCED AN M4/1N FLARE AT 19/1354Z.
GROWTH WAS REPORTED IN REGIONS 5974 (S33E06) AND 5983 (S32E56).
NEW REGIONS 5987 (S16E66), 5988 (S07E75), AND 5989 (N15E64)
WERE NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE TO HIGH. M-CLASS ACTIVITY MAY OCCUR FROM REGIONS 5969,
5974, 5983, AND 5987. REGIONS 5969 AND 5974 ARE ALSO CAPABLE OF
ISOLATED MAJOR FLARE PRODUCTION.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 18/2100Z TO 19/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO ACTIVE LEVELS
FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS. EPISODES OF MINOR STORMING OCCURRED
AT HIGH LATITUDES EARLY IN THE PERIOD. A SATELLITE LEVEL
PROTON EVENT AT GREATER THAN 10 MEV BEGAN AT 19/0705Z AND WAS
IN PROGRESS AT THE END OF THE PERIOD. THE MAXIMUM GREATER
THAN 10 MEV FLUX OBSERVED THUS FAR WAS 620 PFU AT 19/2035Z.
A POLAR CAP ABSORPTION EVENT BEGAN AT 19/1015Z AND REACHED A
MAXIMUM OF 9.1 DB AT 19/1615Z. IT REMAINED IN PROGRESS AT THE
END OF THE PERIOD. BOTH EVENTS WERE A RESULT OF TODAY'S MAJOR
FLARE.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT ACTIVE TO MAJOR STORM LEVELS. THE SHOCK FROM
TODAY'S FLARE SHOULD REACH EARTH LATE ON 21 MARCH AFTER WHICH
TIME MINOR TO MAJOR STORMING IS EXPECTED. BOTH THE GREATER
THAN 10 MEV PROTON AND POLAR CAP ABSORPTION EVENTS ARE
EXPECTED TO CONTINUE THROUGH TOMORROW.
