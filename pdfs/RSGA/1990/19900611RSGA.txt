FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 162
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 11 JUN 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 10/2100Z
TO 11/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY HAS BEEN MODERATE.  THREE M-CLASS
FLARES HIGHLIGHTED THE DAYS ACTIVITY.  EARLY IN THE PERIOD
REGION 6089 (N10W32) PRODUCED AN M1/SF WHICH REACHED A MAXIMUM
AT 10/2205Z AND HAD A TYPE IV SWEEP ASSOCIATED.  AT 11/0945Z
REGION 6089 PRODUCED AN M4/2B.  THE THIRD M-CLASS FLARE
ORIGINATED IN REGION 6100 (S15E37), AN M1/2N AT 11/1855Z.
ADDITIONAL SUBFLARING WAS OBSERVED FROM REGION 6095 (N24W26)
AND THIS REGION HAS SHOWN CONTINUED GROWTH IN SIZE AND SPOT
COUNT, ALTHOUGH MAGNETIC COMPLEXITY HAS REMAINED STABLE.
THESE THREE REGIONS, 6089, 6095, AND 6100, REMAIN THE LARGEST
AND MOST ACTIVE ON THE DISK.  THE REMAINDER OF THE SPOTTED
REGIONS WERE SMALL AND MOSTLY STABLE.  REGION 6103 (N16E45)
WAS ASSIGNED TODAY.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE.  REGIONS 6089, 6095, AND 6100 CONTINUE TO HAVE
CONSIDERABLE POTENTIAL FOR M-CLASS ACTIVITY.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 10/2100Z TO 11/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN MOSTLY UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE
THE PAST 24 HOURS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  GEOMAGNETIC ACTIVITY IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE LEVELS FOR THE FORECAST
PERIOD.
