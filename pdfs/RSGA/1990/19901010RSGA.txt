JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 283 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 10 OCT 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 09/2100Z
TO 10/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 6314
(S17E54) DISPLAYED SIGNIFICANT GROWTH IN SPOT AREA WHILE
CONTINUING TO PRODUCE THE MAJORITY OF THE DAYS EVENTS.
A C1/SF FLARE FROM THIS AREA AT 10/0654Z WAS ACCOMPANIED
BY A WEAK TYPE II SWEEP AND MODEST RADIO EMISSIONS AT 410MHZ.
THE LARGEST EVENT OF THE PERIOD, AN M1/SN FLARE AT 10/1804Z,
WAS ALSO ATTRIBUTED TO THIS AREA. REGIONS 6311 (N14E41) AND
6312 (N10E46) HAVE BECOME INCREASINGLY ACTIVE DURING THE
PERIOD WITH SOME GROWTH NOTED. THE REMAINING REGIONS ON THE
DISK WERE GENERALLY STABLE. NEW REGION 6318 (S15W00) WAS
ASSIGNED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE. REGION 6314 CONTINUES AS THE BEST SOURCE FOR AN
ISOLATED M-FLARE.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 09/2100Z TO 10/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT UNSETTLED TO MINOR
STORM LEVELS AT MIDDLE LATITUDES WITH MAJOR STORMING
OBSERVED AT HIGH LATITUDES. THE SOURCE OF THIS ACTIVITY
IS UNCERTAIN.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE ACTIVE AT THE START OF THE PERIOD WITH
ACTIVE TO UNSETTLED CONDITIONS EXPECTED THEREAFTER.
