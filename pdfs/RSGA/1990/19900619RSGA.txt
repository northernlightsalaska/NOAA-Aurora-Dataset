IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 18/2100Z
TO 19/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW.  ONE C1/SF FLARE OCCURRED
FROM REGION 6106 (S30W35).  ONLY TWO OTHER SUBFLARES OCCURRED.
THE OTHER REGIONS ON THE DISK HAVE BEEN GENERALLY QUIET.  TWO
FILAMENT DISAPPEARANCES OCCURRED ON THE SOUTHERN PORTION OF
THE DISK BETWEEN 18/1729Z AND 18/2316Z AT S46E18 AND S53W15.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
GENERALLY LOW.  ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO INCREASE ON THE THIRD
DAY AS OLD REGION 6093 ROTATES ONTO THE DISK.  THIS REGION WAS
A QUICK EMERGER AND PRODUCED TWO M CLASS FLARES.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 18/2100Z TO 19/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN QUIET TO UNSETTLED.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO INCREASE TO ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM LEVELS DURING
THE NEXT 24 HOURS DUE TO THE FILAMENT DISAPPEARANCE THAT
OCCURRED AT 16/1725Z.  ACTIVITY WILL SUBSIDE TO UNSETTLED TO
ACTIVE CONDITIONS DURING DAYS TWO AND THREE.
