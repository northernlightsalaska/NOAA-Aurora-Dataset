IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 22/2100Z
TO 23/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 5988 (S08E26)
WAS THE DOMINANT REGION ON THE DISK TODAY PRODUCING MOST OF
THE X-RAY EVENTS OBSERVED TO INCLUDE THE LARGEST: AN M1/1N
FLARE AT 23/1255Z. REGION 5974 (S34W44) BECAME MORE ACTIVE
THROUGH THE PERIOD PRODUCING NUMEROUS SUBFLARES AND A C8/SF
EVENT AT 23/0627Z. IT REMAINS THE LARGEST AND MOST COMPLEX
REGION ON THE DISK BUT IS STARTING TO DECAY IN AREA NOW.
REGION 5984 (S13W03) CONTINUED TO GROW IN SIZE AND COMPLEXITY.
THREE NEW REGIONS WERE ASSIGNED: 5991 (N21W00), 5992 (S11W64),
AND 5993 (N09E74).
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE. M-CLASS EVENTS ARE POSSIBLE FROM REGIONS 5974 AND
5988 WITH 5974 SHOWING THE BEST POTENTIAL FOR AN ISOLATED
MAJOR FLARE. REGIONS 5983 (S34E09) AND 5984 MAY ALSO
CONTRIBUTE TO ACTIVITY LEVELS.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 22/2100Z TO 23/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS WITH HIGH LATITUDES EXPERIENCING
MINOR STORM CONDITIONS BETWEEN 0900-1800Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE ACTIVE FOR THE NEXT THREE DAYS.
