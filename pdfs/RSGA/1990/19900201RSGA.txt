IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 31/2100Z
TO 01/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. THE LARGEST EVENT OF THE
PERIOD WAS A C5/SF FLARE AT 01/0211Z FROM REGION 5897 (S13W92).
MODERATE RADIO EMISSIONS AND A BRIGHT SURGE ON THE LIMB WERE
ASSOCIATED WITH THIS EVENT. REGION 5900 (S10W69) IS DECAYING
IN ITS TRAILING SPOT STRUCTURE BUT MAINTAINS ITS MAGNETIC
COMPLEXITY. REGION 5919 (N09E20) CONTINUED TO GROW IN OVERALL
SPOT AND PLAGE COVERAGE. LOW LEVEL C-CLASS ACTIVITY WAS NOTED
FROM REGIONS 5906 (N12W75), 5904 (N12W40), AND 5911 (N30W14).
REGIONS 5920 (N14E15) AND 5921 (S24E41) WERE ASSIGNED TODAY.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE WITH PREDOMINANTLY C-CLASS ACTIVITY OCCURING.
ISOLATED M-CLASS EVENTS REMAIN POSSIBLE FROM REGION 5900.

EXPECTED TO BE QUIET TO UNSETTLED FOR THE NEXT THREE DAYS.
