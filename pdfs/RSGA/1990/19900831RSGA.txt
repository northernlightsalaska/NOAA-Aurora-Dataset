JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 243 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 31 AUG 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 30/2100Z
TO 31/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. DEPARTING REGION 6226
(S23W93) PRODUCED TODAY'S ONLY M-CLASS EVENT: AN M1/1F AT 0415Z.
THE DEPARTURE OF 6226 AND OTHER REGIONS IN THE SAME AREA WERE
REFLECTED IN THE SIGNFICANT DECREASE OF 10.7 CM FLUX LEVELS.
REGION 6233 (N13W32) PRODUCED SEVERAL C-CLASS SUBFLARES DURING
THE PERIOD AND RETAINS ITS MAGNETIC COMPLEXITY. REGION 6244
(N13E43) SHOWED SOME GROWTH DURING THE PERIOD AS IT DEVELOPED
PENUMBRA ON LEADER AND FOLLOWER SPOTS AND IT PRODUCED SOME SMALL
FLARES DURING THE PERIOD.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE. REGION 6233 IS EXPECTED TO BE THE MAIN SOURCE FOR
ADDITIONAL M-CLASS FLARES. DUE TO THE COMPLEXITY AND SIZE
OF 6233 THERE CONTINUES TO BE A CHANCE FOR AN ISOLATED MAJOR
FLARE FROM THIS REGION.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 30/2100Z TO 31/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS AT ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM LEVELS FROM
THE BEGINNING OF THE PERIOD UNTIL ABOUT 1200UT. SINCE THEN
CONDITIONS HAVE BEEN UNSETTLED TO SLIGHTLY ACTIVE.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE GENERALLY UNSETTLED FOR THE NEXT TWO DAYS,
DECLINING TO QUIET TO UNSETTLED LEVELS ON THE THIRD DAY.
