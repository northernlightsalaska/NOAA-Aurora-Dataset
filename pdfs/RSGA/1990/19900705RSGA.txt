JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 05 JUL 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 04/2100Z
TO 05/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE.  THE SIGNIFICANT
EVENT WAS AN M1/2B FROM REGION 6133 (N17W07) AT 0345Z.  EARLIER
IN THE PERIOD A SUBFLARE FROM REGION 6133 PRODUCED A TYPE II
RADIO SWEEP.  ADDITIONAL C-CLASS FLARES WERE REPORTED FROM
REGIONS 6126 (S12W56) AND 6138 (N22E11).  NEW REGION 6141
(S06W16) WAS NUMBERED TODAY.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE.  NUMEROUS REGIONS HAVE THE POTENTIAL FOR PRODUCING AN
ISOLATED M-CLASS FLARE.  THE REGIONS WITH THE BEST POTENTIAL
APPEAR TO BE 6126, 6131 (N21W39), 6133, AND 6138.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 04/2100Z TO 05/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS RANGED FROM QUIET TO ACTIVE
CONDITIONS AT MID LATITUDES.  SEVERAL PERIODS OF MINOR STORMING
WERE OBSERVED AT HIGH LATITUDES.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE LEVELS THE FIRST 24 HOURS.
ON DAYS TWO AND THREE, ACTIVE CONDITIONS ARE EXPECTED IN
RESPONSE TO THE COMBINED EFFECT OF A MID-DISK CORONAL HOLE AND
YESTERDAY'S M7/2B TENFLARE.
