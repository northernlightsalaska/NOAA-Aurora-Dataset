IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 13/2100Z
TO 14/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE.  REGION 5969 (N33E15)
PRODUCED THE PERIOD'S ONLY M FLARE, AN M1.1 AT 13/2142UT.  THIS
FLARE WAS ACCOMPANIED BY MINOR RADIO EMISSIONS.  REGION 5969
CONTINUES TO EVOLVE AND REMAINS THE LARGEST REGION ON THE DISK.
REGION 5974 (S34E65) WAS THE OTHER MAIN FLARE PRODUCER.  TWO
LONG DURATION XRAY EVENTS, PEAKING AT 0433UT AND 1845UT APPEAR
TO BE RELATED TO SIMULTANEOUS BRIGHTENINGS IN REGIONS 5969
AND 5974.  MINOR ACTIVITY WAS REPORTED IN REGION 5967 (N20W13).
REGION 5975 (S20E67) WAS ASSIGNED.  THERE APPEARS TO BE REGIONS
AT OR NEAR THE EAST LIMB.  A LARGE N-S ORIENTATED FILAMENT IS
CENTERED OVER THE EQUATOR NEAR W20.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE.  ADDITIONAL LOW LEVEL M FLARES ARE POSSIBLE
FROM REGIONS 5969 AND 5974.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 13/2100Z TO 14/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO MINOR STORM
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS.  THE STORM WHICH BEGAN AT
12/1501UT IS DIMINISHING.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE FOR THE NEAR FUTURE.
