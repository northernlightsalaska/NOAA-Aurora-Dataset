IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 29/2100Z
TO 30/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. ONLY A FEW SUBFLARES WERE
OBSERVED DURING THE PERIOD, THE LARGEST BEING A C1/SF AT 1057Z
(TODAY'S ONLY C-CLASS EVENT). THE LARGEST REGIONS ARE 6042
(S19W73) AND 6045 (N20E26), BOTH BASICALLY STABLE WITH AREA 120
MILLIONTHS. THE REMAINDER OF THE DISK AND LIMBS WERE QUIET AND
STABLE.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW. REGION 6042 (WHICH WILL EXIT THE DISK IN 2 DAYS) MAY PRO-
DUCE SOME ADDITIONAL SMALL FLARES. THERE ARE NO SIGNIFICANT
RETURNING REGIONS FOR THE NEXT WEEK, SO UNLESS FLUX EMERGES
RAPIDLY SOMEWHERE ON THE SUN, SOLAR ACTIVITY WILL BE MINIMAL.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 29/2100Z TO 30/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS GENERALLY UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE. A
BRIEF PERIOD OF MINOR STORM CONDITIONS OCCURRED FROM 00-03Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE GENERALLY ACTIVE TOMORROW, AND IS EXPECTED TO
DECLINE TO UNSETTLED LEVELS FOR THE SECOND AND THIRD DAYS.
