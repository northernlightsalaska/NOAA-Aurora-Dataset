SDF NUMBER 058
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 27 FEB 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 26/2100Z
TO 27/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. REGION 5947 (S17W29) AND
5961 (N30E51) CONTRIBUTED THE MAJORITY OF C-CLASS EVENTS
TODAY, MANY OF WHICH WERE ASSOCIATED WITH STRONG RADIO
EMISSIONS. REGION 5947 SHOWED ONLY SLIGHT DECAY, MOST
NOTABLY IN THE FORM OF PENEUMBRAL FRAGMENTATION. THE REGION
REMAINS THE MOST MAGNETICALLY COMPLEX, HOWEVER IT IS SLOWLY
WEAKENING. REGION 5961 HAS GROWN RAPIDLY SINCE ITS EMERGENCE
WITH OCCASIONAL SURGING NOTED. REGION 5960 (N12E29) SHOWED
SLIGHT GROWTH IN ITS SPOT STRUCTURE. REGION 5948 (N17W24) IS
DECAYING RAPIDLY NOW MOSTLY IN THE TRAILING PORTION OF THE
REGION. THE LARGEST EVENT OF THE PERIOD WAS A C7/1N FLARE
AT 27/0708Z FROM REGION 5947. REGION 5962 (S25W78) WAS
ASSIGNED TODAY.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. REGION 5947 CONTINUES TO DISPLAY THE BEST
POTENTIAL FOR M-CLASS ACTIVITY. RAPIDLY DEVELOPING REGION
5961 MAY ALSO CONTRIBUTE AN ISOLATED M-CLASS EVENT LATER IN
THE PERIOD.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 26/2100Z TO 27/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE FOR THE NEXT THREE DAYS.
