JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 25 JUN 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 24/2100Z
TO 25/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 6122 (S23E45)
DISPLAYED A GRADUAL INCREASE IN MAGNETIC COMPLEXITY AND PRODUCED
THE ONLY M-CLASS EVENT OF THE PERIOD: AN M1/2B FLARE AT 25/0131Z
WHICH WAS ASSOCIATED WITH TYPES II AND IV RADIO SWEEPS. SEVERAL
C-CLASS FLARES WERE PRODUCED BY THIS REGION AS WELL. SLIGHT
GROWTH WAS NOTED IN REGION 6114 (S09W13). THE REMAINING REGIONS
WERE STABLE. NEW REGIONS 6124 (S09E03) AND 6125 (N05E53) WERE
NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. REGION 6122 IS EXPECTED TO PRODUCE SEVERAL
C-CLASS FLARES AND ISOLATED M-CLASS FLARES. REGION 6114 APPEARS
CAPABLE OF C-CLASS FLARE PRODUCTION.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 24/2100Z TO 25/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO UNSETTLED LEVELS
FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED AT MIDDLE LATITUDES. THE HIGH
LATITUDE FIELD IS EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE. THE
ACTIVE LEVELS ARE EXPECTED IN RESPONSE TO A CORONAL HOLE NOW
LOCATED IN THE NORTHWEST QUADRANT.
