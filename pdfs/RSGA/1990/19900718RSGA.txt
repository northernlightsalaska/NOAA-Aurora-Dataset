JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 199 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 18 JUL 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 17/2100Z
TO 18/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE.  REGION 6161 (S28E53)
PRODUCED THE LARGEST FLARE DURING THE PAST 24 HOURS, AN M1/SF
AT 18/0632Z, AS WELL AS SEVERAL C CLASS FLARES.  THIS REGION
CONTINUES TO DEVELOP AND HAS UNDERGONE CONSIDERABLE SUNSPOT
GROWTH SINCE YESTERDAY, INCLUDING THE FORMATION OF A DELTA
CONFIGURATION. REMAINING REGIONS WERE QUIET.  REGION 6162
(N10E75) WAS NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE.  THE LIKELIHOOD OF MAJOR ACTIVITY WILL INCREASE
AS REGION 6161 DEVELOPS.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 17/2100Z TO 18/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS QUIET TO UNSETTLED.  A MINOR SHOCK
FRONT ARRIVAL WAS OBSERVED AT SATELLITE ALTITUDES AT ABOUT
18/0745Z BUT HARDLY REGISTERED ON GROUND BASED MAGNETOMETERS.
THIS SHOCK MAY BE ASSOCIATED WITH THE DSF/LDE OF 15 JULY.
THE FIELD REMAINED AT QUIET TO UNSETTLED LEVELS AFTERWARDS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE QUIET TO UNSETTLED.
