JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 266 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 23 SEP 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 22/2100Z
TO 23/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE DUE TO AN M2 X-RAY
EVENT WHICH OCCURRED AT 1313Z AND WAS ASSOCIATED WITH A WEAK
TYPE II SWEEP. OPTICAL OBSERVATIONS INDICATE THAT THE EVENT
MAY HAVE BEEN ASSOCIATED WITH DEPARTING REGION 6967 (S13W89)
AS SOME BRIGHTENINGS WERE OBSERVED BRIEFLY THROUGH CLOUDS AT
ONE OBSERVATORY. THE REST OF THE DAY'S ACTIVITY CONSISTED OF
ONLY A FEW SUBFLARES, MOSTLY FROM REGIONS 6280 (N13E32) AND
6263 (N12W94). THE OTHER REGIONS WERE QUIET AND STABLE.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW. THERE IS A SLIGHT CHANCE FOR AN ISOLATED M-CLASS FLARE FROM
REGION 6280.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 22/2100Z TO 23/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS GENERALLY UNSETTLED AT MIDDLE LAT-
ITUDES AND WAS UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE AT HIGH LATITUDES.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE MOSTLY UNSETTLED TOMORROW. UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE
LEVELS ARE EXPECTED ON THE SECOND AND THIRD DAYS IN RESPONSE
TO TODAY'S M2 FLARE.
