JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 335 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 01 DEC 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 30/2100Z
TO 01/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. AN UNCORRELATED
LONG DURATION M1 X-RAY EVENT WAS OBSERVED AT 01/0111Z. THIS
EVENT WAS FOLLOWED AT 0623Z BY A LONG DURATION C6 EVENT. A
POSSIBLE SOURCE FOR THIS ACTIVITY MAY BE NEWLY ASSIGNED
REGION 6395 (N16E73), A LARGE SPOT AREA WHICH EMERGED TODAY
FROM THE NORTHEAST LIMB WHERE FREQUENT SURGING HAD BEEN NOTED
THROUGHOUT THE PERIOD. THIS SPOT AREA MAY BE RETURNING OLD
REGION 6358 (N16, L-151) WHICH PRODUCED NUMEROUS C-CLASS EVENTS
DURING ITS LAST ROTATION. OCCASIONAL SUBFLARES FROM REGIONS
6388 (S06W10) AND 6390 (N35E48) ROUNDED OUT THE PERIOD. NEW
REGIONS 6394 (N05W59) AND 6396 (S22W34) WERE ALSO ASSIGNED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. NEW REGION 6395 MAY PRODUCE AN ISOLATED
M-CLASS FLARE.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 30/2100Z TO 01/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET LEVELS FOR
THE PAST 24 HOURS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE QUIET TO UNSETTLED.
