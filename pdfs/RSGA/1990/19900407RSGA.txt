JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 07 APR 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 06/2100Z
TO 07/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. REGION 6012 (N30E54)
PRODUCED THE DAY'S LARGEST EVENT, A C9/1N TENFLARE WITH TYPE II
AT 1532Z. LITTLE ELSE OF SIGNIFICANCE OCCURRED. TWO NEW REGIONS
WERE ASSIGNED, 6013 (S09W02) AND 6014 (N15E50).
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE. REGIONS 6001 (N21W28), 6006 (N31W51), 6007 (N24E27),
AND 6012 ARE ALL CAPABLE OF A SMALL M-CLASS FLARE.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 06/2100Z TO 07/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO UNSETTLED
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS. AN ENHANCEMENT OF THE GREATER
THAN 10 MEV PROTON FLUX BEGAN NEAR 0600Z. THE FLUX IS 6
P.F.U. AT THIS WRITING. THE SPECTRAL PROFILE OF THE INCREASE
SUGGESTS AN EASTERN HEMISPHERE PARENT FLARE. REGION 6007
PRODUCED SIGNIFICANT ACTIVITY FROM THAT LOCATION OVER THE
PAST FEW DAYS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE. THE CURRENT PROTON ENHANCE-
MENT MAY REACH EVENT LEVEL DURING THE NEXT 12 HOURS AND,
CHARACTERISTIC OF EASTERN HEMISPHERE FLARES, MAY LINGER ABOVE
10 P.F.U. FOR THE NEXT THREE DAYS. ADDITIONAL GEOMAGNETIC
ACTIVITY IS NOT EXPECTED TO RESULT FROM TODAY'S C9/1N FLARE.
