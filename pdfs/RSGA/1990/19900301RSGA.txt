JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 01 MAR 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 28/2100Z
TO 01/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 5961 (N30E26)
PRODUCED AN M1/1B FLARE AT 01/0344Z AND AN M1/SN FLARE AT
01/0536Z. NUMEROUS C-CLASS EVENTS HAVE BEEN OBSERVED FROM
THIS REGION AS WELL. THIS REGION HAS BECOME MORE COMPLEX
MAGNETICALLY BUT HAS SLOWED IN SPOT AREA GROWTH.
REGION 5947 (S16W54) IS BECOMING MORE ACTIVE. AN M1/2N EVENT
AT 01/0411Z WAS ATTRIBUTED TO THIS REGION ALONG WITH
OCCASIONAL SUBFLARES THROUGHOUT THE PERIOD. REGIONS 5948
(N18W54) CONTINUED TO DECAY WHILE REGION 5960 (N13E02)
REMAINED GENERALLY STABLE. REGION 5963 (S13E43) WAS ASSIGNED
TODAY.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
GENERALLY MODERATE. REGION 5961 IS THE DOMINANT REGION FOR
M-CLASS FLARE PRODUCTION. REGION 5947 MAY ALSO CONTRIBUTE
AN OCCASIONAL M-FLARE. BOTH REGIONS ALSO HAVE THE POTENTIAL
FOR VERY ISOLATED MAJOR FLARE ACTIVITY.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 28/2100Z TO 01/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE FOR THE NEXT THREE DAYS.
