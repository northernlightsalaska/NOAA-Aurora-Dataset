IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 21/2100Z
TO 22/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 5984 (S13E11)
PRODUCED THE DAY'S SOLE M-CLASS EVENT: AN M1/SF AT 19/0559Z.
THIS REGION, ALONG WITH 5988 (S08E38) AND 5983 (S33E17), HAVE
CONTINUED TO GROW THROUGHOUT THE PERIOD PRODUCING SPORADIC
SUBFLARES AND OCCASIONAL C-CLASS EVENTS. REGION 5974 (S21W31)
REMAINED GENERALLY STABLE WHILE CONTINUING TO SPORT IT'S COMPLEX
MAGNETIC STRUCTURE. AN ERUPTIVE PROMINENCE WAS NOTED FROM REGION
5969 (N34W89). AN UNCORRELATED TYPE II SWEEP WAS OBSERVED AT
19/0430Z. NO NEW REGIONS WERE ASSIGNED TODAY.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE TO HIGH. ISOLATED M-CLASS ACTIVITY IS POSSIBLE
FROM REGIONS 5983, 5984, 5988, OR 5974. REGION 5974 IS ALSO
CAPABLE OF PRODUCING AN ISOLATED MAJOR FLARE.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 21/2100Z TO 22/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE ACTIVE THROUGHOUT THE PERIOD WITH RESIDUAL
ENHANCEMENT FROM THE 19 MARCH X1/2B FLARE DOMINATING THE
FIRST DAY. EFFECTS FROM A CORONAL HOLE SHOULD CHARACTERIZE
THE REMAINDER OF THE PERIOD.
