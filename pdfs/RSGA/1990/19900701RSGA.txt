FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 182
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 01 JUL 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 30/2100Z
TO 01/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 6133
(N18E43) CONTINUES TO APPEAR IMPRESSIVE WITH AN INCREASE
IN COMPLEXITY NOTED. ONLY ISOLATED SUBFLARING HAS BEEN
OBSERVED IN THIS AREA HOWEVER. REGION 6127 (N09E08) DISPLAYED
MARKED GROWTH IN SPOT AREA COUPLED WITH THE MERGING OF ITS
TWO LARGE LEADER SPOTS. MAGNETIC COMPLEXITY HOWEVER REMAINS
SIMPLE WITH ONLY OCCASIONAL SUBFLARING OBSERVED HERE.
REGION 6126 (S12E01) PRODUCED THE BULK OF THE DAYS C-CLASS
ACTIVITY WITH A C6/1F FLARE AT 01/2038Z COMING IN AS THE
LARGEST. THIS GROUP IS AT THE HEAD OF A COMPLEX CLUSTER OF
REGIONS IN THE SOUTHEAST HEMISPHERE WHICH INCLUDES REGION
6131 (S21E14) WHICH PRODUCED THE LARGEST EVENT OF THE PERIOD:
AN M1/1F FLARE AT 01/0153Z. NEW REGION 6140 (S04W01) WAS
ASSIGNED TODAY.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE. ISOLATED M-CLASS FLARING IS POSSIBLE FROM REGION
6133 WITH REGIONS 6127, 6126, AND 6131 SHOWING POTENTIAL
TO A LESSER EXTENT.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 30/2100Z TO 01/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET LEVELS AT MIDDLE
LATITUDES WITH BRIEF UNSETTLED PERIODS NOTED AT HIGH
LATITUDE STATIONS DURING THE PAST 24 HOURS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE QUIET TO UNSETTLED.
