IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 02/2100Z
TO 03/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH. REGION 5900 (S12W86)
PRODUCED AN M6/2F TENFLARE WITH ASSOCIATED STRONG TYPE IV
SWEEP AT 03/0112Z. A VERY WEAK PROTON ENHANCEMENT OF 1.3 PFU
AT 10 MEV WAS NOTED AS A RESULT OF THIS EVENT AT 03/0745Z.
REGION 5913 (N14W59) PRODUCED A C4/SF AT 03/0656Z AND A
C3/SF AT 03/1832Z. SLIGHT GROWTH IN LEADER PORTION OF ITS
SPOT GROUP HAS BEEN NOTED. REGIONS 5919 (N08W06) AND 5920
(N14W12) HAVE SHOWN MODEST GROWTH IN PLAGE COVERAGE AND
SPOT AREA WITH AN INCREASE IN MAGNETIC COMPLEXITY NOTED IN
REGION 5919 WHICH HAS ALSO PRODUCED SEVERAL SUB FLARES THIS
PERIOD.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
PREDOMINANTLY LOW. THE POTENTIAL FOR AN ISOLATED M-CLASS EVENT
FROM REGION 5900 HOWEVER WILL CONTINUE UNTIL IT ROTATES AROUND
THE LIMB TOMORROW.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 02/2100Z TO 03/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO UNSETTLED
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE QUIET TO UNSETTLED FOR THE NEXT THREE DAYS
WITH AN ENHANCED FIELD POSSIBLE BY DAY THREE AS A RESULT
OF EFFECTS FROM A WEAK CORONAL HOLE.
