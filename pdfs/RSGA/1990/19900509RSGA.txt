JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 09 MAY 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 08/2100Z
TO 09/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. AN OPTICALLY
UNCORRELATED M1 X-RAY BURST OCCURRED AT 09/0413Z. REGION 6054
(S18E39) PRODUCED A C8/2B PARALLEL-RIBBON FLARE AT 09/1326Z.
MINOR RADIO EMISSIONS WERE ASSOCIATED WITH THIS FLARE. REGION
6049 (N18W10) INCREASED ITS SPOT COUNT AND REMAINED THE MOST
COMPLEX OF THE VISIBLE GROUPINGS; IT PRODUCED SEVERAL SUBFLARES.
AN ACTIVE PROMINENCE REGION AND INTENSE IRON XIV CORONAL
EMISSIONS WERE OBSERVED ON THE SOUTHEAST LIMB POSSIBLY HERALDING
THE RETURN OF OLD ACTIVE REGION 6021. NEW REGION 6060 (N22E74),
AN H-TYPE GROUP, WAS ASSIGNED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE. REGIONS 6049 AND 6054 DISPLAY POTENTIAL FOR M-CLASS
FLARE PRODUCTION.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 08/2100Z TO 09/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO ACTIVE LEVELS
FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS. A PERIOD OF MINOR TO MAJOR STORMING
OCCURRED FROM 09/1200-1500Z AT HIGH LATITUDES. THE STORMING
MAY HAVE BEEN IN RESPONSE TO A LARGE FILAMENT WHICH DEPARTED
THE SOUTHWEST QUADRANT BETWEEN 04/2100-05/0300Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE LEVELS. THE ACTIVE
LEVELS ARE EXPECTED IN RESPONSE TO CORONAL HOLE EFFECTS.
