JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 203 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 22 JUL 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 21/2100Z
TO 22/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. NEW REGION 6171 (N12E76)
ROTATED INTO VIEW AND PRODUCED A C1/SF FLARE AT 22/1815Z. REGION
6162 (N11E27) GREW SLOWLY AND PRODUCED A FEW SUBFLARES. BRIGHT
SURGING WAS OBSERVED FROM THE SOUTHEAST LIMB NEAR S09 AND S22.
THIS MAY HAVE BEEN A SIGN OF THE RETURN OF OLD REGION 6131
(CLASS/AREA EAI/360 ON 2 JULY). THIS REGION PRODUCED MODERATE
LEVELS OF ACTIVITY DURING ITS LAST TRANSIT. NEW REGION 6170
(S16W36) WAS ALSO NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. REGIONS 6161 (S28E02), 6162, AND 6171 ARE
LIKELY TO PRODUCE C-CLASS FLARES. REGION 6162 MAY ALSO PRODUCE
AN ISOLATED M-CLASS FLARE.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 21/2100Z TO 22/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO UNSETTLED LEVELS
FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED AT MIDDLE LATITUDES AND UNSETTLED TO
ACTIVE AT HIGH LATITUDES. THE ACTIVE LEVELS ARE EXPECTED IN
RESPONSE TO A SMALL CORONAL HOLE NOW CENTERED NEAR N07W17.
