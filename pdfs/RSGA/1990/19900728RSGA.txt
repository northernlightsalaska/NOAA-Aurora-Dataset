JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 209 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 28 JUL 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 27/2100Z
TO 28/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. REGION 6162 (N10W51)
CONTINUED TO DECAY IN SPOT AREA AND COMPLEXITY. A C2/SF
FLARE AT 28/0245Z WAS THE ONLY SIGNIFICANT EVENT ASSOCIATED
WITH THIS REGION TODAY. REGION 6171 (N10W01) PRODUCED A
C1/SF FLARE AT 28/1002Z. ISOLATED SUBFLARING CHARACTERIZED
THE REMAINDER OF THE DAY'S ACTIVITY. NEW REGIONS 6181
(S09E42) AND 6182 (N13W35) WERE ASSIGNED TODAY.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. ONLY ISOLATED M-CLASS ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED
WITH REGIONS 6162, 6177 (S20E19), AND 6174 (S12E13) POSING
THE GREATEST THREAT.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 27/2100Z TO 28/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT UNSETTLED TO MAJOR STORM
LEVELS. MINOR STORM LEVELS COMMENCED AT 28/0112Z WHEN AN
IMPULSE MEASURING 41 NANOTESLAS WAS OBSERVED. A SECOND
IMPULSE WAS NOTED AT 28/0334Z (68 NANOTESLAS). THE LIKELY
SOURCE FOR THIS ACTIVITY WAS THE LONG DURATION M2/2N FLARE
NOTED LATE 25 JULY. THE SATELLITE LEVEL PROTON EVENT THAT
BEGAN 26/1720Z AND PEAKED AT 26/2315Z (21 PFU), CONTINUES
IN PROGRESS WITH THE GREATER THAN 10 MEV LEVEL PROTONS
STILL FLUCTUATING NEAR THE 10 PFU THRESHOLD.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT MINOR STORM LEVELS AT THE BEGINING OF THE
PERIOD WITH THE FIELD SETTLING TO ACTIVE CONDITIONS TOWARDS
THE END OF THE PERIOD. THE GREATER THAN 10 MEV PROTONS
SHOULD BEGIN TO RETURN TO BACKGROUND LEVELS WITHIN THE NEXT
24 HOURS.
