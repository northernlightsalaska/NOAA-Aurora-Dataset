JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 24 APR 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 23/2100Z
TO 24/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW.  THE LARGEST EVENT OF THE
DAY WAS AN UNCORRELATED C9 AT 1120Z.  MOST REGIONS ON THE DISK
APPEAR STABLE OR DECLINING.  ONE NEW REGION 6041 (S40W42)
WAS ASSIGNED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE.  THERE IS STILL THE PROBABILITY OF ENERGETIC
ACTIVITY FROM REGION 6022 (N34W69).  SOLAR ACTIVITY WILL
DIMINISH IN THE NEXT SEVERAL DAYS AS REGIONS 6022 AND 6021
(S12W67) PASS BEYOND THE WEST LIMB.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 23/2100Z TO 24/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT UNSETTLED TO MINOR STORM
LEVELS THE PAST 24 HOURS.  THE DISTURBANCES ARE BELIEVED TO
BE ISOLATED.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE ACTIVE THE NEXT 24 HOURS, BECOMING UNSETTLED TO
ACTIVE THE NEXT TWO DAYS.  PERIODS OF ELEVATED ACTIVITY ARE
EXPECTED TO BE ISOLATED AND RELATIVELY SHORT IN DURATION.
