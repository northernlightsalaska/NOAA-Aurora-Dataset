JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 360 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 26 DEC 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 25/2100Z
TO 26/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH. REGION 6420 (S25E09) WAS
RESPONSIBLE FOR AN X1/2B TENFLARE AT 1402Z. THIS WAS AN
IMPULSIVE FLARE WITH NO TYPE II OR IV RADIO SWEEPS DETECTED.
EALIER IN THE PERIOD THIS REGION PRODUCED TWO M-CLASS FLARES,
AN M1/1N AT 0226Z AND AN M1/1F AT 1128Z. NEW REGION 6427
(S11E74) ROTATED INTO VIEW AND WAS NUMBERED TODAY.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE. REGION 6420 REMAINS THE MOST LIKELY SOURCE OF THIS
ACTIVITY.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 25/2100Z TO 26/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET LEVELS FOR
THE PAST 24 HOURS. SATELLITE LEVEL PROTONS RETURNED TO
BACKGROUND LEVELS AFTER THE EFFECTS OF THE 24 DECEMBER X1
FLARE.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE QUIET TO UNSETTLED. SOME EFFECTS OF THE RECENT
X-CLASS FLARING ARE STILL POSSIBLE THROUGH THE SECOND DAY OF
THE PERIOD.
