IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 19/2100Z
TO 20/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW.  THE LARGEST EVENT OF THE
DAY WAS A C7/1F AT 0627Z FROM REGION 6281 (N05E66). OTHER LOW
LEVEL C-CLASS FLARES WERE OBSERVED FROM REGIONS 6263 (N13W55)
AND 6266 (S26W43).  REGION 6272 (S14W06) HAS SHOWN CONSIDERABLE
DECAY TODAY BUT REMAINS THE LARGEST REGION ON THE DISK. REGION
6280 (N13E70) NOW DISPLAYS A DAO TYPE SPOT GROUP.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE.  SEVERAL REGIONS, INCLUDING 6272, 6280, AND
6279 (S09E19) POSSESS THE ABILITY TO PRODUCE AN ISOLATED
M-CLASS FLARE.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 19/2100Z TO 20/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN MOSTLY QUIET TO UNSETTLED
THE PAST 24 HOURS.  A COUPLE OF ISOLATED PERIODS OF ACTIVE
CONDITIONS WERE OBSERVED AT MIDDLE LATITUDES AND ONE AT
HIGH LATITUDES.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE MOSTLY UNSETTLED.  THE ACTIVITY ANTICIPATED
FROM THE M5 OF SEPT 17 FOR TODAY HAS SO FAR FAILED TO
MATERIALIZE.  ACTIVITY IS STILL POSSIBLE FROM THIS
OR FROM THE DISAPPEARING FILAMENTS REPORTED RECENTLY,
INCLUDING THE 20 DEGREE FILAMENT FROM S26W02 WHICH
DISAPPEARED LATE ON 17 SEPT.
