IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 07/2100Z
TO 08/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE.  AN M1/2N FLARE AT
08/0416Z WAS THE ONLY M CLASS FLARE OBSERVED DURING THE PAST
DAY.  THIS FLARE OCCURRED IN REGION 6007 (N23E16), A SMALL BUT
RELATIVELY ACTIVE SUNSPOT GROUP.  REGION 6001 (N22W40) IS THE
LARGEST REGION ON THE DISK BUT HAS ONLY PRODUCED A FEW C CLASS
FLARES SINCE YESTERDAY.  THIS REGION APPEARS TO BE MAGNETICALLY
MORE SIMPLE THAN 6007.  THE REMAINING DISK REGIONS WERE QUIET.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE.  A LOW LEVEL M CLASS FLARE IS POSSIBLE FROM
REGIONS 6007 AND 6012 (N30E42).
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 07/2100Z TO 08/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS MOSTLY QUIET.  A PROTON ENHANCEMENT
AT GREATER THAN 10 MEV EXCEEDED THE 10 PFU EVENT THRESHOLD
AT 07/2240Z AND REMAINS IN PROGRESS.  THE PRELIMINARY PEAK
IS 18 PFU AT 08/1330Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE QUIET TO UNSETTLED.  THE PROTON EVENT IS EXPECTED
TO END WITHIN THE NEXT 24 HOURS.
