JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 259 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 16 SEP 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 15/2100Z
TO 16/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. ONLY OCCASIONAL SUB-
FLARING OFFSET BY ISOLATED LOW LEVEL C-CLASS EVENTS WERE
OBSERVED. NO MAJOR GROWTH IN REGION SPOT AREAS WAS NOTED.
A C2/1F FLARE AT 16/1425Z FROM REGION 6257 (S10W22) WAS
THE HIGHLIGHT OF THE PERIOD. NO NEW REGIONS WERE ASSIGNED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW. THE THREAT OF MODERATE LEVELS SHOULD INCREASE NEAR THE
END OF THE PERIOD WITH THE RETURN OF OLD REGION 6233 (N13
L-034) WHICH PRODUCED NUMEROUS M-CLASS EVENTS DURING ITS LAST
ROTATION.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 15/2100Z TO 16/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO ACTIVE LEVELS
FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS. MINOR STORMING WAS NOTED AT HIGH
LATITUDE STATIONS AND MAY BE IN RESPONSE TO THE M-CLASS
ACTIVITY OBSERVED ON THE 14TH.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE GENERALLY ACTIVE THROUGHOUT THE PERIOD.
