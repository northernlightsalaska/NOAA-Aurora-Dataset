JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 216 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 04 AUG 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 03/2100Z
TO 04/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW.  THE LARGEST EVENT OF THE
DAY WAS A C4.9 AT 04/0640Z FROM REGION 6172 (S25W81).  REGIONS
6172 AND 6186 (N17E23) WERE THE MOST ACTIVE REGIONS WITH SEVERAL
C-CLASS FLARES AND SUBFLARES.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE.  REGIONS 6172 AND 6186 HAVE POTENTIAL
FOR PRODUCING AN M-CLASS FLARE.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 03/2100Z TO 04/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN MOSTLY QUIET TO UNSETTLED
WITH A BRIEF PERIOD OF ACTIVE LEVELS FROM 06 TO 09Z.  THE
SATELLITE LEVEL PROTON EVENT AT GREATER THAN 10 MEV ENDED
AT 03/1145Z WITH A MAXIMUM OF 230 PFU AT 01/2015Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE MOSTLY UNSETTLED.  LEVELS MAY INCREASE SLIGHTLY
ON THE THIRD DAY DUE TO A DISAPPEARING FILAMENT THAT OCCURRED
ON 03 AUGUST.
