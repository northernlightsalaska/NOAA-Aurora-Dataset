IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 12/2100Z
TO 13/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. ONLY C-CLASS X-RAY FLARES
WERE OBSERVED DURING THIS PERIOD. REGION 5969 (N32E28) CONTINUES
TO GROW EXHIBITING SOME COMPLEXITY AS A D-TYPE SPOT GROUP. TWO
NEW REGIONS NUMBERED TODAY: 5973 (N04E76) AND 5974 (S35E79).
5974 PRODUCED A C5/1N FLARE AT 2002Z. ACTIVE SURGING WAS ALSO
OBSERVED AT SE20 AND AN ACTIVE PROMINENCE CENTERED AT SE24.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW. AN ISOLATED M-CLASS FLARE IS POSSIBLE FROM REGION 5969.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 12/2100Z TO 13/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT UNSETTLED TO MINOR STORM
LEVELS. THIS DISTURBANCE BEGAN ON 12 MARCH AT 1501Z BUT LOOKS
TO BE WEAKENING. THE SOURCE OF THIS STORM IS STILL UNKNOWN.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE THE NEXT THREE DAYS.
