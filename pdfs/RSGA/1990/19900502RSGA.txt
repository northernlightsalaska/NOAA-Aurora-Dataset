FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 122
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 02 MAY 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 01/2100Z
TO 02/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW.  A FEW OPTICALLY
UNCORRELATED C CLASS FLARES HAVE OCCURRED SINCE YESTERDAY.
NEW ACTIVE REGION 6049 (N16E80) IS ROTATING AROUND THE EAST
LIMB AND MAY BE THE SOURCE OF THESE FLARES.  THIS REGION SHOULD
BE IN COMPLETE VIEW BY TOMORROW.  THE OTHER DISK REGIONS REMAIN
SMALL AND RELATIVELY STABLE.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW.  C CLASS ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED IN NEW REGION 6049.  THE
CHANCE OF MODERATE ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO INCREASE OVER THE
NEXT FEW DAYS.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 01/2100Z TO 02/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS QUIET TO UNSETTLED FOR MOST OF
THE DAY.  ISOLATED PERIODS OF ACTIVE TO STORM CONDITIONS
OCCURRED FROM 02/1030UT TO 02/1330UT.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE QUIET TO UNSETTLED.  ISOLATED PERIODS OF ACTIVE
CONDITIONS ARE POSSIBLE.
