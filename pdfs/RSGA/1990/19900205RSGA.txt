JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 05 FEB 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 04/2100Z
TO 05/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. A C9/0F FROM REGION 5904
(N12W89) AT 1708Z WAS THE HIGHLIGHT OF THE PERIOD. SMALLER
FLARES ALSO OCCURRED IN THIS REGION AS IT BEGAN WEST LIMB
PASSAGE. THE STATE OF THE REST OF THE DISK WAS MOSTLY STABLE.
JUST SEVEN SPOTTED REGIONS ARE NOW VISIBLE AND TODAY'S 10.7
CM. FLUX VALUE IS THE LOWEST SINCE 24 SEPT. 1989.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW. C-CLASS ACTIVITY MAY ORIGINATE IN 5904 AND 5920 (N15W38)
BUT M-CLASS FLARES APPEAR UNLIKELY.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 04/2100Z TO 05/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT UNSETTLED TO MINOR STORM
LEVELS. A FAVORABLY POSITIONED CORONAL HOLE IS THE LIKELY
SOURCE OF THIS ACTIVITY.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE MOSTLY ACTIVE THE NEXT 24 HOURS AS THE CURRENT
DISTURBANCE PERSISTS. UNSETTLED CONDITIONS SHOULD CLOSE OUT
THE INTERVAL.
