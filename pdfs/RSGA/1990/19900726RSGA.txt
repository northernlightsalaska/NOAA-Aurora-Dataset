JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 207 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 26 JUL 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 25/2100Z
TO 26/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 6174
(S12E38), A SIMPLE UNIPOLAR SPOT AREA, PRODUCED AN M2/2N
TENFLARE WITH ASSOCIATED MINOR TYPE IV SWEEP AT 26/2335Z.
THIS WAS A LONG DURATION EVENT AND IS THE LIKELY SOURCE
OF THE PROTON EVENT NOTED BELOW. REGION 6162 (N10W27)
PRODUCED THE SECOND M-CLASS EVENT OF THE PERIOD: AN M3/1B
FLARE AT 26/1704Z. THE FLARE ERUPTED IN THE TRAILING
PORTION OF THIS REGION WHERE A WEAK DELTA MAGNETIC
CONFIGURATION EXISTS. THE REMAINDER OF THE PERIOD WAS
CHARACTERIZED BY LOW-LEVEL C-CLASS EVENTS AND SUBFLARING.
NEW REGION 6179 (N16E68) WAS ASSIGNED TODAY.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE. REGIONS 6161 (S27W50), 6162, AND 6174 POSE THE MOST
NOTABLE THREAT FOR M-CLASS FLARING.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 25/2100Z TO 26/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT MOSTLY QUIET LEVELS
FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS. ACTIVE CONDITIONS WERE NOTED NEAR
THE END OF THE PERIOD WITH THE OCCURRENCE OF A SUDDEN
IMPULSE OF 19 NANOTESLAS AT 26/2056Z. SATELLITE LEVEL PROTONS
BECAME ENHANCED AT 26/0030Z IN RESPONSE TO THE M2/2N
FLARE FROM REGION 6174 NOTED ABOVE. THE GREATER THAN 10 MEV
PROTONS REACHED EVENT LEVEL AT 26/1720Z WITH A TENTATIVE
PEAK OF 13 PFU NOTED AT 26/1735Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE QUIET TO UNSETTLED. THE PROTON EVENT NOTED IN
PARAGRAPH IIA. ABOVE SHOULD CONTINUE IN PROGRESS FOR THE
NEXT 24 HOURS SLOWLY RETURNING TO BACKGROUND LEVELS
THEREAFTER.
