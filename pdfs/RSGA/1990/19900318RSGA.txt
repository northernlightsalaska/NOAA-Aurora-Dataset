IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 17/2100Z
TO 18/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 5974 (S33E19)
PRODUCED THE PERIOD'S SOLE M-FLARE: AN M3/1B AT 18/1128Z WHICH
WAS ASSOCIATED WITH A TYPE II RADIO SWEEP (EST. SHOCK VELOCITY
WAS 800 KM/SEC) AND MINOR DISCRETE RADIO BURSTS. SLIGHT GROWTH
OCCURRED IN THIS REGION DURING THE DAY. REGIONS 5969 (N33W37)
AND 5983 (S32E67) PRODUCED OCCASIONAL LOW-LEVEL C-CLASS FLARES.
NEW REGIONS 5984 (S12E65), 5985 (S23W55), AND 5986 (S33E02) WERE
NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. C-CLASS FLARES ARE EXPECTED FROM REGIONS 5969,
5974, AND 5983. REGION 5974 IS CAPABLE OF ISOLATED M-CLASS
FLARE PRODUCTION AS WELL.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 17/2100Z TO 18/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO MINOR STORM LEVELS
FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS. THE STORMING, WHICH OCCURRED FROM
18/1500-2100Z, HAS NO DISCERNABLE SOURCE (AT THIS JUNCTURE).
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE LEVELS DURING THE FIRST
TWO DAYS INCREASING TO ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM LEVELS ON THE
FINAL DAY. THE STORMING IS EXPECTED IN RESPONSE TO CORONAL
HOLE EFFECTS. THE 27-DAY RECURRENT PATTERN ALSO SUPPORTS THIS
FORECAST.
