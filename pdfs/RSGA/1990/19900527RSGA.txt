JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 27 MAY 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 26/2100Z
TO 27/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE DUE TO AN OPTICALLY
UNCORRELATED M1 FLARE AT 27/0333Z.  REGION 6077 (N21E40)
CONTINUED TO DEVELOP SLOWLY OVER THE PAST 24 HOURS AND PRODUCED
SEVERAL SUBFLARES.  THE REMAINING DISK REGIONS WERE RELATIVELY
QUIET.  REGIONS 6080 (N15E69) AND 6081 (S18E07) WERE NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE.  REGION 6077 IS CAPABLE OF ISOLATED M CLASS
ACTIVITY.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 26/2100Z TO 27/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS AT STORM LEVELS FOR SEVERAL HOURS
FOLLOWING AN SSC AT 26/2025Z.  THIS DISTURBANCE IS PROBABLY THE
RESULT OF THE X9 FLARE ON 24 MAY.  THE PROTON EVENT CONTINUES IN
PROGRESS.  SO FAR, THE PEAK FLUX AT GREATER THAN 10 MEV, 50 MEV,
AND 100 MEV WAS 180 PFU AT 25/0115Z, 41 PFU AT 24/2200Z, AND
19 PFU AT 24/2155Z, RESPECTIVELY.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT ACTIVE TO STORM LEVELS FOR THE FIRST 24
HOURS OF THE FORECAST PERIOD AND AT UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE LEVELS
THEREAFTER.  THE PROTON EVENT IS EXPECTED TO END WITHIN THE
NEXT 24 HOURS.
