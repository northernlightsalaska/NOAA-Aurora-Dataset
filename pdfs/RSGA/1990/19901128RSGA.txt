JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 332 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 28 NOV 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 27/2100Z
TO 28/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. REGION 6377 (S05W56) WAS
THE MOST NOTABLE SOURCE FOR ACTIVITY TODAY WITH OCCASIONAL
SUBFLARING AND A C6/SF EVENT OBSERVED AT 28/0658Z. LITTLE
CHANGE WAS NOTED IN THE SPOT STRUCTURE OF THIS GROUP WHICH
MAINTAINS A WELL DEFINED ARCH FILAMENT SYSTEM. OCCASIONAL
UNCORRELATED C-CLASS EVENTS IMBEDDED WITHIN AN ENHANCED X-RAY
BACKGROUND CHARACTERIZED THE MAJORITY OF THE PERIOD. BRIGHT
SURGING WAS NOTED ON THE NORTHWEST LIMB ATTRIBUTED TO OLD
REGION 6368 (N17, L-025) AND A SMALL ERUPTIVE PROMINENCE WAS
VIEWED ON THE SOUTHWEST LIMB AT 28/1135Z. NEW REGIONS 6387
(S23E73), 6388 (S09E32), AND 6389 (N02E14) WERE ASSIGNED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW. REGION 6377 PROVIDES A SLIGHT CHANCE FOR AN ISOLATED
M-CLASS FLARE.
