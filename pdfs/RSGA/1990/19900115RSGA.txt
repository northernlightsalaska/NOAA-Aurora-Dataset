IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 14/2100Z
TO 15/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. A C7/0F AT 0656Z FROM
REGION 5882 (N20E26) WAS THE LARGEST FLARE OF THE PERIOD. 5882
HAS GROWN IN BOTH WHITE LIGHT AND H-ALPHA TODAY AS IT YIELDED
OCCASIONAL C-CLASS SUBFLARES. THREE NEW REGIONS, 5887 (N26E62),
5888 (S09E29), AND 5889 (S10E76), WERE ASSIGNED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE. REGION 5882 REMAINS AN EXCELLENT PROSPECT FOR AN
ISOLATED M-CLASS FLARE. OLD REGION 5852 (S26, L-030) IS DUE
AT EAST LIMB EARLY ON 17 JANUARY. THAT REGION WAS POTENT AT
WEST LIMB PASSAGE AND DID PRODUCE X-CLASS ACTIVITY LAST
ROTATION.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 14/2100Z TO 15/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO UNSETTLED
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO REMAIN AT QUIET TO UNSETTLED LEVELS THE NEXT TWO
DAYS. A RECURRENT UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE CONDITION SHOULD PREVAIL
18 JANUARY.
