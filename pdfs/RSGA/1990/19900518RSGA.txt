JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 18 MAY 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 17/2100Z
TO 18/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. ONLY ONE LOW LEVEL
C-CLASS FLARE OCCURRED TODAY. IT WAS A C2/SF FLARE AT 0923Z
IN REGION 6064 (S16E01). THIS SPOT GROUP ALONG WITH REGION
6063 (N33W09) CONTINUE TO BE THE MOST COMPLEX REGIONS ON THE
SOLAR DISK. BOTH REGIONS DISPLAY BETA-GAMMA-DELTA MAGNETIC
CONFIGURATIONS ALONG WITH INCREASING SPOT AREA AND NUMBER.
NEW REGION 6071 (S11E76) WAS NUMBERED. REMAINING SPOT GROUPS
STABLE OR UNIMPRESSIVE.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE TO HIGH. REGIONS 6063 AND 6064 HAVE THE POTENTIAL
TO PRODUCE M-CLASS, ISOLATED X-CLASS AND PROTON FLARE ACTIVITY.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 17/2100Z TO 18/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO MAJOR STORM
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS. A SUDDEN COMMENCEMENT OF 40
GAMMMAS BEGAN AT 0736Z CAUSING MOSTLY UNSETTLED TO MINOR
STORM CONDITIONS. AN ISOLATED PERIOD OF MAJOR STORM LEVELS
OCCURRED AT MID LATITUDES FROM 15-18Z. SOURCE OF THIS
DISTURBANCE MAY HAVE BEEN THE X1/3B FLARE ON MAY 15 FROM
REGION 6063.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE.
