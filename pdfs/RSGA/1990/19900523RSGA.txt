FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 143
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 23 MAY 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 22/2100Z
TO 23/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH.  REGION 6063 (N35W70)
PRODUCED AN M8/1B FLARE AT 0402Z WHICH WAS ASSOCIATED WITH
MODERATE TO STRONG RADIO BURSTS AND A STRONG TYPE IV SWEEP.
THE REGION HAS BEEN UNUSUALLY QUIET SINCE.  REGION 6064
(S16W65) PRODUCED AN M2/1B FLARE AT 0816Z.  IT HAS ALSO
PRODUCED SEVERAL SUBFLARES THROUGHOUT THE PERIOD.  AN
UNASSOCIATED M2 FLARE OCCURRED AT 0904Z.  REGIONS 6070
(S19E01), 6071 (S12E09), AND 6072 (N26E20) APPEAR TO
BE GROWING STEADILY.  A 25 DEGREE DISAPPEARING FILAMENT WAS
NOTED AT S26W55.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE TO HIGH EARLY IN THE PERIOD.  REGIONS 6063 AND 6064
ARE EXPECTED TO ROTATE OFF THE DISK ON 26 MAY, LOWERING SOLAR
ACTIVITY AND BACKGROUND LEVELS SIGNIFICANTLY AT THAT TIME.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 22/2100Z TO 23/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE LEVELS
FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS.  THE PROTON EVENT THAT BEGAN AT 2355Z
21 MAY ENDED AT 1640Z ON 23 MAY.  THE PEAK FLUX REACHED
410 PFU AT GREATER THAN 10MEV AT 0750Z 22 MAY.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE GENERALLY ACTIVE FOR THE NEXT THREE DAYS WITH
PERIODS OF MINOR STORMING AT HIGH LATITUDES ON THE SECOND DAY.
EFFECTS FROM THE X5 FLARE THAT OCCURRED ON 21 MAY ARE EXPECTED
TO ARRIVE LATE ON THE FIRST DAY AND LAST THROUGH THE
SECOND DAY.  RECURRENT EFFECTS ARE EXPECTED ON THE THIRD
DAY.
