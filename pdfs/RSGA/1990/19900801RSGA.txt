JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 213 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 01 AUG 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 31/2100Z
TO 01/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE.  REGION 6172 (S27W42)
WHICH HAS SHOWN SUBSTANTIAL GROWTH OVERNIGHT, PRODUCED AN M1/1F
AT 2005Z.  ADDITIONAL SUBFLARING WAS ADDED BY REGIONS 6185
(N25E60) AND 6184 (S08E03).
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW.  REGION 6172 HAS A CHANCE OF PRODUCING ANOTHER ISOLATED
M-CLASS FLARE AND C-CLASS FLARING IS PROBABLE FROM REGIONS 6185
AND 6186 (N16E62).
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 31/2100Z TO 01/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN QUIET TO ACTIVE THE PAST 24
HOURS.  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD BEGAN THE PERIOD AT QUIET
LEVELS, THEN AT 0743Z A SUDDEN IMPULSE OF 39 GAMMA WAS
OBSERVED AT BOULDER FOLLOWED BY SEVERAL PERIODS OF ACTIVE
CONDITIONS.  YESTERDAY'S PROTON ENHANCEMENT PASSED EVENT
LEVELS AT 0005Z, HAS REACHED A TENTATIVE MAXIMUM OF 220 PFU
AT GREATER THAN 10 MEV AT 1955Z AND CONTINUES IN PROGRESS.
A POLAR CAP ABSORPTION EVENT BEGAN AT 1330Z AND CONTINUES
IN PROGRESS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE.  ADDITIONAL PERIODS OF
ACTIVE CONDITIONS ARE LIKELY THROUGH 03 AUGUST DUE TO THE
CONTINUED EFFECTS OF THE M4/2B LDE OF 30 JULY AND A CORONAL
HOLE.
