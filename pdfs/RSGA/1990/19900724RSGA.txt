JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 205 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 24 JUL 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 23/2100Z
TO 24/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. REGION 6162 (N11E01)
REMAINED IN A GRADUAL GROWTH PHASE AND PRODUCED SEVERAL
SUBFLARES. REGION 6171 (N11E51) ALSO PRODUCED SEVERAL FLARES
INCLUDING THE LARGEST EVENT OF THE DAY: A C6/1B AT 23/2201Z.
THIS REGION DISPLAYED GROWTH DURING MOST OF THE PERIOD. AN
ERUPTIVE PROMINENCE WAS OBSERVED ON THE WEST LIMB NEAR S14 AT
24/0048Z. REGION 6163 (N23W26) PRODUCED A SUBFAINT FLARE AT
24/1532Z WHICH WAS ASSOCIATED WITH A 190 SFU TENFLARE. NO X-RAY
BURST ACCOMPANIED THE FLARE. NEW REGIONS 6175 (S19E12), 6176
(S18W41), AND 6177 (S20E70) WERE NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE. REGIONS 6162 AND 6171 DISPLAY POTENTIAL FOR ISOLATED
M-CLASS FLARE PRODUCTION.

EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED.
