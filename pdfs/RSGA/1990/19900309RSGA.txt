JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 09 MAR 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 08/2100Z
TO 09/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. A FEW LOW LEVEL C-FLARES
WERE OBSERVED DURING THE PERIOD FROM REGIONS 5964 (N29W20) AND
5965 (S11W22). THE OVERALL TREND OF THE REGIONS CURRENTLY ON THE
DISK IS TOWARD DECLINE. NEW REGION 5969 (N32E75) ROTATED INTO
VIEW AS A QUIET, STABLE H-TYPE GROUP.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW.  REGIONS 5964 AND 5965 ARE  THE MOST LIKELY SOURCES FOR
OCCASIONAL SMALL FLARES. THERE IS A SLIGHT CHANCE FOR MODERATE
LEVELS.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 08/2100Z TO 09/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS MOSTLY QUIET TO UNSETTLED. A BRIEF
ACTIVE PERIOD WAS OBSERVED FROM 0900-1200Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE GENERALLY UNSETTLED.
