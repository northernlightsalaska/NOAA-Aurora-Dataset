JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 293 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 20 OCT 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 19/2100Z
TO 20/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH. REGION 6311 (N15W90) PRO-
DUCED AN M8/1N FLARE AT 1806Z. THE EVENT WAS ACCOMPANIED BY
STRONG RADIO BURSTS AT HIGH FREQUENCIES, BUT VERY WEAK BURSTS
AT LOW FREQUENCIES. POST FLARE LOOPS BECAME VISIBLE AT 1841Z
AND CONTINUED IN PROGRESS FOR A COUPLE HOURS AFTER THE EVENT.
THE REGION ALSO PRODUCED TODAY'S 2ND LARGEST EVENT: A C8/SF
AT 0510Z. THE REST OF THE ACTIVITY CONSISTED OF A FEW SMALL
C-CLASS FLARES. A 30 DEGREE FILAMENT LOCATED BETWEEN N48E01
AND N32W23 DISAPPEARED SOMETIME BETWEEN 19/1513Z AND 20/0058Z.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE FOR THE NEXT 24 HOURS: THE MAIN THREAT FOR M-
FLARES WILL BE REGION 6311 WHICH WILL BE ONLY ONE DAY BEYOND
THE LIMB. CONDITIONS SHOULD BE GENERALLY LOW THEREAFTER, BUT
WITH A SLIGHT CHANCE FOR AN ISOLATED M-FLARE FROM REGION 6322.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 19/2100Z TO 20/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS QUIET TO UNSETTLED UNTIL ABOUT
1200Z WHEN THE FIELD ATTAINED ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM LEVELS.
THE DISTURBANCE APPEARED TO BE GRADUALLY SUBSIDING NEAR
THE END OF THE DAY.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO SLIGHTLY ACTIVE TOMORROW. LEVELS
ARE EXPECTED TO BE GENERALLY UNSETTLED ON THE SECOND AND THIRD
DAYS.
