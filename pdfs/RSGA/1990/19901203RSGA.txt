JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 337 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 03 DEC 1990
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 02/2100Z
TO 03/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 6397 (N10E57)
PRODUCED AN M1/SN FLARE AT 03/1924Z. THIS REGION WAS THE MOST
ACTIVE DURING THE PERIOD, BUT THERE WERE NO SIGNS OF SIGNIFICANT
GROWTH OBSERVED. REGION 6379 (S06W64) GREW SLIGHTLY AND PRODUCED
ISOLATED SUBFLARES. REGION 6395 (N16E51) REMAINED THE LARGEST OF
THE VISIBLE REGIONS, BUT MANAGED TO PRODUCE ONLY A SINGLE
C-CLASS SUBFLARE DURING THE PERIOD. NEW REGIONS 6400 (S25W47),
6401 (S34E02), AND 6402 (S11E28), ALL SIMPLE BIPOLES, WERE
ASSIGNED TODAY.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. REGION 6397 MAY PRODUCE AN ISOLATED M-CLASS
FLARE. OTHERWISE, MOSTLY C-CLASS SUBFLARES ARE EXPECTED.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 02/2100Z TO 03/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET LEVELS FOR THE PAST
24 HOURS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE QUIET TO UNSETTLED.
