FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 276
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 03 OCT 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 02/2100Z
TO 03/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. THE LARGEST EVENT WAS A
C5/1N FLARE FROM REGION 5721 (N27E63). REGIONS 5712 (S17W10)
AND 5721 ARE THE MOST COMPLEX SPOT GROUPS ON THE SUN. BOTH
REGIONS DISPLAY A BETA-GAMMA MAGNETIC STRUCTURE. ACTIVE
SURGING WAS OBSERVED ON THE EAST LIMB FROM NE05-35. TWO NEW
REGIONS NUMBERED TODAY: 5725 (N35E77) AND 5726 (S13E71).
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. REGIONS 5712 AND 5721 MAY PRODUCE AN ISOLATED
M-CLASS FLARE.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 02/2100Z TO 03/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS MOSTLY AT QUIET TO UNSETTLED LEVELS
FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS. ONE PERIOD OF MINOR STORM CONDITONS
AT MID LATITUDES AND ACTIVE CONDITIONS AT HIGH LATITUDES WAS
OBSERVED AT 15-18Z. THE PROTON EVENT REMAINS IN PROGRESS.
THE GREATER THAN 10MEV FLUX WAS 12 P.F.U. AT 03/2100Z. THE
POLAR CAP ABSORPTION EVENT THAT BEGAN NEAR 29/1235Z SEP 89
ENDED AT 03/1815Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED FOR THE NEXT THREE DAYS. ACTIVE
CONDITIONS ARE EXPECTED IN THE HIGH LATITUDES ON OCT 5TH.
THE PROTON EVENT SHOULD END EARLY ON OCT 4TH.
