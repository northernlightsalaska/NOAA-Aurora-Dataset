JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 31 OCT 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 30/2100Z
TO 31/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE.  SMALL M CLASS XRAY
EVENTS OCCURRED AT 0450UT, 1613UT, AND 2037UT.  THESE EVENTS
APPEAR TO BE CORRELATED WITH ACTIVITY IN REGION 5769 (N24E43).
THIS REGION IS GROWING AND BRIGHT POINTS HAVE BEEN OBSERVED IN
AREAS OF NEW FLUX.  THE REGION IS BECOMING MAGNETICALLY COMPLEX.
LOOPS WERE OBSERVED ON THE EAST LIMB NEAR N18 AND MAY HERALD A
NEW REGION.  REGION 5775 (S13W09) WAS NUMBERED TODAY AND IS
GROWING.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE.  THERE IS A SLIGHT CHANCE OF ENERGETIC ACTIVITY
FROM REGION 5769.  EAST LIMB SHOULD BE MONITORED FOR ADDITIONAL
SIGNS OF ANY NEW REGION.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 30/2100Z TO 31/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS GENERALLY QUIET TO UNSETTLED.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO TURN FROM UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE CONDITIONS EARLY IN
PERIOD AS A RESULT OF THE EFFECTS OF AN EQUATORIAL CORONAL HOLE
NOW NEAR W45 AND A DISAPPEARING FILAMENT WHICH WAS LOCATED NEAR
N55 W08 ON 28 OCT.
