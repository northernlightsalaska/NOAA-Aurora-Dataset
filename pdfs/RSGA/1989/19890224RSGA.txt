JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 24 FEB 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 23/2100Z
TO 24/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. REGION 5368 (S12W64)
PRODUCED A NUMBER OF C-CLASS FLARES BUT WAS UNABLE TO SPAWN AN
M-CLASS EVENT. IT REMAINS THE MOST POTENT REGION VISIBLE AND
MAY BE EXPERIENCING RENEWED GROWTH AT THIS TIME. THE EASTERN
HEMISPHERE HAS BECOME MORE INTERESTING OF LATE AS THREE NEW
REGIONS WERE ASSIGNED THERE AND MORE MAY STILL BE COMING.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. REGION 5368 IS LIKELY TO DOMINATE FLARE
ACTIVITY FOR ANOTHER DAY, AND MAY YIELD AN ISOLATED M-CLASS
EVENT.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 23/2100Z TO 24/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS UNSETTLED.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO CONTINUE AT UNSETTLED LEVELS THE NEXT THREE DAYS.
