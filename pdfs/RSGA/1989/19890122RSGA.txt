IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 21/2100Z
TO 22/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 5330 (S18E71)
MAY BE MAGNETICALLY COMPLICATED. IT PRODUCED A SMALL M-CLASS
SUBFLARE LATE IN THE PERIOD. REGION 5329 (N19E49) WAS A STACKED
COMPLEX OF AT LEAST THREE GROUPS APPARENTLY ALONG A COMMON
NEUTRAL LINE.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE. REGION 5330 PROVIDES A VERY SLIGHT CHANCE OF HIGH
ACTIVITY AND A PROTON PRODUCING FLARE.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 21/2100Z TO 22/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS ACTIVE. BREIF STORM PERIODS OCCURRED
AT BOTH HIGH AND MIDDLE LATITUDES UNTIL LATE IN THE PERIOD, WHEN
UNSETTLED TO QUIET CONDITIONS THEN PREVAILED.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE ACTIVE. A DISTURBANCE IS EXPECTED IN RESPONSE
TO A SOUTHERN CORONAL HOLE. BRIEF STORM CONDITIONS ARE
PROBABLE AT HIGHER LATITUDES.
