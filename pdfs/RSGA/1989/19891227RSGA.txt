IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 26/2100Z
TO 27/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE.  REGION 5852 (S26W07)

LARGE SUNSPOT GROUP BUT HAS BEEN RELATIVELY VOID OF ACTIVITY
FOR THE PAST DAY.  NEW REGIONS 5861 (S23W28) AND 5862 (N17E78)
WERE NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE TO HIGH.  A MAJOR FLARE IS EXPECTED IN REGION 5852.
REGIONS 5853 (S10E18) AND 5854 MAY PRODUCE M-CLASS ACTIVITY.

THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD RANGED FROM QUIET TO STORM LEVELS.  THE
SOURCE OF THIS ACTIVITY IS BELIEVED TO BE A FAVORABLY LOCATED
CORONAL HOLE.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT MINOR STORM TO ACTIVE LEVELS FOR THE NEXT
THREE DAYS.  SEVERAL SOURCES MAY CONTRIBUTE TO THE EXPECTED
LEVELS OF ACTIVITY INCLUDING CORONAL HOLE, DISAPPEARING
FILAMENT, AND LONG DURATION X-RAYS.
