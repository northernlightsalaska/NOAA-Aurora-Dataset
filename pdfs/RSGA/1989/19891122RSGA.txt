JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 22 NOV 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 21/2100Z
TO 22/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 5793 (S25W73)
PRODUCED TWO M-CLASS EVENTS. THEY WERE AN M2/1B TENFLARE
AT 0840Z AND AN M3/0N AT 1338Z. BOTH FLARES WERE QUITE IMPULSIVE
AND LACKED TYPE II AND/OR TYPE IV SWEEP. REGION 5793 SEEMS TO BE
SIMPLIFYING AS IT NEARS WEST LIMB. ELSEWHERE REGION 5796
(N11W04) HAS SLOWED IN ITS GROWTH AND THE PLAGE CORRIDOR HAS
WIDENED BETWEEN THE LEADER AND TRAILER SPOTS. A PROMINENCE
ERUPTED FROM S31E90 AT 0239Z.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE TO HIGH. MAJOR FLARES MAY COME FROM EITHER REGION
5793 OR REGION 5796. ALSO REGION 5800 (N25E42) MAY CONTRIBUTE
AN ISOLATED M-CLASS EVENT.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 21/2100Z TO 22/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN MOSTLY QUIET.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE THE NEXT THREE DAYS.
