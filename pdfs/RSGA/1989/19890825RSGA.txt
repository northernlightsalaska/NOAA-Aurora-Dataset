JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 25 AUG 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 24/2100Z
TO 25/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW.  ALL DISK REGIONS ARE
RELATIVELY SMALL AND SIMPLE.  THE DEPARTURE OF REGION 5643, NOW
BEYOND THE WEST LIMB, AND THE DECAY OF SEVERAL SMALL REGIONS
WAS MOST LIKELY RESPONSIBLE FOR THE LARGE DROP IN 10.7 CM
FLUX (21 SFU).  NEW REGION 5664 (S28E01) WAS NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW.  AN ISOLATED M-FLARE IS POSSIBLE IN REGION 5662 (S17E57).
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 24/2100Z TO 25/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS QUIET.  THE PROTON FLUX AT GREATER
THAN 10 MEV HAS BEEN BELOW THE 10 PFU EVENT THRESHOLD SINCE
25/1950Z.  THIS MAY MARK THE END OF THE PROTON EVENT WHICH
BEGAN ON 12 AUGUST.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE QUIET TO UNSETTLED.
