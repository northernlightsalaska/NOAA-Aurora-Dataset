FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 295
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 22 OCT 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 21/2100Z
TO 22/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH. REGION 5747 (S26W36)
PRODUCED AN X2/2B FLARE AT 22/1805Z. THE EVENT WAS ACCOMPANIED
BY A 22000 SFU TENFLARE (ALONG WITH OTHER MAJOR DISCRETE BURSTS
ACROSS THE SPECTRUM) AND MAJOR TYPES II AND IV SWEEP BURSTS.
SUNSPOT OBSERVATIONS RECEIVED IN THE WAKE OF THE X-FLARE
INDICATED THE REGION DID NOT DECREASE IN SIZE OR COMPLEXITY.
THE REGION PRODUCED OCCASIONAL M-CLASS FLARES PRIOR TO THE X2.
REGION 5750 (N12W76), FORMERLY A PLAGE REGION, REEMERGED AS A
B-TYPE GROUP EARLY IN THE PERIOD AND PRODUCED OCCASIONAL
SUBFLARES. NEW REGION 5759 (S13E07) WAS NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE TO HIGH. REGION 5747 REMAINS CAPABLE OF M-CLASS AND
ISOLATED X-CLASS FLARE PRODUCTION. REGIONS 5750 AND 5754
(S20E45) ARE LIKELY TO PRODUCE C-CLASS FLARES.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 21/2100Z TO 22/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT MOSTLY MINOR STORM
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS. THE SATELLITE PROTON EVENT AT
GREATER THAN 10 MEV CONTINUED. HOWEVER, AN INCREASE IN THE
GREATER THAN 10 MEV FLUX OCCURRED NEAR 1810Z AS A RESULT OF
THE X2/2B FLARE. THE MAXIMUM FLUX SINCE THE INCREASE WAS
2800 PFU AT 22/2100Z. A SATELLITE PROTON EVENT AT GREATER
THAN 100 MEV BEGAN IN THE WAKE OF TODAY'S MAJOR FLARE. IT
BEGAN AT 22/1830Z AND REACHED A MAXIMUM OF 230 PFU AT
22/1855Z. THIS EVENT REMAINED IN PROGRESS AT THE END OF THE
SUMMARY PERIOD.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE MOSTLY ACTIVE UNTIL LATE TOMORROW. THERERAFTER,
THE FIELD IS EXPECTED TO BE AT MOSTLY MAJOR STORM LEVELS IN
RESPONSE TO TODAY'S X-FLARE. THE SATELLITE PROTON EVENTS AT
GREATER THAN 10 MEV AND GREATER THAN 100 MEV ARE EXPECTED TO
CONTINUE.
