FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 013
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 13 JAN 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 12/2100Z
TO 13/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH.  REGION 5312 (S30W08)
PRODUCED THREE MAJOR FLARES SINCE YESTERDAY- AN X2/2B AT
13/1021Z, AN M9 AT 13/1242Z, AND AN M6/1N AT 13/1734Z.  THIS
REGION REMAINS EXTREMELY LARGE AND COMPLEX.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
HIGH.  REGION 5312 APPEARS TO HAVE POTENTIAL FOR ADDITIONAL
MAJOR FLARES.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 12/2100Z TO 13/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT MOSTLY QUIET LEVELS
UNTIL 13/1330Z WHEN A SHOCK ARRIVAL WAS OBSERVED AT THE GOES
SATELLITE MAGNETOMETERS.  GROUND BASED OBSERVATORIES RECORDED
MOSTLY ACTIVE CONDITIONS THEREAFTER.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE ACTIVE TO UNSETTLED FOR THE NEXT 24 HOURS,
REACHING STORM CONDITIONS ON THE SECOND DAY OF THE FORECAST
AND DECLINING TO UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE BY THE THIRD DAY.  PROTONS
AT GREATER THAN 10 MEV HAVE NOT YET ARRIVED FROM TODAY'S
FLARES, AND THE PROSPECT OF PROTONS FROM THESE FLARES IS
BECOMING LESS LIKELY.
