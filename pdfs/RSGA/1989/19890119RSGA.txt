IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 18/2100Z
TO 19/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. REGION 5312 (S32W82) WAS
QUIET WHILE CONTINUING TO GRADUALLY DECAY, BUT REMAINS
MAGNETICALLY COMPLEX. REGION 5317 (N22W52) WAS RELATIVELY QUIET
AS WELL, DESPITE ITS HIGH DEGREE OF MAGNETIC COMPLEXITY. IT
PRODUCED A C6/SF FLARE AT 19/0946Z. THREE NEW REGIONS WERE
NUMBERED DURING THE PERIOD: 5326 (S19E57), 5327 (N30W73), AND
5328 (N21E05).
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE TO HIGH. REGIONS 5312 AND 5317 PROVIDE A GOOD CHANCE
FOR M-CLASS AND A FAIR CHANCE FOR MAJOR FLARE ACTIVITY.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 18/2100Z TO 19/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT MOSTLY QUIET LEVELS
FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT MOSTLY MINOR STORM LEVELS TOMORROW AS A
RESULT OF YESTERDAY'S M9/3B FLARE. THE REMAINDER OF THE PERIOD
IS EXPECTED TO BE ACTIVE.
