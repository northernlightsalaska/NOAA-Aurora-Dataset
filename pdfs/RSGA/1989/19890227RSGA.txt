FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 058
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 27 FEB 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 26/2100Z
TO 27/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 5379 (S20E19)
PRODUCED TODAY'S ONLY M-CLASS FLARE: AN M1/1N AT 0139Z. THE
REGION EXHIBITED SLIGHT GROWTH DURING THE PERIOD. ADDITIONAL
SOLAR ACTIVITY CONSISTED OF SMALL C-CLASS SUBFLARES FROM A
VARIETY OF REGIONS. SURGE ACTIVITY WAS OBSERVED EARLY IN THE
PERIOD ON THE WEST LIMB AT ABOUT S12, ASSOCIATED WITH THE
DEPARTURE OF REGION 5368. THE REMAINDER OF THE SOLAR DISK AND
LIMBS WERE QUIET.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW. THERE IS A SLIGHT CHANCE FOR ISOLATED M-FLARES FROM REGIONS
5373 AND 5379. ACTIVITY LEVELS AND SOLAR FLUX VALUES WILL LESSEN
AS 5373 EXITS THE DISK ON 01 MARCH.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 26/2100Z TO 27/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS PREDOMINANTLY QUIET. SOME BRIEF
UNSETTLED CONDITIONS OCCURRED IN THE LATTER PART OF THE
PERIOD.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE MOSTLY UNSETTLED OVER THE NEXT THREE DAYS.
