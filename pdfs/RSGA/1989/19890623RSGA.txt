FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 174
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 23 JUN 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 22/2100Z
TO 23/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW DURING THE PAST 24 HOURS.
REGION 5555 (N27E28) PRODUCED NUMEROUS C-CLASS SUBFLARES AND
MAINTAINED ITS WHITE LIGHT AND MAGNETIC COMPLEXITY. TWO NEW
REGIONS WERE NUMBERED IN THE NORTHEAST. AN ACTIVE PROMINENCE
WAS REPORTED ON THE SOUTHWEST LIMB AND SURGING ACCOMPANIED THE
WEST LIMB PASSAGE OF REGIONS 5533 (S17) AND 5534 (N17).
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. REGION 5555 POSSESSES THE POTENTIAL FOR
ISOLATED M-CLASS FLARE ACTIVITY.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 22/2100Z TO 23/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO UNSETTLED
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS. A SUDDEN IMPULSE WAS OBSERVED
AT MIDDLE LATITUDE MAGNETIC OBSERVATORIES ON 22 JUNE AT
2225UT, BUT GEOMAGNETIC STORM CONDITIONS DID NOT DEVELOP.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM LEVELS TOMORROW,
WITH DECREASING CONFIDENCE, IN RESPONSE TO SOLAR ACTIVITY ON
20 JUNE. INCREASED GEOMAGNETIC ACTIVITY TOWARD THE END OF THE
FORECAST PERIOD IS EXPECTED AS A RESULT OF A LARGE NEGATIVE
POLARITY NORTHERN CORONAL HOLE THAT WILL BE NEAR CENTER DISK
ON 24 JUNE.
