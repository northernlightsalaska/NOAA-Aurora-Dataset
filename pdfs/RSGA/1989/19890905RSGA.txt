FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 248
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 05 SEP 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 04/2100Z
TO 05/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 5669 (S17W10)
PRODUCED AN M1/SN AT 05/0501Z AND AN M3/SB AT 05/1342Z. THE
LATTER FLARE ALSO PRODUCED A 10CM BURST OF 280 FLUX UNITS.
REGION 5669 SHOWED SOME AREA DECAY BUT REMAINED A LARGE,
MAGNETICALLY COMPLEX REGION. REGIONS 5672 (N24E22) AND 5676
(N27E53) GREW DURING THE PERIOD AND BOTH PRODUCED A HIGH CLASS
C FLARE EACH. NEW REGION 5680 (N16E75) ROTATED ONTO THE DISK AS
A MODERATE SIZE CLASS F GROUP BUT DID NOT PRODUCE ANY FLARE
ACTIVITY DURING THE PERIOD.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY SHOULD BE
GENERALLY MODERATE WITH REGION 5669 BEING THE PRIMARY SOURCE
OF CLASS M FLARES. A CLASS X FLARE FROM THIS REGION REMAINS
POSSIBLE DURING THE PERIOD.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 04/2100Z TO 05/2100Z:
ACTIVE TO MINOR GEOMAGNETIC STORM CONDITIONS WERE EXPERIENCED
DURING THE FIRST HALF OF THE PERIOD. MOSTLY UNSETTLED LEVELS
WERE OBSERVED DURING THE LATTER HALF. THE PROTON EVENT WHICH
BEGAN AT 04/0120Z ENDED AT 05/0600Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD
SHOULD BE ACTIVE FOR THE NEXT THREE DAYS. PERIODS AT MINOR
STORM LEVELS ARE PROBABLE. A PROTON EVENT AND PCA ARE
POSSIBLE WITH THE APPROPRIATE FLARE FROM REGION 5669.
