FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 067
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 08 MAR 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 07/2100Z
TO 08/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH. REGION 5395 (N35E46)
REMAINED LARGE AND MAGNETICALLY COMPLEX. IT PRODUCED SEVERAL
M-CLASS FLARES INCLUDING AN M5/1B AT 08/0837Z, WHICH WAS
ASSOCIATED WITH MINOR RADIO EMISSIONS, AN M1/2B AT 08/1450Z
ASSOCIATED WITH A TYPE IV RADIO SWEEP BURST, AND AN M4
(OBSERVED BY THE SOLAR MAXIMUM MISSION SATELLITE) AT 08/1857Z.
ONE OPTICALLY UNCORRELATED M2 X-RAY BURST OCCURRED AT 08/0833Z.
TWO NEW REGIONS WERE ASSIGNED TODAY: 5397 (N35E61) AND 5398
(S16E68). BOTH WERE SMALL BIPOLES.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
HIGH. THERE IS A GOOD CHANCE FOR MAJOR FLARE ACTIVITY FROM
REGION 5395.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 07/2100Z TO 08/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO ACTIVE LEVELS FOR
THE PAST 24 HOURS. A SUDDEN IMPULSE OF 38 NANOTESLAS WAS
RECORDED AT BOULDER AT 08/1755Z. A SATELLITE PROTON EVENT AT
GREATER THAN 10 MEV BEGAN AT 08/1735Z AND REMAINS IN
PROGRESS. THUS FAR, THE MAXIMUM OBSERVED FLUX HAS BEEN
55 PFU AT 08/1755Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM LEVELS WITH MOST OF
THE STORMING EXPECTED TOMORROW. THIS ACTIVITY WILL BE IN
RESPONSE TO THE X15/3B FLARE OF 6 MARCH. THE SATELLITE PROTON
EVENT AT GREATER THAN 10 MEV IS EXPECTED TO END WITHIN THE
NEXT 12 TO 24 HOURS.
