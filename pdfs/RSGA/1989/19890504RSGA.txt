JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 04 MAY 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 03/2100Z
TO 04/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH. REGION 5464 (S19W38) GREW
DRAMATICALLY DURING THE PERIOD AND IS NOW A LARGE CLASS EKC
SUNSPOT GROUP WITH A BETA GAMMA DELTA MAGNETIC CONFIGURATION.
THE REGION PRODUCED THE FOLLOWING FLARES - AN M4/2B AT 04/0424Z
WITH A TYPE II, AN M2/SN AT 04/0824Z, AN M5/2N AT 04/1115Z
WITH A WEAK TYPE II AND STRONG TYPE IV, AN M1/1B AT 04/1842Z,
AND AN M1/SB AT 04/2041Z. REGION 5470 (N28E11) ALSO GREW DURING
THE PERIOD, PRODUCING AN M1/1N AT 03/2104UT AND A MODERATE
DURATION M2/SN AT 04/1927Z.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY SHOULD CONTINUE
AT A GENERALLY HIGH LEVEL. GROWTH AND MAGNETIC COMPLEXITY
IN BOTH REGIONS 5464 AND 5470 BODE WELL FOR THE PRODUCTION
OF CLASS M FLARES AND BOTH ARE CAPABLE OF X CLASS FLARE
PRODUCTION.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 03/2100Z TO 04/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS UNSETTLED FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD
SHOULD BE AT ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM LEVELS FOR THE ENTIRE
PERIOD. INTERMITTENT MAJOR STORM CONDITIONS ARE POSSIBLE
ESPECIALLY DURING 05 AND 06 MAY.  A PROTON EVENT DURING THE
PERIOD IS ALSO POSSIBLE.
