IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 03/2100Z
TO 04/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. LARGEST EVENT WAS A C7/1B
FLARE IN REGION 5721 (N28E53). 5721 IS THE MOST COMPLEX SPOT
GROUP WITH A BETA GAMMA MAGNETIC STRUCTURE. TWO WEAK TYPE II
BURSTS WERE OBSERVED AT 0427Z AND 0519Z. A C2/NO OPTICAL FLARE
WAS ASSOCIATED WITH THE 0427 SWEEP BUT NO X-RAY OR OPTICAL
FLARE ACCOMPANIED THE 0519Z SWEEP. NEW REGION 5727 (N14E24)
WAS NUMBERED TODAY.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. REGION 5721 MAY PRODUCE AN ISOLATED M-CLASS
FLARE.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 03/2100Z TO 04/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO UNSETTLED
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS. THE PROTON EVENT THAT BEGAN
AT 29/1205Z SEP 89 ENDED AT 04/0145Z OCT 89.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT MOSTLY UNSETTLED LEVELS THE NEXT THREE DAYS.
ACTIVE CONDITIONS ARE STILL EXPECTED AT THE HIGHER LATITUDES
ON OCT 5TH.
