IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 03/2100Z
TO 04/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 5428 (S18W00)
PRODUCED AN M1/SN FLARE AT 03/2210Z. THIS REGION REMAINS LARGE
AND MAGNETICALLY COMPLEX; ITS SUBFLARE PRODUCTION INCREASED
DURING THE PERIOD. ENHANCED YELLOW-LINE (CALCIUM XV) EMISSION
HAS BEEN OBSERVED IN THE VICINITY OF REGIONS 5440 (N34E56) AND
NEW REGION 5441 (N34E76).
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. THERE IS A FAIR CHANCE FOR ISOLATED M-LEVEL
ACTIVITY FROM 5428 AND 5441. THE CHANCE FOR MAJOR FLARES FROM
THESE REGIONS IS VERY SLIGHT.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 03/2100Z TO 04/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE LEVELS
AT MIDDLE LATITUDES FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS. HIGH LATITUDE
FIELD ACTIVITY RANGED FROM ACTIVE TO MAJOR STORM LEVELS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE MOSTLY UNSETTLED AT MIDDLE LATITUDES AND ACTIVE
AT HIGHER LATITUDES.
