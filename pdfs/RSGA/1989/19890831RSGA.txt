IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 30/2100Z
TO 31/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 5669 (S16E45)
REMAINED A PROLIFIC FLARE PRODUCER. IT PROVIDED TWO M-CLASS
FLARES DURING THE PERIOD: AN M1/1F AT 31/0101Z AND AN M1/1N AT
31/1608Z. THE LATTER FLARE WAS ASSOCIATED WITH A 280 SFU
TENFLARE. A SUBFAINT FLARE OCCURRED FROM WITHIN THE REGION AT
31/1620Z AND WAS ASSOCIATED WITH A 280 SFU TENFLARE.
THIS REGION ALSO PRODUCED FOUR C-CLASS FLARES AS WELL. REGION
5669 WAS SPLIT INTO TWO SEPARATE REGIONS TODAY WITH THE EASTERN
HALF OF THE SPLIT NUMBERED AS NEW REGION 5671 (S19E59). BOTH
REGIONS ARE LARGE AND MAGNETICALLY COMPLEX WITH EACH CONTAINING
A MAGNETIC DELTA CONFIGURATION. REGION 5670 (S27E15) GREW
GRADUALLY THROUGHOUT THE PERIOD.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE TO HIGH. BOTH REGIONS 5669 AND 5671 ARE LIKELY TO
PRODUCE M-CLASS FLARES AND ISOLATED X-CLASS ACTIVITY.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 30/2100Z TO 31/2100Z:

FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED.
