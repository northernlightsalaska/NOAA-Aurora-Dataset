JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 26 APR 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 25/2100Z
TO 26/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. NUMEROUS SUBFLARES
OCCURRED FROM A VARIETY OF REGIONS. MOST WERE WITHOUT X-RAY OR
OPTICAL CORRELATION. REGION 5464 (S19E61) GREW GRADUALLY
THROUGH THE PERIOD. THE REMAINING REGIONS WERE STABLE OR
DECLINING. TWO NEW REGIONS WERE NUMBERED TODAY: 5466 (S31W22),
A SMALL BIPOLE, AND 5467 (N28E58), A SMALL UNIPOLE.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MOSTLY LOW. REGIONS 5454 (S20W47) AND 5464 ARE CAPABLE OF
C-CLASS AND ISOLATED M-CLASS FLARE ACTIVITY.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 25/2100Z TO 26/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT UNSETTLED TO MAJOR STORM
LEVELS AT MIDDLE LATITUDES FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS. HIGHER
LATITUDES EXPERIENCED MINOR TO MAJOR STORMING.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT ACTIVE LEVELS AT MIDDLE LATITUDES AND AT
ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM LEVELS AT HIGHER LATITUDES.
