IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 12/2100Z
TO 13/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. AN OPTICALLY
UNCORRELATED M1 X-RAY BURST OCCURRED AT 13/2017Z. NO RADIO
EMISSIONS WERE ASSOCIATED WITH THIS EVENT. REGION 5783 (N18W52)
SHOWED SIGNS OF GRADUAL DECAY WHILE PRODUCING SEVERAL SUBFLARES,
THE LARGEST OF WHICH WAS A C2/SF AT 13/0846Z. MODEST DISCRETE
RADIO EMISSIONS ACCOMPANIED THE FLARE. NO SIGNIFICANT GROWTH OR
DECAY WAS NOTED IN THE REMAINING REGIONS. NEW REGION 5789
(N35E23) WAS NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE TO HIGH. REGION 5783 PROVIDES A GOOD CHANCE FOR
M-CLASS ACTIVITY AND A SLIGHT CHANCE FOR AN ISOLATED X-CLASS
FLARE. REGIONS 5781 (N12W72), 5786 (N13W09), AND 5788 (S23E31)
APPEAR CAPABLE OF ISOLATED M-CLASS ACTIVITY.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 12/2100Z TO 13/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO MAJOR STORM
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS. A GRADUAL STORM COMMENCEMENT
BEGAN NEAR 13/0300Z. MAJOR STORM PERIODS OCCURRED NEAR
13/0600Z AND 13/1500-1800Z. THE CAUSE OF THE STORMING IS
UNCLEAR AT THIS TIME.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE LEVELS. BRIEF PERIODS
OF MINOR STORMING MAY OCCUR DURING THE FIRST HALF OF DAY ONE
AND DURING THE FINAL DAY OF THE FORECAST PERIOD.
