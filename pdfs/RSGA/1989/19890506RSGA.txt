FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 126
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 06 MAY 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 05/2100Z
TO 06/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 5476 (S31E51)
PRODUCED TWO M-LEVEL X-RAY FLARES. THE LARGEST WAS AN M4/2B AT
06/1702Z. THIS REGION APPEARS TO BE IN A RAPID GROWTH PHASE.
REGION 5464 (S19W39) PRODUCED AN M1/SN AT 06/1451Z, THIS FLARE
WAS ACCOMPANIED BY A TYPE II AND TYPE IV RADIO EMISSION. REGION
5464 AND 5470 (N29W12) HAVE MAINTAINED A BETA-GAMMA-DELTA
MAGNETIC CONFIGURATION. REGION 5464 APPEARS TO BE IN A SLOW
DECAY PHASE. ONE NEW REGION WAS NUMBERED TODAY, REGION 5477
(N09E41) A SIMPLE UNIPOLAR SPOT GROUP.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE TO HIGH. THE THREE REGIONS MENTIONED ABOVE 5464, 5470,
AND 5476 ARE EXPECTED TO CONTINUE THEIR M-LEVEL FLARE PRODUCTION
AND MAY ELEVATE ACTIVITY TO HIGH CONDITIONS.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 05/2100Z TO 06/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN MOSTLY AT ACTIVE LEVELS FOR
BOTH MIDDLE AND HIGH LATITUDES. THE PROTON EVENT THAT BEGAN
AT 05/0905Z ENDED AT 05/2210Z. ANOTHER PROTON EVENT BEGAN ON
06/0235Z AND HAS REACHED A TENTATIVE MAXIMUM OF 110 PFU AT
GREATER THAN OR EQUAL TO 10MEV AT 06/1045Z. THIS EVENT IS
STILL IN PROGRESS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT MINOR STORM LEVELS THROUGHOUT THE PERIOD.
THIS IS BASED ON THE X2/3B FLARE OF 05 MAY AS WELL AS THE FLARE
ACTIVITY OUTLINED IN SECTION 1A ABOVE.
