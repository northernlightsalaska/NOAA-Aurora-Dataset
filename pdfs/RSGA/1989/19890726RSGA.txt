FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 207
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 26 JUL 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 25/2100Z
TO 26/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW, ONLY SMALL C LEVEL FLARES
HAVE BEEN OBSERVED.  ACTIVITY FROM DEPARTING REGION 5603 (N28
WEST LIMB) HAS DECREASED AND WAS PROBABLY RESPONSIBLE FOR MANY
OF THE UNCORRELATED C FLARES EARLY IN THE DAY.  REGIONS 5597
(S13W69) AND 5598 (S23W55) APPEAR TO BE DECLINING, ALTHOUGH
AFS HAD BEEN REPORTED.  THE MOST ACTIVE REGION AT THIS TIME IS
REGION 5608 (S19E20).  REGION 5611 (S38W20) WAS NUMBERED AND
ANOTHER REGION MAY BE ROTATING ON TO THE DISK NEAR S20.  THE
10.7CM RADIO FLUX IS DOWN TO 164, THE LOWEST VALUE SINCE MARCH.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW.  THERE IS A SMALL CHANCE OF AN ISOLATED CLASS M FLARE FROM
THE REGIONS IN THE SOUTHWEST AND FROM THE RETURNING REGIONS ON
THE EAST LIMB.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 25/2100Z TO 26/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE FOR THE
PAST 24 HOURS.  THE SOLAR PROTON EVENT WHICH BEGAN AT
25/0900Z REACHED A MAXIMUM OF 54PFU AT 25/1225Z AND WENT
BELOW EVENT THRESHOLD LEVELS AT 26/1305Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE FOR MOST OF THE PERIOD.
