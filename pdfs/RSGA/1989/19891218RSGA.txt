JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 18 DEC 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 17/2100Z
TO 18/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 5846 (N19E42)
PRODUCED AN M2/1F TENFLARE AT 18/0853Z. SEVERAL C-CLASS FLARES
HAVE ALSO BEEN NOTED FROM THIS REGION WHICH IS NOW SHOWING
DECAY IN ITS PENUMBRAL SPOT AREA. REGIONS 5844 (S10W52) AND 5847
(N33W55) BOTH CONTINUE TO GROW BOTH IN WHITE LIGHT AND PLAGE
AREA WITH OCCASIONAL C-CLASS FLARES NOTED. REGION 5836 (N13E01)
CONTINUED ITS SLOW DECAY AND HAS BEEN QUIET.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
PREDOMINANTLY LOW WITH MOSTLY C-CLASS FLARES EXPECTED FROM
REGIONS 5844, 5846, AND 5847. THESE REGIONS ALSO HAVE
THE POTENTIAL, TO A LIMITED EXTENT, FOR ISOLATED LOW LEVEL
M-CLASS EVENTS. REGION 5836 SHOULD CONTINUE ITS SLOW DECAY.
REGION 5806 (N18 CARRINGTON 034) IS DUE TO RETURN LATER IN
THE PERIOD. THIS REGION PRODUCED SEVERAL M-CLASS EVENTS
DURING ITS LAST ROTATION AND MAY CONTRIBUTE TO THE ACTIVITY
LEVELS WITHIN THE NEXT FEW DAYS.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 17/2100Z TO 18/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET LEVELS FOR
THE PAST 24 HOURS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT QUIET LEVELS THE NEXT THREE DAYS.
