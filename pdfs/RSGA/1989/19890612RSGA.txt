FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 163
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 12 JUN 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 11/2100Z
TO 12/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE.  REGION 5533 (S19E45)
PRODUCED THE ONLY M-CLASS FLARE OF THE PERIOD, AN M1/SF AT
12/0219Z.  THIS REGION HAS GROWN SINCE YESTERDAY AND MAY
NOW HAVE A DELTA CONFIGURATION IN THE TRAILER SUNSPOTS.  REGION
5528 (N21E26) APPEARS TO BE THE MOST COMPLEX SUNSPOT GROUP ON
THE DISK BUT DID NOT PRODUCE SIGNIFICANT FLARE ACTIVITY TODAY.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE TO HIGH.  MAJOR FLARES ARE POSSIBLE FROM REGIONS 5528
AND 5533.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 11/2100Z TO 12/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE.  AN ISOLATED
PERIOD OF STORM CONDITIONS OCCURRED FROM 12/0600-0900Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE MOSTLY UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE.
