FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 002
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 02 JAN 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 01/2100Z
TO 02/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY CONTINUES MODERATE. A LONG-
DURATION M2 TENFLARE REACHED MAXIMUM AT 1910Z AND IS STILL WELL
ABOVE BACKGROUND.  EARLY REPORTS SUGGEST THE FLARE ORIGINATED
AT THE NORTHWEST LIMB WHERE LOOPS WERE SEEN IN THE VICINITY OF
REGION 5290 (N23W82). ANOTHER M-CLASS EVENT - AN M1/0F - WAS
OBSERVED IN REGION 5292 (S17W76) AT 1036Z. NEW REGION 5305
(N10E68) BECAME VISIBLE NEAR EAST LIMB AND THERE MAY BE TWO
MORE AS YET UNNUMBERED REGIONS ALSO COMING ON THE DISK NEARBY.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE THROUGHOUT THE PERIOD.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 01/2100Z TO 02/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE FOR A SHORT
TIME AT THE BEGINNING OF THE INTERVAL. QUIET TO UNSETTLED
LEVELS HAVE PREVAILED SINCE.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO REMAIN AT QUIET TO UNSETTLED LEVELS THE NEXT THREE
DAYS.
