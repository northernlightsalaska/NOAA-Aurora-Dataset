IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 16/2100Z
TO 17/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. REGION 5452 (S17E55)
PRODUCED TODAY'S LARGEST FLARE: A C5/SN AT 1746Z. REGIONS
5451 (N10E10) AND 5449 (S20W21) ARE THE MOST IMPRESSIVE
GROUPS IN SIZE AND MAGNETIC COMPLEXITY BUT PRODUCED ONLY
SMALL, SUBFLARE FLUCTUATIONS DURING THE PERIOD. SURGE ACTIVITY
WAS OBSERVED ON THE EAST LIMB AT S23 (FROM REGION 5454) AND
AT N15. THE REMAINDER OF THE SOLAR DISK AND LIMBS WERE STABLE.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MOSTLY LOW. THERE IS A FAIR CHANCE FOR A SMALL, ISOLATED M-CLASS
FLARE FROM REGION 5451 OR REGION 5449.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 16/2100Z TO 17/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS MOSTLY QUIET TO UNSETTLED. A BRIEF
PERIOD OF ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM CONDITIONS OCCURRED EARLY IN
THE DAY.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE MOSTLY UNSETTLED WITH OCCASIONAL ACTIVE PERIODS
AT HIGH LATITUDES.
