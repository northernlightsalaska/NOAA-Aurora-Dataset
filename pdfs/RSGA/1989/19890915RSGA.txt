JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 15 SEP 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 14/2100Z
TO 15/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. A LONG DURATION C6 FLARE
WAS OBSERVED FROM 0627-0943Z AND WAS ASSOCIATED WITH AN ERUPTIVE
PROMINENCE ON THE SOUTHWEST LIMB (S10) AND WITH A WEAK TYPE II
RADIO SWEEP. ADDTIONAL C-CLASS SUBFLARES WERE OBSERVED THROUGH-
OUT THE DAY. REGION 5694 (N26E16) CONTINUES TO GROW AND APPEARS
TO BE DEVELOPING A DELTA CONFIGURATION. SURGING WAS OBSERVED ON
THE SOUTHEAST LIMB AND OPTICAL REPORTS RECEIVED TOWARD THE END
OF THE PERIOD INDICATED THAT SOME NEW REGIONS MAY BE ROTATING
INTO VIEW IN THAT AREA.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MOSTLY LOW. AN ISOLATED M-CLASS EVENT IS POSSIBLE DURING THE
NEXT THREE DAYS WITH REGION 5694 BEING THE MOST LIKELY SOURCE
OF SUCH AN EVENT.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 14/2100Z TO 15/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS MOSTLY UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE WITH
SOME BRIEF, ISOLATED PERIODS OF MINOR TO MAJOR STORM
CONDITIONS. A SUDDEN IMPULSE WAS OBSERVED IN BOULDER AT
0048Z OF 27 NANOTESLA. THE INCREASE IN ACTIVITY WAS PROBABLY
CAUSED BY THE LONG DURATION X-RAY EVENT THAT OCCURRED ON 12
SEPTEMBER.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE MOSTLY UNSETTLED WITH OCCASIONAL PERIODS OF
ACTIVE CONDITIONS AT HIGH LATITUDES.
