FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 285
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 12 OCT 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 11/2100Z
TO 12/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. FREQUENT LOW CLASS C
FLARES WERE OBSERVED FROM SEVERAL REGIONS. REGION 5740 (S18E52)
IS GROWING SLOWLY AND PRODUCED SEVERAL OF THE CLASS C EVENTS.
REGION 5738 (N12E08) CONTINUED TO EMERGE RAPIDLY AND IS NOW A
MODERATE SIZE CLASS D SUNSPOT GROUP. NEW REGION 5743 (N26W62)
EMERGED AND A SMALL H TYPE SPOT ROUNDED THE LIMB WHERE OLD
REGION 5698 WAS EXPECTED AND WAS NUMBERED AS NEW REGION
5744 (S22E75).
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY SHOULD CONTINUE
PREDOMINANTLY LOW. REGIONS 5740 AND 5738 APPEAR CAPABLE OF
PRODUCING ISOLATED CLASS M FLARES.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 11/2100Z TO 12/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS QUIET DURING THE PAST 24 HOURS. A
SUDDEN IMPULSE OF 15 GAMMA WAS OBSERVED AT 12/0740Z BUT THE
FIELD REMAINED CALM.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  AT LOW GEOMAGNETIC
LATITUDES, THE FIELD SHOULD BE QUIET TO UNSETTLED FOR THE
NEXT THREE DAYS. UNSETTLED TO SLIGHTLY ACTIVE GEOMAGNETIC
CONDITIONS ARE FORECAST FOR HIGH LATITUDES FOR THE SAME
PERIOD.
