FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 084
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 25 MAR 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 24/2100Z
TO 25/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. REGION 5409 (N20W60)
PRODUCED THE DAY'S LARGEST EVENT: A C6/SN AT 1535Z. THIS REGION
IS THE ONLY GROUP ON THE SOLAR DISK PRODUCING SUBFLARES AND
CONTINUES TO DECLINE IN SIZE AND COMPLEXITY. AN ERUPTIVE PROM-
INENCE WAS OBSERVED ON THE WEST LIMB AT N36 AT THE SAME TIME
THE C6 FLARE WAS IN PROGRESS. THE REMAINDER OF THE DISK AND
LIMBS WERE QUIET AND STABLE.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. REGION 5409 CONTINUES TO POSE A THREAT FOR AN
ISOLATED M-CLASS FLARE OVER THE NEXT THREE DAYS.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 24/2100Z TO 25/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS QUIET FROM THE BEGINNING OF THE
PERIOD UNTIL ABOUT 1200Z, WHEN CONDITIONS BECAME QUIET TO
UNSETTLED. SINCE ABOUT 1800Z CONDITIONS HAVE BEEN MOSTLY
UNSETTLED AT MIDDLE LATITUDES AND ACTIVE AT HIGH LATITUDES.
THE INCREASE MAY MARK THE BEGINNING OF EFFECTS FROM THE
X1/3B FLARE THAT OCCURRED ON 23 MARCH.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM LEVELS TOMORROW AND
PARTWAY THROUGH THE SECOND DAY AS THE DISTURBANCE INTENSIFIES.
CONDITIONS SHOULD GRADUALLY SUBSIDE TO UNSETTLED LEVELS WITH
OCCASIONAL ACTIVE PERIODS THEREAFTER.
