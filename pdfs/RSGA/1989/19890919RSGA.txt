FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 262
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 19 SEP 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 18/2100Z
TO 19/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE.  REGION 5698 (S25E28)
PRODUCED TWO M-CLASS FLARES SINCE YESTERDAY, AN M1/SN AT
19/1003Z AND AN M4/(NO OPTICAL CLASS) AT 19/1614Z.  THE M4
FLARE WAS ASSOCIATED WITH A 290 SFU RADIO BURST AT 2695 MHZ.
THIS REGION HAS GROWN DURING THE PAST 24 HOURS, INCREASING IN
OPTICAL AND MAGNETIC COMPLEXITY.  REGION 5686 (N17W83) HAS
BEEN RELATIVELY QUIET.  NEW REGION 5702 (S21E03) WAS NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE.  ADDITIONAL M-CLASS ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED FROM
REGION 5698.  REGION COMPLEXITY COULD SUPPORT MAJOR FLARES.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 18/2100Z TO 19/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD RANGED FROM UNSETTLED TO SEVERE
STORM.  SEVERE STORM CONDITIONS OCCURRED AT BOTH MIDDLE AND HIGH
LATITUDES FROM 19/0300-0900Z.  THE SOURCE OF THIS DISTURBANCE IS
THOUGHT TO BE THE M2 LONG DURATION EVENT ON 15 SEPTEMBER.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE LEVELS FOR THE NEXT
24 HOURS WITH ISOLATED PERIODS OF SUBSTORM ACTIVITY NEAR
LOCAL MIDNIGHT.  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD SHOULD RETURN TO QUIET
TO UNSETTLED CONDITIONS BY THE THIRD DAY OF THE FORECAST.
