FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 209
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 28 JUL 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 27/2100Z
TO 28/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW.  SEVERAL SMALL FLARES
OCCURRED, NONE WERE ENERGETIC, HOWEVER, THERE WERE NUMEROUS
TYPE III SWEEPS OBSERVED.  REGION 5608 (S18W09) IS STILL THE
BRIGHTEST REGION ON THE DISK AND SOME GROWTH WAS REPORTED.
THE SMALL SPOT GROUP TO THE WEST OF 5608 WAS NUMBERED REGION
5616 (S17W15).  REGION 5612 (S17E61) ALTHOUGH LARGE IN WHITE
LIGHT, APPEARS TO BE SIMPLE AND INACTIVE.  A SMALL REGION
NUMBERED 5615 (S06E71)HAS EMERGED NEAR THE BASE OF THE LARGE
FILAMENT NEAR THE SE LIMB, WHICH MAY RESULT IN THE FILAMENT
ERUPTING IN THE NEAR FUTURE.  A LARGE POLAR CROWN FILAMENT
NEAR N65 W30 ERUPTED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 27/2100Z TO 28/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO UNSETTLED
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE BASICALLY UNSETTLED FOR THE NEAR FUTURE.
