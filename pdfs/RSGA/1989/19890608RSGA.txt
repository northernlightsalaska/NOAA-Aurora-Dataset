FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 159
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 08 JUN 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 07/2100Z
TO 08/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 5521 (S19E11)
PRODUCED AN M1/1N FLARE AT 0922Z. THE REGION CONTINUES TO BE
THE MOST FLARE PRODUCTIVE ON THE DISK AS IT SLOWLY DECAYS.
AN UNCORRELATED M2 FLARE OCCURRED AT 1405Z. AN ERUPTIVE PROM-
INENCE WAS OBSERVED ON THE WEST LIMB AT N10 AND WAS CORRELATED
WITH A LONG DURATION X-RAY EVENT FROM 1715-1850Z: THE PEAK FLUX
WAS M1 AT 1753Z AND THE MAXIMUM EXTENT OBSERVED WAS 0.28 RADII.
NEW REGION 5528 (N21E72) PRODUCED NUMEROUS SUBFLARE FLUCTUATIONS
DURING THE DAY AND HAS MORE FULLY ROTATED INTO VIEW AS A LARGE,
D-TYPE REGION. THE REMAINDER OF THE DISK WAS RELATIVELY STABLE
AND QUIET.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE TO HIGH. THE PRIMARY THREATS FOR MAJOR FLARE ACTIVITY
ARE REGIONS 5521 AND 5528.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 07/2100Z TO 08/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS GENERALLY QUIET TO UNSETTLED WITH
OCCASIONAL ACTIVE PERIODS AT HIGH LATITUDES. A SUDDEN IMPULSE
OF 48 GAMMA, HOWEVER, OCCURRED AT 1955Z AND WAS FOLLOWED BY
MINOR STORM CONDITIONS FOR THE LAST HOUR OF THE PERIOD. THE
MOST LIKELY SOURCE FOR THE EVENT WAS THE M6/2F FLARE FROM
REGION 5521 THAT OCCURRED LATE ON 04 JUNE.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE LEVELS FOR THE NEXT
THREE DAYS. OCCASIONAL SUBSTORM PERIODS MAY OCCUR IN LOCAL
NIGHTTIME SECTORS.
