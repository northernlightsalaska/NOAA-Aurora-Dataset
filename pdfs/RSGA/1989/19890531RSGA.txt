JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 31 MAY 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 30/2100Z
TO 31/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. THE MOST ENERGETIC
EVENT OF THE INTERVAL WAS AN UNOBSERVED M1 X-RAY FLARE AT 0039Z.
THE SOUTHWEST QUADRANT REMAINS CLUTTERED WITH ABUTTED ACTIVE
REGIONS BUT AS YET THE INTERACTIONS HAVE NOT YIELDED SIGNIF-
ICANT FLARE ACTIVITY. NEWLY ASSIGNED REGION 5517 (S19E80)
PRODUCED A SMALL FLARE AS IT CAME INTO VIEW.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. NEIGHBORING GROUPS 5511 (S22W06) AND 5515
(S22W15) ARE THE MOST LIKELY CANDIDATES FOR M-LEVEL ACTIVITY.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 30/2100Z TO 31/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN QUIET TO UNSETTLED AT MID-
LATITUDES. HIGH LATITUDES OBSERVED UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE
CONDITIONS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE THROUGHOUT THE PERIOD.
