JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 31 DEC 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 30/2100Z
TO 31/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH.  REGION 5852 (S27W57)
KEPT ACTIVITY AT HIGH LEVELS BY PRODUCING AN X2/2B AT 31/0944Z.
THIS REGION CONTINUES TO EXHIBIT OVERALL SIMPLIFICATION IN
WHITE LIGHT AND H-ALPHA STRUCTURE.  REGION 5858 (S19W13) ALSO
CONTINUES TO DECAY AND HAS PRODUCED ONLY SUBFLARES SINCE
YESTERDAY.  NEW REGION 5866 (N15W08) WAS NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE TO HIGH.  AN ISOLATED MAJOR FLARE IS POSSIBLE IN
REGION 5852 OR 5858.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 30/2100Z TO 31/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD RANGED FROM UNSETTLED TO STORM LEVELS.
THE MAJOR GEOMAGNETIC STORM ENDED AT MIDDLE LATITUDES AT
30/0000Z.  THE MIDDLE LATITUDE FIELD FOLLOWING THE END OF THE
STORM WAS UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE.  THE HIGH LATITUDE FIELD HAS
BEEN MOSTLY ACTIVE WITH STORM CONDITIONS EXPERIENCED FROM
31/0300 TO 31/1800Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE ACTIVE TO UNSETTLED FOR THE NEXT 24 HOURS AND
UNSETTLED TO QUIET BY THE THIRD DAY OF THE FORECAST PERIOD.
