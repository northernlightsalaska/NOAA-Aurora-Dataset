FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 142
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 22 MAY 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 21/2100Z
TO 22/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH. REGION 5497 (S20E05)
PRODUCED AN M5/2B FLARE AT 22/0037Z. THE FLARE WAS ACCOMPANIED
BY SIGNIFICANT TYPES II AND IV SWEEP BURSTS AND SIGNIFICANT
DISCRETE FREQUENCY BURSTS (ON THE LOWER HALF OF THE SOLAR RADIO
SPECTRUM). REGION 5497 GREW THROUGHOUT THE PERIOD. REGION 5495
(N22W51) ENTERED A SLOW DECAY PHASE. IT PRODUCED AN M1/1N FLARE
AT 22/1533Z WHICH WAS ASSOCIATED WITH MINOR DISCRETE RADIO
EMISSIONS. NEW REGION 5503 (S21E72) EMERGED AS A SMALL A-TYPE.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE. M-CLASS FLARING IS EXPECTED FROM REGION 5497 AND TO A
LESSER EXTENT FROM REGION 5495. REGION 5497 MAY ALSO PRODUCE AN
ISOLATED MAJOR FLARE.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 21/2100Z TO 22/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO UNSETTLED LEVELS
FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE ACTIVE AT MIDDLE LATITUDES AND AT ACTIVE TO
MINOR STORM LEVELS AT HIGH LATITUDES. DISTURBED CONDITIONS ARE
EXPECTED IN RESPONSE TO RECENT FLARE ACTIVITY. IN ADDITION,
CORONAL HOLE EFFECTS ARE EXPECTED TO COMMENCE ON THE FINAL DAY
OF THE FORECAST PERIOD.
