IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 26/2100Z
TO 27/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 5497 (S18W63)
PRODUCED A MODERATE DURATION M1/SN AT 26/2247Z. ONLY MINOR
SUBFLARES WERE OBSERVED FROM THIS REGION DURING THE REMAINDER
OF THE PERIOD. REGION 5497 REMAINS A MODERATELY LARGE STABLE
SUNSPOT GROUP WITH MIXED POLARITIES. OTHER DISK REGIONS
ARE GENERALLY STABLE AND UNIMPRESSIVE.  TWO SMALL REGIONS
EMERGED AND WERE NUMBERED 5512 (N11W51) AND 5513 (S19E61).
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY SHOULD CONTINUE
AT A GENERALLY MODERATE LEVEL - DUE ALMOST EXCLUSIVELY TO THE
FLARE POTENTIAL OF REGION 5497. AN ISOLATED MAJOR FLARE FROM
THIS REGION REMAINS A POSSIBILITY BEFORE WEST LIMB TRANSIT
ON 30 MAY.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 26/2100Z TO 27/2100Z:
THE MID LATITUDE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS PREDOMINANTLY
UNSETTLED FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS. ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM LEVELS
WERE EXPERIENCED AT HIGH LATITUDES.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD
SHOULD BE AT UNSETTLED TO SLIGHTLY ACTIVE LEVELS FOR THE
DURATION OF THE FORECAST PERIOD.
