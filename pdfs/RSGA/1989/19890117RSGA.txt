FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 017
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 17 JAN 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 16/2100Z
TO 17/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH. REGION 5312 (S33W57)
REMAINED IN A PERIOD OF GRADUAL DECAY, BUT MAINTAINED ITS
COMPLEX MAGNETIC STRUCTURE. THIS REGION WAS RESPONSIBLE FOR THE
ONLY M-LEVEL EVENT OF THE PERIOD: AN M7/SB FLARE AT 16/2220Z.
THE EVENT WAS ACCOMPANIED BY MINOR DISCRETE FREQUENCY RADIO
EMISSIONS. TWO NEW REGIONS WERE ASSIGNED TODAY: 5324 (N23E71),
AN H-TYPE, AND 5325 (N27W74), A SMALL UNIPOLAR GROUP.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
HIGH. REGION 5312 MAY PRODUCE A MAJOR FLARE DURING THE PERIOD.
REGION 5317 (N22W26) HAS THE POTENTIAL TO PRODUCE M-CLASS
ACTIVITY.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 16/2100Z TO 17/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT UNSETTLED TO MINOR STORM
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS. THIS ACTIVITY WAS POSSIBLY IN
RESPONSE TO THE X2/2B FLARE THAT OCCURRED ON 14 JANUARY.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TOMORROW. THE SECOND DAY IS EXPECTED
TO BE ACTIVE DUE TO YESTERDAY'S M2/1F/TYPE II SWEEP EVENT.
THE FINAL DAY OF THE PERIOD IS EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED. HIGH
LATITUDES MAY EXPERIENCE ACTIVE LEVELS THROUGHOUT THE PERIOD.
