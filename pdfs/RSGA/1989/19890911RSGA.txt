FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 254
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 11 SEP 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 10/2100Z
TO 11/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH. THE ONLY M-CLASS EVENT OF
THE PERIOD WAS A RECENT M6/2B AT 1943Z FROM REGION 5680
(N17E01). A BURST OF 480 SFU AT 10 CM. AND BOTH TYPE II AND
TYPE IV SWEEP ACCOMPANIED THE FLARE. FREQUENT C-CLASS FLARES
OCCURRED THROUGHOUT THE DAY AND THESE WERE DISTRIBUTED AMONG
ELEVEN ACTIVE REGIONS. THE BIGGEST CONTRIBUTOR WAS REGION 5683
(N17W14) WHICH GREW DRAMATICALLY DURING THE INTERVAL. FREQUENT
SURGING WAS REPORTED ON THE SOUTHWEST LIMB WHERE REGION 5669
(S19W82) IS NOW POSITIONED. ONE NEW REGION WAS BORN ON THE
DISK, 5693 (S10E06).
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE TO HIGH. WATCH REGIONS 5669, 5680, AND 5683 FOR
CONTINUED MAJOR FLARE ACTIVITY.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 10/2100Z TO 11/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET LEVELS FOR
THE PAST 24 HOURS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM LEVELS THE NEXT 24
TO 36 HOURS DUE TO FLARE ACTIVITY ON 09 SEPT. ACTIVITY SHOULD
THEN WEAKEN WITH UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE CONDITIONS THE RULE BY
THE END OF THE INTERVAL.
