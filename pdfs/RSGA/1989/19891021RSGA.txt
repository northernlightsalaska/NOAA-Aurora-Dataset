JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 21 OCT 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 20/2100Z
TO 21/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 5747 (S27W24)
PRODUCED THREE M-CLASS EVENTS DURING THE PERIOD. IT'S LARGEST
EVENT OF THE PERIOD WAS AN M2/1N FLARE AT 21/0156Z WHICH WAS
ACCOMPANIED BY MINOR DISCRETE RADIO EMISSIONS. SPLITTING OF THE
REGION'S LARGE ASSYMETRIC MAIN SPOT WAS EVIDENT, BUT NO CLEAR
INDICATION OF DECAY EXISTED. THE GROUP REMAINED LARGE AND
MAGNETICALLY COMPLEX. REGION 5754 (S19E57) CONTINUED IT'S GROWTH
PHASE AND PRODUCED ELEVEN FLARES THE LARGEST OF WHICH WAS A
C2/1F AT 21/0556Z. NEW REGIONS 5756 (N11E59), 5757 (N25E25), AND
5758 (S12E67) WERE NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE TO HIGH. REGION 5747 IS EXPECTED TO PRODUCE M-CLASS
FLARES. AN ISOLATED X-CLASS FLARE IS POSSIBLE FROM THIS REGION.
REGION 5754 MAY PRODUCE AN ISOLATED M-CLASS FLARE.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 20/2100Z TO 21/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT ACTIVE TO SEVERE STORM
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS. THE GREATER THAN 10 MEV PROTON
EVENT WHICH COMMENCED AT 19/1305Z CONTINUED. THE GREATER THAN
100 MEV PROTON EVENT ENDED AT 20/2200Z. ALL OF THE ABOVE
EFFECTS OCCURRED IN THE WAKE OF THE X13/4B FLARE OF 19 OCT.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE MOSTLY ACTIVE AT MIDDLE LATITUDES AND AT MINOR
TO MAJOR STORM LEVELS AT HIGHER LATITUDES DURING THE FIRST
DAY OF THE FORECAST PERIOD. THE REMAINDER OF THE PERIOD SHOULD
SEE THE FIELD AT UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE LEVELS AS FLARE EFFECTS
SUBSIDE. THE PROTON EVENT AT GREATER THAN 10 MEV IS EXPECTED
TO CONTINUE.
