FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 256
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 13 SEP 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 12/2100Z
TO 13/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 5686 (N15W09)
PRODUCED TODAY'S LARGEST FLARE: AN M3/2N AT 0340Z WHICH WAS
ACCOMPANIED BY MODEST DISCRETE RADIO BURSTS. ADDITIONAL
M-FLARES OCCURRED AT 0006Z, AN M1/SN FROM 5669 (S19W90),
AND 0837Z, AN M1/SF FROM 5676 (N27W53). THE OTHER REGIONS
APPEAR TO BE EITHER STABLE OR DECAYING. A SIGNIFICANT DECREASE
OF 43 FLUX UNITS WAS OBSERVED TODAY IN THE 10.7 CM FLUX, MARKING
THE DEPARTURE OF REGION 5669.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. REGION 5686, 5687 (N17W00) AND 5676 ARE THE
MOST LIKELY SOURCES FOR ISOLATED M-FLARES OVER THE NEXT FEW
DAYS.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 12/2100Z TO 13/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS QUIET TO UNSETTLED. A GREATER THAN
10 MEV PARTICLE EVENT CONTINUED IN PROGRESS THROUGHOUT THE
DAY. A MAXIMUM FLUX OF 57 PFU AT GREATER THAN 10 MEV WAS
OBSERVED AT 0825Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE QUIET TO UNSETTLED TOMORROW. MOSTLY ACTIVE
CONDITIONS ARE FORECAST FOR 15 SEPTEMBER IN RESPONSE TO
THE LONG DURATION X-RAY EVENT THAT OCCURRED ON 12 SEPTEMBER.
CONDITIONS SHOULD DECLINE TO MOSTLY UNSETTLED LEVELS BY THE
THIRD DAY.
