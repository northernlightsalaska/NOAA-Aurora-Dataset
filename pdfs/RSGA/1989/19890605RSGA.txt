JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 05 JUN 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 04/2100Z
TO 05/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH. REGION 5521 (S19E49)
PRODUCED TODAY'S LARGEST EVENT, AN M6/2F AT 04/2124Z. THIS
REGION DOMINATES THE DISK IN SIZE AND MAGNETIC COMPLEXITY
WITH AN EAST-WEST INVERSION LINE ACROSS A MAGNETIC DELTA
CONFIGURATION. THE REGION PRODUCED ADDITIONAL M-LEVEL ACTIVITY
AS FOLLOWS: M1/SF AT 0124Z, M1/SB AT 0857Z, AND AN M3/1B AT
1114Z. ADDITIONAL UNCORRELATED M-CLASS EVENTS OCCURRED AT
0725Z AND 2013Z. THREE NEW REGIONS EMERGED TODAY.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE TO HIGH. REGION 5521 IS CONSIDERED TO BE THE PRIMARY
THREAT FOR MAJOR FLARES AND MAGNETICALLY COMPLEX REGION 5517
MAY ALSO CONTRIBUTE TO M-CLASS LEVEL ACTIVITY.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 04/2100Z TO 05/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS PREDOMINANTLY QUIET TO UNSETTLED.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE OVER THE NEXT THREE DAYS.
