JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 29 JAN 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 28/2100Z
TO 29/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. TWO M-CLASS EVENTS
OCCURRED: AN M1/1B TENFLARE AT 28/2144Z WITH TYPE II FROM
REGION 5334 (S20E30) AND AN M1/0F AT 1106Z FROM REGION 5330
(S18W11). IT IS LIKELY THAT THE OPTICAL MAXIMUM WAS NOT SEEN
FOR THE LATTER FLARE DUE TO THE TIME OF LOCAL SUNRISE. TWO
NEWLY ASSIGNED REGIONS ARE 5337 (S20W52) AND 5338 (N21E41).
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY SHOULD CONTINUE
MODERATE. REGIONS 5330, 5334, AND 5329 (N17W46) ARE ALL
CAPABLE OF ADDITIONAL M-CLASS ACTIVITY.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 28/2100Z TO 29/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS MOSTLY QUIET AT MID-LATITUDES.
HIGH LATITUDES WERE GENERALLY QUIET EARLY AND ACTIVE LATE
IN THE INTERVAL.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE QUIET TO UNSETTLED AT MID-LATITUDES AND
UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE AT HIGH LATITUDES.
