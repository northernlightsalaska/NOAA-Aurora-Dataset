JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 26 FEB 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 25/2100Z
TO 26/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. REGION 5380 (N38E24)
PRODUCED THE DAY'S LARGEST FLARE: A C6/SF AT 0335Z. REGION
5368 (S12W89) EXITED THE DISK QUIETLY AND PRODUCED SURGE
ACTIVITY ON THE WEST LIMB AT S13. THE REMAINDER OF THE DISK
AND LIMBS WERE ESSENTIALLY QUIET AND STABLE.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW. THERE IS A SLIGHT CHANCE FOR AN ISOLATED M-CLASS EVENT,
PRIMARILY DUE TO THE PRESENCE OF REGIONS 5373 (N17W61) AND
5368 (S12W89). FLUX LEVELS ARE EXPECTED TO FALL AS REGION 5368
ROTATES OUT OF VIEW.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 25/2100Z TO 26/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS PREDOMINANTLY QUIET AT ALL LATITUDES.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE MOSLY UNSETTLED FOR THE NEXT THREE DAYS.
