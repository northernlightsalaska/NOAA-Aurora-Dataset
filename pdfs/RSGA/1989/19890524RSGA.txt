FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 144
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 24 MAY 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 23/2100Z
TO 24/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 5497 (S19W23)
PRODUCED AN M1/1N FLARE AT 24/1354Z. THE REGION ALSO PRODUCED
NUMEROUS SUBFLARES DURING THE PERIOD. TWO NEW REGIONS WERE
NUMBERED TODAY: 5507 (N28E68) AND 5508 (S26E61).
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE. REGION 5497 IS EXPECTED TO PRODUCE AN M-CLASS FLARE.
THIS REGION IS ALSO CAPABLE OF PRODUCING AN ISOLATED MAJOR
FLARE. REGION 5495 IS EXPECTED TO PRODUCE C-CLASS FLARES.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 23/2100Z TO 24/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS. THE HIGH LATITUDE FIELD
FLUCTUATED BETWEEN MINOR AND MAJOR STORM LEVELS. A SATELLITE
PROTON EVENT AT GREATER THAN 10 MEV BEGAN AT 24/0730Z AND
CONTINUED THROUGH THE END OF THE PERIOD. THE ABOVE ACTIVITY
IS PROBABLY THE RESULT OF THE M5/2B FLARE OF 22 MAY.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM LEVELS TOMORROW. THE
FINAL TWO DAYS OF THE PERIOD ARE EXPECTED TO BE ACTIVE. THIS
ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED IN RESPONSE TO THE EFFECTS OF A CORONAL
HOLE PRESENTLY LOCATED NEAR N15W31. THE PROTON EVENT AT
GREATER THAN 10 MEV IS EXPECTED TO END DURING THE PERIOD.
