JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 10 MAY 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 09/2100Z
TO 10/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. THE ONE M-CLASS
EVENT OF THE PERIOD WAS AN M1 AT 0426Z. IT MAY BE RELATED TO
THE DISAPPEARANCE OF A 15 DEGREE-LONG FILAMENT FROM N36E28.
CURRENTLY THERE ARE 13 SPOTTED REGIONS VISIBLE BUT ALL ARE
SIMPLE AND BENIGN. SURGING WAS OBSERVED AT BOTH LIMBS.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO
REMAIN MODERATE. AN ISOLATED M-CLASS FLARE, PERHAPS FROM
REGION 5476 (S29W04) OR 5478 (N28E23), MAY OCCUR ON A DAILY
BASIS.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 09/2100Z TO 10/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS MOSTLY QUIET.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE QUIET TO UNSETTLED AT MID-LATITUDES AND
UNSETTLED TO MILDLY ACTIVE AT HIGH LATITUDES.
