IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 05/2100Z
TO 06/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH. REGION 5776 (N17E13)
PRODUCED AN M9/2F FLARE AT 06/1350Z. THE FLARE WAS ACCOMPANIED
BY A STRONG TYPE II SWEEP AND A 310 SFU TENFLARE. THE REGION
ALSO PRODUCED TWO ADDITIONAL M-CLASS FLARES DURING THE PERIOD:
AN M3/1N AT 06/1214Z WHICH WAS ASSOCIATED WITH A MAJOR TYPE II
SWEEP AND A 360 SFU TENFLARE, AND AN M2/3B AT 06/2055Z WHICH
WAS ACCOMPANIED BY MINOR RADIO EMISSIONS. REGION 5769 (N24W34)
SLOWLY DECAYED THROUGH THE PERIOD. NEW REGIONS 5785 (N25W18) AND
5786 (N11E75) WERE NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE TO HIGH. REGION 5776 IS EXPECTED TO PRODUCE M-CLASS
FLARES. THIS REGION ALSO PROVIDES A SLIGHT CHANCE FOR AN
ISOLATED X-CLASS FLARE. REGION 5769 IS CAPABLE OF PRODUCING AN
ISOLATED M-CLASS FLARE.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 05/2100Z TO 06/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO UNSETTLED LEVELS
FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE AT MIDDLE LATITUDES AND
AT ACTIVE LEVELS AT HIGHER LATITUDES IN RESPONSE TO RECENT
ENERGETIC FLARE ACTIVITY. NO STORMING IS EXPECTED IN THE WAKE
OF TODAY'S M9/2N FLARE.
