IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 12/2100Z
TO 13/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY CONTINUED LOW. REGION 5836 (N12E67)
SHOWED ITSELF AS A MODERATE AREA CLASS E GROUP WITH SOME
MAGNETIC COMPLEXITY. THIS REGION PRODUCED SEVERAL CLASS C
SUBFLARES DURING THE PERIOD. ITS WIDTH ALSO INDICATES THIS IS
A MATURE REGION PROBABLY READY TO BEGIN ITS DECAY PHASE. NEW
REGION 5837 (N20E88) PRODUCED TWO SMALL C CLASS ENHANCEMENTS
WITH ACTIVE SURGING AND A SHORTLIVED LOOP PROMINENCE SYSTEM.
NO SPOTS WERE VISIBLE IN THIS REGION DURING THE PERIOD.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY SHOULD BECOME
MODERATE. REGION 5836 IS CAPABLE OF PRODUCING A CLASS M FLARE
EVERY 1-2 DAYS. NEW REGION 5837 MAY ALSO BE CAPABLE OF
CLASS M FLARES BUT A CONFIDENT FORECAST MUST AWAIT SUNSPOT
OBSERVATIONS.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 12/2100Z TO 13/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS QUIET TO UNSETTLED DURING THE PAST
24 HOURS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD
SHOULD BE PREDOMINANTLY UNSETTLED FOR THE NEXT THREE DAYS.
