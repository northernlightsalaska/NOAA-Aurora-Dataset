SDF NUMBER 235
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 23 AUG 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 22/2100Z
TO 23/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. AN M1 X-RAY FLARE
OCCURRED AT 0928Z. NO OPTICAL CORRELATION ASSOCIATED WITH THIS
EVENT. REGION 5643 (N16W82) LOOKS TO BE THE ONLY SPOT GROUP WITH
ANY REAL SPOT COMPLEXITY. REMAINDER OF DISK LOOKS QUIET AND
UNIMPRESSIVE. SAC PEAK REPORTS SOME YELLOW LINE EMISSION AT
SE15.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. REGION 5643 MAY PRODUCE AN ISOLATED M-CLASS
FLARE AS IT APPROACHES THE WEST LIMB.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 22/2100Z TO 23/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN MOSTLY QUIET TO UNSETTLED
AT MID LATITUDES WITH ONE PERIOD SHOWING ACTIVE CONDITIONS
FROM 03-06Z. HIGH LATITUDES DISPLAYED MOSTLY QUIET TO
UNSETTLED CONDITIONS WITH ONE PERIOD OF ACTIVE LEVELS
FROM 03-06Z DURING THE FIRST HALF OF THE REPORTING PERIOD.
THE REMAINDER OF THE DAY AT HIGH LATITUDES WAS UNSETTLED TO
ACTIVE WITH TWO PERIOD OF MINOR STORM CONDITIONS AT 15-18Z
AND 18-21Z. THE DISTURBANCE WAS PROBABLY CAUSED BY THE 19
AUGUST FILAMENT DISAPPEARANCE. PROTON EVENT AT GREATER THAN
10 MEV IS STILL IN PROGRESS. THIS EVENT STARTED ON 12 AUGUST
AND WAS AT 37 PFU AT 23/2100Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE ACTIVE AT MID LATITUDES WITH MINOR STORMING
OCCURRING AT HIGH LATITUDES TOMORROW. REST OF PERIOD SHOULD BE
AT UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE CONDITIONS.
