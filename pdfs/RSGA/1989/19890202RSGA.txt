FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 033
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 02 FEB 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 01/2100Z
TO 02/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 5334 (S21W22)
PRODUCED TODAY'S ONLY M-CLASS FLARE: AN M1/1N AT 1731Z. THE
REGION CONTINUES TO DOMINATE THE SOLAR DISK BUT IS SLOWLY
DECLINING AND SIMPLIFYING. TWO ADDITIONAL TINY NEW REGIONS
EMERGED ON THE DISK AND NEW REGION 5347 (N16E73) ROTATED INTO
VIEW.  SURGE ACTIVITY  CONTINUED ON  THE WEST  LIMB AT  N21
ASSOCIATED WITH THE EXIT OF REGION 5329 (N21W90).
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. REGION 5334 MAY PRODUCE ADDITIONAL ISOLATED
M-FLARES OVER THE NEXT FEW DAYS. OLD REGION 5312 (S33, L-300,
MAX CLASS/AREA FKC/1800 ON 15 JAN), WHICH PRODUCED 33 M-CLASS
FLARES AND 6 X-CLASS FLARES ON ITS PREVIOUS TRANSIT IS DUE TO
RETURN TOMORROW AND IS EXPECTED TO INCREASE ACTIVITY AND BACK-
GROUND LEVELS AT THAT TIME.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 01/2100Z TO 02/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS MOSTLY QUIET TO UNSETTLED, BUT
TWO PERIODS OF SUBSTORMING OCCURRED. THE FIRST ONE WAS FROM
2100-0300Z AND THE SECOND WAS FROM 1800-2100Z. DURING THE
SUBSTORMS MIDDLE LATITUDES WERE ACTIVE AND HIGH LATITUDES
ATTAINED MINOR STORM LEVELS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE MOSTLY UNSETTLED TOMORROW AND IS EXPECTED TO
BECOME ACTIVE ON 04-05 FEBRUARY WITH A SLIGHT CHANCE FOR
PERIODS OF STORM CONDITIONS. THIS FORECAST IS BASED ON THE
DISAPPEARANCE OF A LARGE SOLAR FILAMENT ON 01 FEBRUARY.
