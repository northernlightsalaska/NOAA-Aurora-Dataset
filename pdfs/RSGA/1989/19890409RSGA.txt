JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 09 APR 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 08/2100Z
TO 09/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH. REGION COMPLEX 5441 (N35
E08) PRODUCED AN X3/4B FLARE AT 09/0105Z WITH RADIO PARAMETERS
INCLUDING A TYPE IV SWEEP AND 10.7 CM BURST. ITS RAPID GROWTH
DURING THE PRIOR 36 HOURS WITH THE APPARENT EMERGENCE OF THREE
CLOSE GROUPS CULMINATED IN THE FLARE EVENT. WHITE LIGHT ASPECTS
AND ASSOCIATED MAGNETIC CONFIGURATION HAVE BOTH SIMPLIFIED SINCE
THE EVENT. THE OVERALL X-RAY BACKGROUD THRESHOLD HAS ALSO AGAIN
DROPPED TO RELATIVELY LOWER LEVELS.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 08/2100Z TO 09/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS ACTIVE TO UNSETTLED.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO MOSTLY ACTIVE. A DISTURBANCE IS
EXPECTED TO START MIDWAY THROUGH THE PERIOD IN RESPONSE TO THE
X3/4B FLARE TODAY.
