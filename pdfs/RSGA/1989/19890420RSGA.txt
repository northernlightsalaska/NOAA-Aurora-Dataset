FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 110
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 20 APR 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 19/2100Z
TO 20/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 5451 (N10W29)
PRODUCED TODAY'S ONLY M-CLASS FLARE: AN M1/1N AT 0526Z. THE
REGION APPEARS TO HAVE ENTERED A NEW GROWTH PHASE, PARTICULARLY
IN THE TRAILER SPOTS. THE BALANCE OF ACTIVITY CONSISTED OF SMALL
C-CLASS SUBFLARES, PRIMARILY FROM REGION 5451 BUT WITH CONTRI-
BUTIONS FROM 5449 (S21W60), 5457 (N28W51) AND NEW REGION 5459
(S11W24). THE REMAINDER OF THE SOLAR DISK WAS ESSENTIALLY STABLE
OR DECLINING.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW, BUT WITH A CONTINUED SLIGHT CHANCE FOR AN ISOLATED M-CLASS
FLARE FROM REGION 5451.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 19/2100Z TO 20/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS MOSTLY QUIET TO UNSETTLED. A PERIOD
OF ACTIVE CONDITIONS OCCURRED FROM 0300-0900Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE OVER THE NEXT THREE DAYS.
A GRADUAL INCREASE IS FORECAST DUE TO RECURRENT PATTERNS OF
ACTIVITY. THE CORONAL MASS EJECTION OF 18 APRIL MAY ALSO
CONTRIBUTE TO ACTIVITY TOMORROW.
