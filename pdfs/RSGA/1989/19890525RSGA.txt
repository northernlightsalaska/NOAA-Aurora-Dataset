JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 25 MAY 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 24/2100Z
TO 25/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 5495 (N23W88)
PRODUCED AN M2 XRAY BURST AT 24/2150Z ASSOCIATED WITH AN
ERUPTIVE PROMINENCE ON LIMB (EPL) TO .16 SOLAR RADII. MINOR
RADIO EMISSIONS ACCOMPANIED THE EVENT. REGION 5497 (S18W37)
PRODUCED AN M1/1B FLARE AT 25/1559Z ACCOMPANIED BY MINOR RADIO
EMISSIONS. THIS REGION MAY HAVE ENTERED A SLOW DECAY PHASE. NEW
REGION 5509 (S07W18) EMERGED AS A SMALL BIPOLE.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE. REGION 5497 IS LIKELY TO PRODUCE AN M-CLASS FLARE
DURING THE PERIOD. THIS REGION IS ALSO CAPABLE OF PRODUCING AN
ISOLATED MAJOR FLARE. REGION 5495 MAY ALSO PRODUCE AN ISOLATED
M-CLASS FLARE AS IT CONTINUES ITS WEST LIMB TRANSIT.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 24/2100Z TO 25/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT UNSETTLED TO MINOR STORM
LEVELS. BRIEF PERIODS OF MAJOR STORMING OCCURRED AT HIGH
LATITUDES. THIS ACTIVITY MAY HAVE BEEN DUE TO THE EFFECTS OF
A CORONAL HOLE LOCATED NEAR N24W44. THE SATELLITE PROTON
EVENT AT GREATER THAN 10 MEV WHICH BEGAN AT 24/0730Z ENDED
AT 25/1005Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE ACTIVE THROUGHOUT THE PERIOD.
