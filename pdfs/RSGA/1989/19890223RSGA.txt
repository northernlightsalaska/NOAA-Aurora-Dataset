JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.

IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 22/2100Z
TO 23/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 5368 (S11W52)
DECAYED AND SIMPLIFIED DURING THE PAST 24 HOURS BUT DID PRODUCE
ONE M-CLASS FLARE, AN M1/0F AT 0500Z. ALTHOUGH MUCH LESS
IMPRESSIVE THAN YESTERDAY, THAT REGION IS STILL THE MOST
PRODUCTIVE ON THE DISK. THERE ARE AT LEAST THREE SMALL BIPOLAR
REGIONS COMING ONTO THE DISK NEAR EAST LIMB BUT AT THIS TIME
ALL SEEM QUITE SIMPLE.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. REGION 5368 IS STILL CAPABLE OF INTERMITTENT
M-CLASS ACTIVITY.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 22/2100Z TO 23/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN QUIET AT MID-LATITUDES. HIGH
LATITUDES EXPERIENCED QUIET TO UNSETTLED CONDITIONS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED THROUGHOUT THE PERIOD.
