SDF NUMBER 253
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 10 SEP 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 09/2100Z
TO 10/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 5669 (S18W71)
PRODUCED AN M2/0N AT 0802Z AND AN M1/2F AT 0545Z. LIMB
PROXIMITY IS BEGINNING TO HINDER ANALYSIS OF THE REGION BUT IT
SEEMS TO BE CONTINUING ITS SLOW DECAY. OTHER M-LEVEL ACTIVITY
CONSISTED OF AN M1/0N AT 0140Z FROM REGION 5687 (N16E38) AND
AN M1/0N AT 0632Z FROM REGION 5676 (N27W15). FREQUENT SUB-
FLARE ACTIVITY PERSISTED THROUGHOUT THE INTERVAL WITH THE
BRUNT OF IT FROM REGIONS IN THE COMPLICATED NORTHEAST QUADRANT.
NEWLY NUMBERED REGIONS ARE 5691 (S15W58) AND 5692 (S22E64).
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE TO HIGH. REGIONS 5669 AND 5680 (N15E15) ARE THE MOST
OMINOUS AND ARE LIKELY SITES FOR M OR X-LEVEL FLARES. THERE
ARE AT LEAST FIVE OTHER REGIONS VISIBLE WITH THE POTENTIAL FOR
AN ISOLATED M-CLASS FLARE.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 09/2100Z TO 10/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS MOSTLY UNSETTLED.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE TO START AND END THE
FORECAST PERIOD. A DISTURBANCE IS ANTICIPATED MIDWAY THROUGH
THE INTERVAL DUE TO FLARE AND DISAPPEARING FILAMENT ACTIVITY
09 SEPT.
