FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 334
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 30 NOV 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 29/2100Z
TO 30/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH. REGION 5800 (N26W59) PRO-
DUCED AN X2/3B FLARE AT 1229Z WHICH WAS ASSOCIATED WITH FLARE
LOOPS AND MODERATE DISCRETE RADIO BURSTS. THE EVENT WAS OF LONG
DURATION: THE TIME FROM START TO HALF POWER WAS 68 MINUTES AND
X-RAY EMISSION WAS ABOVE THE M-LEVEL FROM ABOUT 12-16Z. THE RE-
MAINDER OF ACTIVITY CONSISTED OF A FEW LOW LEVEL C-CLASS SUB-
FLARES.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MOSTLY MODERATE, BUT WITH A CHANCE FOR HIGH LEVELS. THE PRIN-
CIPAL THREATS FOR MAJOR ACTIVITY APPEAR TO BE 5800 (PERSIS-
TENCE), AND REGION 5806 (N18W18) WHICH HAS RECENTLY DEVELOPED
A DELTA CONFIGURATION. REGION 5811 (S16E41), A RELATIVELY SMALL
GROUP, APPEARED TO BE DEVELOPING A DELTA DURING THE PERIOD AND
MIGHT ALSO CONTRIBUTE TO MODERATE LEVELS OF ACTIVITY.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 29/2100Z TO 30/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS GENERALLY UNSETTLED AT MIDDLE LAT-
ITUDES. HIGH LATITUDES WERE AT MOSTLY ACTIVE LEVELS WITH OC-
CASIONAL MINOR STORM PERIODS. A GREATER THAN 10 MEV PROTON
EVENT BEGAN AT 30/1345Z IN RESPONSE TO TODAY'S X3/3B FLARE.
THE HIGHEST FLUX AT GREATER THAN 10MEV SO FAR WAS 570 PFU
AT 30/2100Z, BUT THE LEVELS WERE STILL CLIMBING AT THE END
OF THE PERIOD. A PCA EVENT BEGAN AT 1825Z AND CONTINUES IN
PROGRESS. THE FORBUSH DECREASE WHICH BEGAN AT 28/10Z APPEARED
TO END AROUND 30/06Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE MOSTLY UNSETTLED FOR THE NEXT TWO DAYS. ACTIVE
CONDITIONS ARE EXPECTED ON THE THIRD DAY IN RESPONSE TO TODAY'S
X2/3B FLARE.
