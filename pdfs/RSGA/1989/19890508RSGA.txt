JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 08 MAY 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 07/2100Z
TO 08/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 5464 (S19W89)
PRODUCED AN M2/1B FLARE AT 7/2119Z AND HAS BEEN THE SOURCE OF
SURGES AND WEAK SPRAYS SINCE. REGION 5470 (N30W37) ALSO
GENERATED AN M-CLASS EVENT: AN M1/1F AT 0146Z. A THIRD M-LEVEL
FLARE (M1 AT 0230Z) OCCURRED AND MAY BE ASSOCIATED WITH A
SECOND MAXIMUM FROM THE EARLIER EVENT IN 5470. A NUMBER OF
SMALLER SUBFLARES WERE DISTRIBUTED AMONG THE TWELVE SPOT GROUPS
NOW VISIBLE. ONE NEW REGION, 5482 (S26E42), WAS NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS LIKELY TO
PERSIST AT THE MODERATE LEVEL. M-LEVEL ACTIVITY MAY OCCUR IN
REGIONS 5464, 5470, OR 5476 (S30E25).
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 07/2100Z TO 08/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN QUIET TO UNSETTLED SINCE EARLY
IN THE INTERVAL. YESTERDAY'S MINOR STORM WAS SHORT-LIVED AND
ENDED ABRUPTLY AT 2400Z ON 7 MAY. THE 10 MEV PROTON EVENT
ALSO ENDED DURING THE PERIOD, WITH THE END TIME 0945Z TODAY.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED THE NEXT THREE DAYS.
