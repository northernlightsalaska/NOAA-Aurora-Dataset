SDF NUMBER 027
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 27 JAN 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 26/2100Z
TO 27/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH. REGION 5330 (S17E14)
PRODUCED AN X1/2B TENFLARE AT 1917Z AND AN M1/1B FLARE AT 0335Z.
MODEST DISCRETE FREQUENCY RADIO BURSTS OCCURRED WITH BOTH
FLARES. REGION 5329 (N17W19) YIELDED AN M1/1B AT 1525Z, AND
JOINED WITH REGION 5334 (S20E56) TO GENERATE AN M3 TENFLARE
VERY EARLY IN THE PERIOD AT 26/2356Z. ALL THREE OF THOSE REGIONS
WERE RESTRUCTURING DURING THE INTERVAL.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE TO HIGH. CONTINUED M-CLASS AND ISOLATED X-CLASS
ACTIVITY MAY OCCUR IN REGIONS 5330, 5329, AND TO A LESSER
DEGREE, 5334.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 26/2100Z TO 27/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS GENERALLY UNSETTLED.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE QUIET TO UNSETTLED THE NEXT THREE DAYS.
ELEVATED LEVELS OF GEOMAGNETIC ACTIVITY ARE NOT ANTICIPATED
TO RESULT FROM TODAY'S FLARES.
