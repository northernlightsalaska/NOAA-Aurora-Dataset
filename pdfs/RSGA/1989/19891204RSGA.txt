SDF NUMBER 338
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 04 DEC 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 03/2100Z
TO 04/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE.  REGION 5806 (N17W71)
PRODUCED TWO LOW LEVEL M-CLASS EVENTS DURING THE PAST DAY, AN
M1/1N AT 04/0328Z AND AN M1/1N AT 04/1948Z.  AN OPTICALLY
UNCORRELATED LONG DURATION EVENT REACHED A MAXIMUM OF M2 AT
04/1453Z.  NEW REGIONS 5826 (N16E27) AND 5827 (N13E78) WERE
NUMBERED.  REGION 5827 IS NEAR THE LOCATION OF OLD REGION
5786 (N13,L-254).  THERE APPEARS TO BE MORE OF REGION 5827,
OR POSSIBLY A SEPARATE REGION, STILL BEYOND THE EAST LIMB.
THIS AREA MAY HAVE BEEN RESPONSIBLE FOR THE LDE ABOVE.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE.  REGION 5806 IS EXPECTED TO PRODUCE ADDITIONAL M-CLASS
FLARES.  ACTIVITY LEVELS MAY INCREASE DUE TO THE NEW ACTIVE
REGION AT THE NORTHEAST LIMB.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 03/2100Z TO 04/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD RANGED FROM UNSETTLED TO MINOR STORM
LEVELS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE ACTIVE TO UNSETTLED.
