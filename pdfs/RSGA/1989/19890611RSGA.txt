IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 10/2100Z
TO 11/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE.  REGION 5533 (S19E55)
PRODUCED THE ONLY M-CLASS FLARE DURING THE PAST 24 HOURS, AN
M1/SF AT 11/0915Z.  ALTHOUGH ITS ACTIVITY LEVEL HAS DECREASED,
THIS REGION APPEARS LITTLE CHANGED IN SUNSPOT AND H-ALPHA
COMPLEXITY.  REGION 5528 (N20E38) REMAINS A LARGE, COMPLEX
SUNSPOT GROUP BUT CONTINUES TO GENERATE ONLY INFREQUENT
SUBFLARE ACTIVITY.  REGION 5521 (S18W29) CONTINUES TO DECAY.
NEW REGION 5535 (N30E80) WAS NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE TO HIGH.  ENERGETIC FLARES ARE POSSIBLE FROM REGIONS
5521, 5528, AND 5533.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 10/2100Z TO 11/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE.  YESTERDAY'S
DISTURBANCE (MINOR STORM AT MIDDLE LATITUDES AND MAJOR STORM
AT HIGH LATITUDES) ENDED AT BOULDER BY 10/2100Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE MOSTLY UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE.
