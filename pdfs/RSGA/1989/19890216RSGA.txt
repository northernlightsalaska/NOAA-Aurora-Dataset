IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 15/2100Z
TO 16/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 5362 (N20E12)
PRODUCED AN M2/2N FLARE WITH MINOR RADIO BURSTS EARLY IN THE
PERIOD. IT HAS BEEN RELATIVELY QUIET SINCE. REGION 5354 (N31W72)
REMAINS THE LARGEST GROUP ON THE DISK AND ALSO PRODUCED A SMALL
M-CLASS EVENT. REGION 5368 (S12E45) GREW SUDDENLY AND ALSO HAD
A SMALL M-CLASS X-RAY EVENT WITH A 1B OPTICAL CLASS.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE. REGION 5354, AND TO LESSER DEGREES REGION 5362 AND
REGION 5368 PROVIDE A VERY SLIGHT CHANCE OF MAJOR ACTIVITY.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 15/2100Z TO 16/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS ACTIVE TO UNSETTLED. MINOR STORM
CONDITIONS OCCURRED AT HIGH LATITUDES EARLY IN THE PERIOD.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE GENERALLY UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE.
