IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 04/2100Z
TO 05/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH.  REGION 5806 (N18W82)
PRODUCED THE LARGEST FLARE OF THE PAST DAY, AN M5/1B AT
05/1535Z.  THIS FLARE DECAYED QUICKLY AND HAD LITTLE OUTPUT
AT RADIO WAVELENGTHS.  THIS REGION CONTINUES TO DECAY AS IT
APPROACHES THE WEST LIMB.  NEW REGION 5828 (S17E72) WAS
NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE.  FLARE OCCURRENCE IS EXPECTED TO DECREASE
OVER THE NEXT FEW DAYS AS REGION 5806 DEPARTS THE DISK.

THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS ACTIVE TO QUIET.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE MOSTLY UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE.  THE MOST DISTURBED
CONDITIONS ARE EXPECTED ON 06 AND 07 DECEMBER DUE TO A CORONAL
HOLE.
