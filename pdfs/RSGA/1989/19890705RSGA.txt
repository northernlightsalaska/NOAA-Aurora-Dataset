JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 05 JUL 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 04/2100Z
TO 05/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE.  REGION 5575 (N24E27)
PRODUCED THE ONLY M-LEVEL FLARE DURING THE PAST DAY - AN M1/1N
AT 05/1648Z.  THIS REGION ALSO GENERATED SEVERAL C-CLASS FLARES.
NEW REGIONS NUMBERED TODAY ARE 5581 (N17E68) AND 5582 (N25E73).
REGION 5582 IS BELIEVED TO BE THE RETURN OF OLD REGION 5528.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERTE. M-CLASS FLARES ARE POSSIBLE FROM REGIONS 5575
AND 5582.  OLD REGION 5533 (S19, L-071) MAY INCREASE LEVELS
AT ITS RETURN ON 07 JULY.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 04/2100Z TO 05/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS MOSTLY QUIET UNTIL 05/1500Z AND
UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE THEREAFTER.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE FOR THE FIRST DAY OF THE
FORECAST PERIOD AND MOSTLY UNSETTLED FOR THE REMAINING TWO DAYS.
