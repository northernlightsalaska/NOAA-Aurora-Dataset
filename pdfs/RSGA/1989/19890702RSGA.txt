FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 183
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 02 JUL 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 01/2100Z
TO 02/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. FLARE PRODUCTION WAS
LIMITED TO SMALL, C-CLASS SUBFLARES. MOST OF THESE WERE FROM
REGIONS 5569 (N22W42) AND 5575 (N23E65). NEW REGION 5576
(N09E69) ROTATED INTO VIEW LATE IN THE DAY WITH OCCASIONAL
SUBFLARES AND SURGE ACTIVITY. A PROMINENCE WAS VISIBLE ON THE
EAST LIMB FROM N21 TO N41. THE REMAINDER OF THE DISK WAS
ESSENTIALLY STABLE AND QUIET.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. THE PRIMARY THREAT FOR MODERATE LEVELS
CONTINUES TO BE REGION 5569.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 01/2100Z TO 02/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS AT ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM LEVELS
AT THE BEGINNING OF THE PERIOD BUT DECLINED TO QUIET TO
UNSETTLED LEVELS AROUND 02/0300Z. THE > 10MEV PARTICLE
LEVELS WERE ENHANCED ABOVE BACKGROUND FROM THE BEGINNING
OF THE PERIOD THROUGH ABOUT 0400Z BUT DID NOT ATTAIN EVENT
LEVELS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE TOMORROW AND MOSTLY ACTIVE
FOR THE SECOND AND THIRD DAYS. THE INCREASE IS EXPECTED IN
RESPONSE TO A HIGH SPEED SOLAR WIND STREAM ORIGINATING FROM
A FAVORABLY POSITIONED CORONAL HOLE.
