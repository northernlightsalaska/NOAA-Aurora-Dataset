IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 24/2100Z
TO 25/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 5329 (N18E08)
AND 5330 (S17E40) RETAINED THEIR COMPLICATED MAGNETIC CONFIGURA-
TION, AND BOTH PRODUCED ONE VERY SMALL M-CLASS FLARE. THE LARGER
ONE, FROM REGION 5330, HAD AN ASSOCIATED TENFLARE RADIO BURST.
NO SIGNIFICANT RADIO SWEEPS ACCOMPANIED THE EVENTS. NEW REGION
5334 (S20E79) ROTATED ONTO THE DISK WITH MANY C-CLASS SUBFLARES.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE. BOTH REGION 5329 AND 5330 PROVIDE A VERY SLIGHT CHANCE
OF HIGH AND PROTON PRODUCING FLARE ACTIVITY.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 24/2100Z TO 25/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS UNSETTLED.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED.
