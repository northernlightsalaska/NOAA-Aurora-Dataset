FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 167
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 16 JUN 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 15/2100Z
TO 16/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH. TWO MAJOR FLARES OCCURRED
DURING THE PERIOD. THE FIRST WAS AN M6/SN FROM REGION 5528
(N21W30) AT 16/0525Z WHICH WAS ACCOMPANIED BY MINOR RADIO
EMISSIONS. THE SECOND WAS AN X3/2B AT 16/0742Z FROM REGION 5533
(S20W08) WHICH WAS ASSOCIATED WITH A MAJOR TYPE II SWEEP. IN
ADDITION, SEVERAL M-CLASS FLARES WERE RECORDED. THE LARGEST OF
THESE WAS AN M4/1B FLARE AT 16/1906Z FROM REGION 5533 WHICH WAS
ACCOMPANIED BY MINOR RADIO EMISSIONS. THE OTHER M-CLASS FLARES
OCCURRED AT 15/2222Z, 16/0454Z, 16/1206Z, 16/1227Z, AND
16/1700Z. TWO NEW REGIONS WERE ASSIGNED TODAY: 5547 (N09W68)
AND 5548 (S22W66), BOTH OF WHICH WERE SMALL UNIPOLES.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE TO HIGH. M-CLASS FLARING IS LIKELY FROM REGIONS 5528
AND 5533. BOTH REGIONS HAVE THE POTENTIAL FOR PRODUCING
ISOLATED MAJOR FLARE ACTIVITY.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 15/2100Z TO 16/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT MOSTLY UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS. BRIEF PERIODS OF MINOR STORMING
OCCURRED AT HIGH LATITUDES.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT MINOR TO MAJOR STORM LEVELS IN RESPONSE TO
RECENT MAJOR FLARE ACTIVITY.
