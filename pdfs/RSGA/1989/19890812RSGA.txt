FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 224
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 12 AUG 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 11/2100Z
TO 12/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH. REGION 5629 (S16W44)
PRODUCED A POWERFUL X2/2B AT 12/1427Z. STRONG TYPE II, MODERATE
TYPE IV, AND STRONG CENTIMETER BURSTS ACCOMPANIED THIS LONG
DURATION FLARE. REGION 5629 ALSO PRODUCED AN M5/1B AT 11/2302Z,
AN M1/SF AT 12/0307Z, AND AN M2/1N AT 12/0802Z. NO SIGNIFICANT
RADIO BURSTS ACCOMPANIED THESE LESSER FLARES. REGION 5629
MAINTAINS ITS LARGE SUNSPOT AREA AND MIXED MAGNETIC POLARITIES.
REGION 5643 (N15E62) IS A MODERATE SIZE CLASS E GROUP WHICH NOW
APPEARS AS BIPOLAR. NEW REGION 5644 (N22E82) IS ROTATING OVER
THE EAST LIMB.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY SHOULD BE AT A
GENERALLY MODERATE LEVEL. REGION 5629 REMAINS A THREAT TO
PRODUCE ANOTHER CLASS X FLARE OVER THE NEXT SEVERAL DAYS.
INFREQUENT CLASS M FLARES CAN BE EXPECTED FROM REGION 5643.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 11/2100Z TO 12/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS UNSETTLED. A SATELLITE PROTON
EVENT BEGAN AT 12/1600Z. A MAXIMUM FLUX OF 800 P.F.U. WAS
OBSERVED AT THE END OF THE PERIOD AND LEVELS WERE STILL
CLIMBING AT THAT TIME. A POLAR CAP ABSORPTION EVENT BEGAN
AT 12/1800Z AND A MAXIMUM ABSORPTION OF 8DB WAS OBSERVED,
ALSO AT THE END OF THE REPORTING PERIOD.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD
SHOULD BE UNSETTLED UNTIL THE LATTER HALF OF 13 AUG WHEN
STORM CONDITIONS ARE EXPECTED TO BEGIN AS A RESULT OF THE
FLARE ACTIVITY ON 11 AND 12 AUG. MAJOR STORM CONDITIONS ARE
FORECAST FOR 14 AUG, RELENTING SOMEWHAT TO MINOR STORM
LEVELS ON 15 AUG. STORMING ON 14-15 AUG IS FORECAST IN
RESPONSE TO THE X2 FLARE ON 12 AUGUST. THE SATELLITE PROTON
EVENT AND PCA ARE EXPECTED TO CONTINUE DURING 13 AUG.
