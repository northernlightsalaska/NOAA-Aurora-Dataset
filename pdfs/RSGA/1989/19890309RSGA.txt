FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 068
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 09 MAR 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 08/2100Z
TO 09/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH. REGION 5395 (N34E32)
PRODUCED AN X4/4B FLARE AT 09/1538Z. MINOR RADIO EMISSIONS
ACCOMPANIED THE EVENT. THIS REGION ALSO PRODUCED SEVERAL M-CLASS
EVENTS INCLUDING AN M2/1N AT 09/1345Z AND AN M1/1B AT 09/1928Z,
NEITHER OF WHICH WERE ACCOMPANIED BY SIGNIFICANT RADIO
EMISSIONS. THERE WAS AN OPTICALLY UNCORRELATED M7 X-RAY BURST AT
09/1011Z WHICH WAS ASSOCIATED WITH A STRONG TYPE IV RADIO SWEEP.
NEW REGION 5399 (N10W01) WAS ASSIGNED TODAY.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
HIGH. REGION 5395 REMAINS CAPABLE OF PRODUCING MAJOR FLARE
ACTIVITY.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 08/2100Z TO 09/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS. MOST OF THE MINOR STORMING
OCCURRED AT HIGH LATITUDES. THIS ACTIVITY WAS IN RESPONSE
TO THE X15/3B FLARE OF 6 MARCH. THE SATELLITE PROTON EVENT
AT GREATER THAN 10 MEV WHICH BEGAN AT 08/1735Z REMAINS IN
PROGRESS. THE LARGEST FLUX OBSERVED THUS FAR WAS 220 PFU
AT 18/2040Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM LEVELS IN RESPONSE TO
RECENT MAJOR FLARE ACTIVITY. THE SATELLITE PROTON EVENT AT
GREATER THAN 10 MEV IS EXPECTED TO CONTINUE THROUGH THE
PERIOD.
