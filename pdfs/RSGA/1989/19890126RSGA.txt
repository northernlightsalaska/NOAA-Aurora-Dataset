IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 25/2100Z
TO 26/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 5329 (N18W07)
GENERATED TWO M-CLASS FLARES: AN M2/2N AT 0438Z AND AN M1/0N AT
1413Z. THE REGION REMAINS IMPRESSIVE IN BOTH WHITE LIGHT AND
H-ALPHA AND IS COMPLEX MAGNETICALLY. THE OTHER LARGE GROUP NEAR
DISK CENTER, 5330 (S17E26), PRODUCED BUT A FEW SMALL SUBFLARES
AS IT SIMPLIFIED AND ELONGATED. MINOR SURGING WAS NOTED NEAR S18
AND N14 AT EAST LIMB.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO
PERSIST AT THE MODERATE LEVEL. OCCASIONAL M-CLASS FLARES ARE
LIKELY FROM REGION 5329.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 25/2100Z TO 26/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO UNSETTLED
LEVELS AT MID-LATITUDES. INTERVALS OF ACTIVE CONDITIONS WERE
OBSERVED AT HIGH LATITUDE SITES.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE PREDOMINANTLY UNSETTLED THE NEXT THREE DAYS.
