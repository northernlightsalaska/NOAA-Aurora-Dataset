IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 02/2100Z
TO 03/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. NEW REGION 5440
(N34E69), WHICH IS THE RETURN OF OLD 5395, PRODUCED AN M1/SF
FLARE AT 02/2330Z. ACTIVE SURGING AND A SPRAY TO 0.17RV WERE
OBSERVED FROM THIS REGION DURING THE PERIOD. REGION 5428
(S19E14) REMAINED LARGE AND MAGNETICALLY COMPLEX AND PRODUCED
SEVERAL SMALL FLARES DURING THE PERIOD. FOUR NEW REGIONS WERE
ASSIGNED TODAY: 5437 (N38W56), 5438 (S15E71), 5439 (S12E29),
AND 5440.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE. M-CLASS ACTIVITY MAY OCCUR FROM REGIONS 5428 AND
5440. THERE IS A VERY SLIGHT CHANCE FOR AN X-CLASS FLARE FROM
THESE REGIONS.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 02/2100Z TO 03/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT MOSTLY UNSETTLED LEVELS FOR
THE PAST 24 HOURS. PERIODS OF ACTIVE LEVELS WERE OBSERVED AT
HIGH LATITUDES LATE IN THE PERIOD.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED AT MIDDLE LATITUDES AND ACTIVE AT
HIGHER LATITUDES.
