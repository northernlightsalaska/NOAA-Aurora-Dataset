FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 230
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 18 AUG 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 17/2100Z
TO 18/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. REGION 5643 (N16W15)
PRODUCED MOST OF THE FEW SUBFLARES REPORTED. TWO NEW REGIONS
WERE NUMBERED, ONE IN THE SOUTHEAST, ONE IN THE NORTHEAST.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. REGION 5643, LARGE AND SOMEWHAT COMPLEX, HAS
BEEN VERY STABLE. IT SEEMS CAPABLE OF M-CLASS ACTIVITY BUT
NEEDS A TRIGGER. REGION 5645 (N28E10) SIMPLIFIED DURING THE
DAY BUT ALSO MAY YIELD AN ISOLATED LOW-LEVEL CLASS M FLARE.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 17/2100Z TO 18/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN MOSTLY ACTIVE WITH EPISODES
OF MINOR STORM CONDITIONS. THE POLAR CAP ABSORPTION AND
SATELLITE PROTON EVENTS ARE STILL IN PROGRESS BUT DECLINING.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE ACTIVE TO UNSETTLED AT MID-LATITUDES. HIGH
LATITUDES ARE LIKELY TO BE RELATIVELY MORE DISTURBED.
