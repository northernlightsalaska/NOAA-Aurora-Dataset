FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 091
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 01 APR 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 31/2100Z
TO 01/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. SEVERAL SUBFLARES OCCURRED
DURING THE PERIOD INCLUDING ONE C-CLASS X-RAY BURST. REGION 5428
(S20E40) DISPLAYED GRADUAL DEVELOPMENT THROUGH THE PERIOD. THIS
REGION REMAINED LARGE AND COMPLEX, BUT WAS RELATIVELY QUIET.
ENHANCED YELLOW-LINE (CALCIUM XV) EMISSION WAS OBSERVED FROM
THE NORTHEAST LIMB, POSSIBLY HERALDING THE RETURN OF OLD REGION
5395 WHICH IS EXPECTED TO TRANSIT THE EAST LIMB TOMORROW. REGION
5433 (N12E58) EMERGED AS A SMALL UNIPOLE. NEW REGION 5434
(S23E71) ROTATED INTO VIEW AS A SMALL BIPOLE.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. ISOLATED M-CLASS FLARING MAY OCCUR FROM WITHIN
REGION 5428. THE RETURN OF OLD 5395 (N34, L-257 CLASS/AREA
FKC/3600 ON 17 MAR) IS EXPECTED TO USHER IN A PERIOD OF
STEADILY INCREASING ACTIVITY.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 31/2100Z TO 01/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS. INTERVALS OF HIGH LATITUDE MAJOR
STORMING OCCURRED DURING THE FIRST HALF OF THE PERIOD.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM LEVELS TOMORROW DUE TO
THE EFFECTS OF AN EQUATORIAL CORONAL HOLE PRESENTLY LOCATED
NEAR W43. THE REMAINDER OF THE PERIOD IS EXPECTED TO BE MOSTLY
ACTIVE.
