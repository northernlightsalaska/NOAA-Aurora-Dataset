JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 18 JUN 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 17/2100Z
TO 18/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 5528 (N20W54)
PRODUCED THE ONLY M-CLASS EVENT OF THE PERIOD: AN M1/SN AT
18/1905Z THAT WAS ACCOMPANIED BY MINOR RADIO EMISSIONS. THIS
REGION HAS BEEN IN A STATE OF GRADUAL DECAY, BUT REMAINS A LARGE
AND VERY COMPLEX REGION. REGION 5533 (S19W32) WAS RELATIVELY
QUIET AND CONTINUED TO DECAY. A MAJOR TYPE II SWEEP OCCURRED AT
18/1451Z ASSOCIATED WITH A SUBFAINT FLARE FROM REGION 5534
(N17W24). TWO NEW REGIONS EMERGED: 5550 (S21E03) AND 5551
(S12E41). BOTH WERE SMALL BIPOLES.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE TO HIGH. M-CLASS FLARING IS LIKELY FROM REGIONS 5528
AND 5533. REGION 5528 PROVIDES A FAIR CHANCE FOR A MAJOR FLARE
DURING THE PERIOD.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 17/2100Z TO 18/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT MOSTLY QUIET LEVELS FOR
THE PAST 24 HOURS. A SATELLITE PROTON EVENT AT GREATER THAN
10 MEV BEGAN AT 18/1650Z. MAXIMUM FLUX OBSERVED THUS FAR WAS
18 PFU AT 18/1910Z. THIS PROTON EVENT PROBABLY ORIGINATED
FROM YESTERDAY'S M8/2B FLARE.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT MINOR TO MAJOR STORM LEVELS TOMORROW
SUBSIDING TO MINOR STORM TO ACTIVE LEVELS FOR THE REMAINDER OF
THE PERIOD. THE STORMING IS EXPECTED IN RESPONSE TO THE MAJOR
FLARE THAT OCCURRED AT 17/1614Z. THE SATELLITE PROTON EVENT
MENTIONED ABOVE IS EXPECTED SLOWLY DECLINE DURING THE PERIOD.
