JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 24 AUG 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 23/2100Z
TO 24/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW.  ONLY C-CLASS FLARES
HAVE OCCURRED SINCE YESTERDAY, MOST OF WHICH ORIGINATED IN
REGION 5643 (N16W93).  FOUR NEW REGIONS WERE NUMBERED TODAY -
5660 (N14W04), 5661 (N14E54), 5662 (S18E67), AND 5663 (N19E09).
REGION 5662 APPEARS TO BE RETURNING REMNANTS OF OLD REGION
5612 (S17, L-165).  THIS REGION PRODUCED M-CLASS ACTIVITY
DURING ITS LAST DISK PASSAGE.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE.  C-CLASS ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED.  AN ISOLATED
M-CLASS FLARE IS POSSIBLE.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 23/2100Z TO 24/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS QUIET TO UNSETTLED.  THE PROTON
EVENT THAT STARTED ON 12 AUGUST REMAINS IN PROGRESS.  THE
FLUX AT GREATER THAN 10 MEV WAS 13 PFU AT 24/2100Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE QUIET TO UNSETTLED FOR THE NEXT THREE DAYS.
