JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 13 OCT 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 12/2100Z
TO 13/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE.  THE LARGEST EVENT
OF THE PAST 24 HOURS WAS A LONG DURATION M4 X-RAY FLARE WHICH
REACHED A MAXIMUM AT 13/0612Z.  THIS FLARE OCCURRED MAINLY TO
THE EAST OF REGION 5744 (S23E62), IN ASSOCIATION WITH ADDITIONAL
SUNSPOTS ROTATING INTO VIEW NEAR THIS LATITUDE.  THESE MAY
BE TRAILER SPOTS TO REGION 5744 OR POSSIBLY A NEW SEPARATE
REGION.  CONSEQUENTLY, THIS AREA MAY RECEIVE A NEW NUMBER
AFTER ALL OF THE SUNSPOTS ARE IN VIEW.  ANOTHER FLARE OF NOTE
WAS A M1/2N AT 13/0000Z IN NEW REGION 5745 (N18W46).  THIS IS A
SPOTLESS PLAGE AREA WHICH APPARENTLY PRODUCED THIS M-FLARE
WITHOUT AN ASSOCIATED FILAMENT ERUPTION, A FAIRLY UNUSUAL
OCCURRENCE.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE TO HIGH.  ADDITIONAL ENERGETIC ACTIVITY, INCLUDING
A POSSIBLE MAJOR FLARE, IS EXPECTED FROM THE SOUTHEAST LIMB.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 12/2100Z TO 13/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS QUIET.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE QUIET TO UNSETTLED.
