FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 161
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 10 JUN 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 09/2100Z
TO 10/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH.  SEVERAL M-CLASS FLARES
WERE OBSERVED DURING THE PAST DAY.  THE LARGEST FLARES OCCURRED
IN REGION 5533 (S17E68) - AN M6/SF AT 09/2254Z AND AN M5/2N AT
10/0625Z.  THIS REGION CONTINUES TO ROTATE INTO VIEW AND IS
PRESENTLY SEEN AS A MODERATELY LARGE E-TYPE SUNSPOT GROUP.
REGIONS 5521 (S21W17) AND 5528 (N20E45) ALSO PRODUCED M-CLASS
ACTIVITY.  REGION 5528 IS THE LARGEST SUNSPOT GROUP ON THE
DISK AS A LARGE F-TYPE GROUP.  REGION 5521 IS DECAYING.  NEW
REGION 5534 (N12E75) WAS NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
HIGH.  ADDITIONAL ENERGETIC FLARES ARE LIKELY FROM REGIONS
5521, 5528, AND 5533.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 09/2100Z TO 10/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS AT STORM LEVELS FOR MOST OF THE PAST
DAY.  A MINOR GEOMAGNETIC STORM BEGAN AT MIDDLE LATITUDES AS
A GRADUAL ONSET AT ABOUT 10/0215Z.  THE HIGH LATITUDE FIELD
HAS BEEN AT MAJOR STORM LEVELS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT MINOR STORM TO ACTIVE LEVELS FOR THE NEXT
24 HOURS.  THE FIELD IS EXPECTED TO RETURN TO ACTIVE TO
UNSETTLED CONDITIONS BY THE THIRD DAY OF THE FORECAST AS THE
PRESENT DISTURBANCE ENDS.
