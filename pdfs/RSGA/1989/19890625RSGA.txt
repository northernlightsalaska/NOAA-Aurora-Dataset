FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 176
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 25 JUN 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 24/2100Z
TO 25/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 5555 (N26E03)
PRODUCED THE DAY'S ONLY M-CLASS FLARE, AN M1/0N AT 0352Z. THE
REGION IS STILL IMPRESSIVE IN APPEARANCE ALTHOUGH THE TRAILER
SPOTS HAVE DECAYED. OTHER REGIONS OF NOTE ARE REGION 5561
(S18E18) WHERE A WEAK DELTA IS PRESENT, AND 5563 (S16E33),
THE SITE OF STRONG ARCH FILAMENTS AND A MODERATE DEGREE OF
MAGNETIC COMPLEXITY. LIMB ACTIVITY HAS BEEN MINIMAL. ONE NEW
REGION WAS BORN NEAR CENTER DISK, 5567 (N16W07).
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. AN ISOLATED M-CLASS FLARE MAY OCCUR IN ANY
OF THE MATURE REGIONS CITED IN PART IA.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 24/2100Z TO 25/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS QUIET TO UNSETTLED.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD
SHOULD BE MOSTLY UNSETTLED THROUGHOUT THE PERIOD. THE EFFECTS
OF A HIGH LATITUDE CORONAL HOLE ARE POSSIBLE EARLY ON BUT
UNLIKELY.
