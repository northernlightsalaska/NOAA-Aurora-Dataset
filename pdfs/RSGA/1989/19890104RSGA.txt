FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 004
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 04 JAN 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 03/2100Z
TO 04/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY REMAINS AT A MODERATE LEVEL. REGION
5303 (S18W60) PRODUCED MOST OF THE DAY'S FLARES WITH ITS LARGEST
A LONG-DURATION M4/1N TENFLARE AT 1805Z. RADIO BURSTS OF
MODERATE SIZE WERE RECORDED ACROSS THE SPECTRUM IN CONJUNCTION
WITH THE EVENT. THE REGION POSSESSES A SLIGHT DEGREE OF
MAGNETIC COMPLEXITY. OTHER DISK REGIONS WERE STABLE OR DECAYING
FOR THE MOST PART. SURGING CONTINUED AT NW20 AND WAS ALSO SEEN
NEAR SE30.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE. REGION 5303 SHOWS NO SIGNS OF ABATING ITS FLARE
ACTIVITY. ALSO WATCH EAST LIMB FOR THE IMMINENT RETURN OF OLD
REGION 5273 (S30, L=323) WHICH PRODUCED X-CLASS ACTIVITY ON
18 DEC.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 03/2100Z TO 04/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS MOSTLY QUIET. TWO SMALL SUDDEN
IMPULSES OCCURRED AT 0850 AND 1856Z. BOTH MEASURED ABOUT 10
NANOTESLAS AT BOULDER.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE QUIET TO UNSETTLED THE NEXT THREE DAYS.
