JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 15 AUG 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 14/2100Z
TO 15/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH. REGION 5629 (S16W84)
YIELDED YET ANOTHER X-CLASS FLARE, THIS ONE AN X1/0F AT 0315Z.
LARGE DISCRETE FREQUENCY RADIO BURSTS (16,000 SFU AT 2695 MHZ)
AND A VERY LONG-LIVED TYPE IV SWEEP ACCOMPANIED THE FLARE.
THE REGION ALSO PRODUCED AN M3/1N AT 1409Z AND IS THE PROBABLE
SOURCE OF AN M5 AT 2051Z. REGION 5643 (N15E23), LARGE AND
COMPLEX AGAIN TODAY, GENERATED ONLY SUBFLARES THE LAST 24
HOURS. ONE OR TWO NEW SPOT GROUPS ARE ROTATING ONTO THE DISK
IN THE NORTHEAST BUT ARE AS YET UNNUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
AT THE MODERATE TO HIGH LEVEL THROUGHOUT THE PERIOD. WATCH
WEST LIMB FOR AN EVENTFUL DEPARTURE OF REGION 5629 AND CENTER
DISK FOR ENERGETIC ACTIVITY FROM REGION 5643.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 14/2100Z TO 15/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT MINOR TO MAJOR STORM LEVELS
THE PAST 24 HOURS. RECENT X-CLASS FLARE ACTIVITY FROM REGION
5629 IS THE PROBABLE CAUSE OF THE DISTURBANCE. THE POLAR CAP
ABSORPTION AND SATELLITE PROTON EVENTS ARE STILL IN PROGRESS.
A SUDDEN IMPULSE OF ABOUT 50 NANOTESLAS WAS OBSERVED AT
BOULDER AT 2018Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT MINOR STORM LEVELS THROUGH 17 AUGUST.
TODAY'S X1 FLARE SHOULD PROLONG THE CURRENT DISTURBANCE UNTIL
THEN. UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE CONDITIONS ARE ANTICIPATED AT THE END
OF THE PERIOD. THE POLAR CAP ABSORPTION AND SATELLITE PROTON
EVENTS SHOULD PERSIST FOR PERHAPS ANOTHER 24 HOURS.
