JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 11 OCT 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 10/2100Z
TO 11/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. THE LARGEST EVENT OF THE
PERIOD WAS A C5/SF FROM REGION 5721 (N27W36) AT 11/0455Z. NEW
REGION 5740 (S16E68) ROTATED ONTO THE DISK AS A SMALL SIZE
CLASS E GROUP. NO FLARES WERE REPORTED FROM THIS REGION.
REGION 5738 (N12E22) CONTINUED TO EMERGE RAPIDLY AND
BACKGROUND PLAGE INTENSITY WAS HIGH. MINOR SUBFLARES WERE
OBSERVED FROM SEVERAL OTHER REGIONS. MINOR NEW REGIONS 5741
(S18W03) AND 5742 (N22E48) WERE NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY SHOULD BE
PREDOMINANTLY LOW FOR THE PERIOD. REGION 5740 APPEARS CAPABLE
OF PRODUCING ISOLATED CLASS M FLARES. AS TIME PASSES WITH
ALMOST NO INDICATION OF AN ENERGETIC RETURN OF OLD REGION
5698, ONLY VERY ISOLATED CLASS M FLARES CAN BE EXPECTED FROM
THAT AREA.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 10/2100Z TO 11/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS QUIET DURING THE PERIOD.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  GENERALLY UNSETTLED
GEOMAGNETIC CONDITIONS ARE FORECAST FOR THE NEXT THREE DAYS.
