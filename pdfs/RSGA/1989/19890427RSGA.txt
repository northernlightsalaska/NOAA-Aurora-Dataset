FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 117
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 27 APR 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 26/2100Z
TO 27/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. REGIONS 5454 (S19W60) AND
5464 (S19E49) EACH PRODUCED SEVERAL SUBFLARES, NONE OF WHICH
WERE SIGNIFICANT IN OPTICAL OR RADIO ASPECTS. REGION 5464
GREW GRADUALLY THROUGHOUT THE PERIOD. ITS MAGNETIC STRUCTURE IS
BECOMING INCREASINGLY MORE COMPLEX. NO NEW REGIONS WERE ASSIGNED
TODAY.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. REGIONS 5454 AND 5464 ARE EXPECTED TO PRODUCE
C-CLASS SUBFLARES. BOTH REGIONS, ESPECIALLY 5464, APPEAR
CAPABLE OF ISOLATED M-CLASS ACTIVITY.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 26/2100Z TO 27/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT UNSETTLED TO MINOR STORM
LEVELS AT MIDDLE LATITUDES. THE HIGHER LATITUDES RECORDED
MINOR TO MAJOR STORM LEVELS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE ACTIVE AT MIDDLE LATITUDES AND AT ACTIVE TO
MINOR STORM LEVELS AT HIGHER LATITUDES DUE TO THE EFFECTS OF
A TRANSEQUATORIAL CORONAL HOLE PRESENTLY LOCATED NEAR N04W35.
