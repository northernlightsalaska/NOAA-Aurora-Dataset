JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 30 OCT 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 29/2100Z
TO 30/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 5769 (N23E56)
PRODUCED AN M2/2N FLARE AT 29/2155Z. A TENFLARE OF 210 FLUX
UNITS WAS ASSOCIATED WITH THIS FLARE AND OCCURRED AT 29/2135Z.
REGIONS 5763 (N21W15) AND 5769 CONTINUE TO BE THE MOST COMPLEX
SPOT GROUPS ON THE DISK. BOTH ARE D-TYPE SPOT GROUPS WITH BETA
MAGNETIC CONFIGURATIONS. THREE NEW SPOT GROUPS NUMBERED TODAY:
5772 (S18E68), 5773 (N14E69) AND 5774 (N16E70).
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. REGIONS 5763 AND 5769 HAVE THE POTENTIAL FOR
LOW LEVEL M-CLASS ACTIVITY.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 29/2100Z TO 30/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS. THE PROTON EVENT THAT BEGAN AT
19/1305Z ENDED AT 30/1135Z. THE RECENT ACTIVE CONDITIONS WERE
CAUSED BY THE X1/1N FLARE FROM OLD REGION 5747 (S27, L-207)
AT 27/1904Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE LEVELS.  A CORONAL HOLE
AND DSF (REPORTED MISSING 29 OCT) SHOULD CREATE ACTIVE
CONDITIONS AT BOTH MID AND HIGH LATITUDES ON NOV 2.
