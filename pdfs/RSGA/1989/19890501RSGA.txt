SDF NUMBER 121
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 01 MAY 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 30/2100Z
TO 01/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH. REGION 5470 (N29E48)
PRODUCED A MODERATE DURATION M5/SN AT 01/0114Z. CORRELATIVE
RADIO BURSTS WERE SMALL TO MODERATE WITH NO TYPE II OR IV
SWEEPS REPORTED. THIS REGION GREW RAPIDLY DURING THE LAST TWO
DAYS AND IS EMERGING IN AN AREA NEAR WHERE RECORD SETTING REGION
5395 (N34 L=257) WAS LOCATED IN MARCH. REGION 5464 (S18W03)
WAS STABLE.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY SHOULD BE
MODERATE FOR THE NEXT THREE DAYS. THE RAPID GROWTH IN REGION
5470 MAKES CLASS M FLARES FROM THIS REGION LIKELY. SHOULD
THIS GROWTH TREND CONTINUE, CLASS X FLARES WILL BECOME A
DISTINCT POSSIBILITY.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 30/2100Z TO 01/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS QUIET TO UNSETTLED FOR MOST OF THE
PERIOD. SLIGHTLY ACTIVE LEVELS WERE EXPERIENCED AT THE END
OF THE PERIOD.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD
SHOULD BE UNSETTLED TO SLIGHTLY ACTIVE FOR THE NEXT THREE
DAYS.
