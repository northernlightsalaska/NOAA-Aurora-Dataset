IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 13/2100Z
TO 14/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE.  RGN 5451 (N09E52)
PRODUCED AN M1.5 X-RAY EVENT 13/2146UT.  RGN 5449 (N20E15) HAS
INCREASED IN COMPLEXITY AND PRODUCED SEVERAL SUB-FAINT FLARES.
NO NEW REGIONS WERE NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE AS REGIONS 5449 AND 5451 HAVE THE POTENTIAL FOR
M-CLASS EVENTS.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 13/2100Z TO 14/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS UNSETTLED AT MID LATITUDES AND
UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE AT HIGH LATITUDES WITH PERIODS OF MINOR
STORM.  THE POLAR CAP ABSORPTION EVENT ENDED AT 14/1415UT
AFTER REACHING A MAXIMUM ABSORPTION OF 5.1 DB AT 12/0101UT.
A SUDDEN IMPULSE OF 22 NANOTESLA OCCURRED AT 13/2225UT.
THE SATELLITE PROTON EVENT THAT BEGAN 11/1455UT REMAINS IN
PROGRESS.  AN INCREASE IN PROTON FLUX FROM 37 PFU AT
13/2220UT TO 120 PFU AT 14/0330UT ACCOMPANIED THE SUDDEN
IMPULSE. THE 14/2100UT PROTON FLUX WAS 25 PFU.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED WITH PERIODS OF ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM
CONDITIONS AT HIGH LATITUDES.
