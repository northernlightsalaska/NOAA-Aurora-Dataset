FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 123
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 03 MAY 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 02/2100Z
TO 03/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH. REGION 5470 (N29E24)
PRODUCED A MODERATE DURATION X2/3B FLARE AT 03/0358Z. NO TYPE
II OR IV EVENTS WERE OBSERVED BUT A TEN CENTIMETER BURST OF
1100 FLUX UNITS WAS OBSERVED. REGION 5470 BEGAN TO GROW AGAIN
DURING THE PERIOD AND NOW A DELTA CONFIGURATION IS VISIBLE.
THE SMM CORONAGRAPH OBSERVED A CORONAL MASS EJECTION OFF THE
EAST LIMB AT ABOUT 03/0515Z - NO SPEED INFORMATION AVAILABLE.
THIS CME IS LIKELY RELATED TO THE CLASS X FLARE FROM 5470.
REGION 5464 (S19W26) PRODUCED AN M1/1N FLARE AT 03/1948Z. THIS
FLARE WAS ACCOMPANIED BY A TYPE II BURST. A DELTA MAY BE
FORMING IN REGION 5464.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY SHOULD BE
MODERATE. THE RESURGENCE OF GROWTH AND INCREASE IN MAGNETIC
COMPLEXITY IN REGION 5470 MAKE THIS REGION A GOOD CANDIDATE
FOR CONTINUED CLASS M AND OCCASIONAL CLASS X FLARE
PRODUCTION. REGION 5464 SHOULD ADD TO CLASS M FLARE TOTALS.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 02/2100Z TO 03/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS UNSETTLED FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD
SHOULD BE AT ACTIVE LEVELS ON 04 MAY IN RESPONSE TO A FLARE
FROM REGION 5464 ON 02 MAY. MINOR STORM TO OCCASIONALLY
MAJOR STORM LEVELS SHOULD BE EXPERIENCED ON 05-06 MAY IN
RESPONSE TO THE AFOREMENTIONED X2 FLARE FROM REGION 5470.
