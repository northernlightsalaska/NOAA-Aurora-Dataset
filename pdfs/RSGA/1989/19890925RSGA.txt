IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 24/2100Z
TO 25/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 5698 (S26W51)
PRODUCED THE PERIOD'S ONLY M-FLARE, AN M1/SF AT 25/1322Z. THE
REGION DECAYED SLIGHTLY IN SPOT COUNT/AREA, BUT RETAINED ITS
HIGH DEGREE OF MAGNETIC COMPLEXITY. REGION 5708 (S13E81),
BELIEVED TO BE THE RETURN OF OLD REGION 5669, AND 5709 (S11E53)
WERE NUMBERED TODAY.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE. REGION 5698 IS EXPECTED TO PRODUCE AN ISOLATED
M-CLASS FLARE. A MAJOR FLARE MAY ALSO OCCUR FROM THIS REGION.
REGION 5708 PROVIDES A SLIGHT CHANCE FOR AN ISOLATED M-FLARE.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 24/2100Z TO 25/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT MOSTLY QUIET LEVELS FOR
THE PAST 24 HOURS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE MOSTLY ACTIVE TOMORROW. THE FINAL TWO DAYS OF
THE PERIOD ARE EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED. TOMORROW'S ACTIVE
LEVELS ARE EXPECTED IN RESPONSE TO THE TYPE II SWEEPS WHICH
OCCURRED ON 23 SEPTEMBER.
