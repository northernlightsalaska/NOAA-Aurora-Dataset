FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 157
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 06 JUN 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 05/2100Z
TO 06/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH. REGION 5521 (S19E36)
CONTINUES TO DOMINATE THE SOLAR DISK AND PRODUCED TODAY'S
LARGEST EVENT, AN X1/2B AT 2138Z WHICH WAS ACCOMPANIED BY
MODEST DISCRETE RADIO BURSTS. THE GROUP PRODUCED TWO ADDITIONAL
M-CLASS FLARES: AN M2/SN AT 0121Z AND AN M1/SN AT 1636Z.
THE GROUP CONTINUES TO BE LARGE AND MAGNETICALLY COMPLEX WITH
A DELTA CONFIGURATION IN THE LARGE TRAILER PENUMBRA. AN
ADDITIONAL UNCORRELATED M-CLASS EVENT OCCURRED AT 1104Z.
SURGE ACTIVITY WAS REPORTED ON THE WEST LIMB AT N24,
ASSOCIATED WITH THE EXIT OF REGION 5506, AND AT S20
ASSOCIATED WITH REGION 5511. THE REMAINDER OF THE DISK
AND LIMBS WERE RELATIVELY QUIET AND STABLE.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE TO HIGH. REGION 5521 IS CONSIDERED TO BE THE PRIMARY
THREAT FOR MAJOR FLARES.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 05/2100Z TO 06/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS MOSTLY QUIET TO UNSETTLED. SOME
ISOLATED PERIODS OF ACTIVE CONDITIONS WERE REPORTED AT HIGH
LATITUDES.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE MOSTLY UNSETTLED WITH OCCASIONAL ACTIVE PERIODS
AT HIGH LATITUDES.
