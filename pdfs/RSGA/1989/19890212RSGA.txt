FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 043
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 12 FEB 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 11/2100Z
TO 12/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. REGION 5354 (N31W23) WAS
IN A SLOW DECAY PHASE. IT PRODUCED ISOLATED SUBFLARES DURING THE
PERIOD. REGION 5355 (N22W06) DISPLAYED SIGNIFICANT DECAY AND WAS
QUIET THROUGHOUT THE DAY. REGION 5356 (S17E17) SHOWED
SIGNIFICANT GROWTH AND PRODUCED SEVERAL C-CLASS SUBFLARES. THREE
NEW REGIONS WERE ASSIGNED: 5363 (S38W60), 5364 (N22E03), AND
5365 (N17W11). ALL WERE SIMPLE IN STRUCTURE.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE. REGION 5354 IS LIKELY TO PRODUCE AN M-CLASS FLARE
DURING THE PERIOD. THERE IS ALSO A VERY SLIGHT CHANCE FOR A
MAJOR FLARE FROM THIS REGION. REGIONS 5356, 5357 (N17E18), AND
5362 (N20E62) EACH PROVIDE A VERY SLIGHT CHANCE FOR AN M-CLASS
FLARE.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 11/2100Z TO 12/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO UNSETTLED LEVELS
FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE ACTIVE DURING THE FIRST TWO DAYS DUE TO THE
X1/3B FLARE THAT OCCURRED ON 10 FEBRUARY. PERIODS OF MINOR
STORMING MAY OCCUR TOMORROW AT HIGH LATITUDES. THE THIRD DAY
SHOULD SEE THE FIELD SUBSIDE TO UNSETTLED LEVELS.
