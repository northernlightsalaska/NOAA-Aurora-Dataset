JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 16 DEC 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 15/2100Z
TO 16/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. REGION 5836 (N12E27)
PRODUCED FREQUENT LOW LEVEL CLASS C FLARES. THIS REGION DECAYED
SLIGHTLY AND IS SIMPLIFYING MAGNETICALLY. AN OPTICALLY
UNCORRELATED C2 WITH A MODERATE INTENSITY TYPE II OCCURRED AT
16/0648Z. TWO RETURNING REGIONS WHICH WERE EXPECTED TO ELEVATE
FLARE LEVELS RETURNED AS MINOR SUNSPOT GROUPS. SEVERAL NEW
REGIONS EMERGED ON THE DISK.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS FORECAST TO BE
LOW FOR THE NEXT THREE DAYS. REGION 5836 IS CAPABLE OF
PRODUCING INFREQUENT LOW LEVEL CLASS M FLARES.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 15/2100Z TO 16/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS QUIET UNTIL NEAR 16/1230Z. ACTIVE
CONDITIONS WERE THEN EXPERIENCED AT MID LATITUDES AND MINOR
STORM LEVELS WERE OBSERVED AT HIGH LATITUDES. THIS
DISTURBANCE ABATED NEAR 16/1500Z. UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE
CONDITIONS WERE EXPERIENCED UNTIL THE END OF THE PERIOD.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD
IS FORECAST TO BE PREDOMINANTLY UNSETTLED FOR THE NEXT
THREE DAYS.
