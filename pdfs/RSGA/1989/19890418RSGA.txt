JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 18 APR 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 17/2100Z
TO 18/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. REGION 5452 (S17E42)
PRODUCED TODAY'S LARGEST FLARE: A C3/SF AT 0641Z WHICH WAS
ACCOMPANIED BY MODERATE TYPE II AND TYPE IV SWEEPS AND WAS
CORRELATED WITH A CORONAL MASS EJECTION. REGIONS 5449 (S20W33)
AND 5452 (S17E42) PRODUCED OCCASIONAL SUBFLARE FLUCTUATIONS.
THE REMAINDER OF THE DISK AND LIMBS WERE ESSENTIALLY QUIET AND
STABLE.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW. THERE CONTINUES TO BE A FAIR CHANCE FOR A SMALL, ISOLATED
M-CLASS FLARE OVER THE NEXT THREE DAYS, PRIMARILY DUE TO REGION
5449 WHICH SHOWS SOME MAGNETIC COMPLEXITY.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 17/2100Z TO 18/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS PREDOMINATELY QUIET TO UNSETTLED. A
BRIEF PERIOD OF ACTIVE CONDITIONS OCCURRED AT HIGH LATITUDES
EARLY IN THE DAY.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE MOSTLY UNSETTLED WITH OCCASIONAL ACTIVE PERIODS
AT HIGH LATITUDES.
