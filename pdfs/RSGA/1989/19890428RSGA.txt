JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 28 APR 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 27/2100Z
TO 28/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. SEVERAL SUBFLARES OCCURRED
DURING THE PERIOD MOST OF WHICH WERE PRODUCED BY REGIONS 5454
(S20W73) AND 5464 (S19E34). ALL FLARES WERE UNIMPRESSIVE IN
RADIO AND OPTICAL ASPECTS. TWO NEW REGIONS WERE ASSIGNED: 5468
(N26E01) AND 5469 (S36W15). BOTH WERE SMALL AND MAGNETICALLY
SIMPLE.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. C-CLASS ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED FROM REGIONS
5454, 5460 (S18W42), AND 5464. REGIONS 5454 AND 5464 ARE ALSO
CAPABLE OF ISOLATED M-CLASS ACTIVITY.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 27/2100Z TO 28/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT MOSTLY ACTIVE TO MINOR
STORM LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS. THIS ACTIVITY IS MOST
LIKELY DUE TO THE EFFECTS OF A CORONAL HOLE LOCATED NEAR
N10W51.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE ACTIVE AT MIDDLE LATITUDES. THE HIGH LATITUDE
FIELD IS EXPECTED TO BE AT ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM LEVELS.
