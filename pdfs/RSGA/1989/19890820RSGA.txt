JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 20 AUG 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 19/2100Z
TO 20/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. REGION 5643 (N16W41) PRO-
DUCED TODAY'S LARGEST EVENT: A C6.9 AT 19/2138Z. THIS GROUP
CONTINUES TO BE THE LARGEST ON THE DISK BUT IS DECLINING
QUIETLY. AN ERUPTIVE PROMINENCE WAS OBSERVED ON THE WEST
LIMB AT S22 FROM 0110-0213Z AND WAS ASSOCIATED WITH AN X-RAY
ENHANCEMENT UP TO C4.8. THE REMAINDER OF ACTIVITY CONSISTED
OF OCCASIONAL, SMALL C-CLASS SUBFLARES.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW. REGION 5643, HOWEVER, CONTINUES TO POSE SOMEWHAT OF A
THREAT FOR AN ISOLATED M-CLASS EVENT.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 19/2100Z TO 20/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD RANGED FROM QUIET TO MINOR STORM LEVELS.
CONDITIONS WERE MOSTLY QUIET AT THE BEGINNING OF THE PERIOD,
BUT BECAME SOMEWHAT DISTURBED FROM 0600Z-1630Z. MID-LATITUDES
WERE AT ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM LEVELS AND HIGH LATITUDES WERE
AT MOSTLY MINOR STORM LEVELS DURING THE DISTURBANCE.
CONDITIONS SUBSIDED TO GENERALLY UNSETTLED LEVELS FOR THE
REMAINDER OF THE DAY. A GREATER THAN 10MEV PROTON EVENT CON-
TINUES IN PROGRESS. FLUX LEVELS WERE RISING AT THE BEGINNING
OF THE PERIOD BUT PEAKED AT 0510Z (510 PFU AT GREATER THAN
10MEV) AND HAVE BEEN DECLINING SINCE THEN. THE PCA EVENT
ENDED AT ABOUT 20/1845Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE MOSTLY ACTIVE TOMMOROW DUE TO POSSIBLE EFFECTS
FROM A DISAPPEARING FILAMENT. CONDITIONS ARE EXPECTED TO  GRAD-
UALLY SUBSIDE TO UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE LEVELS FOR THE SECOND AND
THIRD DAYS. THE GREATER THAN 10MEV PROTON EVENT IS EXPECTED TO
END SOMETIME IN THE NEXT 24 HOURS.
