FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 199
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 18 JUL 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 17/2100Z
TO 18/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. ONLY LOW CLASS C FLARES
WERE OBSERVED. A LARGE PROMINENCE ERUPTED BETWEEN 18/1440-1530Z
AT N35E90. H-ALPHA MATERIAL FADED FROM VIEW AT 0.58R ABOVE THE
LIMB. A BRIGHT SURGE AT N21W90 OCCURRED BETWEEN 18/1438-1509Z
AND REACHED A MAXIMUM HEIGHT OF 0.15R ABOVE THE LIMB. NEITHER
EVENT PRODUCED SIGNIFICANT X-RAY OR RADIO EMISSIONS. A SMALL
REGION ROTATED ONTO THE DISK AT S20E71 AND WAS NUMBERED 5600.
ANOTHER SMALL GROUP ROUNDED THE LIMB AT N25E80 AND BECAME
ACTIVE REGION 5601.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY SHOULD CONTINUE
LOW FOR THE NEXT THREE DAYS. AN ISOLATED LOW LEVEL CLASS M
FLARE IS POSSIBLE FROM REGION 5598 (S22E49).
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 17/2100Z TO 18/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS ACTIVE BECOMING UNSETTLED DURING
THE PERIOD. BRIEF MAJOR STORM CONDITIONS WERE EXPERIENCED
AT HIGH LATITUDES BETWEEN 18/0500-0730Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD
SHOULD BE UNSETTLED TO SLIGHTLY ACTIVE FOR THE DURATION OF
THE FORECAST PERIOD.
