SDF NUMBER 364
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 30 DEC 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 29/2100Z
TO 30/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH.  REGION 5858 (S18E01)
WAS THE MOST ACTIVE SUNSPOT GROUP THIS PAST DAY, PRODUCING
AN M5/1N AT 30/0208Z AND AN X1/1N AT 30/0419Z.  THIS REGION
RETAINS SOME MAGNETIC COMPLEXITY ALTHOUGH THE SUNSPOT STRUCTURE
HAS SIMPLIFIED SINCE THESE TWO MAJOR FLARES.  REGION 5852
(S26W44) IS THE LARGEST SUNSPOT GROUP ON THE DISK BUT
GENERATED ONLY ONE LOW LEVEL M-FLARE SINCE YESTERDAY, AN M2/SF
AT 29/2322Z.  THIS REGION HAS ALSO HAD SOME WHITE LIGHT DECAY
BUT STILL REMAINS LARGE AND COMPLEX.  NEW REGION 5865 (S37E72)
WAS NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE TO HIGH.  REGIONS 5852 AND 5858 MAY PRODUCE MAJOR
FLARES.  M-CLASS ACTIVITY IS POSSIBLE IN REGION 5854 (N23W27).
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 29/2100Z TO 30/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD RANGED FROM ACTIVE TO MAJOR STORM.
THE MIDDLE LATITUDE FIELD HAS BEEN BELOW STORM LEVEL SINCE
30/0000Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE FOR THE NEXT 24 HOURS AS
YESTERDAY'S DISTURBANCE ENDS.  THE REMAINING TWO DAYS SHOULD
BE QUIET TO UNSETTLED.
