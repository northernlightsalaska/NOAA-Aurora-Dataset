IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 19/2100Z
TO 20/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW.  ONLY C-CLASS ACTIVITY
OCCURRED SINCE YESTERDAY.  MOST FLARES WERE PRODUCED BY
REGION 5698 (S26E03).  THIS REGION CONTINUES TO GROW,
INCREASING PENUMBRAL AREA AND MAGNETIC COMPLEXITY.  REGION
5694 (N26W51) PRODUCED A FEW SUBFLARES BUT REMAINS A FAIRLY
SMALL SUNSPOT GROUP.  NEW REGION 5703 (S22E14) WAS NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE.  REGION 5698 MAY PRODUCE ENERGETIC ACTIVITY.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 19/2100Z TO 20/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS QUIET TO UNSETTLED.  THE MAJOR
GEOMAGNETIC STORM ENDED AT 19/1200Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE QUIET TO UNSETTLED.
