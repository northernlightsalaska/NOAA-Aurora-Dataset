FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 028
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 28 JAN 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 27/2100Z
TO 28/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. NEWLY NUMBERED
REGION 5336 (S20W12), JUST WEST OF REGION 5330 (S18E03),
PRODUCED THE DAY'S ONLY M-CLASS EVENT, AN M1/0F AT 1306Z. THERE
MAY BE SOME INTERACTION BETWEEN THE TWO REGIONS. REGIONS 5330
AND 5329 (N17W31) GENERATED OCCASIONAL C-CLASS SUBFLARES.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE AS CONTINUED M-CLASS ACTIVITY IS LIKELY. REGION 5330,
ALTHOUGH LESS IMPRESSIVE SINCE ITS X-CLASS FLARE YESTERDAY,
STILL MUST BE REGARDED AS A POTENTIAL X-CLASS THREAT.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 27/2100Z TO 28/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN QUIET TO UNSETTLED AT MID-
LATITUDES. HIGH LATITUDES HAVE HAD ACTIVE CONDITIONS SINCE
1330Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE MOSTLY QUIET TO UNSETTLED. SOME ACTIVE
CONDITIONS MAY OCCUR AT HIGH LATITUDES, ESPECIALLY ON 30 JAN.
