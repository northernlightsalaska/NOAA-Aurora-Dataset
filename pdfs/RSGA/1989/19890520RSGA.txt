FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 140
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 20 MAY 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 19/2100Z
TO 20/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW.  REGION 5495 (N22W25)
CONTINUED TO EMERGE RAPIDLY, INCREASING IN SUNSPOT AREA
BY A FACTOR OF THREE.  FURTHER DEVELOPMENT MAY LEAD TO A
DELTA CONFIGURATION IN THE TRAILER SUNSPOTS.  REGION 5497
(S20E31) ALSO CONTINUED TO GROW BUT AT A SLOWER RATE.  SUBFLARE
ACTIVITY HAS INCREASED IN BOTH REGIONS.  AN OPTICALLY
UNCORRELATED LONG DURATION C5 FLARE OCCURRED AT 20/0929Z.
THIS FLARE WAS ASSOCIATED WITH A TYPE II SWEEP AND MINOR
DISCRETE RADIO BURSTS.  THE SOURCE OF THIS FLARE MAY BE BEYOND
THE EAST LIMB.  THREE REGIONS WERE NUMBERED SINCE YESTERDAY -
5499 (S06W00), 5500 (S12W16), AND 5501 (N33E35).
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE.  THE CHANCE OF AN M-CLASS FLARE IS EXPECTED
TO INCREASE OVER THE NEXT THREE DAYS.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 19/2100Z TO 20/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS MOSTLY QUIET TO ACTIVE.
MAJOR STORM CONDITIONS WERE OBSERVED AT BOTH MIDDLE AND
HIGH LATITUDES FROM 20/0900Z-1200Z.  THIS DISTURBANCE
BEGAN AS A GRADUAL ONSET AT ABOUT 20/0730Z AND IS BELIEVED
TO BE ASSOCIATED WITH THE DSF/LDE OF 17 MAY.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE MOSTLY UNSETTLED FOR THE NEXT THREE DAYS.
ISOLATED ACTIVE CONDITIONS ARE POSSIBLE DURING THE FIRST DAY
OF THE FORECAST PERIOD AS THE CURRENT DISTURBANCE ENDS.
