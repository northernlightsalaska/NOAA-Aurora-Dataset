IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 27/2100Z
TO 28/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. TWO M-CLASS EVENTS
OCCURRED DURING THIS PERIOD. THE LARGEST WAS A LONG DURATION M2
(NO OPTICAL CORRELATION) STARTING AT 28/0510Z, MAXIMUM AT
28/0755Z AND ENDING AT 28/0950Z. AN M1/BSL OCCURRED AT 28/1125Z
AND WAS CREDITED TO REGION 5747 (SW28). A TENFLARE OF 800 FLUX
UNITS WAS ASSOCIATED WITH THE M1 FLARE. REGION 5763 (N20E11)
HAS GROWN FROM A B SPOT GROUP TO A D SPOT GROUP OVER THE PAST
24 HOURS. ALL OTHER SPOT GROUPS LOOK STABLE OR UNIMPRESSIVE.
TWO NEW REGIONS NUMBERED TODAY: 5767 (N27W48) AND 5768 (S11W06).
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. REGION 5763 MAY PRODUCE AN ISOLATED M-CLASS
FLARE IF SPOT AND MAGNETIC COMPLEXITY CONTINUES.

LEVELS EXCEPT FOR ONE PERIOD OF ACTIVE CONDITIONS AT HIGH

EVENT AT GREATER THAN 10MEV IS STILL IN PROGRESS. THE PCA
THAT STARTED AT 19/1315Z ENDED AT 28/0300Z. THE PARTICLE
FLUX FOR 10MEV AT 28/2100Z WAS 16 P.F.U.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE QUIET TO UNSETTLED FOR THE REMAINDER OF THE
FORECAST PERIOD.
