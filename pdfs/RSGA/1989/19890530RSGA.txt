SDF NUMBER 150
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 30 MAY 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 29/2100Z
TO 30/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH. REGION 5497 (S20W99)
PRODUCED AN M6/SF AT 30/0110Z WITH MINOR RADIO EMISSION, AN
M3/LOOP PROMINENCE SYSTEM AT 30/0721Z, AND WAS THE LIKELY
SOURCE OF AN M2 AT 30/1318Z. REGION 5511 (S22E07) GREW DURING
THE PERIOD. ANOTHER SEPARATE BIPOLE EMERGED NEARBY AND
WAS NUMBERED AS REGION 5515 (S22W00). REGION 5516 (N14E57)
EMERGED JUST SOUTH OF REGION 5514 (N19E59).
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY SHOULD DECREASE
TO A MODERATE LEVEL FOR 31 MAY AS REGION 5497 ROTATES FARTHER
BEHIND THE LIMB AND TOTALLY BEYOND VIEW. ISOLATED CLASS M
FLARES ARE POSSIBLE FOR 01-02 JUNE IF TRENDS IN CURRENT DISK
REGIONS CONTINUE.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 29/2100Z TO 30/2100Z:
THE MID LATITUDE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS MOSTLY UNSETTLED.
THE HIGH LATITUDE FIELD WAS UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE FOR THE
MAJORITY OF THE PERIOD EXCEPT NEAR 30/0600Z WHEN BRIEF
MINOR STORM CONDITIONS WERE EXPERIENCED.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD
SHOULD CONTINUE GENERALLY UNSETTLED AT MID LATITUDES AND
ACTIVE AT HIGH LATITUDES FOR THE DURATION OF THE FORECAST
PERIOD.
