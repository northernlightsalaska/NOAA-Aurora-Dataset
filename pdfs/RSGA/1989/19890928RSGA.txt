JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 28 SEP 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 27/2100Z
TO 28/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 5712 (S18E55)
PRODUCED AN M1/SN AT 27/2359Z AND AN M3/1B AT 28/1348Z. NEITHER
FLARE PRODUCED SIGNIFICANT RADIO BURSTS AND THE LATTER FLARE
WAS ACCOMPANIED BY A BRIGHT SURGE EXTENDING OVER THE SOUTHEAST
LIMB TO 0.28 RADIUS. REGION 5712 APPEARS TO BE AN
EXTENDED CLASS F GROUP WITH MUCH LESS AREAL COVERAGE WHEN
COMPARED TO LAST ROTATION. AN OPTICALLY UNCORRELATED M2 FLARE
OCCURRED AT 28/0926Z. REGION 5698 (S26W92) IS MAKING ITS LIMB
TRANSIT IN A NON-ENERGETIC MANNER. IT DID PRODUCE A BRIGHT
SURGE TO .16R BETWEEN 28/1255-1414Z WITH LITTLE X-RAY
ENHANCEMENT. SMALL REGIONS 5713 (N23E32) AND 5714 (N29E76) WERE
NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY SHOULD CONTINUE
AT A MODERATE LEVEL FOR THE DURATION OF THE FORECAST PERIOD.
AN ISOLATED MAJOR FLARE IS POSSIBLE FROM REGIONS 5712 AND 5698.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 27/2100Z TO 28/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS QUIET FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD
SHOULD BE UNSETTLED FOR 29 SEP. SLIGHTLY ACTIVE CONDITIONS
ARE FORECAST FOR 30 SEP - 01 OCT IN RESPONSE TO A FILAMENT
WHICH FADED BETWEEN 26/2335Z-27/1520Z FROM THE SOUTHEAST
QUADRANT. THERE IS A SLIGHT POSSIBILITY OF A PROTON EVENT
AND PCA FROM DEPARTING REGION 5698.
