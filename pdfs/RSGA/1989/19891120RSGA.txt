IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 19/2100Z
TO 20/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 5793 (S25W47)
APPEARED TO STABILIZE AFTER SEVERAL DAYS OF SIGNIFICANT GROWTH,
BUT REMAINED MAGNETICALLY COMPLEX WITH A MAGNETIC DELTA
STRUCTURE EVIDENT WITHIN ITS TRAILER SPOTS. THE REGION PRODUCED
THREE M-CLASS FLARES, THE LARGEST OF WHICH WAS AN M3/1N AT
20/1432Z WHICH WAS ASSOCIATED WITH MINOR RADIO EMISSIONS.
REGIONS 5795 (S10E06) AND 5796 (N10E23) GREW SLIGHTLY. THE
REMAINING REGIONS WERE STABLE OR DECLINING. NEW REGION 5801
(S17E65) WAS NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
HIGH. REGION 5793 IS EXPECTED TO PRODUCE M-CLASS FLARES AND AN
ISOLATED MAJOR FLARE.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 19/2100Z TO 20/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO UNSETTLED LEVELS
FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT MOSTLY ACTIVE LEVELS DURING THE FIRST TWO
DAYS IN RESPONSE TO THE X1/2B OF 19 NOVEMBER. THE FINAL DAY IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE LEVELS AS FIELD EFFECTS
SUBSIDE.
