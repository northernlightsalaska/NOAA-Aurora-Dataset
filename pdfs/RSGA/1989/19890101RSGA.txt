JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 01 JAN 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 31/2100Z
TO 01/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 5292 (S18W63)
PRODUCED NUMEROUS FLARES WITH THE MOST ENERGETIC AN M1/0F WITH
TYPE II AT 0625Z. OCCASIONAL MID-C CLASS ACTIVITY ALSO OCCURRED
IN REGIONS 5290 (N22W72) AND 5303 (S18W18). NEWLY ASSIGNED
REGION 5304 (N22E69) ROTATED FULLY INTO VIEW. SURGING WAS NOTED
AT VARIOUS LATITUDES ON THE NE LIMB.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. REGIONS 5290, 5292, AND TO A LESSER DEGREE,
5303, MAY YIELD ISOLATED M-CLASS ACTIVITY AT SOME POINT DURING
THE PERIOD.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 31/2100Z TO 01/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO PERSIST AT UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE LEVELS FOR THE
NEXT 24 HOURS, PROBABLY DUE TO THE FLARE ACTIVITY OF 30 DEC.
QUIET TO UNSETTLED CONDITIONS SHOULD END THE INTERVAL.
