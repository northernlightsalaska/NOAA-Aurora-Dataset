FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 292
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 19 OCT 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 18/2100Z
TO 19/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH. REGION 5747 (S26E01)
PRODUCED AN X13/4B FLARE AT 19/1258Z (X13 IS ESTIMATED DUE TO
SENSOR SATURATION). MAJOR TYPES II AND IV SWEEPS, MAJOR
DISCRETE RADIO BURSTS, AND FLARE LOOPS ACCOMPANIED THE FLARE.
SUNSPOT REPORTS INDICATE THE REGION RETAINED ITS HIGH DEGREE OF
COMPLEXITY AFTER THE FLARE. THE FLARE REMAINED IN PROGRESS AT
THE END OF THE SUMMARY PERIOD. MOST REMAINING REGIONS WERE
QUIET AND STABLE. NO NEW REGIONS WERE ASSIGNED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE TO HIGH. REGION 5747 IS CAPABLE OF PRODUCING BOTH
M- AND X-CLASS FLARES DURING THE PERIOD.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 18/2100Z TO 19/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO ACTIVE LEVELS
AT MIDDLE LATITUDES. THE HIGH LATITUDE FIELD RANGED FROM
QUIET TO MINOR STORM LEVELS. A SATELLITE PROTON EVENT AT
GREATER THAN 10 MEV BEGAN AT 19/1305Z. THE MAXIMUM GREATER
THAN 10 MEV FLUX OBSERVED THUS FAR WAS 2700 PFU AT 19/1935Z.
A SATELLITE PROTON EVENT AT GREATER THAN 100 MEV BEGAN AT
19/1710Z. ITS MAXIMUM THUS FAR HAS BEEN 190 PFU AT 19/2040Z.
BOTH EVENTS REMAINED IN PROGRESS AT THE END OF THE SUMMARY
PERIOD. THESE EVENTS WERE PRODUCED BY TODAY'S MAJOR FLARE.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT MINOR TO MAJOR STORM LEVELS DURING THE
FIRST TWO DAYS OF THE PERIOD IN THE WAKE OF TODAY'S MAJOR
FLARE. CONDITIONS ARE EXPECTED TO SUBSIDE TO ACTIVE LEVELS
DURING THE FINAL DAY OF THE FORECAST PERIOD.
