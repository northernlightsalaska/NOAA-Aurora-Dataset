IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 29/2100Z
TO 30/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY CONTINUED MODERATE. REGION 5329
(N17W59) WAS TYHE DAY'S MOST PROLIFIC FLARE PRODUCER. ITS MOST
ENERGETIC EVENT WAS AN M1/1B WITH TYPE II AT 0354Z. IT ALSO
JOINED FORCES WITH REGION 5334 (S20E77) TO YIELD AN M2
(OPTICALLY, REGION 5334 WAS 1B, REGION 5329 WAS 0F) WITH TYPE
II AND IV SWEEP AT 0110Z. REGION 5334 INCREASED IN MAGNETIC
COMPLEXITY TO A BETA-DELTA CLASSIFICATION. AT WEST LIMB, TYPE II
SWEEP ACCOMPANIED THE ERUPTION OF A PROMINENCE NEAR REGION
5324 (N24) AT 0155Z. MATERIAL WAS SEEN OUT TO .36 SOLAR RADII.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE THE NEXT THREE DAYS. REGIONS 5329, 5330, AND 5334
SHOULD CONTINUE TO PRODUCE M-CLASS ACTIVITY.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 29/2100Z TO 30/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE 31 JAN. AND 02 FEB. ACTIVE
TO MINOR STORM CONDITIONS ARE LIKELY MIDWAY THROUGH THE
INTERVAL IN RESPONSE TO SOLAR ACTIVITY EARLIER TODAY.
