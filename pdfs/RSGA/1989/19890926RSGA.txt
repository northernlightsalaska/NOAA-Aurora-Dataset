FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 269
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 26 SEP 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 25/2100Z
TO 26/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 5708 (S16E63)
PRODUCED AN M3/1F AT 25/2346Z. THE FLARE WAS ASSOCIATED WITH AN
1100 SFU BURST ON 245 MHZ AND A 120 SFU BURST ON 2695 MHZ. VERY
STRONG CALCIUM XV EMISSION HAS BEEN OBSERVED FROM THIS REGION.
REGION 5698 (S26W62) PRODUCED FOUR C-CLASS FLARES, THE LARGEST
OF WHICH WAS A C8/SF AT 26/1442Z ACCOMPANIED BY A 4200 SFU BURST
ON 245 MHZ. THIS REGION REMAINED COMPLEX, BUT CONTINUED TO
SLOWLY DECAY IN MAGNETIC AND WHITE-LIGHT ASPECTS. NO NEW REGIONS
WERE ASSIGNED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE. REGIONS 5698 AND 5708 ARE CAPABLE OF PRODUCING
M-CLASS ACTIVITY. BOTH REGIONS PROVIDE A SLIGHT CHANCE FOR AN
ISOLATED MAJOR FLARE.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 25/2100Z TO 26/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO MINOR STORM
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS. THE HIGHER LATITUDE FIELD
REACHED MAJOR STORM LEVELS LATE IN THE PERIOD. A SUDDEN
IMPULSE OF 11 NANOTESLA OCCURRED AT BOULDER AT 26/0546Z. THE
PROBABLE SOURCE OF THE STORMING WAS A PAIR OF TYPE II SWEEPS
WHICH OCCURRED ON 23 SEPTEMBER.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED AT MIDDLE LATITUDES. THE HIGHER
LATITUDE FIELD IS EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE TOMORROW
DECLINING TO UNSETTLED LEVELS FOR THE BALANCE OF THE PERIOD.
