SDF NUMBER 219
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 07 AUG 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 06/2100Z
TO 07/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH. REGION 5622 (S27W37)
PRODUCED THE LARGEST EVENT OF THE PERIOD, AN M7/1B FLARE AT
07/2058Z. A 320 SFU TENFLARE AND TYPE IV SWEEP WERE ASSOCIATED
WITH THE EVENT. THIS REGION ALSO PRODUCED AN M4/1N FLARE AT
06/2349Z. THE FLARE WAS ACCOMPANIED BY A 1100 SFU TENFLARE AND
A STRONG TYPE IV SWEEP. REGION 5629 (S16E34) REMAINED THE MOST
COMPLEX GROUP ON THE DISK, ALTHOUGH THERE WERE INDICATIONS THE
REGION WAS BEGINNING TO ENTER A DECAY PHASE. IT PRODUCED AN
M1/1F AT 07/0046Z. REGION 5633 (S27E27) GREW SLOWLY THROUGH THE
PERIOD AND PRODUCED AN M2/1B AT 07/0930Z. A 410 SFU TENFLARE
ACCOMPANIED THE EVENT. NEW REGION 5636 (N34E59), A SMALL
UNIPOLE, WAS NUMBERED TODAY.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MOSTLY MODERATE. REGIONS 5622, 5629, 5633, AND 5634 (S14E57)
ARE CAPABLE OF M-CLASS FLARE ACTIVITY. REGIONS 5622 AND 5629
ARE ALSO CAPABLE OF ISOLATED MAJOR FLARE ACTIVITY.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 06/2100Z TO 07/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO UNSETTLED LEVELS
FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE QUIET TO UNSETTLED AT MIDDLE LATITUDES AND
UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE AT HIGHER LATITUDES.
