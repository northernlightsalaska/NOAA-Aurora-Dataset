SDF NUMBER 319
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 15 NOV 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 14/2100Z
TO 15/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH. REGION 5786 (N13W36)
PRODUCED TWO MAJOR FLARES. THE FIRST WAS AN X3/3B AT 15/0659Z
ACCOMPANIED BY A 1900 SFU TENFLARE AND TYPES II AND IV RADIO
SWEEPS. THE SECOND WAS AN X1/2B AT 15/1939Z. THIS FLARE WAS
ASSOCIATED WITH A 740 SFU TENFLARE AND TYPES II AND IV RADIO
SWEEPS. REGION 5786 MAINTAINED ITS HIGH DEGREE OF MAGNETIC
COMPLEXITY IN THE WAKE OF THESE FLARES. REGION 5783 (N18W77)
PRODUCED NUMEROUS SUBFLARES AS IT CONTINUED IN A DECAY PHASE.
NEW REGIONS 5791 (N28E63) AND 5792 (N10E34) WERE ASSIGNED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
HIGH. REGION 5786 IS LIKELY TO PRODUCE M-CLASS FLARES. AN
ISOLATED X-CLASS FLARE IS POSSIBLE FROM THIS REGION. REGIONS
5783 AND 5788 (S15E02) MAY PRODUCE ISOLATED M-CLASS FLARES.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 14/2100Z TO 15/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET LEVELS FOR THE PAST
24 HOURS. A SATELLITE LEVEL PROTON EVENT AT GREATER THAN
10 MEV BEGAN AT 15/0735Z, REACHED A MAXIMUM OF 71 PFU AT
15/0910Z, AND REMAINED IN PROGRESS AT THE END OF THE PERIOD.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE ACTIVE TOMORROW FOLLOWED BY TWO DAYS OF MINOR
TO MAJOR STORMING. THE STORMING IS EXPECTED IN RESPONSE TO
TODAY'S MAJOR FLARE ACTIVITY. ANOTHER INFLUX OF GREATER THAN
10 MEV PARTICLES IS EXPECTED TO ARRIVE NEAR 15/2200Z IN THE
WAKE OF TODAY'S X1/2B FLARE.
