FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 323
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 19 NOV 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 18/2100Z
TO 19/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH. REGION 5793 (S27W35)
PRODUCED AN X1/2B FLARE AT 0626Z WHICH WAS ACCOMPANIED BY
MODERATE DISCRETE RADIO BURSTS AND A STRONG TYPE II SWEEP.
THE FLARE WAS IMPULSIVE WITH A DURATION OF ONLY 16 MINUTES.
REGION 5793 PRODUCED ADDITIONAL M-CLASS EVENTS DURING THE DAY:
AN M1/SB AT 1134 AND AN M1/SN AT 1613Z. THE  GROUP CONTINUES
TO GROW RAPIDLY AND THERE IS EVIDENCE OF A DELTA CONFIGURATION
FORMING IN THE TRAILER SPOTS. REGION 5799 (N20E38) ALSO SHOWED
SOME GROWTH, AND NEW REGION 5800 (N24E75) ROTATED INTO VIEW
TODAY.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
HIGH. THE RAPID GROWTH AND MAGNETIC COMPLEXITY OF REGION 5693
WITHIN A FAIRLY UNSTABLE CONFIGURATION ALL SUGGEST THAT ADD-
ITIONAL MAJOR FLARES ARE POSSIBLE DURING THE NEXT THREE DAYS.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 18/2100Z TO 19/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS QUIET TO UNSETTLED.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE ACTIVE TOMORROW IN RESPONSE TO THE LONG DURATION
M7 FLARE THAT OCCURRED YESTERDAY. UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE CONDITIONS
ARE EXPECTED FOR THE SECOND AND THIRD DAYS.
