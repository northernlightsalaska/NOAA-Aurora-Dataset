FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 164
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 13 JUN 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 12/2100Z
TO 13/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE.  REGION 5528
(N21E15) WAS THE PRIMARY CONTRIBUTOR TO A LONG DURATION M1
FLARE THAT REACHED A MAXIMUM AT 13/1327Z.  REGIONS 5533
(S19E32) AND NEW REGION 5536 (N16E23) ALSO ADDED TO THIS M-LEVEL
FLARE.  REGION 5536 IS A SIGNIFICANT EMERGENCE OF NEW FLUX NEAR
REGION 5528.  REGION 5528 REMAINS THE LARGEST, MOST COMPLEX
REGION ON THE DISK.  THREE OTHER NEW REGIONS WERE NUMBERED SINCE
YESTERDAY - 5537 (N31E44), 5538 (N19E58), AND 5539 (S06W80).
REGION 5539 EMERGED TO THE WEST OF OLD SPOTLESS REGION 5532
(S11W63).
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE TO HIGH.  THE LIKELIHOOD OF AN ENERGETIC FLARE IN
REGION 5528 HAS INCREASED SINCE THE EMERGENCE OF REGION 5536.
REGION 5533 IS ALSO CAPABLE OF ENERGETIC FLARE ACTIVITY.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 12/2100Z TO 13/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS QUIET TO UNSETTLED.  A SUDDEN IMPULSE
WAS OBSERVED AT MIDDLE LATITUDE STATIONS (7 NANOTESLA AT
BOULDER)  AT 13/1747Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE MOSTLY UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE FOR THE DURATION OF
THIS FORECAST PERIOD.
