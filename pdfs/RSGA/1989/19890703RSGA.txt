FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 184
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 03 JUL 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 02/2100Z
TO 03/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. REGION 5574 (S14E30)
PRODUCED THE EVENT OF THE DAY: A C6/SF AT 0140Z. THE REST
OF THE DAY WAS CHARACTERIZED BY OCCASIONAL SUBFLARES FROM
A VARIETY OF REGIONS, NOTABLY 5575 (N24E53) AND 5576 (N09E58).
SURGE ACTIVITY WAS OBSERVED ON WEST LIMB AT S20, AND N08.
AN ACTIVE PROMINENCE CONTINUED TO BE VISIBLE ON THE EAST LIMB
AT N20-N30. THE REMAINDER OF THE DISK AND LIMBS WERE QUIET AND
STABLE.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW. THERE IS A SLIGHT CHANCE FOR MODERATE LEVELS FROM 5572
(S18E28), 5574, 5575 OR 5576.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 02/2100Z TO 03/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS MOSTLY QUIET.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE OVER THE NEXT THREE DAYS.
THE INCREASE IS EXPECTED AS A RESPONSE TO A HIGH SPEED SOLAR
WIND STREAM ASSOCIATED WITH A FAVORABLY POSITIONED CORONAL
HOLE.
