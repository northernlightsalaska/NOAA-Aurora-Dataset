FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 233
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 21 AUG 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 20/2100Z
TO 21/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. REGION 5644 (N19W40)
PRODUCED THE LARGEST FLARE OF THE PERIOD, A C4/SF AT 20/2302Z.
ONLY A FEW ADDITIONAL SUBFLARES WERE OBSERVED. REGION 5643
(N17W54), THE LARGEST GROUP ON THE DISK, CONTINUES TO DECAY
STEADILY AND QUIETLY. THE REMAINDER OF THE DISK WAS QUIET
AND STABLE.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW. THERE CONTINUES TO BE A SLIGHT CHANCE FOR AN ISOLATED
M-EVENT FROM REGION 5643.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 20/2100Z TO 21/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD RANGED FROM QUIET TO MINOR STORM LEVELS.
CONDITIONS WERE MOSTLY QUIET FROM THE BEGINNING OF THE PERIOD
UNTIL ABOUT 1415Z WHEN A PERIOD OF DISTURBED CONDITIONS
STARTED. FOR THE REMAINDER OF THE DAY MIDDLE LATITUDES WERE
UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE AND HIGH LATITUDES WERE AT ACTIVE TO
MINOR STORM LEVELS. THE DISTURBANCE WAS PROBABLY CAUSED BY A
FILAMENT DISAPPEARANCE NOTED ON 18 AUGUST. A GREATER THAN
10MEV PROTON EVENT CONTINUES IN PROGRESS. FLUX LEVELS
CONTINUED TO DECLINE DURING THE PERIOD AND ARE CURRENTLY
HOVERING 1-5 PFU ABOVE EVENT CRITERIA.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE ACTIVE TOMMOROW AS THE DISTURBANCE CONTINUES.
CONDITIONS ARE EXPECTED TO DECLINE TO UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE
LEVELS FOR THE SECOND DAY AND TO MOSTLY UNSETTLED LEVELS BY
THE THIRD DAY.
