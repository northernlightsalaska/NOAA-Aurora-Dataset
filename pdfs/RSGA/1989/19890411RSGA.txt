FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 101
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 11 APR 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 10/2100Z
TO 11/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. AN M1 X-RAY FLARE,
ASSOCIATED WITH THE ERUPTION OF A FILAMENT LOCATED NEAR N69E24,
OCCURRED AT 10/2323Z. LITTLE FLARE ACTIVITY OF SIGNIFICANCE
OCCURRED SINCE THAT TIME. REGIONS 5441 (N34W15) AND 5438
(S16W36), THE TWO MOST INTERESTING REGIONS VISIBLE, DECAYED AND
SIMPLIFIED.  THREE NEW REGIONS WERE ASSIGNED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE.  REGIONS 5441 AND 5438 ARE STILL CAPABLE OF
AN ISOLATED M-CLASS FLARE.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 10/2100Z TO 11/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS MOSTLY QUIET TO UNSETTLED AT ALL
LATITUDES UNTIL A 10 NANOTESLA SUDDEN IMPULSE WAS DETECTED AT
GEOSYNCHRONOUS ORBIT AT 1437Z. A SATELLITE PROTON EVENT AT
GREATER THAN 10 MEV BEGAN SHORTLY THEREAFTER WITH THE MAXIMUM
TO THIS POINT 130 PFU AT 2030Z. A POLAR CAP ABSORPTION BEGAN
AROUND 1900Z AND IS STILL IN PROGRESS. ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM
LEVELS HAVE PREVAILED SINCE THE SUDDEN IMPULSE. THIS ACTIVITY
IS THOUGHT TO BE ASSOCIATED WITH THE X3/4B FLARE EARLY ON
09 APRIL.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM LEVELS THE NEXT 24
HOURS. THE DISTURBANCE SHOULD THEN ABATE WITH UNSETTLED TO
ACTIVE CONDITIONS EVIDENT 14 APRIL.
