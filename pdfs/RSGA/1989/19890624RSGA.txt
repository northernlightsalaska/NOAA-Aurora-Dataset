FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 175
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 24 JUN 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 23/2100Z
TO 24/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE.  REGION 5555 (N27E16)
PRODUCED THE DAYS ONLY M FLARE, AN M2.4 AT 0318UT.  REGION 5555
IS SLOWLY CHANGING; THERE IS EVIDENCE ON SHEAR OCCURRING ALONG
ITS NORTHERN BORDER AND GROWTH WAS REPORTED EAST OF THE LEADER.
A DELTA IS STILL PRESENT IN THIS REGION.  TRAILER POLARITY SPOTS
HAVE GROWN BETWEEN REGIONS 5552 (S18E07) AND REGION 5556
(S19E18).  SMALL FLARES HAVE BEEN OCCURRING IN THE LONG STRING
OF REGIONS IN THE SE WHICH EXTENDS FROM REGION 5552 TO REGION
5563 (S15E46).  NEW REGION 5566 (N14E22) WAS ASSIGNED.  A
PROMINENCE ON THE EAST LIMB NEAR S52 ERUPTED EARLY IN THE DAY.
A POSSIBLE TYPE II FOLLOWED BY TYPE IV IS IN PROGRESS.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE.  REGION 5555 AND TO A LESSER EXTENT THE REGIONS
IN THE SE ARE CAPABLE OF ISOLATED ENERGETIC ACTIVITY.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 23/2100Z TO 24/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO UNSETTLED LEVELS
FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE.  THERE IS A CHANCE OF
INCREASED ACTIVITY LATE IN THE PERIOD FROM TWO SMALL CORONAL
HOLES JUST CROSSING CENTERAL MERIDIAN AND THE UNKNOWN SOURCE
OF THE TYPE II AND IV IN PROGRESS.
