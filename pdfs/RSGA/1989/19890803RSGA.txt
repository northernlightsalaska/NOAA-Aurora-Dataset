IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 02/2100Z
TO 03/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE.  TWO M-CLASS FLARES
OCCURRED DURING THE PAST 24 HOURS - AN M2/1B AT 02/2312Z IN
REGION 5623 (S15E37) AND AN OPTICALLY UNCORRELATED M1 AT
03/0750Z.  REGION 5612 (S18W14), THE LARGEST REGION PRESENTLY
ON THE DISK, REMAINS RELATIVELY QUIET.  NEW REGIONS NUMBERED
TODAY INCLUDE 5628 (N10E09), 5629 (S17E73), AND 5630 (N21E53).
REGION 5629 APPEARS TO BE A LARGE, AND POSSIBLY COMPLEX, RETURN
OF OLD REGION 5586 (S18, L-077).  THIS REGION PRODUCED ONE
M-CLASS FLARE DURING ITS LAST DISK PASSAGE.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE.  THE GENERAL LEVEL OF ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED
TO INCREASE OVER THE NEXT FEW DAYS.  MODERATE ACTIVITY IS
POSSIBLE FROM REGIONS 5612, 5623, AND 5629.  REGION 5629 MAY
BE CAPABLE OF MAJOR FLARE ACTIVITY.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 02/2100Z TO 03/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS AT QUIET TO UNSETTLED LEVELS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE QUIET TO UNSETTLED FOR THE NEXT 24 HOURS.
UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE CONDITIONS ARE POSSIBLE DURING THE
