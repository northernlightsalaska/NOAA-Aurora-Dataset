FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 064
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 05 MAR 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 04/2100Z
TO 05/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE DUE TO AN
OPTICALLY UNCORRELATED M1 FLARE AT 05/0222Z.  DISK REGIONS
HAVE CHANGED LITTLE SINCE YESTERDAY.  ACTIVE SURGING AND
SPRAY ACTIVITY FROM BEHIND THE EAST LIMB WERE OBSERVED AT
ABOUT NE28 AND MAY BE THE SOURCE OF THE M1 FLARE MENTIONED
ABOVE.  ANOTHER ACTIVE REGION APPEARS TO BE NEARING THE EAST
LIMB AT SE30.  NEW DISK REGIONS 5392 AND 5393 WERE NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE.  THE LIKELIHOOD OF MODERATE ACTIVITY SHOULD INCREASE
AS THE ACTIVE REGIONS NEARING THE EAST LIMB ROTATE ONTO THE
DISK.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 04/2100Z TO 05/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD RANGED FROM QUIET TO ACTIVE LEVELS
WITH PERIODS OF MAJOR STORM CONDITIONS AT HIGH LATITUDES.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE.
