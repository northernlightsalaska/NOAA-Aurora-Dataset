FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 148
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 28 MAY 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 27/2100Z
TO 28/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 5497 (S19W75)
PRODUCED AN M4/1N FLARE AT 28/1228Z. THE EVENT HAD SEVERAL PEAKS
AND WAS ACCOMPANIED BY A MODERATE INTENSITY TYPE II BURST AND A
BRIGHT SURGE SEEN TO .21R ABOVE THE LIMB. REGION 5497 MAY BE
DECAYING SLOWLY BUT LIMB PROXIMITY MADE VERIFICATION DIFFICULT.
OTHER DISK REGIONS SHOWED LITTLE CHANGE AND PRODUCED ONLY MINOR
SUBFLARES.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY SHOULD BE LOW TO
MODERATE FOR THE NEXT THREE DAYS. INFREQUENT CLASS M FLARES
ARE POSSIBLE FROM REGION 5497 FOR THE NEXT TWO DAYS. FOLLOWING
WEST LIMB TRANSIT OF THIS REGION ON 30 MAY, ONLY CLASS C
FLARES ARE EXPECTED FROM CURRENT DISK REGIONS.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 27/2100Z TO 28/2100Z:
THE MID LATITUDE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS PREDOMINANTLY
UNSETTLED WHILE THE HIGH LATITUDE FIELD WAS PREDOMINANTLY
ACTIVE.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD
SHOULD CONTINUE BEING UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE FOR THE DURATION
OF THE FORECAST PERIOD.
