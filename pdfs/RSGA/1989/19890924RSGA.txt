IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 23/2100Z
TO 24/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 5698 (S26W37)
PRODUCED AN M1/SF AT 24/0821Z ACCOMPANIED BY MODEST DISCRETE
RADIO EMISSIONS. THE REGION WAS RELATIVELY STABLE DESPITE ITS
FORMIDABLE SIZE AND MAGNETIC COMPLEXITY. AN OPTICALLY
UNCORRELATED M1 X-RAY BURST OCCURRED AT 24/2044Z. BRIGHT SURGING
WAS OBSERVED FROM THE SOUTHEAST LIMB POSSIBLY HERALDING THE
RETURN OF OLD 5669. NEW REGION 5707 (S15W30) WAS NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE. REGION 5698 PROVIDES A GOOD CHANCE FOR AN M-CLASS
FLARE. A MAJOR FLARE MAY ALSO OCCUR FROM THIS REGION.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 23/2100Z TO 24/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET LEVELS FOR THE PAST
24 HOURS. SOME PERIODS OF UNSETTLED LEVELS OCCURRED AT HIGHER
LATITUDES.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE ACTIVE DURING THE FIRST TWO DAYS OF THE PERIOD.
THE FINAL DAY IS EXPECTED TO BE QUIET TO UNSETTLED.
