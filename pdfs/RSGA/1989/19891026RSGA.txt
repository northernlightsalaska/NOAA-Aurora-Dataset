FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 299
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 26 OCT 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 25/2100Z
TO 26/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH. REGION 5747 (S30W84)
PRODUCED A VERY IMPULIVE M8/SF AT 26/2048Z. A 10CM BURST OF
870 FLUX UNITS ACCOMPANIED THE FLARE AS DID MAJOR BURSTS AT
HIGHER WAVELENGTHS. REGION 5747 WAS NEARLY QUIET DURING THE
PERIOD PRIOR TO THIS EVENT. SMALL NEW REGIONS 5763 (N22E34)
AND 5764 (N25E83) WERE NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY SHOULD BE
PREDOMINANTLY MODERATE FOR 27-28 OCT WITH A CHANCE OF AN
ISOLATED CLASS X FLARE FROM DEPARTING REGION 5747. X-RAY
BURST ACTIVITY SHOULD DECREASE BEGINNING ON 29 OCT AS
REGION 5747 ROTATES COMPLETELY FROM VIEW.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 25/2100Z TO 26/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE DURING THE
FIRST HALF OF THE PERIOD. A SUDDEN IMPULSE OF 14 GAMMA WAS
OBSERVED IN BOULDER AT 26/1428Z. ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM
CONDITIONS WERE EXPERIENCED FOLLOWING THE SHOCK. THIS
DISTURBANCE IS ATTRIBUTED TO THE X5 FLARE ON 24 OCT. THE GT
10MEV PROTON EVENT AND PCA REMAINED IN PROGRESS. THE GT 10MEV
FLUX AT 25/2100Z WAS 1400 P.F.U. AND DECAYED SMOOTHLY TO
330 P.F.U. AT THE END OF THE PERIOD.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE MID LATITUDE
GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS FORECAST TO BE ACTIVE FOR 27-28 OCT
CALMING TO UNSETTLED ON 29 OCT. AT HIGH LATITUDES, MINOR
TO MAJOR STORM CONDITIONS ARE EXPECTED FOR 27-28 OCT ALSO
CALMING TO MOSTLY UNSETTLED LEVELS ON 29 OCT. THE GT 10MEV
PROTON EVENT AND PCA ARE EXPECTED TO CONTINUE THROUGH 27 OCT.
