FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 171
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 20 JUN 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 19/2100Z
TO 20/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH. REGION 5528 (N21W70)
PRODUCED AN X1/3B FLARE THAT MAXED AT 1508 UT. AN ERUPTIVE
PROMINENCE ON THE LIMB WITH THE FLARE EXTENDED TO 0.55 SOLAR
RADII. RADIO BURSTS AND SWEEPS ASSOCIATED WITH THE EVENT WERE
NOT IMPRESSIVE ALTHOUGH A MODERATE TYPE IV SWEEP PRECEDED THE
FLARE ONSET. FLARE LOOPS AND A BRIGHT SURGE ON THE DISK WERE
ALSO OBSERVED. A DARK FILAMENT NEAR S33W23 ERUPTED BETWEEN
1237 AND 1500 UT. THE ERUPTION WAS CAUGHT IN PROGRESS ON THE
BOULDER PHOTO TAKEN AT 1417 UT. TWO NEW REGIONS WERE NUMBERED.
REGION 5554 (S26W04) EMERGED ON THE DISK LATE ON 19 JUNE AND
REGION 5555 (N26E74) ROTATED OVER THE EAST LIMB TODAY.
5555 IS LOCATED NEAR THE POSITIONS OF OLD REGIONS 5506 AND 5507.
SURGING ON THE EAST LIMB NEAR S18 MAY BE ASSOCIATED WITH THE
RETURN OF OLD REGION 5511.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE.  SIGNIFICANT FLARES ARE POSSIBLE FROM REGION 5528
UNTIL IT IS AT LEAST ONE DAY BEYOND WEST LIMB PASSAGE.  NO
OTHER REGION ON THE VISIBLE DISK HAS THE IMPRESSIVE QUALITIES
USUALLY ASSOCIATED WITH MAJOR FLARE ACTIVITY.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 19/2100Z TO 20/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE LEVELS
FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS. PERIODS AT MINOR STORM LEVELS WERE
REPORTED AFTER 0900 UT. THIS ACTIVITY IS ATTRIBUTED TO THE
EFFECTS OF MAJOR FLARES ON 16 AND 17 JUNE.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE MOSTLY UNSETTLED TOMORROW AND THE NEXT DAY,
BUT IS EXPECTED TO REACH MINOR TO MAJOR STORM LEVELS ON JUNE
23 AND 24 IN RESPONSE TO TODAY'S FLARE AND/OR FILAMENT
DISAPPEARANCE.
