SDF NUMBER 139
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 19 MAY 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 18/2100Z
TO 19/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW.  INTERMITTENT C-CLASS
X-RAY FLARES CONTINUED IN REGIONS 5488 (N17W14) AND
5495 (N22W10).  REGIONS 5495 AND 5497 (S21E43) ARE EMERGING
RAPIDLY.  REGION 5488 HAS SHOWN SIGNS OF DECAY.  NEW REGION
5498 (N32E21) WAS NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW.  INCREASED FREQUENCY OF C-CLASS X-RAY FLARES IS EXPECTED.
AN ISOLATED M-CLASS FLARE IS POSSIBLE.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 18/2100Z TO 19/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS MOSTLY QUIET.  THE DISTURBANCE
FORECAST AS A RESULT OF THE 17 MAY DSF/LDE HAS NOT YET
MATERIALIZED.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE MOSTLY UNSETTLED.  A DISTURBANCE FROM THE
DSF/LDE MENTIONED ABOVE IS INCREASINGLY UNLIKELY.
