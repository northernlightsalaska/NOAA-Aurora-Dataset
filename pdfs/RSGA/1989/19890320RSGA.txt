FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 079
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 20 MAR 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 19/2100Z
TO 20/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. NEW REGION 5417
(S26E74) ROTATED ONTO THE DISK AND PROMPTLY PRODUCED 3 M-LEVEL
FLARES, AN M2/SN AT 20/0401Z, AN M1/SF AT 20/1440Z AND AN M2/SF
AT 20/1547Z. REGION 5416 (N20E04) WAS MERGED WITH REGION 5409
(N18E08). BOTH OF THESE REGIONS ARE NOW CLASSIFIED AS REGION
5409. FREQUENT SURGING AND LOOPS HAVE BEEN OBSERVED ON THE WEST
LIMB IN THE VICINITY OF REGION 5395 (N34).
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO
REMAIN MODERATE. BOTH REGION 5417 AND 5409 (N18E08) HAVE
EXHIBITED THE POTENTIAL TO PRODUCE ENERGETIC EVENTS.
REGION 5395 STILL POSES A SLIGHT THREAT FOR A BEHIND-THE-LIMB
EVENT.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 19/2100Z TO 20/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN MOSTLY QUIET TO UNSETTLED AT
MIDDLE LATITUDES AND UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE AT HIGH LATITUDES.
THE POLAR CAP ABSORPTION EVENT ENDED AT 20/0600Z. THE
PROTON EVENT ENDED AT 20/1720Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED AT MIDDLE LATITUDES AND ACTIVE TO
MINOR STORM AT HIGH LATITUDES.
