FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO

JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 24 MAR 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 23/2100Z
TO 24/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 5409 (N18W45)
PRODUCED TODAY'S ONLY M-CLASS FLARE: AN M1/2B AT 2031Z. THE
EVENT WAS ACCOMPANIED BY STRONG TYPE II AND TYPE IV SWEEPS AND
MODERATE DISCRETE RADIO BURSTS. THE REGION CONTINUED TO SHOW
SIGNS OF DECAY AND FRAGMENTATION DURING THE PERIOD. THE DISK
AND LIMBS WERE OTHERWISE REMARKABLY QUIET, WITH ONLY SUBFLARE
FLUCTUATIONS FROM 5409, 5417 (S24E25), 5411 (N14W21), AND
5420 (N16W11).
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. REGION 5409 IS CONSIDERED TO BE THE PRINCIPAL
THREAT FOR AN ISOLATED M-CLASS EVENT.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 23/2100Z TO 24/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS AT PREDOMINANTLY ACTIVE LEVELS EARLY
IN THE PERIOD BUT HAS BEEN QUIET TO UNSETTLED SINCE 0900Z.
A BRIEF MINOR STORM PERIOD OCCURRED FROM 0000-0300Z. THE
PROTON EVENT OF GREATER THAN 10 PFU AT GREATER THAN OR EQUAL
TO 10MEV WHICH BEGAN AT 23/2040Z REACHED A MAXIMUM OF 53 PFU
AT 24/0110Z AND ENDED AT 24/0845Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM LEVELS FOR THE NEXT
TWO DAYS IN RESPONSE TO YESTERDAY'S X1/3B FLARE. THE DIS-
TURBANCE IS EXPECTED TO BEGIN LATE TOMORROW, REACH MAXIMUM
INTENSITY ON THE SECOND DAY, AND GRADUALLY SUBSIDE THROUGHOUT
THE THIRD DAY.
