JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 15 DEC 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 14/2100Z
TO 15/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. REGION 5837 (N20E59)
PRODUCED A C7/1N FLARE AT 15/1400Z WITH MINOR RADIO BURSTS.
REGION 5836 (N12E42) PRODUCED ONLY MINOR SUBFLARES AND THE
REGION WAS STABLE DURING THE PERIOD.  NEW REGION 5843
(S05E77) ROTATED ONTO THE DISK AS A MODERATE SIZE CLASS
H SPOT AND IS NEAR WHERE THE LEADER OF OLD REGION 5808 WOULD
BE IF REGION ELONGATION CONTINUED ON THE INVISIBLE HEMISPHERE.
NO ENERGETIC ACTIVITY WAS OBSERVED FROM THIS AREA.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY SHOULD REACH
MODERATE LEVELS DURING THE FORECAST PERIOD. REGION 5836 IS
CAPABLE OF ISOLATED CLASS M FLARES. NEW REGION 5843 MAY BE
TOO STRETCHED OUT TO BE A FREQUENT CLASS M FLARE PRODUCER.
OLD REGION 5800 IS ALSO DUE BACK DURING THE PERIOD AND MAY
INCREASE THE FREQUENCY OF MODERATE SIZE FLARES.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 14/2100Z TO 15/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS QUIET TO UNSETTLED FOR THE PAST
24 HOURS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD
SHOULD BE PREDOMINANTLY UNSETTLED FOR THE DURATION OF THE
FORECAST PERIOD.
