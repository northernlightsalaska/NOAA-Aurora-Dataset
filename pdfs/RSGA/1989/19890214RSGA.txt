JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 14 FEB 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 13/2100Z
TO 14/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE.  REGION 5354 (N31W45)
PRODUCED THE ONLY SIGNIFICANT FLARE DURING THE PERIOD; AN M2/1F
AT 14/0519UT.  REGION 5354 CONTINUED TO SUPPORT ITS MAGNETICALLY
COMPLEX DELTA CONFIGURATION WHILE EXHIBITING SIGNS OF DECAY.
REGION 5356 (S17W06) WAS SECOND IN RELATIVE ACTIVITY, PRODUCING
A FEW C FLARES DURING THE PERIOD AND CONTINUING ITS DEVELOPMENT.
TWO NEW REGIONS WERE NUMBERED:  REGION 5366 (N24W06), A DSO
BETA TYPE SPOT GROUP, AND REGION 5367 (N30E26), A SMALL BXO
BETA GROUP.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE.  FURTHER GROWTH AND ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED FROM
REGIONS 5356 AND 5366 WHILE REGION 5354 IS EXPECTED TO DECLINE
IN RELATIVE ACTIVITY AS IT ROTATES OFF THE DISK.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 13/2100Z TO 14/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN GENERALLY UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE
DURING THE PAST 24 HOURS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE GENERALLY UNSETTLED DURING THE NEXT THREE DAYS
WITH ACTIVE CONDITIONS EXPECTED AT HIGH LATITUDES TOWARD THE
END OF THE PERIOD.
