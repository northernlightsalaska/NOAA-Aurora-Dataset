FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 333
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 29 NOV 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 28/2100Z
TO 29/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. REGION 5809 (S18E31) PRO-
DUCED TODAY'S LARGEST EVENT: A C9/1F AT 1816Z. THE GROUP CON-
TINUED TO GROW SLOWLY TODAY BUT REMAINS SMALL AND ONLY PRODUCED
A COUPLE OF ADDITIONAL SUBFLARES. REGION 5800 (N26W46) CONTINUED
TO SLOWLY DECAY AND PRODUCED A FEW SUBFLARES. A TOTAL OF 5 NEW
REGIONS WERE ASSIGNED TODAY: 4 OF THESE ARE NEWLY EMERGED AND
ONE ROTATED INTO VIEW FROM THE EAST LIMB AT N13. THESE REGIONS
AS WELL AS THE REMAINING REGIONS ON THE DISK WERE STABLE AND
QUIET.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW, BUT WITH A CONTINUED CHANCE FOR MODERATE LEVELS. REGIONS
5809, 5800, AND 5808 (S05W62) APPEAR TO BE MAIN GROUPS ON THE
DISK WITH FLARE POTENTIAL. OLD REGION 5783 (N18, L-299) WHICH
PRODUCED SEVERAL M-CLASS AND ONE X-CLASS FLARE ON ITS PREVIOUS
TRANSIT IS DUE TO RETURN TOMORROW AND MAY ALSO CONTRIBUTE TO
SOLAR ACTIVITY WHEN IT APPEARS.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 28/2100Z TO 29/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS MOSTLY UNSETTLED WITH A FEW ACTIVE
PERIODS. THE DISTURBANCE WHICH BEGAN AT 27/2141Z APPEARS TO
BE GRADUALLY SUBSIDING. THE GREATER THAN 10 MEV PROTON EVENT
WHICH BEGAN AT 27/2000Z ENDED AT 29/1555Z. THE PEAK FLUX WAS
380 PFU AT 28/1105Z. A FORBUSH DECREASE BEGAN AT ABOUT 28/10Z,
AND ATTAINED A MAXIMUM DEPRESSION OF ABOUT 14% NEAR 29/07Z.
THE LEVELS ARE RECOVERING BUT CONTINUE TO BE DEPRESSED AT THIS
TIME.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO OCCASIONALLY ACTIVE TOMORROW AND
IS EXPECTED TO BE MOSTLY UNSETTLED ON THE SECOND AND THIRD
DAYS.
