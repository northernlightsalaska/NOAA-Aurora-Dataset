FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 177
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 26 JUN 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 25/2100Z
TO 26/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. NEWLY NUMBERED REGION
5569 (N19E27) PRODUCED AN M1/1N FLARE AT 1600Z. THIS REGION HAS
GROWN RAPIDLY IN BOTH SPOT AND MAGNETIC STRUCTURE. REGIONS 5552
(S18W18) AND 5555 (N26W09) STILL SHOW SUFFICIENT COMPLEXITY FOR
M-CLASS ACTIVITY BUT HAVE BEEN RELATIVELY QUIET THE PAST 24
HOURS. BESIDES REGION 5569 TWO ADDITIONAL SPOT GROUPS WERE
NUMBERED: 5568 (S29W42) AND 5570 (N10W17).
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE. REGIONS 5552, 5555 AND 5569 SHOULD BE THE
CONTRIBUTORS OF M-CLASS ACTIVITY.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 25/2100Z TO 26/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO UNSETTLED
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT QUIET TO UNSETTLED LEVELS. THE EFFECTS OF A
HIGH LATITUDE CORONAL HOLE ARE POSSIBLE BUT DUE TO ITS POSITION
SHOULDN'T DISTURB THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD.
