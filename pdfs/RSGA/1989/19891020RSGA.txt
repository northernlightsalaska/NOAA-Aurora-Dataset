FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 293
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 20 OCT 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 19/2100Z
TO 20/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 5747 (S26W12)
DISPLAYED SLIGHT DECAY BUT REMAINED VERY LARGE AND COMPLEX. IT
PRODUCED TWO M-CLASS FLARES DURING THE PERIOD. THE FIRST
WAS AN M1/SN AT 20/0341Z AND THE SECOND WAS AN M1/1B AT
20/1512Z. NO SIGNIFICANT RADIO EMISSIONS WERE ASSOCIATED WITH
EITHER OF THESE FLARES. THE REMAINING REGIONS WERE EITHER
STABLE OR DECLINING. NEW REGIONS 5754 (S20E66) AND 5755 (S15W24)
WERE ASSIGNED TODAY.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE TO HIGH. REGION 5747 IS CAPABLE OF PRODUCING M-CLASS
FLARES. AN X-CLASS FLARE MAY ALSO OCCUR FROM THIS REGION. NEW
REGION 5754 PROVIDES A VERY SLIGHT CHANCE FOR A M-FLARE.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 19/2100Z TO 20/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO SEVERE STORM
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS. THE DISTURBANCE BEGAN SOMETIME
BETWEEN 20/0900-1200Z (NO SUDDEN STORM COMMENCEMENT WAS
OBSERVED). THE STORM WAS A RESULT OF THE X13/4B FLARE THAT
OCCURRED YESTERDAY. THE SATELLITE PROTON
EVENT THAT BEGAN YESTERDAY REMAINED IN PROGRESS. THE MAXIMUM
GREATER THAN 10 MEV FLUX WAS 73000 PFU AT 20/1530Z. THE
MAXIMUM GREATER THAN 100 MEV FLUX WAS 680 PFU AT 20/1530Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT MAJOR TO SEVERE STORM LEVELS TOMORROW. MINOR
TO MAJOR STORM LEVELS ARE POSSIBLE AT HIGHER LATITUDES FOR
THE REMAINDER OF THE PERIOD WHILE THE MIDDLE AND LOWER
LATITUDES ARE EXPECTED TO BE AT MOSTLY ACTIVE LEVELS. THE
PROTON EVENT IS EXPECTED TO CONTINUE.
