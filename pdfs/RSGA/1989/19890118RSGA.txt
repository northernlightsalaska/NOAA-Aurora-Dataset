FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 018
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 18 JAN 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 17/2100Z
TO 18/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH. REGION 5312 (S34W69)
PRODUCED TWO MAJOR FLARES DURING THE PERIOD. THE FIRST WAS AN
X1/1F FLARE AT 18/0718Z ACCOMPANIED BY MINOR DISCRETE RADIO
BURSTS. THE SECOND WAS AN M9/1N AT 18/0908Z, ALSO ACCOMPANIED
BY MINOR DISCRETE BURSTS. SPOTLESS REGION 5318 (N28W32) ALSO
PRODUCED A MAJOR FLARE: AN M9/3B AT 18/1826Z WHICH WAS
ACCOMPANIED BY A 1500 SFU TENFLARE, OTHER MINOR DISCRETE RADIO
BURSTS, AND A TYPE II SWEEP BURST. ANOTHER TYPE II SWEEP
OCCURRED EARLIER IN THE DAY AT 18/0026Z. THIS WAS ASSOCIATED
WITH AN OPTICALLY UNCORRELATED C5 X-RAY BURST. NO NEW REGIONS
WERE ASSIGNED TODAY.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
HIGH. THE 5317 (N21W41)/5318 COMPLEX APPEARS CAPABLE OF
PRODUCING MORE MAJOR FLARE ACTIVITY. THE SAME IS TRUE OF REGION
5312.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 17/2100Z TO 18/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT MOSTLY UNSETTLED
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM LEVELS. THE BRUNT OF
THE MINOR STORMING IS EXPECTED ON 20 JANUARY IN RESPONSE TO
TODAY'S M9/3B FLARE. A PROTON EVENT AT GREATER THAN 10 MEV IS
EXPECTED TO BEGIN NEAR 18/2200Z AND REACH A MAXIMUM FLUX OF
84 P/CM-2/SEC/STER NEAR 19/0100Z. THIS EVENT IS ALSO EXPECTED
IN RESPONSE TO TODAY'S M9/3B FLARE.
