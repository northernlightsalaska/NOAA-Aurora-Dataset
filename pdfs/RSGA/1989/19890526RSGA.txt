FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 146
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 26 MAY 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 25/2100Z
TO 26/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. REGION 5497 (S18W48) WAS
STABLE DURING THE PERIOD. SOME MIXED POLARITIES REMAINED IN THIS
REGION BUT ONLY SMALL CLASS C FLARES WERE PRODUCED. REGION 5495
(N24W96) MADE WEST LIMB TRANSIT EXHIBITING ONLY MINOR SURGING.
A LARGE FILAMENT NEAR S48W25-S37W61 FADED BETWEEN 25/2312-2338Z.
AN AVERAGE VELOCITY CORONAL MASS EJECTION WAS OBSERVED BY THE
SMM CORONAGRAPH WITH THIS DISAPPEARANCE.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY SHOULD RESUME
MODERATE LEVELS. REGION 5497 SHOULD PRODUCE ISOLATED CLASS
M FLARES. A CLASS X EVENT FROM REGION 5497 IS POSSIBLE PRIOR
TO ITS WEST LIMB TRANSIT.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 25/2100Z TO 26/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS UNSETTLED AT MID LATITUDES AND
SLIGHTLY ACTIVE AT HIGH LATITUDES.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD
SHOULD CONTINUE AT UNSETTLED TO SLIGHTLY ACTIVE LEVELS FOR
THE NEXT THREE DAYS.
