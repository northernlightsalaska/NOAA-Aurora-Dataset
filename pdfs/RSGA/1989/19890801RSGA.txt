SDF NUMBER 213
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 01 AUG 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 31/2100Z
TO 01/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 5623 (S14E65)
PRODUCED TODAY'S ONLY M-CLASS EVENT: AN M1/1N AT 2324Z. THE
REGION CONTINUED TO PRODUCE OCCASIONAL SMALL C-CLASS FLARES
AS IT ROTATED INTO VIEW AS A SMALL D-TYPE GROUP. REGION
5612 (S17E12) IS THE LARGEST GROUP ON THE DISK WITH AN AREA
OF 620, BUT CONTRIBUTED ONLY A FEW SMALL FLARES. SURGE ACTIVITY
ON THE EAST LIMB NEAR N20 APPEARS TO BE PRECEDING A NEW REGION
WHICH WAS JUST ROTATING INTO VIEW AT FORECAST ISSUE TIME. THE
REMAINDER OF THE DISK AND LIMBS WERE ESSENTIALLY QUIET.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. REGIONS 5612 AND 5623 ARE THE PRIMARY THREATS
FOR M-CLASS FLARES.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 31/2100Z TO 01/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS MOSTLY QUIET. SOME BRIEF PERIODS
OF UNSETTLED LEVELS WERE REPORTED AT HIGH LATITUDES.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE QUIET TO UNSETTLED.
