JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 15 JUL 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 14/2100Z
TO 15/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. ONLY MINOR CLASS C
SUBFLARES WERE OBSERVED. NEW REGIONS ASSIGNED TODAY WERE 5595
(S09E54), 5596 (N18E74), AND 5597 (S11E80).
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY SHOULD
CONTINUE LOW. REGION 5589 (N28E19) COULD PRODUCE AN
ISOLATED LOW LEVEL CLASS M FLARE.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 14/2100Z TO 15/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS UNSETTLED AT MIDDLE LATITUDES.
THE HIGH LATITUDE FIELD WAS ALSO UNSETTLED UNTIL LATE IN
THE PERIOD WHEN ACTIVE LEVELS WERE OBSERVED.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD
SHOULD BE SLIGHTLY ACTIVE ON 16-17 JUL IN RESPONSE TO
DISAPPEARING FILAMENTS ON 12 JUL. GENERALLY UNSETTLED
CONDITIONS ARE EXPECTED FOR 18 JUL.
