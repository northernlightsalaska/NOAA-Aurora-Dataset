JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 14 DEC 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 13/2100Z
TO 14/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 5836 (N12E55)
PRODUCED AN M1/1F AT 14/0216Z WITH AN IMPRESSIVE BRIGHT SURGE
TO ALMOST 0.5R ABOVE THE LIMB. A MODERATE INTENSITY TYPE II
ACCOMPANIED THE FLARE AND SURGE, WHILE ONLY WEAK CENTIMETER
BURSTS WERE OBSERVED. THIS REGION ALSO PRODUCED SEVERAL SMALL
CLASS C FLARES. A SMALL AMOUNT OF MIXED POLARITIES WERE SEEN
IN THAT REGION. REGION 5837 (N20E71) SHOWED ITSELF AS A SMALL
CLASS H SPOT GROUP AND PRODUCED MINOR SUBFLARES. SEVERAL SMALL
NEW REGIONS EMERGED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY SHOULD BE
PREDOMINANTLY LOW. REGION 5836 IS EXPECTED TO PRODUCE
INFREQUENT CLASS M FLARES. TWO REGIONS (5808, S05, L-093
AND 5800 N25, L-080) ARE DUE TO RETURN AROUND 16 DEC AND
COULD ADD TO CLASS M FLARE PRODUCTION.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 13/2100Z TO 14/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS QUIET UNTIL NEAR 14/1030Z WHEN
ACTIVE CONDITIONS BEGAN. THIS DISTURBANCE ENDED NEAR 14/1800Z
AND UNSETTLED LEVELS WERE EXPERIENCED AT THE END OF THE PERIOD.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
FORECAST TO BE PRIMARILY UNSETTLED FOR THE DURATION OF THE
FORECAST PERIOD.
