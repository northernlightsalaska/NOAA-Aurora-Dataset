IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 12/2100Z
TO 13/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW WITH ONLY OCCASIONAL
C-CLASS SUBFLARE FLUCTUATIONS DURING THE PERIOD. THE LARGEST
WAS A C4/1N PRODUCED AT 1503Z BY NEW REGION 5488 (N16E65)
WHICH ROTATED INTO VIEW TODAY. SURGE ACTIVITY OCCURRED ON
THE WEST LIMB AT N23 ASSOCIATED WITH THE DEPARTURE OF REGION
5470 AND ON THE EAST LIMB ASSOCIATED WITH THE ARRIVAL OF
REGION 5488. THE REMAINDER OF THE SOLAR DISK AND LIMBS WERE
ESSENTIALLY QUIET AND STABLE.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
GENERALLY LOW. THERE IS A FAIR CHANCE FOR AN ISOLATED M-CLASS
EVENT FROM REGION 5476 (S30W42), 5484 (N09E13), OR 5488 OVER
THE NEXT THREE DAYS.

IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
