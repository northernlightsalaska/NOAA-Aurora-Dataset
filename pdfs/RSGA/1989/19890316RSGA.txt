FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 075
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 16 MAR 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 15/2100Z
TO 16/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH. REGION 5395 (N33W52) HAS
CHANGED VERY LITTLE OVERNIGHT. IT PRODUCED ANOTHER LARGE, MAJOR
FLARE LATE IN THE PERIOD, AN X3/2B FLARE. THIS FLARE CONTAINED
MAJOR RADIO BURSTS, BUT NO SIGNIFICANT RADIO SWEEPS. IT PEAKED
AT 16/1527Z. OTHER MAJOR AND MINOR FLARES WERE ALSO PRODUCED BY
THE REGION IN THE PERIOD. IT RETAINED THE VERY LARGE SIZE AND
COMPLEX CLASSIFICATION CONSISTENT WITH IT'S DISK HISTORY.
THE 10.7 CM FLUX WAS NOT AVAILABLE.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
HIGH. REGION 5395 STILL PROVIDES A GOOD CHANCE OF A PROTON
PRODUCING FLARE, AND A CONTINUED VERY GOOD CHANCE OF MAJOR
FLARE ACTIVITY.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 15/2100Z TO 16/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS AT GENERALLY MINOR STORM LEVELS. A
SUDDEN IMPULSE OCCURRED AT 16/0532Z WITH A 67 NANOTESLA JUMP.
MAJOR PERIODS FOLLOWED AT MIDDLE AND HIGH LATITUDES. THIS IS
CONSIDERED A CONTINUATION OF THE SEVERE STORM THAT STARTED
MONDAY. THE FORBUSH DECREASE REMAINS IN PROGRESS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE ACTIVE. THE ALMOST CONTINUAL MAJOR FLARE ACTIVITY
RECENTLY, SUPPORTS ENHANCED GEOMAGNETIC LEVELS ABOVE RECURRENT
UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE CONDITIONS. THE LACK OF RELIABLE STORM INDI-
CATORS PRECLUDES FORCASTING GREATER LEVELS.
