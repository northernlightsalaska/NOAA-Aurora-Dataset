IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 29/2100Z
TO 30/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW.  REGION 5428 (S19E67)
IS NOW IN VIEW AND APPEARS TO BE A FAIRLY LARGE (ABOUT 1000
MILLIONTHS) F-TYPE SUNSPOT GROUP.  SO FAR IT HAS PRODUCED ONLY
C-CLASS SUBFLARES.  REGION 5417 (S25W51) HAS DECAYED FURTHER AND
HAS NOT FLARED THIS UT DAY.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE.  REGION 5428 APPEARS CAPABLE OF M-CLASS
ACTIVITY.  OLD REGION 5395 (N34, L-257 CLASS/AREA FKC/3600
ON 17 MAR) IS DUE TO RETURN AT THE EAST LIMB ON 2 APRIL.
THIS REGION PRODUCED 11 X-CLASS AND 48 M-CLASS FLARES DURING
ITS LAST DISK TRANSIT.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 29/2100Z TO 30/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS AT MINOR STORM TO ACTIVE LEVELS.
THE GEOMAGNETIC STORM WHICH HAS BEEN IN PROGRESS SINCE 27
MAR APPEARS TO BE NEARING ITS END.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE MOSTLY ACTIVE FOR THE NEXT THREE DAYS.
ACTIVITY ON 1 AND 2 APRIL IS FORECAST DUE TO THE FAVORABLE
LOCATION OF AN EQUATORIAL CORONAL HOLE.
