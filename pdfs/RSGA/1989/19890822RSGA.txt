FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 234
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 22 AUG 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 21/2100Z
TO 22/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. REGION 5652 (S20E28)
PRODUCED TODAY'S LARGEST FLARE, A C3/SF AT 0228Z. THE REGION
PRODUCED A FEW ADDITIONAL SUBFLARES DURING THE PERIOD AND IS
GROWING. THE REGION 5655 (N25E68) ROTATED INTO VIEW AND NEW
REGIONS 5656 (N26E25) AND 5657 (S19E09) WERE ALSO ASSIGNED
DURING THE PERIOD. SURGING WAS OBSERVED ON THE WEST LIMB AT
N13 ASSOCIATED WITH THE EXIT OF REGION 5640. THE REMAINDER OF
THE DISK AND LIMBS WERE QUIET.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW. REGION 5652 POSES A SLIGHT THREAT FOR MODERATE LEVELS AS
IT CONTINUES TO EMERGE. REGION 5643 ALSO POSES A SLIGHT THREAT
FOR M-FLARES GIVEN ITS SIZE AND THE STRENGTH OF ITS MAGNETIC
FIELDS.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 21/2100Z TO 22/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD RANGED FROM QUIET TO ACTIVE LEVELS WITH
A BRIEF MINOR STORM PERIOD AT HIGH LATITUDES. THE ACTIVE
PERIOD OCCURRED FROM 21/2100Z THROUGH ABOUT 22/1000Z.
THE FIELD BECAME QUIET THEREAFTER. THE DISTURBANCE WAS
PROBABLY CAUSED BY THE 18 AUGUST FILAMENT DISAPPEARANCE.
A GREATER THAN 10MEV PARTICLE EVENT CONTINUES IN PROGRESS.
TODAY'S FLUX VALUES FLUCTUATED BETWEEN 10 AND 20 PFU.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE MOSTLY UNSETTLED TOMORROW WITH POSSIBLE ACTIVE
SUBSTORM PERIODS IN THE LOCAL NIGHTTIME SECTORS. CONDITIONS
ARE EXPECTED TO BE MOSTLY UNSETTLED FOR THE SECOND AND THIRD
DAYS.
