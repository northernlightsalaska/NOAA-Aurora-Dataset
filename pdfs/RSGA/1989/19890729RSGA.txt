FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 210
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 29 JUL 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 28/2100Z
TO 29/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW.  THE LARGEST EVENT WAS A
A C7 AT 29/1459UT ASSOCIATED WITH A BRIGHT SURGE FROM REGION
5613 (N22W89).  REGIONS 5617 (N18W01) AND 5618 (S11W13) EMERGED
OVER THE PAST 24 HOURS AND HAVE PRODUCED SMALL FLARES. REGION
5617 APPEARS TO HAVE AN EAST-WEST ORIENTATED INVERSION LINE.
REGION 5612 (S17E50) IS THE LARGEST REGION ON THE DISK, BUT
HAS BEEN QUIET.  THE 10.7CM RADIO FLUX AND X-RAY LEVELS ARE
UP SLIGHTLY, MOST OF THE INCREASE APPEARS TO BE COMING FROM
AREAS NEAR THE EAST LIMB.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW.  THERE IS A SLIGHT CHANCE FOR ISOLATED ENERGETIC FLARES.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 28/2100Z TO 29/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO UNSETTLED
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE QUIET TO UNSETTLED FOR THE NEAR FUTURE.
