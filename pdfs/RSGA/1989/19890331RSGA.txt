JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 31 MAR 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 30/2100Z
TO 31/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW.  REGION 5428 (S19E54),
THE LARGEST REGION ON THE DISK, APPEARS SOMEWHAT THREATENING
BUT HAS PRODUCED ONLY C-CLASS SUBFLARES SINCE ITS DISK
ARRIVAL.  SMALL, NEW BIPOLAR SUNSPOT GROUPS NUMBERED SINCE
YESTERDAY ARE 5430 (S28E11), 5431 (N37E56), AND 5432 (S27E60).
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE.  REGION 5428 MAY PRODUCE AN M-CLASS FLARE YET.
OLD REGION 5395 (N34, L-257 CLASS/AREA FKC/3600 ON 17 MAR) IS
DUE TO RETURN ON 2 APRIL.  ACTIVITY LEVELS ARE EXPECTED TO
STEADILY INCREASE OVER THE NEXT FEW DAYS.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 30/2100Z TO 31/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS AT MINOR TO MAJOR STORM LEVELS.
THE GEOMAGNETIC DISTURBANCE WHICH STARTED ON 27 MARCH CONTINUES
IN PROGRESS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE MOSTLY ACTIVE FOR THE NEXT THREE DAYS.  THE
EFFECTS OF AN EQUATORIAL CORONAL HOLE MAY BE FELT BEGINNING
ON 1 APRIL.
