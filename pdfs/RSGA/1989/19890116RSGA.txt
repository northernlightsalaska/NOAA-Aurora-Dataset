JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 16 JAN 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 15/2100Z
TO 16/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH. AN OPTICALLY UNCORRELATED
M5 X-RAY BURST OCCURRED AT 16/1101Z. REGION 5312 (S32W46)
REMAINED MAGNETICALLY COMPLEX WHILE CONTINUING A VERY GRADUAL
RATE OF DECAY. THE REGION PRODUCED THREE M-CLASS EVENTS DURING
THE PERIOD: AN M1/1N AT 16/0631Z, AN M2/1F AT 16/0921Z
(ACCOMPANIED BY A TYPE II SWEEP, ESTIMATED VELOCITY 800 KM/SEC),
AND AN M1/SF AT 16/1517Z. REGION 5317 (N22W13) CONTINUED TO
GROW, BUT AT A REDUCED RATE. IT PRODUCED SEVERAL SUBFLARES
DURING THE PERIOD. REGION 5321 (N18E09) PRODUCED AN M1/SF AT
16/0328Z. NO NEW REGIONS WERE NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
HIGH. MAJOR FLARE ACTIVITY IS POSSIBLE FROM REGION 5312.
M-CLASS FLARING MAY OCCUR WITHIN REGIONS 5317 AND 5321.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 15/2100Z TO 16/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM
LEVELS. THIS ACTIVITY IS POSSIBLY IN RESPONSE TO THE X2/2B
FLARE THAT OCCURRED ON 14 JANUARY.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT ACTIVE LEVELS TOMORROW AS TODAY'S ACTIVITY
CONTINUES. THE SECOND DAY SHOULD SEE THE FIELD DECLINE TO
UNSETTLED LEVELS. THE FINAL DAY OF THE PERIOD IS EXPECTED TO
ACTIVE IN RESPONSE TO THE TYPE II SWEEP MENTIONED ABOVE.
