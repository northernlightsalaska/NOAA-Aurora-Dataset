FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 170
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 19 JUN 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 18/2100Z
TO 19/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 5528 (N21W68)
REMAINED IN A STATE OF GRADUAL DECAY, BUT CONTINUED AS A LARGE,
COMPLEX SPOT GROUP. IT PRODUCED AN M2/0N FLARE AT 19/0556Z THAT
WAS ACCOMPANIED BY MINOR DISCRETE RADIO BURSTS ACROSS THE
SPECTRUM. NEW REGION 5552 (S18E72) PRODUCED TWO M-CLASS FLARES
AS IT ROTATED INTO VIEW. THE FIRST WAS AN M1/SN AT 18/2347Z AND
WAS ASSOCIATED WITH A WEAK TYPE II SWEEP. THE SECOND WAS AN
M3/1N AT 19/0748Z WHICH WAS ACCOMPANIED BY MINOR DISCRETE RADIO
BURSTS. REGION 5533 (S19W44) REMAINED IN A STATE OF GRADUAL
DECAY AND WAS QUIET DURING THE PERIOD. NEW REGION 5553 (S24E30)
EMERGED AS A SMALL UNIPOLAR GROUP.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE TO HIGH. REGIONS 5528 AND 5552 ARE LIKELY TO CONTINUE
PRODUCING M-CLASS FLARES. REGION 5528 RETAINS POTENTIAL FOR
ISOLATED MAJOR FLARE ACTIVITY.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 18/2100Z TO 19/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO UNSETTLED
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS. THE SATELLITE PROTON EVENT AT
GREATER THAN 10 MEV THAT BEGAN AT 18/1650Z REACHED A MAXIMUM
OF 18 PARTICLE FLUX UNITS (PFU) AT 19/1910Z AND ENDED AT
18/2225Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE ACTIVE LEVELS WITH PERIODS OF MINOR STORMING
POSSIBLE AT HIGH LATITUDES. THIS ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO OCCUR
IN RESPONSE TO RECENT MAJOR FLARE ACTIVITY. THE RECURRENT
PATTERN ALSO SUPPORTS THIS FORECAST.
