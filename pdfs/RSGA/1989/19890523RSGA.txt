FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 143
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 23 MAY 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 22/2100Z
TO 23/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 5495 (N23W64)
PRODUCED AN M2/SN FLARE AT 23/0148Z ACCOMPANIED BY MINOR
DISCRETE RADIO BURSTS. REGION 5497 (S20W09) REMAINED THE MOST
IMPRESSIVE GROUP ON THE DISK WHILE INCREASING IN SPOT COUNT AND
MAGNETIC COMPLEXITY. THE REGION PRODUCED SEVERAL SUBFLARES
DURING THE PERIOD THE LARGEST OF WHICH WAS A C7/SF AT 23/0327Z.
A OPTICALLY UNCORRELATED M1 X-RAY BURST OCCURRED AT 23/0809Z.
THREE NEW SPOT GROUPS WERE NUMBERED TODAY: 5504 (N22E02), 5505
(S19E73), AND 5506 (N21E82).
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE. REGIONS 5495 AND 5497 ARE CAPABLE OF M-CLASS FLARING,
ESPECIALLY 5497. REGION 5497 MAY ALSO PRODUCE AN ISOLATED MAJOR
FLARE DURING THE PERIOD.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 22/2100Z TO 23/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO MAJOR STORM
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS. A SUDDEN IMPULSE OF 15
NANOTESLAS OCCURRED AT 23/1128Z FOLLOWED BY A SECOND IMPULSE
OF 68 NANOTESLAS AT 23/1347Z. A GREATER THAN 10 MEV PROTON
EVENT BEGAN AT 23/1135Z, REACHED A MAXIMUM OF 68 PFU AT
23/1350Z, AND ENDED AT 23/1655Z. A POLAR CAP ABSORPTION
EVENT BEGAN AT 23/1312Z, REACHED A MAXIMUM ABSORPTION VALUE
OF 2.4 DB AT 23/1314Z, AND ENDED AT 23/1919Z. ALL OF THE
ABOVE ACTIVITY IS BELIEVED TO BE THE RESULT OF A
LONG-DURATION C5 X-RAY EVENT WHICH OCCURRED ON 20 MAY.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM LEVELS THROUGH 25 MAY.
THE FINAL DAY IS EXPECTED TO BE ACTIVE. THIS ACTIVITY IS
EXPECTED AS A RESULT OF THE M5/2B FLARE WHICH OCCURRED ON
22 MAY. CORONAL HOLE EFFECTS ARE EXPECTED TO DISTURB THE FIELD
ON 25-26 MAY.
