FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 225
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 13 AUG 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 12/2100Z
TO 13/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 5629 (S17W56)
PRODUCED AN M1/1F AT 13/0722Z WITH MINOR RADIO BURSTS. REGION
5629 UNDERWENT SOME DECAY AFTER ITS X2/2B FLARE ON 12 AUG.
REGION 5643 (N16E51) GREW DURING THE PERIOD AND IS NOW A
MODERATE TO LARGE CLASS E SUNSPOT GROUP. FREQUENT SUBFLARES
WERE REPORTED FROM THIS REGION.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY SHOULD
CONTINUE AT A GENERALLY MODERATE LEVEL. ANOTHER MAJOR FLARE
IS STILL POSSIBLE FROM REGION 5629 PRIOR TO ITS WEST LIMB
TRANSIT. REGION 5643 SHOULD BEGIN TO PRODUCE MORE IN THE
WAY OF CLASS M FLARES AND CLASS X FLARES ARE BECOMING MORE
LIKELY FROM THIS REGION.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 12/2100Z TO 13/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS GENERALLY UNSETTLED DURING THE
PERIOD. THE GREATER THAN 10 MEV PROTON EVENT WHICH BEGAN AT
12/1600UT REACHED A MAXIMUM FLUX OF 9200 P.F.U. AT 13/0710Z.
THAT LEVEL IS THE HIGHEST RECORDED SINCE THE GREAT PROTON
EVENT OF AUG 1972 BUT THE CURRENT EVENT IS ABOUT ONE SIXTH
THE PEAK FLUX OBSERVED 17 YEARS AGO. AT THE END OF THE
PERIOD, THE GREATER THAN 10 MEV FLUX HAD DECAYED TO
6200 P.F.U. THE POLAR CAP ABSORPTION EVENT REMAINS IN
PROGRESS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  A MAJOR GEOMAGNETIC
STORM IS EXPECTED TO BEGIN SOON AND CONTINUE DURING 14 AUG.
GENERALLY MINOR STORM LEVELS ARE PREDICTED FOR 15 AUG AND
ACTIVE LEVELS SHOULD BE EXPERIENCED ON 16 AUG. THE PROTON
EVENT AND POLAR CAP ABSORPTION SHOULD CONTINUE DURING
14 AUG.
