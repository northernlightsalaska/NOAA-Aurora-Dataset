FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 076
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 17 MAR 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 16/2100Z
TO 17/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY CONTINUED HIGH. REGION 5395
(N34W69) PRODUCED ITS SECOND LARGEST FLARE TO DATE, AN X6/3B
TENFLARE, VISIBLE IN WHITE LIGHT, AT 1744Z. THE EVENT ALSO
INCLUDED DISCRETE BURSTS OF THOUSANDS OF FLUX UNITS AS WELL AS
IMPORTANCE 3 TYPE II AND TYPE IV SWEEP. THE REGION ALSO
GENERATED OTHER SIGNIFICANT FLARES: AN X1/1B AT 16/2045, AN
M2/3N AT 0247, AN M6/2B AT 0719, AND AN M4/2N AT 1113Z.
REGION 5403 (S14E11) ALSO CONTRIBUTED AN M1/2B AT 2017Z.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY SHOULD PERSIST
AT HIGH LEVELS THROUGHOUT THE FORECAST PERIOD. REGION 5395
IS STILL CAPABLE OF ADDITIONAL X-CLASS ACTIVITY, AND REGION
5403 HAS SHOWN IT CAN PRODUCE M-CLASS FLARES.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 16/2100Z TO 17/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS AT ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM LEVELS AT
MID-LATITUDES AND MINOR TO MAJOR STORM THRESHOLDS AT HIGH
LATITUDES. A SATELLITE PROTON EVENT AT GREATER THAN 10 MEV
BEGAN AT 1855Z, APPROXIMATELY ONE HOUR AFTER THE X6/3B FLARE.
THIS FAST RESPONSE IS REASONABLE FOR A FLARE FROM W60
HELIOLONGITUDE. THE MAXIMUM, AT THIS WRITING, IS 240 PFU. A
POLAR CAP ABSORPTION EVENT BEGAN VERY LATE IN THE SUMMARY
PERIOD.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM LEVELS THE NEXT THREE
DAYS. THE BRUNT OF THE ACTIVITY IS ANTICIPATED TO OCCUR 19
MARCH AS A RESULT OF TODAY'S X6/3B FLARE.
