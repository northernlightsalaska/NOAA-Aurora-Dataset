JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 07 NOV 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 06/2100Z
TO 07/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH.  TWO MAJOR FLARES WERE
PRODUCED SINCE YESTERDAY, AN M6/2B AT 07/0323Z IN REGION
5776 (N17W00), AND AN M8/2B AT 07/2042Z IN REGION 5786 (N13E66).
REGION 5683 (N19E28) ALSO PRODUCED THREE LOW LEVEL M-CLASS
FLARES, AN M2/1N AT 07/0241Z, M1/1F AT 07/0600Z, AND AN M1/1N
AT 07/1659Z.  ALL OF THESE REGIONS ARE FAIRLY SMALL IN SUNSPOT
AREA (LESS THAN 300 MILLIONTHS) BUT APPARENTLY CONTAIN SOME
MAGNETIC COMPLEXITY.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
HIGH.  HIGH LEVEL M-CLASS FLARES, AND POSSIBLY X-CLASS ACTIVITY,
IS EXPECTED.  REGIONS 5776, 5783, AND 5786 COULD PRODUCE
MAJOR FLARES.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 06/2100Z TO 07/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT UNSETTLED TO STORM LEVELS.  THIS FORECAST
IS BASED ON RECENT FLARE AND DSF (07/1355Z) ACTIVITY.
