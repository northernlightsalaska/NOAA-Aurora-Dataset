IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 29/2100Z
TO 30/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS VERY LOW. THREE SUBFLARES
OCCURRED DURING THE PERIOD. NO X-RAY BURSTS OR RADIO EMISSIONS
WERE OBSERVED DURING THESE FLARES. REGION 5454 (S21W98) QUIETLY
CROSSED THE WEST LIMB. REGION 5464 (S18E07) ENTERED A SLOW DECAY
PHASE. REGION 5470 (N29E73) GREW SLOWLY. NO NEW REGIONS WERE
NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW. REGIONS 5464 AND 5470 ARE CAPABLE OF C-CLASS FLARE
PRODUCTION.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 29/2100Z TO 30/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS. HIGHER LATITUDES RECORDED
ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM LEVELS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE ACTIVE TO UNSETTLED AT MIDDLE LATITUDES AND AT
MINOR STORM TO ACTIVE LEVELS AT HIGHER LATITUDES BASED UPON
THE RECURRENT PATTERN.
