JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 14 JUN 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 13/2100Z
TO 14/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE.  M-CLASS FLARE
ACTIVITY OCCURRED IN REGIONS 5521 (S16W74) AND 5528 (N21W04).
REGION 5521 IS A SMALL, DECAYING SUNSPOT GROUP APPROACHING
THE WEST LIMB AND PRODUCED AN M2/1N FLARE AT 14/1355Z AND AN
M1/SF AT 14/1644Z.  THE M2 FLARE WAS ASSOCIATED WITH A SPRAY,
STRONG TYPE IV SWEEP, AND A 2695 MHZ RADIO BURST OF 390 SFU.
REGION 5528 REMAINS THE LARGEST, MOST COMPLEX SUNSPOT GROUP
ON THE DISK AND PRODUCED TWO M1/SF FLARES AT 14/1553Z AND
14/1820Z.  NEW REGIONS NUMBERED TODAY INCLUDE 5540 (S24W03),
5541 (S10E66), AND 5542 (S20E80).
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE TO HIGH.  A MAJOR FLARE IS EXPECTED FROM REGION 5528.
REGION 5521 MAY PRODUCE ADDITIONAL LOW-LEVEL M-CLASS FLARES
AS IT DEPARTS THE DISK.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 13/2100Z TO 14/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS AT ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM LEVELS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE MOSTLY UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE.
