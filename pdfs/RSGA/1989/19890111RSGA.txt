FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 011
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 11 JAN 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 10/2100Z
TO 11/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH.  ACTIVITY LEVELS
REMAINED HIGH DUE TO THE NUMBER OF LOW-LEVEL M-CLASS X-RAY
FLARES, OF WHICH ALL WITH OPTICAL CORRELATION OCCURRED
IN REGION 5312 (S30E14).  THIS REGION REMAINS LARGE AND
COMPLEX.  NEW REGION 5321 (N19E74) WAS NUMBERED TODAY.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
HIGH.  MAJOR FLARES ARE EXPECTED FROM REGION 5312.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 10/2100Z TO 11/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD RANGED FROM QUIET TO MAJOR STORM LEVELS.
IT WAS MOSTLY QUIET UNTIL 11/1206Z WHEN A SUDDEN IMPULSE WAS
OBSERVED (NINE NANOTESLA AT BOULDER) AND WAS AT ACTIVE TO
MAJOR STORM LEVELS THEREAFTER.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT STORM LEVELS FOR THE NEXT 24 HOURS,
BECOMING ACTIVE TO UNSETTLED FOR THE REMAINDER OF THE PERIOD.
