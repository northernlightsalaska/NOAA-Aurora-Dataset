JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 15 MAY 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 14/2100Z
TO 15/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. THE LARGEST EVENT OF THE
DAY WAS A C6/SF FLARE PRODUCED BY REGION 5481 (S20W33) AT 0538Z.
THIS REGION EXHIBITED A STEADY GROWTH TREND DURING THE PERIOD.
IN GENERAL THE SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS WERE SMALL AND GROWING WITH
OCCASIONAL SUBFLARE FLUCTUATIONS.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW. THERE IS A SLIGHT CHANCE FOR AN ISOLATED M-CLASS EVENT.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 14/2100Z TO 15/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS GENERALLY QUIET TO UNSETTLED.
OCCASIONAL ACTIVE PERIODS WERE OBSERVED AT HIGH LATITUDES.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE GENERALLY UNSETTLED FOR THE NEXT TWO DAYS AND
QUIET TO UNSETTLED ON THE THIRD DAY.
