JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 20 JAN 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 19/2100Z
TO 20/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH. REGION 5321 (N17W43)
PRODUCED AN M8/1F AT 20/1558Z. MINOR DISCRETE RADIO BURSTS
ACCOMPANIED THIS EVENT. REGION 5312 (S34W89) WAS RELATIVELY
QUIET WHILE CROSSING THE WEST LIMB PRODUCING ONE SUBFLARE DURING
THE PERIOD. REGION 5317 (N22W66) REMAINED MAGNETICALLY COMPLEX
WHILE CONTINUING TO GRADUALLY DECAY. IT PRODUCED TWO SUBFLARES
DURING THE PERIOD. NEW REGION 5329 (N22E74) ROTATED ACROSS THE
EAST LIMB TODAY.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE TO HIGH. M-CLASS FLARING IS POSSIBLE FROM REGIONS
5312, 5317, AND 5321. REGION 5317 REMAINS CAPABLE OF PRODUCING
A MAJOR FLARE.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 19/2100Z TO 20/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO MAJOR STORM
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS. MINOR TO MAJOR STORMING BEGAN
EARLY IN THE SECOND HALF OF THE PERIOD AND REMAINS IN
PROGRESS. THIS ACTIVITY IS PROBABLY ASSOCIATED WITH THE MAJOR
FLARE ACTIVITY THAT OCCURRED ON 18 JANUARY.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM LEVELS TOMORROW AS
TODAY'S ACTIVITY CONTINUES. THE REMAINDER OF THE PERIOD IS
EXPECTED TO BE ACTIVE WITH ISOLATED PERIODS OF MINOR STORMING.
THIS ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED IN RESPONSE TO CORONAL HOLE EFFECTS.
