FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 059
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 28 FEB 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 27/2100Z
TO 28/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 5378 (N15W65)
AND REGION 5373 (N16W86) COMBINED SUBFLARES TO PRODUCED TODAY'S
ONLY M-CLASS EVENT, AN M1 AT 1824Z. THE EVENT LASTED FOR ABOUT
AN HOUR AND WAS ASSOCIATED WITH A WEAK TYPE II RADIO SWEEP.
REGION 5378 EXHIBITED STEADY GROWTH AND PENUMBRAL DEVELOPMENT
DURING THE PERIOD AND PRODUCED THE MAJORITY OF TODAY'S NUMEROUS
SUBFLARES. REGION 5379 (S20E05) CONTINUED TO GROW SLOWLY AND
PRODUCED OCCASIONAL SUBFLARES. THE REMAINDER OF THE SOLAR DISK
AND LIMBS WERE ESSENTIALLY QUIET.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW. REGIONS 5373, 5378, AND 5379 EACH POSE A SLIGHT THREAT FOR
MODERATE LEVELS. A LULL IN ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED ON THE SECOND
DAY AS REGION 5373 ROTATES OUT OF VIEW. AN INCREASE IS EXPECTED
ON THE THIRD DAY WITH THE RETURN OF OLD REGION 5354 (N30, L-283)
WHICH PRODUCED 11 M-CLASS FLARES AND 1 X-CLASS FLARE ON ITS
PREVIOUS DISK TRANSIT.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 27/2100Z TO 28/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS QUIET FROM 27/2100Z TO 28/0730Z AND
WAS MOSTLY UNSETTLED FOR THE REMAINDER OF THE PERIOD.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE PREDOMINANTLY UNSETTLED FOR THE NEXT THREE DAYS.
