FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 082
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 23 MAR 1989
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 22/2100Z
TO 23/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH. REGION 5409 (N18W34)
PRODUCED AN X1/3B FLARE STARTING AT 23/1959Z. THE EVENT WAS
LONG DURATION AND KEPT THE X-RAY FLUX ABOVE M-LEVEL FOR THE
REMAINDER OF THE PERIOD. THE FLARE WAS ACCOMPANIED BY STRONG
TYPE II AND TYPE IV SWEEPS AND STRONG DISCRETE RADIO BURSTS.
THE REGION HAS BEEN UNDER FRAGMENTATION AND SIMPLIFICATION
THROUGHOUT THE DAY. THERE WERE NO ADDITIONAL CLASS M OR
GREATER FLARES DURING THE DAY. THE REMAINDER OF THE DISK
WAS QUIET AND STABLE.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE. REGION 5409 IS EXPECTED TO CONTINUE TO DECAY AND IS
LIKELY TO PRODUCE ADDITIONAL M-CLASS FLARES. THERE CONTINUES
TO BE A SLIGHT BUT REDUCED CHANCE FOR A MAJOR FLARE FROM THE
REGION: TODAY'S EVENT PROBABLY REDUCED THE MAJOR FLARE
CAPABILITY OF THE REGION.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 22/2100Z TO 23/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS MOSTLY ACTIVE WITH OCCASIONAL MINOR
STORM PERIODS AT HIGH LATITUDES. A PROTON EVENT OF MORE THAN
10 PFU AT GREATER THAN 10MEV BEGAN AT 2025Z IN RESPONSE TO
TODAY'S MAJOR SOLAR FLARE. THE EVENT CONTINUES IN PROGRESS
AT THIS TIME. THE MAXIMUM FLUX OBSERVED SO FAR HAS BEEN 41
PFU AT 2120Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE TOMORROW AND THROUGH MOST
OF THE SECOND DAY. ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM CONDITIONS ARE FORE-
CAST BASED ON TODAY'S FLARE ACTIVITY. THE DISTURBANCE IS EX-
PECTED TO START LATE ON THE SECOND DAY AND SHOULD LAST THROUGH
THE THIRD DAY. THE PROTON EVENT IS EXPECTED TO REACH A MAXIMUM
OF 100 PFU AT GREATER THAN 10MEV EARLY ON THE FIRST DAY.
