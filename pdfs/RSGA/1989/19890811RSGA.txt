IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 10/2100Z
TO 11/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 5629 (S17W31)
PRODUCED AN M2/1B AT 11/1628Z AND AN M1/SF AT 11/2036UT. NEW
REGION 5643 (N14E74) PRODUCED AN M1/SN AT 11/2011Z. MINOR FLUX
EMERGENCE IS OCCURRING IN REGION 5629. REGION 5643 APPEARS TO
BE A MODERATE SIZE CLASS E GROUP, POSSIBLY WITH SOME MIXED
MAGNETIC POLARITIES. A SMALL REGION EMERGED AND WAS NUMBERED
AS 5642 (N04E23).
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY SHOULD CONTINUE
AT A MODERATE LEVEL FOR THE DURATION OF THE FORECAST PERIOD.
AN ISOLATED MAJOR FLARE FROM REGION 5629 REMAINS POSSIBLE
PRIOR TO ITS WEST LIMB TRANSIT. REGION 5643 APPEARS CAPABLE
OF SUSTAINING CLASS M FLARE PRODUCTION.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 10/2100Z TO 11/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS AT ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM LEVELS.
BRIEF MAJOR STORMING OCCURRED AT HIGH LATITUDES BETWEEN
11/0430-0730Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD
SHOULD BE PREDOMINANTLY UNSETTLED FOR THE NEXT THREE DAYS.
