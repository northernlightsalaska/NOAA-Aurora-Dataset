JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 027 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 27 JAN 1993
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 26/2100Z
TO 27/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS VERY LOW. NO OPTICAL FLARES
WERE OBSERVED DURING THE PAST DAY. NEW REGIONS 7411 (S11W23)
AND 7412 (S14E75) WERE NUMBERED. REGION 7411 IS EMERGING
RAPIDLY, ALREADY FORMING PENUMBRA ON LEADER AND TRAILER
SUNSPOTS. REGION 7412 MAY BE THE FIRST OF TWO OR MORE NEW
REGIONS ROTATING INTO VIEW AT THE SOUTHEAST LIMB.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
VERY LOW TO LOW. FLARE ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO INCREASE OVER
THE NEXT FEW DAYS DUE TO THE NEW REGIONS AT THE EAST LIMB AND
THE DEVELOPMENT OF REGION 7411.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 26/2100Z TO 27/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS QUIET TO UNSETTLED AT MOST STATIONS.
STORM CONDITIONS OCCURRED AT SOME HIGH-LATITUDE LOCATIONS FROM
26/0600-1200Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE QUIET TO UNSETTLED FOR MOST OF THE FORECAST
PERIOD. ACTIVE CONDITIONS ARE POSSIBLE ON THE THIRD DAY
DUE TO A RECURRENT DISTURBANCE.
