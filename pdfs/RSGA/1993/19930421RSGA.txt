JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 111 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 21 APR 1993
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 20/2100Z
TO 21/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. REGION 7477 (N11E12) WAS
RESPONSIBLE FOR THREE CLASS C FLARES, THE LARGEST OF WHICH WAS
A C4/SF AT 21/0722UT. GROWTH HAS SLOWED IN THIS REGION. REGION
7483 (S14W09), ALSO GROWING SLOWLY, PRODUCED TWO CLASS B FLARES
AND TWO CLASS C FLARES INCLUDING A C4/SN AT 21/1434UT.THIS
REGION STILL SHOWS SOME MAGNETIC COMPLEXITY.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW. REGIONS 7477 AND 7483 HAVE THE BEST POTENTIAL FOR CLASS C
ACTIVITY AND A CHANCE FOR AN ISOLATED CLASS M FLARE.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 20/2100Z TO 21/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS, POSSIBLY DUE TO THE EFFECTS OF
AN M8/1B FLARE ON APRIL 18. HIGH LATITUDE STATIONS REPORTED
MAJOR TO SEVERE STORM LEVELS IN THE PAST 12 HOURS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE MOSTLY UNSETTLED FOR THE ENTIRE FORECAST PERIOD.
HIGH LATITUDE STATIONS MAY EXPERIENCE ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM
CONDITIONS FOR THE FIRST DAY OF THE PERIOD.
