JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 079 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 20 MAR 1993
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 19/2100Z
TO 20/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. THE PACE OF FLARE
PRODUCTION HAS PICKED UP CONSIDERABLY. REGION 7448 (N17W47)
HAS GENERATED SEVERAL FLARES SINCE YESTERDAY. THE LARGEST WAS
AN M1/1N AT 20/0106Z. THIS FLARE WAS ASSOCIATED WITH MODERATE
TYPE II/IV SWEEPS. MANY OF THE FLARES IN THIS REGION WERE
RADIO RICH, WITH DISCRETE BURSTS AND SWEEP ACTIVITY ACROSS THE
SPECTRUM. ANOTHER FLARE OF NOTE IN 7448 WAS A C3/SN AT
20/1657Z. THIS FLARE WAS ACCOMPANIED BY A STRONG TYPE II SWEEP.
THE REGION HAS SHOWN SOME DECAY OVER THE PAST 24 HOURS. THE
SUNSPOT AREA IS REDUCED AND THE DELTA CONFIGURATION IS NO
LONGER PRESENT.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. ADDITIONAL FLARES ARE EXPECTED IN REGION 7448.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 19/2100Z TO 20/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS QUIET TO ACTIVE. STORM CONDITIONS
WERE OBSERVED AT SOME HIGH LATITUDE STATIONS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE AT MOST LOCATIONS FOR THE
NEXT TWO DAYS. STORM CONDITIONS ARE POSSIBLE AT HIGH LATITUDES.
