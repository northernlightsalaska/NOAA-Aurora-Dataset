JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 110 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 20 APR 1993
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 19/2100Z
TO 20/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. REGION 7477 (N10E25) WAS
THE MOST PROLIFIC REGION TODAY, PRODUCING EIGHT CLASS C FLARES.
THE LARGEST OF THESE WERE A C5/SN FLARE AT 20/1255UT AND A C5/SF
FLARE AT 20/1752UT. THIS REGION SHOWED SIGNIFICANT GROWTH OVER
NIGHT. REGION 7480 (S14E43), SLOWLY GROWING, PRODUCED A SINGLE
C1/SF FLARE AT 20/1026UT. REGION 7483 (S14E05) PRODUCED ONLY
MINOR FLARING BUT NOW SHOWS A BETA-GAMMA MAGNETIC STRUCTURE.
REGIONS 7485 (S11W27), 7486 (N09W16), AND 7487 (S11E14) WERE
NUMBERED TODAY.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. REGION 7477 HAS THE BEST POTENTIAL FOR CLASS
C FLARE ACTIVITY AND AN ISOLATED CLASS M FLARE. REGION 7480 AND
7483 BOTH HAVE A GOOD POTENTIAL FOR CLASS C ACTIVITY.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 19/2100Z TO 20/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE LEVELS
FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS. HIGH LATITUDE STATIONS SAW MINOR TO
MAJOR STORM LEVELS THROUGH THE DAY. THE ACTIVITY IS PROBABLY
A RESULT OF A SOMEWHAT FAVORABLY POSITIONED CORONAL HOLE.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE FOR THE NEXT 12 TO 18 HOURS,
THEN MOSTLY UNSETTLED FOR THE REMAINDER OF THE FORECAST PERIOD.
