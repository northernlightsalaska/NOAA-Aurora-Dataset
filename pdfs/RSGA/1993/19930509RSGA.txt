JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 129 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 09 MAY 1993
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 08/2100Z
TO 09/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS VERY LOW. REGION 7500 (N17E15)
CONTINUED TO GROW, INCREASING PENUMBRAL AREA AND FORMING A
DELTA CONFIGURATION. REGION 7496 (N15W05) ALSO GREW BUT REMAINED
MAGNETICALLY SIMPLE. SPOTLESS PLAGE 7501 (S10,L-040) WAS
COMBINED WITH REGION 7499 (S08W39). NEW REGIONS 7502 (S12W24)
AND 7503 (S07E69) WERE NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. M-CLASS FLARES ARE POSSIBLE IN REGION 7500.
AN ISOLATED MAJOR FLARE IS ALSO POSSIBLE IN THIS REGION.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 08/2100Z TO 09/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS AT UNSETTLED TO STORM LEVELS. THIS
CORONAL HOLE INDUCED DISTURBANCE APPEARS TO BE TAPERING OFF.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE TOMORROW, BECOMING QUIET TO
UNSETTLED FOR THE REMAINDER OF THE FORECAST PERIOD.
