JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 098 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 08 APR 1993
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 07/2100Z
TO 08/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. REGION 7465 (S06W52)
ERUPTED WITH A LONG DURATION C5/1F FLARE AT 08/0306Z. A FORTY
MINUTE TYPE IV RADIO SWEEP ACCOMPANIED THIS FLARE. REGION 7470
(S06W21) DEVELOPED OVERNIGHT WITH TEN NEW SPOTS. ADJACENT
REGION 7469 (S11W25) ALSO SHOWS TEN NEW SPOTS. REGION 7469
PRODUCED A LONG DURATION C6/1N AT 08/2000Z. WHILE THIS C6 FLARE
WAS DECLINING, A FILAMENT IMPACT FLARE BEGAN AT N16E62. THIS
IMPACT FLARE PEAKED AT C9/1N AT 08/2037Z. A TYPE II SWEEP WAS
OBSERVED WITH THIS EAST LIMB FLARE. TWO NEW REGIONS WERE
ASSIGNED: 7471 (N17W09), AND 7472 (N06W20).
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. REGION 7565 IS DECLINING WHILE REGIONS 7469 AND
7470 APPEAR TO BE MERGING. THESE REGIONS ALL HAVE POTENTIAL TO
PRODUCE A MAJOR FLARE. THEY ARE ALSO IN PRIME LOCATION FOR
A PROTON EVENT.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 07/2100Z TO 08/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS. HIGH LATITUDE STATIONS OBSERVED
MAJOR STORM LEVELS FROM 08/0900-1200Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BEGIN AT QUIET TO UNSETTLED LEVELS. A WELL
POSITIONED CORONAL HOLE AND THE EFFECTS OF TODAY'S FLARE
ACTIVITY MAY COMBINE TO CREATE ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM LEVELS FOR
THE LAST TWO DAYS OF THE PERIOD.
