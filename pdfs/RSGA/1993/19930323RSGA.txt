JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 082 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 23 MAR 1993
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 22/2100Z
TO 23/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 7448 (N16W86)
BEGAN ITS WEST LIMB PASSAGE. IT PRODUCED AN M2/SF FLARE AT
23/0153Z WITH AN ASSOCIATED 310 SFU TENFLARE AND A WEAK TYPE IV
SPECTRAL RADIO BURST. A FAINT LOOP PROMINENCE SYSTEM WAS SEEN
IN THE WAKE OF THIS FLARE. REGION 7452 (S12W21) MAINTAINED A
DELTA MAGNETIC STRUCTURE WITHIN ITS LEADER, BUT WAS STABLE
THROUGHOUT THE PERIOD. NEW REGION 7458 (S12E08) WAS NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. REGION 7448 MAY PRODUCE ANOTHER M-CLASS FLARE
AS IT CROSSES THE LIMB. REGION 7452 ALSO PROVIDES A SLIGHT
CHANCE FOR AN ISOLATED M-CLASS FLARE.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 22/2100Z TO 23/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO UNSETTLED LEVELS
FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT QUIET TO UNSETTLED LEVELS DURING THE FIRST
TWO DAYS. CONDITIONS ARE EXPECTED TO INCREASE TO ACTIVE LEVELS
ON THE FINAL DAY DUE TO A FILAMENT WHICH DISAPPEARED LATE ON
21 MARCH. INTERMITTENT STORM PERIODS ARE ALSO POSSIBLE DURING
THE FINAL DAY.
