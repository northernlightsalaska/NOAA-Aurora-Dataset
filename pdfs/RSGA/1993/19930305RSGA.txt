IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 04/2100Z
TO 05/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. REGION 7441 (N08E48), A
RAPIDLY GROWING DAO GROUP, PRODUCED THE SECOND LARGEST FLARE OF
THE PERIOD, A C4/1F AT 05/0046UT. REGION 7435 (N09W41), SLOWLY
DECAYING, PRODUCED THE ONLY OTHER CORRELATED CLASS C FLARE, A
C1/SF AT 05/0646UT. THE LARGEST EVENT TODAY WAS AN UNCORRELATED
C5 AT 05/1126UT. THERE WERE TWO OTHER SMALLER, UNCORRELATED
CLASS C EVENTS AS WELL. REGION 7440 (S07E40), AN IMPRESSIVE
DKC BETA-GAMMA-DELTA SPOT GROUP, HAS ONLY PRODUCED SUB FLARES
SO FAR. REGIONS 7443 AND 7444 WERE NUMBERED TODAY.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. REGION 7440 HAS A STRONG POTENTIAL FOR CLASS
C FLARES, ISOLATED CLASS M FLARES AND AN OUTSIDE CHANCE FOR A
CLASS X FLARE. REGIONS 7441 AND 7435 HAVE GOOD CLASS C FLARE
POTENTIAL AND AN OUTSIDE CHANCE FOR AN ISOLATED CLASS M FLARE.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 04/2100Z TO 05/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT MOSTLY QUIET LEVELS
FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS. YESTERDAY'S PROTON EVENT ENDED AT
04/2000UT WITH A MAXIMUM OF 17 PFU AT GREATER THAN 10 MEV.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE FOR THE NEXT 24 HOURS.
MINOR STORM CONDITIONS ARE EXPECTED FOR THE LAST TWO DAYS OF
THE FORECAST PERIOD AS A RESULT OF FILAMENT AND FLARE ACTIVITY
COUPLED WITH A FAVORABLY POSTIONED CORONAL HOLE. HIGH LATITUDE
STATIONS WILL SEE MAJOR, POSSIBLY SEVERE, STORM LEVELS.
