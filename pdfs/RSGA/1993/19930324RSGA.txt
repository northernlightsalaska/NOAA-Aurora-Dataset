JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 083 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 24 MAR 1993
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 23/2100Z
TO 24/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. REGION 7448 (N16W99)
PRODUCED A COUPLE C-CLASS SUBFLARES AS IT COMPLETED ITS PASSAGE
TO THE FAR SIDE. REGION 7452 (S11W33) WAS STABLE AND MAY HAVE
LOST THE DELTA MAGNETIC STRUCTURE THAT WAS VISIBLE IN ITS LEADER
SPOTS. NEW REGION 7459 (N13W14) WAS NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW. REGIONS 7452 AND 7459 MAY PRODUCE C-CLASS SUBFLARES.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 23/2100Z TO 24/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT UNSETTLED TO MINOR STORM
LEVELS FOLLOWING A SUDDEN IMPULSE OF 27 NANOTESLA RECORDED BY
THE BOULDER USGS MAGNETOMETER AT 23/2156Z. HIGH LATITUDES
RANGED FROM UNSETTLED TO SEVERE STORM LEVELS. THE FIELD
SUBSIDED TO UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE LEVELS NEAR THE END OF THE
SUMMARY PERIOD.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE LEVELS TOMORROW. ACTIVE
TO MINOR STORM LEVELS ARE EXPECTED FOR THE REMAINDER OF THE
PERIOD DUE TO A LARGE FILAMENT WHICH DISAPPEARED LATE ON
21 MARCH.
