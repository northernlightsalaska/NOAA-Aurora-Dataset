IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 04/2100Z
TO 05/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS VERY LOW.  A COUPLE B-CLASS
X-RAY BURSTS OCCURRED DURING THE PERIOD.  REGION 7616 (N07E73)
AND REGION 7617 (S20E61) WERE NUMBERED OVERNIGHT.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
VERY LOW TO LOW.  C-CLASS FLARING, ESPECIALLY FROM REGION 7613
(S12W09), IS STILL POSSIBLE.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 04/2100Z TO 05/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT UNSETTLED TO MINOR STORM
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS AT MIDDLE LATITUDES.  HIGHER
LATITUDES EXPERIENCED UNSETTLED TO MAJOR STORM LEVELS.  THIS
ACTIVITY WAS PROBABLY DUE TO CORONAL HOLE EFFECTS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM LEVELS FOR THE FIRST
COUPLE DAYS DECLINING TO MOSTLY UNSETTLED CONDITIONS ON THE
THIRD DAY.
