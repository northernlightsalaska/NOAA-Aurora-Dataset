IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 29/2100Z
TO 30/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. A FEW C-CLASS X-RAY BURSTS
OCCURRED. THE LARGEST OF THESE WAS A C9 AT 30/0608Z ASSOCIATED
WITH MINOR DISCRETE RADIO EMISSIONS. REGION 7624 (N03W14) SHOWED
MINOR GROWTH AND PRODUCED A SINGLE B-CLASS SUBFLARE. REGION 7627
(S18E70) DISPLAYED ACTIVE SURGING AS IT CONTINUED TO ROTATE INTO
VIEW. IT ALSO PRODUCED AN ERUPTIVE PROMINENCE VERY EARLY IN THE
PERIOD WHICH REACHED O.18 SOLAR RADII. NEW REGION 7628 (S21W58)
WAS ALSO NUMBERED.

LOW. REGIONS 7624 AND 7627 MAY PRODUCE C-CLASS FLARES. REGION
7627 ALSO PROVIDES A SLIGHT CHANCE FOR AN M-CLASS FLARE.
