JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 077 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 18 MAR 1993
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 17/2100Z
TO 18/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. REGION 7448 (N17W22)
CONTINUES AS THE MOST SIGNIFICANT GROUP ON THE DISK. THE REGION
HAS EXPERIENCED RENEWED DEVELOPMENT AFTER SHOWING A SLOW DECLINE
OF MOST PARAMETERS OVER THE PAST FEW DAYS. AN ISOLATED POLE HAS
EMERGED IN THE SOUTHEAST PORTION OF THE REGION. THE SUNSPOT
PENUMBRAL AREA HAS INCREASED AS WELL, FORMING A DELTA
CONFIGURATION. THE REGION HAS PRODUCED ONLY SMALL FLARES SINCE
YESTERDAY. NEW REGION 7453 (S06E65) WAS NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. THE RENEWED DEVELOPMENT OF REGION 7448 MAY
PRECIPITATE A MAJOR FLARE.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 17/2100Z TO 18/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE AT THE BEGINNING
OF THE DAY, BECOMING MOSTLY QUIET AFTER 18/1500Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE QUIET TO UNSETTLED FOR THE NEXT DAY OR SO.
AN INCREASE IN ACTIVITY MAY BEGIN LATE ON 20 MARCH DUE TO A
CORONAL HOLE.
