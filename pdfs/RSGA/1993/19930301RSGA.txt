SDF NUMBER 060 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 01 MAR 1993
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 28/2100Z
TO 01/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. REGION 7435 (N08E13)
PRODUCED THE LARGEST FLARE TODAY, A C3/1N AT 01/1654UT. THIS
REGION CONTINUED TO GROW SLOWLY AND IS BEGINNING TO TAKE ON
SOME MAGNETIC COMPLEXITY. REGION 7439 (S10E82) WAS NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW. REGION 7435 SHOWS THE BEST CLASS C FLARE POTENTIAL AND
HAS A SMALL CLASS M FLARE CHANCE.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 28/2100Z TO 01/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO ACTIVE LEVELS
FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS. HIGH LATITUDE STATIONS SAW A PERIOD OF
MINOR TO MAJOR STORM LEVELS, PROBABLY THE REMAINING EFFECTS
OF DISAPPEARING FILAMENTS ON 26 FEBRUARY.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE MOSTLY UNSETTLED OVER THE NEXT TWO DAYS WITH
PERIODS OF ACTIVE LEVELS AS THE GEOMAGNETIC DISTURBANCE
CONTINUES TO SUBSIDE. QUIET TO UNSETTLED CONDITIONS ARE
PREDICTED FOR THE FINAL DAY OF THE FORECAST PERIOD.
