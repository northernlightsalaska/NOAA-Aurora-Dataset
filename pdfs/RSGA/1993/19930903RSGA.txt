JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 246 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 03 SEP 1993
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 02/2100Z
TO 03/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS VERY LOW. REGION 7576 (N10E39)
SHOWED MODEST GROWTH TODAY BUT WAS STABLE. A SMALL FILAMENT NEAR
N19W64 DISAPPEARED SOMETIME BETWEEN 0024Z AND 1354Z.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
VERY LOW.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 02/2100Z TO 03/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS INTIALLY QUIET BUT BECAME DISTURBED
AFTER 00Z: MID-LATITUDES WERE GENERALLY ACTIVE AND HIGH
LATITUDE SITES EXPERIENCED PREDOMINANTLY ACTIVE TO MINOR
STORM LEVELS. A PERIOD OF MAJOR STORM OCCURRED AT HIGH
LATITUDES FROM 0900-1200Z. THE ACTIVITY WAS MOST LIKELY
CAUSED BY AN EXTENSION OF THE NORTHERN POLAR CORONAL HOLE.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE WITH POSSIBLE HIGH LATITUDE
SUBSTORMS FOR THE NEXT 12-15 HOURS. CONDITIONS SHOULD RETURN
TO PREDOMINANTLY UNSETTLED LEVELS THEREAFTER AND FOR THE RE-
MAINDER OF THE FORECAST PERIOD.
