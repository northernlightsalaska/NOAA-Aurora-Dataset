JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 108 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 18 APR 1993
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 17/2100Z
TO 18/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH. REGION 7477 (N10E52)
PRODUCED AN M8/1B FLARE AT 18/1915Z. THE FLARE WAS ACCOMPANIED
BY A 680 SFU TENFLARE AND MAJOR TYPES II AND IV SPECTRAL RADIO
BURSTS. THE REGION DISPLAYED GRADUAL GROWTH AND SOME SIGNS OF
MAGNETIC RESTRUCTURING PRIOR TO THE FLARE. REGION 7473 (N17E84)
PRODUCED A FEW SUBFLARES, ONE OF WHICH REACHED C-CLASS. NEW
REGION 7480 (S15E70) PRODUCED A C4/1N FLARE WHILE ROTATING
INTO VIEW. NEW REGION 7479 (S16E09) WAS ALSO NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE TO HIGH. REGION 7477 MAY PRODUCE ANOTHER ISOLATED
MAJOR FLARE.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 17/2100Z TO 18/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO ACTIVE LEVELS FOR
THE PAST 24 HOURS. MINOR TO MAJOR STORMING OCCURRED AT SOME
HIGH LATITUDE STATIONS FROM APPROXIMATELY 18/0900-1500Z. THIS
ACTIVITY WAS PROBABLY DUE TO CORONAL HOLE EFFECTS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT MOSTLY ACTIVE LEVELS DURING THE FIRST TWO
DAYS WITH STORM PERIODS POSSIBLE AT HIGH LATITUDES. ACTIVITY
IS EXPECTED TO DECLINE TO UNSETTLED LEVELS ON THE FINAL DAY.
