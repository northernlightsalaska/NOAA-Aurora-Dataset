JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 062 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 03 MAR 1993
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 02/2100Z
TO 03/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH. REGION 7440 (S07E66)
ENDED YESTERDAY WITH AN M5/SF TENFLARE WITH AN ASSOCIATED TYPE
II SWEEP AND LOOP PROMINENCE. THIS LDE REACHED ITS MAXIMUM
AT 02/2149UT AND RESULTED IN A MINOR PROTON ENHANCEMENT. THIS
REGION APPEARS TO HAVE FULLY ROTATED ONTO THE DISK AND IS NOW
CLASSIFIED AS A DKC GROUP. REGION 7435 (N08W15), STILL GROWING,
PRODUCED A C3/SF FLARE AS WELL AS MINOR FLARES. THE 32 DEGREE
FILAMENT TRAILING REGION 7435 DISAPPEARED YESTERDAY AFTERNOON.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. REGIONS 7435 AND 7440 BOTH HAVE A HIGH C CLASS
FLARE POTENTIAL, ISOLATED M CLASS FLARE POTENTIAL AND AN OUTSIDE
CHANCE FOR AN ISOLATED X CLASS FLARE.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 02/2100Z TO 03/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS. HIGH LATITUDE STATIONS SAW MINOR
TO MAJOR STORM LEVELS. THE ACTIVITY CAN BE ATTRIBUTED TO A
FILAMENT THAT DISAPPEARED ON 26 FEBRUARY.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE MOSTLY UNSETTLED FOR THE NEXT THREE DAYS.
