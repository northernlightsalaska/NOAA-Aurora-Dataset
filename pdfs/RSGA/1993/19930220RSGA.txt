JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 051 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 20 FEB 1993
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 19/2100Z
TO 20/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. REGIONS 7425 (N16W40)
AND 7427 (S18W09) CONTINUED TO SLOWLY DECAY. NEITHER REGION
PRODUCED SIGNIFICANT ACTIVITY. NEW REGION 7433 (N12E75) ROTATED
ONTO THE DISK AS A SMALL BIPOLAR SUNSPOT GROUP. THIS REGION WAS
THE BRIGHTEST ON THE DISK IN H-ALPHA WITH FREQUENT NEAR-FLARE-
BRIGHT FLUCTUATIONS. REMAINING REGIONS WERE QUIET.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW. ISOLATED MODERATE ACTIVITY IS POSSIBLE FROM REGIONS 7427
AND 7433.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 19/2100Z TO 20/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS QUIET UNTIL 20/0030Z. AT THAT TIME A
SMALL SHOCK ARRIVAL, REGISTERING ABOUT 9 NANOTESLA AT BOULDER,
WAS OBSERVED AT MIDDLE LATITUDE STATIONS. THE FIELD RANGED FROM
UNSETTLED TO STORM LEVELS THEREAFTER. MAJOR STORM CONDITIONS
WERE OBSERVED AT HIGH LATITUDES FROM 20/0600-0900Z. THIS
DISTURBANCE MAY BE ASSOCIATED WITH THE 18 MARCH FLARE ACTIVITY.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE MOSTLY UNSETTLED. ACTIVE TO STORM CONDITIONS ARE
POSSIBLE DURING LOCAL NIGHTTIME.
