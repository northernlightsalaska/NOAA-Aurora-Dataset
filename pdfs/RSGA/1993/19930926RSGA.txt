JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 269 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 26 SEP 1993
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 25/2100Z
TO 26/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. HOWEVER, STRONG SURGING
NEAR N13E90 AND A LONG-DURATION C2 TENFLARE WITH TYPES II AND IV
SWEEP AT 0237Z HERALDED THE ARRIVAL OF A NEW ACTIVE REGION
TO THE VISIBLE DISK. PRELIMINARY REPORTS INDICATE THERE MAY BE
A MATURE H-TYPE SPOT COMING INTO VIEW FOR THIS AS YET UNNUMBERED
REGION. A JUMP IN TODAY'S 10.7 CM FLUX, AS WELL AS FREQUENT
TYPE III SWEEP AND A 245 MHZ RADIO NOISE STORM, ALL LEND SUPPORT
TO THE POTENTIAL VOLATILITY OF THIS NEW SPOT GROUP. ELSEWHERE,
REGION 7585 (S07W28) CONTINUED TO GROW WHILE PRODUCING
OCCASIONAL SUBFLARES.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW, WITH AN INCREASING CHANCE OF ATTAINING MODERATE LEVELS
DURING THE FORECAST PERIOD.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 25/2100Z TO 26/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN QUIET TO UNSETTLED.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED.
