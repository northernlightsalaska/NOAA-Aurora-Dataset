JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 075 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 16 MAR 1993
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 15/2100Z
TO 16/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 7440
PRODUCED THE LARGEST FLARE SINCE YESTERDAY. NO OPTICAL
CLASSIFICATION WAS POSSIBLE, SINCE IT OCCURRED FROM BEYOND THE
WEST LIMB, BUT POST FLARE LOOPS WERE OBSERVED AT S02W90. THIS
FLARE REACHED AN X-RAY MAXIMUM OF M2 AT 15/2135Z. REGION 7448
(N17W00) IS THE LARGEST REGION PRESENTLY ON THE DISK AND
PRODUCED A FEW SUBFLARES DURING THE PAST DAY. THE LARGEST WAS A
C5/SN AT 16/0247Z. NEW REGIONS 7451 (S19E65) AND 7452 (S14E73)
WERE NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. THE LIKELIHOOD OF MAJOR FLARES HAS DECREASED
WITH THE DEPARTURE OF REGION 7440.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 15/2100Z TO 16/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS AT ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM LEVELS.
THE CONTINUATION OF THIS DISTURBANCE, WHICH BEGAN AT 15/0526Z
IN RESPONSE TO THE M7 FLARE ON 12 MARCH, MAY BE DUE TO A WELL
POSITIONED CORONAL HOLE.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE MOSTLY UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE FOR THE NEXT THREE
DAYS.
