JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 072 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 13 MAR 1993
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 12/2100Z
TO 13/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. REGION 7448 (N16E40) PRO-
DUCED TODAY'S LARGEST FLARE, A C5/SF AT 1536Z. THIS GROUP SHOWED
MODEST GROWTH OVER THE LAST 24 HOURS AND APPEARS TO HAVE A BETA-
GAMMA MAGNETIC CLASS WITH A LONG EAST-WEST INVERSION LINE. THE
GROUP PRODUCED ADDITIONAL SMALL FLARES DURING THE DAY. REGION
7440 (S04W69) LOOKS SIMILAR IN STRUCTURE TO YESTERDAY, BUT WAS
COMPARITIVELY QUIET AND COULD ONLY MUSTER A COUPLE OF C3 FLARES
DURING THE PERIOD.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MOSTLY MODERATE, WITH A CONTINUED CHANCE FOR AN ISOLATED MAJOR
FLARE OR PROTON PRODUCING EVENT. ACTIVITY LEVELS AND BACKGROUND
LEVELS SHOULD DECLINE NOTICABLY WITH THE DEPARTURE OF REGION
7440 FROM THE VISIBLE DISK LATE ON THE 15TH.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 12/2100Z TO 13/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS MOSTLY UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE FOR THE
PAST 24 HOURS. SOME ISOLATED PERIODS OF MINOR STORM LEVEL
CONDITIONS WERE OBSERVED. THE GREATER THAN 10 MEV PROTON
EVENT WHICH BEGAN AT 12/2010Z REACHED A MAXIMUM OF 44 PFU
AT 13/0155Z AND ENDED AT 13/1245Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT MINOR TO MAJOR STORM LEVELS FOR THE NEXT TWO
DAYS AS A RESPONSE TO YESTERDAY'S LONG DURATION M7 FLARE FROM
REGION 7440. CONDITIONS SHOULD SUBSIDE TO UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE
LEVELS BY THE THIRD DAY.
