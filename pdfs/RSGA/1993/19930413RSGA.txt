JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 103 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 13 APR 1993
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 12/2100Z
TO 13/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. THE LARGEST FLARE OF THE
PAST DAY WAS A C1/SF AT 12/2310Z IN REGION 7472 (N07W85). ONLY
A FEW OTHER FLARES OCCURRED, MOST OF WHICH ORIGINATED IN THIS
REGION AND REGION 7469 (S07W88). THESE REGIONS ARE AT THE WEST
LIMB AND UPON THEIR DEPARTURE, THE VISIBLE DISK WILL BE
DOMINATED BY SMALL, UNINTERESTING REGIONS.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO VERY LOW. SMALL FLARES ARE POSSIBLE FROM REGIONS 7469
AND 7472 BEFORE THEY ROTATE BEYOND THE WEST LIMB.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 12/2100Z TO 13/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS QUIET UNTIL 13/0300Z. THE FIELD
GRADUALLY REACHED STORM LEVELS BY 13/0600Z AND REMAINED THERE
UNTIL 13/1500Z. THE FIELD WAS UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE AFTER THAT.
THE SOURCE OF THIS DISTURBANCE IS NOT CLEAR.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE QUIET TO UNSETTLED FOR MOST OF THE FORECAST
PERIOD. CORONAL HOLE AFFECTS MAY BE FELT AS EARLY AS 16 APRIL.
