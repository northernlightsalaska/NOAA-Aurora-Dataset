IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 29/2100Z
TO 30/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 7461 (N05E16)
PRODUCED AN M2/1B TENFLARE WITH AN ASSOCIATED TYPE II SWEEP AT
30/0105UT, A C3/SF FLARE AT 30/1256UT AND TWO SUB FAINT FLARES.
THIS REGION APPEARS TO BE DECAYING WHILE INCREASING IN COMPACT-
NESS. REGION 7463 (S13E36) PRODUCED A C1/SF FLARE AT 30/0332UT,
TWO SMALL CLASS B FLARES AND A SUB FAINT FLARE. IT SHOWED LITTLE
CHANGE. REGION 7465 (S05E77) HAS GROWN QUICKLY BUT REMAINS
GENERALLY UNEXCITING SO FAR.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO OCCASIONALLY MODERATE. REGION 7461 STILL HAS SOME CLASS
M FLARE POTENTIAL AND AN OUTSIDE CHANCE OF PRODUCING AN ISOLATED
CLASS X FLARE. REGIONS 7463 AND 7465 BOTH HAVE POTENTIAL FOR
CLASS C ACTIVITY.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 29/2100Z TO 30/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN MOSTLY UNSETTLED FOR THE PAST
24 HOURS. ALL STATIONS SAW A SHORT PERIOD OF ACTIVE TO MINOR
STORM LEVELS BETWEEN 1500 AND 1800 UT TODAY.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE QUIET TO UNSETTLED FOR THE ENTIRE PERIOD.
