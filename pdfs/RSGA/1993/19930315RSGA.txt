JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 074 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 15 MAR 1993
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 14/2100Z
TO 15/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 7440 (S06W93)
PRODUCED TODAY'S ONLY M-CLASS EVENT, AN M1/2F IN PROGRESS AT
14/2100Z. THE X-RAY SIGNATURE SUGGESTS A GOOD POSSIBILITY FOR
PROTONS. A STRONG CALCIUM EMISSION WAS NOTED ON THE SOUTHEAST
LIMB TODAY.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. REGION 7448 MAY PRODUCE AN ISOLATED M-CLASS
FLARE OVER THE NEXT THREE DAYS. A GENERAL UPWARD TREND IN BACK-
GROUND LEVELS IS EXPECTED AS AN INFERRED NEW REGION ROTATES
AROUND FROM EAST LIMB.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 14/2100Z TO 15/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD RANGED FROM UNSETTLED TO SEVERE STORM
LEVELS. AN INITIALLY UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE FIELD CHANGED TO
STORM LEVELS FOLLOWING A SUDDEN STORM COMMENCEMENT (SSC) AT
0526UT. MID-LATITUDES EXPERIENCED MINOR TO MAJOR STORM LEVELS
AND HIGH LATITUDES REPORTETD MAJOR TO SEVERE STORM LEVELS.
THE DISTURBANCE WAS BEGINNING TO SUBSIDE NEAR DAY'S END.
THIS STORM WAS PROBABLY CAUSED BY THE LONG DURATION M7 FLARE
OF 12 MARCH.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO SLIGHTLY ACTIVE OVER THE NEXT 12
HOURS, AFTER WHICH ACTIVITY SHOULD DECLINE UNTIL DAY THREE IN
RESPONSE TO THE M-FLARE IN PROGRESS. ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM
LEVELS MAY BE ATTAINED ON DAY THREE.
