JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 271 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 28 SEP 1993
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 27/2100Z
TO 28/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 7590 (N12E63)
PRODUCED THE PERIOD'S ONLY M-CLASS EVENT: AN M1/SN AT 27/2249Z.
THE REGION HAS ROTATED MORE FULLY INTO VIEW AS AN IMPRESSIVE FKI
SPOT GROUP WITH AN AREA OF ABOUT 1100 MILLIONTHS. THERE IS SOME
EVIDENCE OF A MAGNETIC DELTA CONFIGURATION IN THE TRAILING SEC-
TION OF THE LARGE LEADER SPOT COMPLEX. REGION 7585 (N07W56)
SHOWED STEADY DECAY DURING THE PERIOD. NEW REGION 7591 (N07E47)
EMERGED ON THE DISK JUST SOUTHWEST OF 7590.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE DUE TO CONTINUED ACTIVITY FROM 7590. THERE IS A SLIGHT
CHANCE FOR AN ISOLATED MAJOR FLARE FROM THE GROUP DURING THE
FORECAST PERIOD.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 27/2100Z TO 28/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS PREDOMINANTLY QUIET TO UNSETTLED.
SOME HIGH LATITUDE SITES REPORTED ISOLATED PERIODS OF ACTIVE
CONDITIONS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE FOR THE NEXT TWO DAYS, BE-
COMING INCREASINGLY ACTIVE BY DAY THREE. THE INCREASE IS EX-
PECTED DUE TO EFFECTS FROM A FAVORABLY POSITIONED CORONAL
HOLE.
