JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 192 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 10 JUL 1992
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 09/2100Z
TO 10/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. REGION 7220 (S11E13)
PRODUCED C-CLASS FLARES. THE REGION IS SLOWLY DECREASING IN
SIZE. REGION 7222 (S09E25) ALSO PRODUCED C-CLASS FLARES. THIS
REGION HAS DOUBLED IN SIZE. SMALL SUBFLARES ALSO EMERGED FROM
REGIONS 7219 (S04W08) AND 7224 (S12W31). NEW REGION: 7226
(S21E38).
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE. REGION 7220 AND 7222 REMAIN COMPLEX AND COMPACT.
M-CLASS FLARES ARE EXPECTED FROM THESE REGIONS. REGION 7205
WILL ALSO INCREASE ACTIVITY WHEN IT REAPPEARS.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 09/2100Z TO 10/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS MOSTLY QUIET.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE QUIET TO UNSETTLED. AN INCREASE IN ACTIVITY
IS EXPECTED AS A CORONAL HOLE MOVES INTO A FAVORABLE POSITION
LATE ON 12 JULY.
