JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 290 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 16 OCT 1992
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 15/2100Z
TO 16/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS VERY LOW. ALL SOLAR ACTIVE
REGIONS WERE QUIET AND STABLE. NEW REGION 7314 (N15E74) WAS
ASSIGNED TODAY AS A SIMPLE H-CLASS SPOT GROUP. SURGING WAS
REPORTED ON EAST LIMB AT S10.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW. AN INCREASE IN BACKGROUND LEVELS IS ANTICIPATED ON THE
THIRD DAY WITH THE RETURN OF OLD REGION 7293 (S07, L-069).
THE GROUP WAS MAGNETICALLY COMPLEX ON ITS PREVIOUS DISK TRANSIT
BUT NEVERTHELESS WAS SMALL AND PRODUCED ONLY OCCASIONAL SUB-
FLARES.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 15/2100Z TO 16/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS INITIALLY AT ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM
LEVELS AS YESTERDAY'S DISTURBANCE PERSISTED. ACTIVITY ABATED
AFTER 0900Z AND HAS BEEN MOSTLY QUIET TO UNSETTLED SINCE
THEN.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE GENERALLY UNSETTLED FOR THE NEXT THREE DAYS
WITH A CHANCE FOR ISOLATED ACTIVE LEVELS AT HIGH LATITUDES
DURING THE NEXT 12 HOURS.
