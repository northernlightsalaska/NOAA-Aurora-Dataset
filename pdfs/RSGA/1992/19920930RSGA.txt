JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 274 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 30 SEP 1992
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 29/2100Z
TO 30/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. REGION 7296 (N07E15)
PRODUCED THE ONLY C-CLASS FLARE OF THE PERIOD: A C2/1N AT
30/0454Z. REGION 7298 (N16E49) GREW FROM AN A- TO A D-TYPE
SUNSPOT GROUP DURING THE PERIOD, BUT PRODUCED NO FLARES. NO NEW
REGIONS WERE ASSIGNED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW. REGIONS 7293 (S07W30), 7296, AND 7298 MAY PRODUCE C-CLASS
FLARES.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 29/2100Z TO 30/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT UNSETTLED TO MINOR STORM
LEVELS. PERIODS OF MAJOR STORMING OCCURRED AT HIGH LATITUDES.
THIS ACTIVITY WAS PROBABLY DUE TO CORONAL HOLE EFFECTS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM LEVELS WITH ISOLATED
PERIODS OF MAJOR STORMING POSSIBLE AT HIGH LATITUDES DUE TO
CONTINUING CORONAL HOLE EFFECTS. THE FILAMENT DISAPPEARANCE
MENTIONED IN SDF NUMBER 273 IS EXPECTED TO PROVIDE MINOR STORM
CONDITIONS DURING THE FINAL DAY OF THE FORECAST AS WELL.
