JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 063 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 03 MAR 1992
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 02/2100Z
TO 03/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS VERY LOW. ONLY ONE SUBFAINT
FLARE WAS REPORTED DURING THE DAY FROM AN UNASSIGNED REGION
AT S11W45. NEW REGION 7088 (S17W26) EMERGED TODAY AND DEVELOPED
SOME RUDIMENTARY PENUMBRA. THE RETURN OF OLD REGION 7050 WAS
ASSIGNED AS 7089 (N10E80). REGION 7070 (N08W77) AND 7083
(S10W83) WERE BOTH QUIET AND SHOWED STEADY DECAY.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW. THERE MAY BE A SLIGHT INCREASE ON THE THIRD DAY DUE TO THE
RETURN OF OLD REGION 7056 (L184, S12) WHICH PRODUCED 1 X-FLARE
AND 9 M-FLARES ON ITS PREVIOUS DISK PASSAGE.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 02/2100Z TO 03/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS MOSTLY QUIET TO UNSETTLED. SOME
BRIEF ACTIVE PERIODS WERE OBSERVED AT HIGH LATITUDES.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE GENERALLY UNSETTLED.
