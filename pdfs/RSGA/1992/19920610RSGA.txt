JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 162 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 10 JUN 1992
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 09/2100Z
TO 10/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. REGIONS 7191 (N12W30) AND
7194 (S12E64) PRODUCED ALL OF TODAY'S SCANT FLARE ACTIVITY. THE
LARGEST FLARE WAS A C3/SF FROM 7191 AT 1321Z. THE OTHER SOLAR
ACTIVE REGIONS ON THE DISK WERE STABLE OR DECAYING.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW. REGION 7191 COULD DEVELOP SHEAR BUT POSSESSES WEAK MAGNETIC
FIELDS AND IS LIKELY ONLY TO PRODUCE SMALL FLARES. REGION 7194
HAS ONE FAIRLY LARGE LEADER SPOT BUT THE LEADER IS FOLLOWED BY
RATHER WIDELY DISPERSED FIELDS OF OPPOSITE POLARITY AND IS EX-
PECTED TO PRODUCE ONLY SMALL FLARES.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 09/2100Z TO 10/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD RANGED FROM QUIET TO MINOR STORM LEVELS.
QUIET TO UNSETTLED CONDITIONS BEGAN THE PERIOD AND WERE FOL-
LOWED BY ABOUT 12 HOURS OF ACTIVE CONDITIONS FROM 10/0300Z TO
10/1500Z; SOME BRIEF MINOR STORM PERIODS WERE REPORTED DURING
THIS TIME AS WELL. ACTIVITY WAS QUIET TO UNSETTLED FROM 1500Z
THROUGH THE END OF THE PERIOD.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE ACTIVE TOMORROW AND PARTWAY THROUGH THE SECOND
DAY IN RESPONSE TO THE M1 FLARE OF 08 JUNE. CONDITIONS SHOULD
DECLINE TO GENERALLY UNSETTLED LEVELS BY THE THIRD DAY.
