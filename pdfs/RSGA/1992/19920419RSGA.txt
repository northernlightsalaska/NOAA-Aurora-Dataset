JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 110 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 19 APR 1992
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 18/2100Z
TO 19/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. REGION 7135 (S16E41) HAD
NUMEROUS C-CLASS FLARES DURING THE INTERVAL, THE LARGEST A C7/0B
AT 1805Z. THE REGION CONTINUED TO GROW AND EVOLVE AND NOW SHOWS
A WEAK DELTA IN THE INTERMEDIATE SPOTS. MOST OF THE FLARES HAVE
BEEN NEAR THE BAND OF BRIGHT PLAGE ENCIRCLING THE TRAILER
SPOTS. TWO NEW REGIONS, 7138 (N09E62) AND 7139 (S16E64), ARE NOW
FULLY VISIBLE. A SMALL FILAMENT DISAPPEARED FROM S21W63 AROUND
MIDDAY.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. REGION 7135 MAY MUSTER AN ISOLATED M-CLASS
EVENT IN THE NEXT 72 HOURS.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 18/2100Z TO 19/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS VARIED FROM QUIET TO MILDLY ACTIVE
CONDITIONS AT ALL LATITUDES.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED THE NEXT THREE DAYS. A SMALL
CORONAL HOLE MAY CAUSE EPISODES OF ACTIVE CONDITIONS IN THE
NEXT 24 HOURS.
