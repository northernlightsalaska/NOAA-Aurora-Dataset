JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 151 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 30 MAY 1992
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 29/2100Z
TO 30/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS VERY LOW.  NO C-CLASS OR HIGHER
X-RAY EVENTS HAVE OCCURRED.  SPOTS THAT EMERGED TODAY IN THE
VICINITY OF PLAGE REGION 7179 (S07W56) HAVE BEEN ASSIGNED TO
THAT REGION.  NEW REGION 7183 (S24E72), AN H-TYPE GROUP, IS THE
RETURN OF OLD REGION 7154, WHICH PRODUCED SIGNIFICANT FLARES
LAST ROTATION.  SURGING AND A SUBFAINT FLARE WERE OBSERVED IN
REGION 7183 TODAY.  NEW REGION 7184 (N10W38), A B-TYPE GROUP,
AND NEW REGION 7185 (N12E25), AN A-TYPE GROUP, EMERGED ON THE
DISK.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
VERY LOW.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 29/2100Z TO 30/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT MOSTLY UNSETTLED
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS.  PERIODS OF ACTIVE TO MINOR
STORM CONDITIONS WERE OBSERVED AT AURORAL LATITUDES.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO PERSIST AT UNSETTLED TO OCCASIONALLY ACTIVE LEVELS
TOMORROW.  GENERALLY UNSETTLED CONDITIONS ARE EXPECTED FOR
1 JUNE, INCREASING TOWARD ACTIVE LEVELS BY THE END OF THE
FORECAST PERIOD IN RESPONSE TO EFFECTS ASSOCIATED WITH A
FILAMENT DISAPPEARANCE BETWEEN 28 AND 29 MAY FROM THE SOLAR
SOUTHWEST QUADRANT.
