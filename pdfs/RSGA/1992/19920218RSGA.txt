JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 049 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 18 FEB 1992
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 17/2100Z
TO 18/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 7056 (S12W52)
PRODUCED SEVEN FLARES INCLUDING AN M1/SF AT 18/1334Z. THERE WERE
SIGNS THE REGION MAY HAVE ENTERED A DECAY PHASE. HOWEVER, IT
REMAINED MAGNETICALLY COMPLEX. REGION 7058 (N14W57) MAY HAVE
ENTERED A DECAY PHASE AS WELL. IT PRODUCED SEVERAL FLARES
INCLUDING A LONG DURATION M1/SN AT 18/0847Z. REGION 7050
(N13W93) PRODUCED A C8/SF FLARE WHILE CROSSING THE WEST LIMB.
NEW REGION 7067 (N04E82) ROLLED ACROSS THE EAST LIMB DISPLAYING
ACTIVE SURGING AND PRODUCED AN M1/1F FLARE AT 18/1707Z. A LOOP
PROMINENCE SYSTEM ACCOMPANIED THIS FLARE. NEW REGION 7066
(S06E08) WAS ALSO NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE TO HIGH. REGION 7056 IS EXPECTED TO PRODUCE M-CLASS
FLARES AND MAY ALSO PRODUCE A MAJOR FLARE. REGION 7067 MAY
CONTINUE TO PRODUCE M-CLASS FLARES AS WELL.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 17/2100Z TO 18/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO UNSETTLED LEVELS
FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS. THE HIGH LATITUDE FIELD RANGED FROM
QUIET TO MINOR STORM LEVELS WITH ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM LEVELS
OCCURRING FROM 18/1200-1800Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT MOSTLY ACTIVE LEVELS DURING THE FIRST TWO
DAYS IN RESPONSE TO RECENT FLARE ACTIVITY. THE FIELD SHOULD
DECLINE TO UNSETTLED LEVELS ON THE FINAL DAY.
