JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 161 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 09 JUN 1992
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 08/2100Z
TO 09/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. REGION 7186 (N09W26) PRO-
DUCED TODAY'S LARGEST FLARE: A C5/1F AT 0232Z. THE GROUP ALSO
PRODUCED A C4/1N AT 0630Z WHICH WAS ACCOMPANIED BY TYPE II AND
TYPE IV RADIO SWEEPS. THE LATTER FLARE APPEARS TO HAVE GREATLY
STABILIZED THE REGION: THE GROUP WAS QUIET AND SHOWED SLOW DECAY
FOR THE REMAINDER OF THE PERIOD. SIGNIFICANT NEW GROWTH WAS
NOTED TODAY IN REGION 7191 (N10W16). THIS GROUP SHOWED FREQUENT
BRIGHT PLAGE FLUCTUATIONS AND THE DEVELOPMENT OF SOME MAGNETIC
COMPLEXITY. REGION 7192 (N09E06) WAS QUIET AND STABLE. NEW
REGION 7194 (S11E74) ROTATED INTO VIEW TODAY AND PRODUCED A
FEW SUBFLARES.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. REGION 7191 AND 7186 ARE CONSIDERED TO BE THE
MOST LIKELY SOURCES FOR AN ISOLATED M-CLASS EVENT SOMETIME OVER
THE NEXT THREE DAYS.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 08/2100Z TO 09/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD RANGED FROM QUIET TO MINOR STORM LEVELS
FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS. THE PERIOD BEGAN AT ACTIVE TO MINOR
STORM LEVELS AS YESTERDAY'S DISTRUBANCE PERSISTED THROUGH
ABOUT 09/1200Z. AFTER 1200Z AND THROUGH THE REMAINDER OF
THE PERIOD THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS MOSTLY QUIET TO UNSET-
TLED.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE MOSTLY QUIET TO UNSETTLED WITH OCCASIONAL ACTIVE
PERIODS DUE TO SUBSTORM EFFECTS OVER THE NEXT 24 HOURS. BY DAY
TWO AN INCREASE TO GENERALLY ACTIVE LEVELS IS ANTICIPATED AS A
RESPONSE TO THE M1/TYPE IV FLARE OF 08 JUNE. CONDITIONS SHOULD
GRADUALLY SUBSIDE DURING THE THIRD DAY.
