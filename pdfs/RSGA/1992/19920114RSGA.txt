JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 014 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 14 JAN 1992
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 13/2100Z
TO 14/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. AN M1 FLARE OCCURRED
AT 1937Z. THE SOURCE WAS FROM BEHIND THE EAST LIMB AT S11. NO
SPOTS HAD BEEN OBSERVED AT FORECAST ISSUE TIME, BUT SURGING AND
ACTIVE PROMINENCE ACTIVITY WERE NOTED AT THIS LOCATION THROUGH-
OUT THE DAY. THE REST OF TODAY'S ACTIVITY CONSISTED OF ONLY A
HANDFUL OF LOW LEVEL C-CLASS FLARES. REGIONS 7004 (S15W84) AND
7008 (S09E36) SHOWED THE BRIGHTEST PLAGE OF THE GROUPS ON THE
DISK.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. ADDITIONAL ISOLATED M-CLASS ACTIVITY IS POS-
SIBLE FROM THE EAST LIMB, AND ALSO FROM EITHER 7007 OR 7008.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 13/2100Z TO 14/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD RANGED FROM QUIET TO MINOR STORM LEVELS.
CONDITIONS WERE MOSTLY UNSETTLED FROM THE START OF THE PERIOD
UNTIL ABOUT 1500Z. AT THAT POINT ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM COND-
ITIONS BEGAN AND CONTINUED THROUGH THE END OF THE PERIOD.
THE MOST LIKELY SOURCE IS A FAVORABLY POSITIONED CORONAL
HOLE.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO CONTINUE ACTIVE FOR THE EARLY PART OF THE FIRST
DAY, BUT IS EXPECTED TO DECLINE TO GENERALLY UNSETTLED LEVELS
BEGINNING SOMETIME TOMORROW AND LASTING THROUGH THE END OF
THE FORECAST PERIOD.
