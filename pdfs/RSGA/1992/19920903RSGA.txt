IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 02/2100Z

SUBFLARES OCCURRED. TWO OPTICALLY UNCORRELATED, MINOR TYPE II

SECOND AT 03/1442Z. NEW REGIONS 7273 (S17W02) AND 7274 (N08E18)
WERE NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
VERY LOW TO LOW. REGION 7272 (S12W66) COULD PRODUCE AN ISOLATED
C-CLASS SUBFLARE.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 02/2100Z TO 03/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT UNSETTLED TO SEVERE STORM
LEVELS. MINOR TO MAJOR STORMING BEGAN NEAR 03/0600Z REACHING
SEVERE LEVELS NEAR 03/0900Z. THEREAFTER, THE FIELD WAS AT
ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM LEVELS. THIS ACTIVITY MAY HAVE BEEN
RELATED TO A FAVORABLY POSITIONED CORONAL HOLE.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO RANGE FROM UNSETTLED TO MINOR STORM LEVELS. MINOR
STORMING IS LIKELY DURING THE FIRST TWO DAYS DUE TO CORONAL
HOLE EFFECTS.
