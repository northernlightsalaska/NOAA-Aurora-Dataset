JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 249 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 05 SEP 1992
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 04/2100Z
TO 05/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 7270 (S09W28)
CONTINUED TO GROW, BUT AT AN ACCELERATED PACE. IT PRODUCED THREE
M-CLASS FLARES: AN M2/SB AT 04/2352Z, AN M4/1N AT 05/1132Z, AND
AN M1/SN AT 05/1624Z. THE M4 WAS ASSOCIATED WITH A WEAK TYPE II
SWEEP AND A 140 SFU TENFLARE. REGION 7272 (S12W90) PRODUCED A
COUPLE C-CLASS SUBFLARES WHILE CROSSING THE LIMB. NEW REGION
7275 (S10W56) WAS ASSIGNED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE. REGION 7270 IS EXPECTED TO PRODUCE MORE M-CLASS
FLARES. THERE IS ALSO A SLIGHT CHANCE FOR AN X-CLASS FLARE FROM
THIS REGION.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 04/2100Z TO 05/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT UNSETTLED TO MINOR STORM
LEVELS AT MIDDLE LATITUDES AND AT UNSETTLED TO MAJOR STORM
LEVELS AT HIGH LATITUDES. THE FIELD DECLINED TO MOSTLY
UNSETTLED LEVELS BY 05/1800Z. THIS ACTIVITY WAS PROBABLY DUE TO
CORONAL HOLE EFFECTS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE MOSTLY UNSETTLED. BRIEF PERIODS OF ACTIVE
LEVELS MAY OCCUR DURING DURING THE FIRST DAY.
