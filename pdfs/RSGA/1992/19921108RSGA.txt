JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 313 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 08 NOV 1992
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 07/2100Z
TO 08/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. ONLY A FEW SMALL C-CLASS
FLARES OCCURRED DURING THE PERIOD. REGION 7330 (S08W23) PRO-
DUCED TODAY'S LARGEST FLARE, A C5/SN AT 1425Z. THIS GROUP HAS
SHOWN SOME GROWTH, INCLUDING THE DEVELOPMENT OF A SMALL DELTA
CONFIGURATION IN THE TRAILERS. REGIONS 7334 (N13E32) AND 7335
(S17E37) CONTINUE TO BE MODERATE SIZED MATURE GROUPS BUT WERE
STABLE. THE REMAINDER OF THE DISK AND LIMBS WERE QUIET AND
STABLE.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. THE RECENT DEVELOPMENTS IN REGION 7330 SUGGEST
THAT IT NOW HAS FAIR POTENTIAL FOR PRODUCING AN ISOLATED M-
CLASS FLARE.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 07/2100Z TO 08/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO UNSETTLED
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE MOSTLY UNSETTLED FOR THE NEXT TWO DAYS. AN IN-
CREASE TO UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE LEVELS IS ANTICIPATED ON THE
THIRD DAY DUE TO RECURRENCE EFFECTS.
