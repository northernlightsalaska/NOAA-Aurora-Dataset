JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 132 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 11 MAY 1992
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 10/2100Z
TO 11/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS VERY LOW. OCCASIONAL SUBFLARES
OCCURRED, NONE OF WHICH REACHED C-CLASS. AN ERUPTIVE PROMINENCE
REACHING 0.18 SOLAR RADII OCCURRED ON THE EAST LIMB NEAR N23 AT
11/0804Z. AN ACTIVE PROMINENCE WAS OBSERVED ON THE EAST LIMB
NEAR S13 AT 11/1333Z AND EXTENDED TO 0.03 RADII. NO NEW REGIONS
WERE ASSIGNED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW. REGIONS 7154 (S26W37) AND 7162 (N06W52) MAY PRODUCE
ISOLATED C-CLASS SUBFLARES.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 10/2100Z TO 11/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT UNSETTLED TO MAJOR STORM
LEVELS AT MIDDLE LATITUDES. THE HIGH LATITUDE FIELD HAS BEEN
AT MAJOR TO SEVERE STORM LEVELS. MIDDLE LATITUDE STORMING
CEASED NEAR 11/1200Z WHILE HIGH LATITUDE STORMING PERSISTED
UNTIL APPROXIMATELY 11/1800Z. THE GREATER THAN 10 MEV PROTON
EVENT ENDED AT 11/0615Z. THE POLAR CAP ABSORPTION EVENT ENDED
AT 11/0000Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT QUIET TO UNSETTLED LEVELS. ACTIVE LEVELS ARE
POSSIBLE AT HIGH LATITUDES DURING THE FIRST DAY.
