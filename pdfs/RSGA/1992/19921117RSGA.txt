JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 322 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 17 NOV 1992
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 16/2100Z
TO 17/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. REGION 7345 (S23E60) GREW
IN SIZE, PRODUCING A C3/SF FLARE AT 2143Z FOR THE PERIOD'S
LARGEST FLARE. NEW REGION 7346 (S06E70) EMERGED AROUND THE EAST
LIMB. REGION 7346 PRODUCED A C-CLASS FLARE ALSO. REGION
7341 (S16W36) GREW ABRUPTLY AND PRODUCED SEVERAL SUBFLARES.
THESE THREE REGIONS ARE PROBABLY RESPONSIBLE FOR THE SHARP
INCREASE IN 10 CM FLUX VALUES (127 TO 152 IN 48 HRS).
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. REGIONS 7341, 7345, AND 7346 ARE ALL CAPABLE
OF PRODUCING ISOLATED M-CLASS FLARES.

IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 16/2100Z TO 17/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT MOSTLY QUIET LEVELS
FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE QUIET FOR THE FIRST 24 HOURS AT MID-LATITUDES AND
THEN BECOME UNSETTLED FOR THE REMAINING 48 HOURS. HIGH LATITUDE
STATIONS ARE EXPECTED TO BE MOSTLY UNSETTLED.
