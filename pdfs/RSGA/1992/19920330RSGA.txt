JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 090 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 30 MAR 1992
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 29/2100Z
TO 30/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. TODAY'S ACTIVITY CONSISTED
OF A FEW SMALL C-CLASS SUBFLARES. MOST OF THESE WERE FROM NEWLY
EMERGING REGION 7122 (S12W14). REGION 7117 (N07W25) CONTINUES
TO BE THE LARGEST GROUP ON THE DISK AT 1270 MILLIONTHS BUT
REMAINS STABLE. THE REGION'S STRONG ARCH FILAMENT SYSTEM DIS-
SIPATED TODAY, INDICATING THE PROBABLE END OF GROWTH IN THE
GROUP. REGION 7120 (S18W06) ALSO COOLED CONSIDERABLY AND SHOWED
SOME SIGNS OF DECAY.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW. THERE CONTINUES TO BE SOME POSSIBILITY FOR AN ISOLATED M-
FLARE FROM REGION 7117.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 29/2100Z TO 30/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS MOSTLY QUIET TO UNSETTLED, WITH
OCCASIONAL ISOLATED ACTIVE PERIODS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE GENERALLY UNSETTLED WITH OCCASIONAL ACTIVE
PERIODS FOR THE NEXT THREE DAYS.
