JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 315 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 10 NOV 1992
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 09/2100Z
TO 10/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. REGION 7330 (S08W49)
STABILIZED DURING THE PAST 24 HOURS, BUT WAS CREDITED WITH A
C1/SF FLARE AT 0811Z. FOUR OTHER C-CLASS FLARES WERE
UNCORRELATED WITH ACTIVE REGIONS. REGION 7335 (S17E10) GREW IN
SIZE AND COMPLEXITY. THE EAST LIMB DISPLAYED SEVERAL BRIGHT
SURGES IN THE SOUTHERN HEMISPHERE. A NEW REGION NUMBER WAS
ASSIGNED: 7337 (N06E80). THIS REGION PRODUCED 3 M-FLARES LAST
ROTATION.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW. REGION 7335 HAS THE HIGHEST POTENTIAL FOR PRODUCING AN
ISOLATED M-CLASS FLARE.

IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 09/2100Z TO 10/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO UNSETTLED
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS. SOME HIGH LATITUDE STATIONS
EXPERIENCED PERIODS OF MINOR STORMING FROM 15-1800Z. A
DISAPPEARING FILAMENT OF 40 SQUARE DEGREES LEFT THE SE QUADRANT
(S02E74) OVERNITE.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE QUIET TO UNSETTLED FOR THE NEXT THREE DAYS.
