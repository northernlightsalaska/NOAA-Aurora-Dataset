JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 051 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 20 FEB 1992
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 19/2100Z
TO 20/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 7067 (N06E55)
PRODUCED SEVERAL C-CLASS SUBFLARES EARLY IN THE PERIOD AND AN
M1/SN EVENT AT 1809Z. THE M EVENT WAS ACCOMPANIED BY A LOOP
PROMINENCE SYSTEM. SOME SPOT AREA GROWTH WAS ALSO NOTED. REGION
7056 (S14W77) WAS GENERALLY STABLE WITH ONLY MINOR PLAGE FIELD
ENHANCEMENTS OBSERVED. NEW REGIONS 7069 (S03E67) AND 7070
(N03E79) WERE ASSIGNED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE. ISOLATED M-CLASS FLARING SHOULD CONTINUE FROM REGIONS
7056 AND 7067.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 19/2100Z TO 20/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO MAJOR STORM
LEVELS. A SUDDEN IMPULSE MEASURING 23 NANOTESLAS WAS RECORDED
BY BOULDER AT 20/0110Z WITH MOSTLY ACTIVE TO MINOR STORMING
NOTED AFTER ITS ARRIVAL. CORONAL HOLE EFFECTS OR THE LONG
DURATION M4 FLARE NOTED 72 HOURS PRIOR TO THE SHOCK ARE
POSSIBLE SOURCES.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM LEVELS FOR THE NEXT 12
TO 24 HOURS. GENERALLY UNSETTLED CONDITIONS ARE EXPECTED THE
REMAINDER OF THE PERIOD.
