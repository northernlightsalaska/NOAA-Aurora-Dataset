JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 251 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 07 SEP 1992
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 06/2100Z
TO 07/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH. REGION 7270 (S10W58)
SHOWED LITTLE FURTHER GROWTH BUT WAS THE ACTIVE REGION TODAY
PRODUCING 7 M-CLASS FLARES. THE LARGEST OF THESE WAS AN M7/2B
FLARE AT 07/0637UT. REGION 7270 WAS ALSO RESPONSIBLE FOR FOUR
C-CLASS FLARES. REGION 7276 (N15E77) WAS NUMBERED TODAY AND IS
THE RETURN OF OLD 7260. IT MADE ITS DEBUT WITH AN M1/SF FLARE AT
07/0009UT, A BSL OUT TO .18RV, A 1N FLARE AT 07/0823UT, A 1F
FLARE AT 07/1851UT, AND FOUR SUB FAINT FLARES. ALL SHOWED NO
ASSOCIATED X-RAYS.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE TO HIGH. REGION 7270 IS EXPECTED TO PRODUCE M-CLASS
FLARES AND HAS A GOOD CHANCE OF PRODUCING AN ISOLATED X FLARE.
REGION 7276 ALSO IS EXPECTED TO PRODUCE ISOLATED M-CLASS FLARES
AND HAS A SMALL CHANCE OF AN X-CLASS FLARE.

IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS

X-CLASS FLARES.
