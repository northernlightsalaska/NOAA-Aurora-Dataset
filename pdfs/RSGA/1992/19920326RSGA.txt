JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 086 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 26 MAR 1992
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 25/2100Z
TO 26/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. MOST OF TODAY'S FLARE PRO-
DUCTION CONSISTED OF SMALL, C-CLASS SUBFLARES FROM REGION 7117
(N09E29). THE LARGEST OF THESE WAS A C4/SF AT 0115Z. THIS GROUP
CONTINUED TO GROW TODAY, ALTHOUGH THE RATE OF GROWTH HAS SLOWED
COMPARED TO YESTERDAY. REGION 7116 (S10E23) ALSO SHOWED GROWTH
TODAY AND PRODUCED A FEW SUBFLARES DURING THE PERIOD. THE OTHER
REGIONS ON THE SUN WERE RELATIVELY QUIET AND STABLE.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. C-CLASS FLARE ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED PREDOMINATE
WITH THE EXPECTATION FOR AN ISOLATED M-CLASS EVENT SOMETIME
OVER THE NEXT THREE DAYS. REGION 7117 IS THE MOST LIKELY SOURCE
FOR ACTIVITY.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 25/2100Z TO 26/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS MOSTLY QUIET TO UNSETTLED. SOME
ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM LEVEL ACTIVITY WAS REPORTED AT HIGH
LATITUDE NEAR MIDDAY.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE GENERALLY UNSETTLED FOR THE NEXT THREE DAYS.
ISOLATED PERIODS OF ACTIVE CONDITIONS ARE EXPECTED AT HIGH
LATITUDES.
