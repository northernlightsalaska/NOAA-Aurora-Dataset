JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 005 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 05 JAN 1992
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 04/2100Z
TO 05/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH. REGION 6994 (S15E10)
GENERATED SEVERAL LOW-LEVEL M-CLASS FLARES. THIS REGIONS SHOWED
SLIGHT GROWTH WHILE APPEARING TO ELONGATE SOMEWHAT. REGION 6993
(S09E04) PRODUCED AN M1/1N FLARE AT 1327Z. M1 X-RAY EVENTS NOTED
AT 1706Z AND 2009Z WERE THE PRODUCT OF FLARES ERUPTING AT THE
SAME TIME IN REGIONS 6993 AND 6994 AS OBSERVED BY BOULDER.
REGION 6988 (S15W84) REMAINED GENERALLY QUIET. NEW REGION 7001
(N26E72) WAS ASSIGNED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE TO HIGH. REGIONS 6993 AND 6994 PROVIDE THE MOST
FAVORABLE SOURCE FOR OCCASIONAL M-CLASS FLARES.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 04/2100Z TO 05/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN MOSTLY QUIET TO UNSETTLED.
SOME ISOLATED MINOR STORMING WAS NOTED AT HIGH LATITUDES.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE QUIET TO UNSETTLED.
