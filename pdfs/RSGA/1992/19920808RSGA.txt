JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 221 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 08 AUG 1992
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 07/2100Z
TO 08/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. REGION 7248 (S12W11)
SHOWED NO SIGNIFICANT CHANGES AND PRODUCED SEVERAL SUBFLARES THE
LARGEST OF WHICH WAS A C6/SN AT 08/2011Z. REGION 7245 (S07W46)
ALSO PRODUCED SEVERAL SUBFLARES WHILE DISPLAYING MODERATE
GROWTH. NEW REGION 7257 (S20W45) EMERGED AND PRODUCED SEVERAL
SUBFLARES, TWO OF WHICH REACHED C-CLASS. A BRIGHT SURGE REACHING
0.15 SOLAR RADII WAS OBSERVED ON THE NE LIMB NEAR N28 AT
08/1610Z.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. REGION 7248 REMAINS CAPABLE OF ISOLATED
M-CLASS ACTIVITY.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 07/2100Z TO 08/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS RANGED FROM QUIET TO MINOR STORM
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS. THE MINOR STORMING OCCURRED
NEAR MID-PERIOD AT BOTH MIDDLE AND HIGH LATITUDES AND COULD
HAVE BEEN DUE TO CORONAL HOLE EFFECTS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE MOSTLY ACTIVE TOMORROW DUE TO CONTINUING
CORONAL HOLE EFFECTS. ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO DECLINE TO
UNSETTLED LEVELS FOR THE BALANCE OF THE PERIOD.
