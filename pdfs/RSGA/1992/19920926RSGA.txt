JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 270 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 26 SEP 1992
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 25/2100Z
TO 26/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. TWO UNCORRELATED C-CLASS
FLARES ERUPTED, ONE AT 0128Z AND THE OTHER AT 0159Z. ONE NEW
REGION WAS ASSIGNED: 7295 (N13E68). REGION 7295 CAUSED A LOT OF
SURGING AND LIMB ACTIVITY AS IT ROTATED ONTO THE DISK.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW FOR THE PERIOD. REGION 7295 SEEMS TO HAVE THE STRONGEST
FLARE POTENTIAL ON THE DISK, HOWEVER, PROBABILITY FOR M-CLASS
FLARE IS FIVE PERCENT.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 25/2100Z TO 26/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO UNSETTLED
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS. SOME HIGH LATITUDE STATIONS
OBSERVED K VALUES OF FIVE FROM 15-18Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE QUIET FOR THE FIRST PART OF THE PERIOD AND
GRADUALLY BECOME UNSETTLED AS A RECURRING CORONAL HOLE ROTATES
INTO FAVORABLE POSITION.
