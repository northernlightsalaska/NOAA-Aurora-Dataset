SDF NUMBER 327 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 22 NOV 1992
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 21/2100Z
TO 22/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE BECAUSE OF AN
UNCORRELATED M1 X-RAY EVENT AT 22/1307. THE REMAINDER OF TODAY'S
ACTIVITY CONSISTED OF SMALL C-CLASS AND SUBFAINT FLARES. REGIONS
7345 (S24W04) AND 7346 (S07E06) CONTINUED TO SHOW DECAY BUT ARE
STILL THE LARGEST REGIONS ON THE DISK. NEW REGIONS 7347 (S07W11)
AND 7348 (N13E76) WERE NUMBERED TODAY.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MOSTLY LOW. REGIONS 7345 AND 7346 STILL HAVE THE BEST FLARE
POTENTIAL. REGION 7347 HAS SHOWN EXPLOSIVE DEVELOPMENT AND IS
EXPECTED TO CONTINUE TO DEVELOPE.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 21/2100Z TO 22/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO UNSETTLED
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS. HIGH LATITUDE STATIONS SAW A
PERIOD OF MINOR TO MAJOR STORMING AROUND MIDDAY.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE FOR THE ENTIRE PERIOD
BECAUSE OF A FAVORABLY POSITIONED CORONAL HOLE.
CORONAL HOLE.
