JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 356 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 21 DEC 1992
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 20/2100Z
TO 21/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. FLARE ACTIVITY WAS
MINIMAL. REGION 7374 (S08E36) HAD THE BRUNT OF THE OCCASIONAL
FLARES, AS IT EVOLVED TO HAVE A WEAK DELTA EMERGE IN THE
INTERMEDIATE SPOTS. ITS BRIGHT PLAGE SUGGESTS THE POTENTIAL FOR
HIGHER ACTIVITY. ELSEWHERE, SURGE AND PROMINENCE ACTIVITY WAS
SEEN AT NE AND SE LIMB.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. ANY M-CLASS ACTIVITY SHOULD ORIGINATE IN
REGION 7374.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 20/2100Z TO 21/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS PREDOMINANTLY QUIET TO UNSETTLED.
SUBSTORMS BROUGHT EPISODES OF MAJOR STORM CONDITIONS TO HIGH
LATITUDES.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO PERSIST AT QUIET TO UNSETTLED LEVELS. ISOLATED
SUBSTORMS MAY CONTINUE, WITH THE MOST PRONOUNCED EFFECTS SEEN
AT HIGH LATITUDES.
