JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 177 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 25 JUN 1992
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 24/2100Z
TO 25/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH DUE TO A X3/2B FLARE IN
REGION 7205 (N10W67). THE FLARE MAXED AT 25/2014UT AND WAS
ASSOCIATED WITH A 2500 SFU RADIO BURST AT 2695 MHZ AND A
TYPE IV SWEEP. THIS REGION ALSO PRODUCED TWO LONG DURATION
M1 FLARES, AT 25/0736UT AND 25/1757UT, AND A NUMBER OF C-CLASS
FLARES. THIS REGION SHOWED CONTINUED SLOW GROWTH DURING THE
PAST 24 HOURS. NEW REGION 7212 (N07E17) WAS ASSIGNED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE. REGION 7205 IS EXPECTED TO PRODUCE C-CLASS FLARES AND
ISOLATED M-CLASS FLARES AS IT APPROACHES THE WEST LIMB. THERE
IS A POSSIBILITY OF AN ADDITIONAL MAJOR FLARE FROM THIS REGION.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 24/2100Z TO 25/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO ACTIVE LEVELS
FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS AS THE CORONAL HOLE DISTURBANCE
SUBSIDES. A SATELLITE PROTON EVENT AT GREATER THAN 10 MEV
REACHED THE 10 PFU EVENT THRESHOLD AT 25/2045UT. THIS IS
ASSOCIATED WITH THE X3/2B FLARE MENTIONED ABOVE.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE QUIET TO UNSETTLED UNTIL LATE ON 26 JUNE WHEN
A FLARE INDUCED DISTURBANCE IS FORECAST TO BEGIN. STORM
CONDITIONS ARE POSSIBLE FOR 24 HOURS FOLLLOWED BY UNSETTLED
TO ACTIVE LEVELS AS THE DISTURBANCE SUBSIDES. THE GREATER THAN
10 MEV PROTON EVENT SHOULD REACH A MAXIMUM BY 27 JUNE,
REMAINING IN PROGRESS FOR THE REMAINDER OF THE FORECAST PERIOD.
