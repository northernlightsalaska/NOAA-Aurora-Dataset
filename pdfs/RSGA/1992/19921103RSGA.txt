JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 308 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 03 NOV 1992
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 02/2100Z
TO 03/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. CLOUDS HAVE HAMPERED
PATROL OVER THE PAST DAY, BUT X-RAY DATA INDICATE LITTLE OF
IMPORTANCE OCCURRING. JUST A FEW C-CLASS EVENTS WERE DETECTED AS
THE OVERALL CHARACTER OF THE DISK HAS QUIETED SUBSTANTIALLY
FROM THE ACTIVITY OF THE PAST FEW DAYS. SIX SPOT GROUPS ARE
NOW VISIBLE WITH REGION 7330 (S07E43) THE MOST INTERESTING.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW. REGION 7330 HAS THE BEST POTENTIAL FOR SMALL FLARES.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 02/2100Z TO 03/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD VARIED FROM QUIET TO MINOR STORM LEVELS.
THE MOST DISTURBED PERIODS OCCURRED IN NIGHTTIME SECTORS. THE
GREATER THAN 10 MEV PROTON EVENT CONTINUES, BUT FLUXES SLOWLY
DECLINED ALL DAY. THE CURRENT VALUE IS 46 PFU (2100Z), DOWN FROM
A MAX OF 2700 PFU AT 31/0710Z, AND A SECONDARY MAX OF 790 PFU
AT 02/0550Z. THE POLAR CAP ABSORPTION EVENT ENDED AT 03/1900Z.
THE PEAK ABSORPTION AT THULE (30 MHZ) WAS 9.0 DB AT 31/1658Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE. SUBSTORMING IS LIKELY
DURING LOCAL NIGHTTIME HOURS.
