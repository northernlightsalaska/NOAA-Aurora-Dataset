JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 152 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 31 MAY 1992
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 30/2100Z
TO 31/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. AN UNASSOCIATED C1 X-RAY
EVENT OCCURRED 30/2229Z. REGION 7185 (N12E13) HAS GROWN RAPIDLY
FROM AN A-TYPE TO A C-TYPE GROUP, AND HAS PRODUCED SEVERAL SUB-
FLARES WITH SMALL X-RAY ENHANCEMENTS. A STRONG ARCH FILAMENT
SYSTEM IS IN PROGRESS THERE. SPOTLESS REGION 7178 (S23W46)
PRODUCED A SUBFAINT PARALLEL RIBBON FLARE AT 31/1228Z. NO NEW
REGIONS WERE IDENTIFIED. TWO FILAMENTS DISAPPEARED BETWEEN
30/2115Z AND 31/1438Z (OVERNIGHT). THE FIRST WAS 15 DEGREES
LONG AND WAS LOCATED NEAR N10E60 ON 30 MAY; THE SECOND WAS
ABOUT 10 DEGREES LONG AND WAS LOCATED NEAR S35W40. THE SOLAR
LIMBS WERE QUIET EXCEPT FOR AN ACTIVE PROMINENCE NEAR S15W90.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW. WITH CONTINUED GROWTH, REGION 7185 MAY BE CAPABLE OF
ERUPTIVE ACTIVITY.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 30/2100Z TO 31/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN GENERALLY QUIET FOR THE PAST
24 HOURS, WITH ONE PERIOD (03-06Z) AT LOCALLY ACTIVE LEVELS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE QUIET TO UNSETTLED TOMORROW, BECOMING ACTIVE ON
02 JUNE IN RESPONSE TO EFFECTS ASSOCIATED WITH A FILAMENT
DISAPPEARANCE BETWEEN 28 AND 29 MAY FROM THE SOLAR SOUTHWEST
QUADRANT. THEREAFTER, THE TREND WILL BE TOWARD QUIET CONDITIONS.
