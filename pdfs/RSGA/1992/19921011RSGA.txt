SDF NUMBER 285 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 11 OCT 1992
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 10/2100Z
TO 11/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. REGION 7303 (S14W74)
EXPERIENCED NEW GROWTH AND DEVELOPMENT THE PAST 24 HOURS, AND
PRODUCED ALL OF THE OBSERVED FLARES. ITS LARGEST WAS A C8/0N
WITH TYPE II AT 0209Z. REGION 7306 (S07W30), THE PREMIER FLARE
SITE OF THE PAST FEW DAYS, WAS QUIET WHILE DECAYING. SURGING WAS
SEEN TO ACCOMPANY THE WEST LIMB PASSAGE OF REGION 7298 (N19W92).
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. REGIONS 7303 AND 7306 PROVIDE THE BEST CHANCE
FOR AN ISOLATED M-CLASS FLARE.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 10/2100Z TO 11/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS VARIED FROM QUIET TO ACTIVE
LEVELS DURING THE INTERVAL.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE. RECURRENT PATTERNS SUGGEST
A DISTURBANCE DUE THE LATTER PART OF THE FORECAST PERIOD.
HOWEVER, AT THIS TIME THERE IS NO IDENTIFIABLE PRECURSOR TO IT.
