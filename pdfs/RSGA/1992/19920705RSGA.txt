JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 187 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 05 JUL 1992
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 04/2100Z
TO 05/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. NEW REGION 7220
(S12E73) PRODUCED AN M1/1N FLARE TODAY AT 2000Z. THIS NEW GROUP
DOMINATED FLARE PRODUCTION TODAY WITH NUMEROUS C-CLASS FLARES,
AND EXHIBITED BRIGHT PLAGE THROUGHOUT THE PERIOD. REGION 7216
(N14W08) CONTINUES AS THE LARGEST GROUP ON THE DISK AND SHOWS
SOME MAGNETIC COMPLEXITY WITH A DELTA CONFIGURATION. NONETHELESS
THE GROUP COULD ONLY MUSTER TWO SUBFLARES DURING THE PERIOD.
AN ERUPTIVE PROMINENCE WAS OBSERVED ON WEST LIMB TODAY AT S14:
THE EVENT WAS ACCOMPANIED BY A C8 X-RAY EVENT.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE. REGIONS 7216 AND 7128 BOTH SHOW POTENTIAL FOR ISOLATED
M-CLASS FLARES OVER THE NEXT THREE DAYS.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 04/2100Z TO 05/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS MOSTLY QUIET TO UNSETTLED WITH OC-
CASIONAL PERIODS OF ACTIVE LEVELS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE MOSTLY UNSETTLED TOMORROW AND PARTWAY THROUGH
THE SECOND DAY. AN INCREASE TO UNSETTLED WITH ACTIVE PERIODS
IS ANTICIPATED ON THE LATTER PART OF THE SECOND DAY AND THROUGH
THE THIRD DAY AS A RESPONSE TO A CORONAL  HOLE.
