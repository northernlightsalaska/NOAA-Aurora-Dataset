JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 032 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 01 FEB 1992
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 31/2100Z
TO 01/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. REGION 7042 (S12E52)
PRODUCED THE LARGEST EVENT OF THE INTERVAL, A C7/0F AT 2052Z.
IT ALSO HAD OTHER SMALLER EVENTS AS IT WAS SEEN TO GROW
SUBSTANTIALLY IN WHITE LIGHT. REGION 7039 (S15E24) ALSO HAD
A NUMBER OF C-CLASS FLARES TODAY. REGION 7031 (S05W36) IS
STILL THE LARGEST SPOT GROUP, BUT IT WAS QUIET BUT FOR ONE
SUBFLARE IN THE PAST 24 HOURS. NEW REGION 7045 (N04W75) WAS
BORN ON THE DISK.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE. REGIONS 7031, 7039, AND 7042 ARE ALL CAPABLE OF
M-LEVEL FLARES.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 31/2100Z TO 01/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS QUIET TO UNSETTLED UNTIL 1500Z.
PERIODS OF ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM CONDITIONS HAVE BEEN
OBSERVED SINCE. THIS RECENT INCREASE MAY BE RELATED TO FLARE
ACTIVITY OF 30 JANUARY.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE THE NEXT 24 HOURS. STRICTLY
UNSETTLED CONDITIONS SHOULD PREVAIL THE LATTER PART OF THE
FORECAST PERIOD.
