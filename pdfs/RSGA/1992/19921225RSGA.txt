JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 360 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 25 DEC 1992
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 24/2100Z
TO 25/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. JUST ONE C-CLASS EVENT
OCCURRED, THAT A C2/1B FLARE AT 0147Z FROM STILL DECAYING REGION
7374 (S09W18). BACKGROUND X-RAY FLUXES ARE NOW AT LEVELS
REMINISCENT OF SOLAR MINIMUM. CERTAINLY THE MOST INTERESTING
OCCURRENCE OF THE DAY WAS THE DISAPPEARANCE OF AN APPROX. 40
DEGREE-LONG FILAMENT/PROMINENCE FROM THE SOUTHEAST LIMB AT
THE START OF THE DAY.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 24/2100Z TO 25/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN QUIET AT ALL LATITUDES. THE
EXPECTED PASSAGE OF A CME RELATED TO THE FILAMENT
DISAPPEARANCE OF 22 DECEMBER HAS YET TO OCCUR.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM LEVELS THE NEXT TWO
DAYS DUE TO EFFECTS FROM TWO RECENT FILAMENT DISAPPEARANCES,
ON 22 AND 23 DECEMBER. THE EAST LIMB EVENT OF EARLIER TODAY IS
NOT LIKELY TO HAVE ANY EFFECT.
