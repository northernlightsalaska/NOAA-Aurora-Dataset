JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 292 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 18 OCT 1992
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 17/2100Z
TO 18/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. THE LARGEST FLARE OF
THE DAY WAS A C2/1F AT 18/1641Z IN REGION 7311 (S17W08) AND
WAS ACCOMPANIED BY A PARTIAL ERUPTION OF THE INVERSION
LINE FILAMENT. THIS REGION HAS GROWN ONLY SLIGHTLY SINCE
YESTERDAY. SEVERAL SMALL FLARES WERE ALSO OBSERVED IN REGIONS
7315 (N07E32) AND 7316 (S11E66). NEW REGION 7317 (N09W77)
WAS NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW. C-CLASS FLARES ARE POSSIBLE IN REGIONS 7311, 7315, AND
7316.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 17/2100Z TO 18/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS QUIET TO UNSETTLED. ISOLATED ACTIVE
CONDITIONS OCCURRED AT SOME HIGH LATITUDE STATIONS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE MOSTLY QUIET AT MIDDLE LATITUDES AND QUIET TO
UNSETTLED AT HIGH LATITUDES. ISOLATED ACTIVE CONDITIONS ARE
POSSIBLE AT HIGH LATITUDES AS WELL.
