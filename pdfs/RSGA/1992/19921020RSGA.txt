IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 19/2100Z
TO 20/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. REGION 7315 (N05E07)
REMAINED THE MOST ACTIVE SUNSPOT GROUP CURRENTLY ON THE DISK
BY PRODUCING SEVERAL SMALL C-CLASS FLARES. THE SUNSPOT AREA HAS
INCREASED SINCE YESTERDAY BUT THE MAGNETIC CONFIGURATION HAS
BECOME LESS COMPLEX. REGION 7316 (S12E43) HAS DEVELOPED SOMEWHAT
DURING THE PAST DAY, INCREASING SUNSPOT AREA AND BECOMING MORE
COMPLEX. IT PRODUCED ONLY ONE FLARE BUT IT WAS THE MOST
INTERESTING OF THE DAY, A C6/1B AT 20/0916Z ASSOCIATED WITH A
TYPE II SWEEP AND A DISCRETE BURST ACROSS THE SPECTRUM.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. MAJOR FLARES IN 7315 APPEAR LESS LIKELY.

CONDITIONS OCCURRED AT SOME STATIONS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE QUIET TO UNSETTLED.
