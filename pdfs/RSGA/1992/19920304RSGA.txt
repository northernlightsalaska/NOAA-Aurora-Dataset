JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 064 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 04 MAR 1992
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 03/2100Z
TO 04/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. ONLY A FEW LOW-LEVEL
C-CLASS EVENTS WERE OBSERVED. REGION 7088 (S18W40) PRODUCED
NEARLY ALL OF TODAY'S LIMITED FLARE ACTIVITY, INCLUDING TODAY'S
BIGGEST EVENT, A LONG DURATION C2 AT 1652Z. THE GROUP SHOWED
STEADY GROWTH THROUGHOUT THE DAY. SURGING WAS REPORTED
ON WEST LIMB FROM REGION 7070 (N08W88), BUT THE GROUP WAS
QUIET AND CONTINUED TO DECAY DURING THE PERIOD.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW. REGION 7088 IS THE MOST LIKELY SOURCE FOR FLARES. THE
RETURN OF OLD REGION 7056 (S13, L-181) ON THE SECOND DAY IS
EXPECTED TO INCREASE ACTIVITY AND BACKGROUND LEVELS SOMEWHAT.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 03/2100Z TO 04/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS MOSTLY QUIET TO UNSETTLED. SOME
BRIEF ACTIVE PERIODS WERE REPORTED AT HIGH LATITUDES NEAR
THE END OF THE PERIOD.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE GENERALLY UNSETTLED FOR THE NEXT THREE DAYS.
