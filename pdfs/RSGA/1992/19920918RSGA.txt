IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 17/2100Z
TO 18/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. REGION 7276 (N16W66) PRO-

EXCEEDED THE C1 LEVEL. THE REGION SHOWS MARKED DECAY AND SIM-
PLIFICATION BUT CONTINUES TO EXHIBIT FAIRLY BRIGHT PLAGE. A
SMALL NEW EMERGING REGION WAS ASSIGNED TODAY AS 7286 (N13E01).

IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MOSTLY LOW. THERE IS A POSSIBILITY FOR AN ISOLATED M-CLASS
EVENT FROM REGION 7276 OVER THE NEXT TWO DAYS BEFORE IT
TRANSITS ACROSS WEST LIMB.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 17/2100Z TO 18/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD RANGED FROM QUIET TO MINOR STORM LEVELS
DURING THE PERIOD. ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM LEVELS PERSISTED
EARLY IN THE PERIOD, EXTENDING YESTERDAY'S STORM THROUGH AN
END TIME OF 1200UT ON 18 SEPTEMBER. CONDITIONS WERE PRE-
DOMINANTLY QUIET TO UNSETTLED FOR THE REMAINDER OF THE
PERIOD.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE FOR THE NEXT 24 HOURS.
THE ACTIVE PERIODS ARE EXPECTED AS A RESULT OF LOCAL NIGHTTIME
SUBSTORM EFFECTS. CONDITIONS ARE EXPECTED TO SUBSIDE TO MOSTLY
UNSETTLED LEVELS FOR THE REMAINDER OF THE FORECAST PERIOD.
