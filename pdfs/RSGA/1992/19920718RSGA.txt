IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 17/2100Z
TO 18/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE.  THE LARGEST FLARE
WAS AN M2 AT 1344Z ASSOCIATED WITH A BRIGHT SURGE ON THE LIMB IN
THE VICINITY OF REGION 7220 (S13W94).  REGION 7227 (N12W23)
PRODUCED AN M1/SF FLARE AT 0250Z. REGION 7222 (S12W78) PRODUCED
SEVERAL SF FLARES.

LOW TO MODERATE.  THE 7220/7222 COMPLEX STILL HAS POTENTIAL FOR
PRODUCING AN ISOLATED M-FLARE AS IT ROTATES OFF THE DISK.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 17/2100Z TO 18/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT MOSTLY QUIET LEVELS FOR
THE PAST 24 HOURS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED EARLY IN THE PERIOD.  ACTIVE CONDITIONS
ARE EXPECTED ON THE THIRD DAY DUE TO A LARGE CORONAL HOLE
MOVING INTO FAVORABLE POSITION.  MINOR STORM LEVELS ARE POSSIBLE
AT HIGH LATITUDES AT THAT TIME.
