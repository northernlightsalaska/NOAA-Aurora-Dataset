JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 235 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 22 AUG 1992
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 21/2100Z
TO 22/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. REGION 7260 (N16W63)
CONTINUED TO DECAY AND SIMPLIFY AS IT PRODUCED JUST C-CLASS
FLARES IN THE PAST 24 HOURS. ITS LARGEST EVENT WAS A C6/0F AT
1453Z. THE REMAINDER OF THE DISK WAS QUIET AND DULL.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. REGION 7260 WOULD BE THE SITE OF ANY
SIGNIFICANT FLARES, SHOULD THEY OCCUR.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 21/2100Z TO 22/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS QUIET TO UNSETTLED AT MID LATITUDES.
HIGH LATITUDES EXPERIENCED SLIGHTLY MORE DISTURBED CONDITIONS
AT TIMES.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE PREDOMINANTLY UNSETTLED THE DURATION OF THE
PERIOD.
