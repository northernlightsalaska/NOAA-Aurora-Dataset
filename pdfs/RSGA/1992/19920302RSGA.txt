JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 062 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 02 MAR 1992
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 01/2100Z
TO 02/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. ONLY ONE C-CLASS FLARE
WAS OBSERVED: A C2 AT 0251Z.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MOSTLY LOW. THERE IS A CHANCE FOR AN ISOLATED M-CLASS EVENT
OVER THE NEXT THREE DAYS. REGIONS 7070 (N07W65) AND 7083
(S09W74) ARE THE MOST LIKELY SOURCES FOR FLARE ACTIVITY.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 01/2100Z TO 02/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS MOSTLY QUIET TO UNSETTLED. SOME
BRIEF ACTIVE PERIODS OCCURRED AT HIGH LATITUDES BETWEEN
0900 AND 1500Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BECOME ACTIVE SOMETIME TOMORROW AND THE DISTURBANCE
SHOULD LAST PART WAY THROUGH THE SECOND DAY. THIS ACTIVITY IS
DUE TO A FAVORABLY POSITIONED CORONAL HOLE. CONDITIONS SHOULD
BECOME QUIET TO UNSETTLED AGAIN BY LATE ON DAY TWO AND ARE
EXPECTED TO CONTINUE AT THESE LEVELS THROUGHOUT THE REMAINDER
OF THE FORECAST PERIOD.
