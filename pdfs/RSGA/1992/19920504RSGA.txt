JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 125 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 04 MAY 1992
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 03/2100Z
TO 04/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 7150 (S06W61)
PRODUCED AN M1/SF FLARE AT 04/1136Z. MINOR GROWTH WAS NOTED IN
THE CENTRAL AND TRAILER PORTIONS OF THIS REGION. REGION 7154
(S26E52) ALSO SHOWED MINOR GROWTH. IT PRODUCED A LONG DURATION
C9/1F FLARE AT 04/2039Z WHICH REMAINED IN PROGRESS AT THE END OF
THE PERIOD. THE FLARE WAS ACCOMPANIED BY MINOR DISCRETE RADIO
EMISSIONS. NEWLY NUMBERED PLAGE REGION 7157 (S15E61) PRODUCED A
C3/1F FLARE AT 04/1002Z. NEW REGIONS 7156 (S05W38) AND 7158
(S19E05) WERE ALSO NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. REGIONS 7150 AND 7154 ARE EXPECTED TO PRODUCE
C-CLASS FLARES. BOTH REGIONS APPEAR CAPABLE OF ISOLATED M-CLASS
FLARING AS WELL.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 03/2100Z TO 04/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO UNSETTLED LEVELS
FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS. ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM LEVELS WERE
OBSERVED AT SOME HIGH LATITUDE STATIONS FROM 04/0600-1800Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE MOSTLY UNSETTLED. ACTIVE LEVELS ARE POSSIBLE ON
THE SECOND DAY DUE TO FLARE EFFECTS.
