JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 289 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 15 OCT 1992
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 14/2100Z
TO 15/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS VERY LOW. ONLY ONE OPTICAL
SUBFLARE WAS OBSERVED OVER THE LAST 24 HOURS. NEW REGION 7313
(S04E64) WAS ASSIGNED TODAY. TWO FILAMENT DISAPPEARENCES WERE
NOTED: 5 DEGREES NEAR S01E25 BETWEEN 14/2310Z AND 15/1328Z,
AND 17 DEGREES NEAR N33W50 BETWEEN 15/1145Z AND 15/1328Z.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 14/2100Z TO 15/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD ATTAINED ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM LEVEL
CONDITIONS EARLY IN THE PERIOD AND CONTINUING THROUGH ABOUT
1500Z. IN ADDITION SOME ISOLATED MAJOR STORM LEVELS WERE
OBSERVED AT HIGH LATITUDE SITES. SINCE THEN CONDITIONS HAVE
SUBSIDED TO MOSTLY QUIET TO UNSETTLED LEVELS. THE DISTURB-
ANCE WAS PROBABLY CAUSED BY A FAVORABLY POSITIONED CORONAL
HOLE.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE AS A RESULT OF SUBSTORM
ACTIVITY DURING THE NEXT 12-24 HOURS. CONDITIONS SHOULD DECLINE
TO GENERALLY UNSETTLED LEVELS THEREAFTER.
