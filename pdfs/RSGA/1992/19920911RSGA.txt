IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 10/2100Z
TO 11/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 7276 (N15E26)

ASSOCIATED TYPE II SWEEP AT 10/2257UT. THIS REGION MAINTAINED
ITS BETA-GAMMA CONFIGURATION AND IS THE BRIGHTEST REGION ON THE
DISK, BUT HAS SHOWN VERY LITTLE AREA GROWTH OR DEVELOPMENT.

ON THE SOUTH WEST LIMB.

LOW TO MODERATE. M CLASS FLARES ARE POSSIBLE FROM REGION 7276.
THIS REGION ALSO HAS A SMALL CHANCE OF PRODUCING AN ISOLATED
X CLASS FLARE.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 10/2100Z TO 11/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO MAJOR STORM LEVELS
FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS. MAJOR STORM LEVELS WERE REACHED AT
11/0900UT. THE FOREBUSH DECREASE THAT BEGAN TWO DAYS AGO
FINALLY ENDED AT APPROXIMATELY 11/1000UT. THE MAJOR GEOMAGNETIC
STORM ENDED AT 11/1500UT.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE FOR THE NEXT 24 HOURS. A
FAVORABLY POSITIONED CORONAL HOLE IS EXPECTED TO INCREASE
GEOMAGNETIC ACTIVITY EARLY ON 13 SEPTEMBER. THE FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO REMAIN ACTIVE FOR THE REMAINDER OF THE PERIOD.
