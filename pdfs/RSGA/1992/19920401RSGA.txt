JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 092 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 01 APR 1992
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 31/2100Z
TO 01/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH. REGION 7116 (S08W58)
PRODUCED AN M5/SF FLARE AT 01/0100Z. THE OPTICAL CLASS OF THIS
EVENT WAS BASED ON A SUNSET OBSERVATION AND IS MOST CERTAINLY
AN UNDERESTIMATE. THIS MAJOR FLARE WAS ASSOCIATED WITH A 240 SFU
RADIO BURST AT 2695 MHZ. REGION 7116 ALSO PRODUCED AN M3/1B
FLARE AT 01/1748Z AS WELL AS SEVERAL C-CLASS EVENTS. THIS REGION
APPEARS TO RETAIN SOME COMPLEXITY IN SPITE OF THE FREQUENT
FLARES. REGION 7117 (N07W50) IS STILL THE LARGEST SUNSPOT GROUP
BUT HAS REMAINED FAIRLY INACTIVE. IT CONTINUES TO DECAY SLOWLY.
NEW REGION 7123 (S07E75) WAS NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE. ADDITIONAL M-CLASS FLARES ARE EXPECTED IN REGION
7116. REGION 7117 MAY PRODUCE M-CLASS ACTIVITY BEFORE IT REACHES
THE WEST LIMB.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 31/2100Z TO 01/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE UNTIL ABOUT
01/1500Z, AND AFTERWARDS IT WAS MOSTLY QUIET.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE QUIET TO UNSETTLED AT MIDDLE LATITUDES AND
ACTIVE TO STORM AT HIGH LATITUDES.
