JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 129 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 08 MAY 1992
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 07/2100Z
TO 08/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH. REGION 7154 (S26E08)
PRODUCED AN IMPRESSIVE M7/4B FLARE WITH PARALLEL RIBBONS AND
NUMEROUS ERUPTIVE CENTERS.  FLARE AREA WAS REPORTED TO BE 1299
MILLIONTHS.  ASSOCIATED WITH THE FLARE WERE A TYPE IV RADIO
SWEEP AND A TENFLARE (2695 MHZ BURST) OF 3100 SFU. A FILAMENT
AT ONE END OF THE REGION BECAME ACTIVE DURING THE FLARE AND
DISAPPEARED.  TWO NEW REGIONS WERE NUMBERED TODAY: 7162 (N07W11)
AND 7163 (S18E67).
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE.  REGION 7154 IS THE MOST LIKELY SOURCE OF
ADDITIONAL M-CLASS FLARES.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 07/2100Z TO 08/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT UNSETTLED TO MINOR STORM
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM LEVELS THE NEXT THREE
DAYS, WITH PREDOMINANTLY MINOR STORM CONDITIONS ON DAY TWO.
ACTIVITY SHOULD INCREASE FAIRLY EARLY ON THE 9TH DUE TO THE
EPL/HYDER FLARE/TYPE II AND IV ON THE 7TH.  THE M7 FLARE
TODAY IS EXPECTED TO PRODUCE MINOR STORM CONDITIONS IN THE
LATTER HALF OF THE 10TH AND INTO THE 11TH. A PROTON EVENT
IS EXPECTED TO START BY 09/00Z FOR >10 MEV PROTONS.  EVENT
CRITERIA IS NOT EXPECTED TO BE REACHED FOR HIGHER ENERGY
PROTONS.
