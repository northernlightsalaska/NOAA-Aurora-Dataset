JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 321 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 16 NOV 1992
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 15/2100Z
TO 16/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. NEW REGION 7345 (S23E72)
PRODUCED SEVERAL SUBFLARES TWO OF WHICH REACHED C-CLASS. THIS
REGION IS THE RETURN OF OLD 7321 WHICH WAS RESPONSIBLE FOR HIGH
LEVELS OF ACTIVITY LAST ROTATION. A LOOP PROMINENCE SYSTEM
OCCURRED IN THE REGION MIDWAY THROUGH THE PERIOD. AN ACTIVE
SURGE REGION WAS OBSERVED ON THE EAST LIMB NEAR S03 DURING THE
SECOND HALF OF THE PERIOD. THE SURGING EXTENDED TO 0.02 SOLAR
RADII. NO SIGNIFICANT CHANGES WERE OBSERVED IN THE REMAINING
REGIONS.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. REGION 7345 IS EXPECTED TO PRODUCE C-CLASS
FLARES AND MAY PRODUCE ISOLATED M-CLASS ACTIVITY.
