JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 081 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 21 MAR 1992
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 20/2100Z
TO 21/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. JUST A FEW C-CLASS FLARES
OCCURRED IN THE PAST 24 HOURS, AND ONLY MINOR CHANGES WERE SEEN
IN THE EIGHT SPOTTED REGIONS ON THE VISIBLE DISK. A 16 DEGREE-
LONG FILAMENT DISAPPEARED FROM THE SOUTHEAST QUADRANT DURING
THE EARLY PART OF THE INTERVAL.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 20/2100Z TO 21/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS QUIET UNTIL MIDWAY THROUGH THE
INTERVAL. LOCALIZED SUBSTORMS BROUGHT ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM
LEVELS TO NIGHTTIME SECTORS FOLLOWED BY GENERALLY UNSETTLED
CONDITIONS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE WITH PERIODS OF ELEVATED
ACTIVITY FROM SUBSTORMS THE NEXT 48 HOURS. A CORONAL HOLE WILL
ROTATE TO A GEO-EFFECTIVE POSITION ON 24 MARCH, AND ACTIVE
TO MINOR STORM LEVELS SHOULD PREVAIL THEN.
