JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 153 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 01 JUN 1992
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 31/2100Z
TO 01/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. REGION 7183 (S26E46)
PRODUCED A C1/1F FLARE 31 MAY/2359Z. NO OTHER C-CLASS OR LARGER
X-RAY EVENTS, OR OPTICAL FLARES HAVE BEEN OBSERVED. NEW REGION
7186 (N08E79) ROTATED OVER THE EAST LIMB WITH SOME ASSOCIATED
SURGING; IT IS PRESENTLY SEEN AS AN H-TYPE SPOT. REGION 7185
(N12E01) APPEARS TO HAVE STABLIZED AS A D-TYPE GROUP WITHOUT
MAGNETIC COMPLEXITY. A SIGNIFICANT FILAMENT (APPROXIMATELY
20 DEGREES LONG) DISAPPEARED FROM NEAR CENTER DISK TODAY. THE
CENTER OF THE FILAMENT ON 31 MAY WAS NEAR S15E05. ACTIVE
PROMINENCES WERE REPORTED NEAR N16W90 AND S20E90.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW.  ONLY OCCASIONAL ERUPTIVE FLARES ARE EXPECTED FROM REGIONS
PRESENTLY ON THE SOLAR DISK.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 31/2100Z TO 01/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO UNSETTLED
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS. SOME ACTIVE PERIODS WERE NOTED
AT AURORAL LATITUDES.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BECOME ACTIVE TOMORROW, REMAINING UNSETTLED TO
ACTIVE ON 03 JUNE IN RESPONSE TO EFFECTS ASSOCIATED WITH
FILAMENT DISAPPEARANCES. QUIET CONDITIONS ARE EXPECTED BY THE
END OF THE FORECAST PERIOD, BUT THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD MAY
BECOME DISTURBED THEREAFTER, AGAIN IN RESPONSE TO FILAMENT
DISAPPEARANCES.
