JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 052 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 21 FEB 1992
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 20/2100Z
TO 21/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. AN M3 X-RAY EVENT WAS
OBSERVED AT 0430Z. A SIGNIFICANT TYPE II SWEEP ACCOMPANIED THIS
ACTIVITY ALONG WITH THE REPORT OF A LOOP PROMINENCE SYSTEM IN
REGION 7070 (N08E68). NO REPORTS OF FLARING WERE OBTAINED, BUT
REGION 7070 APPEARS TO HAVE BEEN THE SOURCE OF THE EVENT. THIS
AREA ALSO PRODUCED AN M2/1F FLARE AT 1020Z. REGION 7067 (N05E41)
DISPLAYED ONLY ISOLATED SUBFLARING WHILE STILL SHOWING SOME
GROWTH. NEW REGIONS 7071 (S06W16), 7072 (S09E69), AND 7073
(S15W37) WERE ASSIGNED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE. REGIONS 7067 AND 7070 POSE THE GREATEST THREAT FOR
ISOLATED M-CLASS FLARING.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 20/2100Z TO 21/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT ACTIVE TO SEVERE STORM
LEVELS. THE ACTIVITY BEGAN AT 20/0110Z AND INCREASED TO MAJOR
STORM LEVELS AT 21/0523Z. THE PERIODS OF SEVERE STORMING
WERE ISOLATED BETWEEN 0600-1200Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM LEVELS FOR THE NEXT 6
TO 12 HOURS. GENERALLY UNSETTLED CONDITIONS ARE EXPECTED
THEREAFTER.
