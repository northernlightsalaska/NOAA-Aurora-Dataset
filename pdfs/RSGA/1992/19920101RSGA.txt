JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 001 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 01 JAN 1992
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 31/2100Z
TO 01/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH. REGION 6993 (S09E55)
PRODUCED AN M5/1F FLARE WITH ASSOCIATED TYPE II AND IV SWEEPS
AT 01/0349Z. THIS REGION ALSO GENERATED AN M3/1N FLARE AT
01/1129Z. THIS AREA IS STACKED ABOVE REGION 6994 (S15E62) WHICH
IS LARGER AND MORE COMPLEX, BUT HAS PRODUCED ONLY ISOLATED
SUBFLARING. REGION 6988 (S15W36) SHOWED SOME GROWTH TODAY WHILE
PRODUCING SEVERAL M-CLASS FLARES. AN M2/2B FLARE FROM THIS AREA
AT 01/2024Z WAS FOLLOWED BY AN M5/1N FLARE ERUPTING IN REGION
6985 (S17W55) AT 01/2036Z. IT IS APPARENT THAT REGION 6988
CONTRIBUTED SOMEWHAT TO 6985'S M5 X-RAY EVENT. A MAJOR TYPE II
SWEEP AND 610 FLUX UNIT TENFLARE BURST ACCOMPANIED THIS LATEST
M5 FLARE. NO NEW REGIONS WERE ASSIGNED TODAY.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE TO HIGH. CONTINUED M-CLASS FLARING IS POSSIBLE FROM
REGIONS 6985, 6988, AND THE 6993/6994 COMPLEX.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 31/2100Z TO 01/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN MOSTLY QUIET TO UNSETTLED.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE AT THE START OF THE PERIOD
IN RESPONSE TO FLARING NOTED ON THE 30TH. A WELL POSITIONED
CORONAL HOLE MAY AFFECT THE FIELD ON THE 4TH.
