JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 265 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 21 SEP 1992
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 20/2100Z
TO 21/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS VERY LOW. REGION 7288 (S18E04)
SHOWED RAPID DEVELOPMENT DURING THE PERIOD AS IT HAS GROWN FROM
A SPOTLESS PLAGE REGION TO A 100 MILLIONTHS DSO SPOT GROUP.
NEVERTHELESS THE REGION COULD ONLY MUSTER A COUPLE SUBFLARES
DURING THE PERIOD. THE REMAINDER OF THE SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS
WERE QUIET AND STABLE.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW. A SMALL INCREASE IS ANTICIPATED ON DAY THREE WITH THE
RETURN OF OLD REGION 7270 (S10, L-038) WHICH PRODUCED 20
M-CLASS FLARES AND 2 X-CLASS FLARES ON ITS PREVIOUS DISK
TRANSIT.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 20/2100Z TO 21/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS GENERALLY QUIET.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE QUIET TO UNSETTLED.
