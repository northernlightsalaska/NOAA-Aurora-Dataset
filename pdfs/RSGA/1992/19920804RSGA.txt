IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 03/2100Z
TO 04/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. REGION 7248 (S12E41) WAS
THE SITE OF TWO C-CLASS FLARES. THE FLARES WERE A C3/1F AT
04/0738Z AND A C1/SN AT 04/1933Z. REGION 7242 (N17W34) WAS
RESPONSIBLE FOR A C4/SN FLARE AT 04/1511Z. NUMEROUS SUBFLARES
WERE SEEN IN REGIONS 7242, 7248, AND 7241 (N14W50). REGION
7248 IS THE STRONGEST REGION TODAY AND HAS DEVELOPED INTO A
BETA-GAMMA-DELTA GROUP.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE. ISOLATED M-CLASS FLARES ARE POSSIBLE FROM REGIONS
7241 AND 7248.

LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS. AN ISOLATED PERIOD OF ACTIVE
LEVELS WAS SEEN AT 04/2100Z. A NINE GAMMA SUDDEN IMPULSE
AT 04/1408Z KICKED OFF UNSETTLED LEVELS AND MAY BE THE RESULT
OF A DSF SEEN ON THE FIRST OF AUGUST.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE QUIET TO UNSETTLED.
