JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 128 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 07 MAY 1992
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 06/2100Z
TO 07/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. A C3/2F HYDER FLARE
AT 0649Z FROM S23E48 WAS THE LARGEST EVENT OF THE PERIOD. THIS
FLARE WAS THE PRECURSOR TO A LARGE EPL AT S21E90 WITH MATERIAL
VISIBLE TO .58 SOLAR RADII. THE FILAMENT WHICH LIFTED OFF WAS
THE SAME ONE ELUDED TO IN YESTERDAY'S FORECAST. STRONG TYPE II
AND IV RADIO SWEEPS WERE ASSOCIATED WITH THIS EVENT AND SHOCK
SPEEDS WERE ESTIMATED AS HIGH AS 2000 KM/SEC. ELSEWHERE,
REGION 7154 (S25, E14) CONTINUES TO BE THE LARGEST AND MOST
COMPLEX GROUP ON THE DISK BUT MAY HAVE STARTED TO DECAY AND
ELONGATE. ONE NEW REGION WAS NUMBERED, REGION 7161 AT (S08,
E21).
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MOSTLY LOW. AN ISOLATED M-CLASS FLARE IS STILL POSSIBLE FROM
REGION 7154.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 06/2100Z TO 07/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO UNSETTLED
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE MOSTLY QUIET TO UNSETTLED. THE POSSIBILITY OF A
SHOCK FROM TODAY'S EPL WITH TYPE II AND IV SWEEPS APPEARS
SLIGHT AT THIS TIME. HOWEVER, IF A SHOCK WAS TO OCCUR, THE
BEST ESTIMATES OF TIME OF ARRIVAL ARE LATE ON DAY TWO TO EARLY
ON DAY THREE.
