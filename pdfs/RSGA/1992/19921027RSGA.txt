JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 301 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 27 OCT 1992
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 26/2100Z
TO 27/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH. REGION 7321 (S24W28) WAS
TODAY'S BIG STORY. IT HAS DEVELOPED INTO A DKC, MAGNETICALLY
COMPLEX SPOT GROUP WITH ALMOST 1300 MILLIONTHS COVERAGE. THIS
REGION LEAD TODAY'S ACTIVITY WITH FOUR M-CLASS FLARES, THE
LARGEST OF WHICH WAS AN M1/1B AT 27/0741UT. IT ALSO PRODUCED
A VARIETY OF C-CLASS AND SUB FAINT FLARES. REGION 7315 (N05W84)
JOINED THE FRAY WITH THE DAY'S LARGEST FLARE, AN M3/2N FLARE
AT 27/0700UT. ONE M-CLASS FLARE, AN M1, WAS UNCORRELATED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE. REGION 7321 HAS THE BEST CHANCE OF PRODUCING M-CLASS
FLARES AND HAS A CHANCE FOR PRODUCING AN ISOLATED X-CLASS FLARE.
REGION 7315 STILL HAS A CHANCE OF AN ISOLATED M-CLASS FLARE
BEFORE IT ROTATES OFF THE DISK.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 26/2100Z TO 27/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM LEVELS FOR THE NEXT
24 HOURS, THEN ACTIVE LEVELS FOR THE REMAINDER OF THE FORECAST
