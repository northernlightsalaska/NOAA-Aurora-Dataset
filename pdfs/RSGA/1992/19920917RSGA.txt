JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 261 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 17 SEP 1992
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 16/2100Z
TO 17/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 7276 (N15W52)
CONTINUED ITS GROWTH AND ACTIVITY TODAY, PRODUCING NUMEROUS
C-CLASS FLARES AND TWO M-CLASS FLARES. THE FIRST WAS AN M1/1N
AT 17/1121Z AND ANOTHER M1 AT 17/2051Z, ALTHOUGH THERE WERE NO
OPTICAL REPORTS AT THE TIME OF THIS WRITING, THE FLARE ALMOST
CERTAINLY CAME FROM REGION 7276. ONE NEW REGION, 7285 (S08E45),
WAS NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE. REGION 7276 IS NOW EXPECTED TO CONTINUE ITS SPORATIC
PRODUCTION M-CLASS FLARES UNTIL IT PASSES OVER WEST LIMB LATE ON
20 SEPTEMBER.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 16/2100Z TO 17/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT MINOR TO MAJOR STORM
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS. A MAJOR GEOMAGNETIC STORM
BEGAN AT 17/1200Z. THE SOURCE OF THIS DISTURBANCE IS NOT KNOWN.
A GRADUAL ONSET BEGAN AT 0300Z AND PERIODS OF MAJOR TO SEVERE
STORM LEVELS WERE OBSERVED AT ALL LATITUDES BY 17/0900Z. A SMALL
SUDDEN IMPULSE OF 13 NANOTESLA WAS MEASURED AT BOULDER AT
17/1750Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT UNSETTLED TO MINOR STORM LEVELS. THE CURRENT
DISTURBANCE IS EXPECTED TO END DURING THE NEXT 6 TO 12 HRS,
BECOMING MOSTLY UNSETTLED BY LATE ON DAY ONE.
