JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 061 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 01 MAR 1992
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 29/2100Z
TO 01/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. ONLY A FEW C-CLASS FLARES
OCCURRED DURING THE DAY. NEWLY NUMBERED REGION 7083 (S10W62)
PRODUCED THE LARGEST OF THESE: A C5/SN AT 1632Z. THIS GROUP
SHOWED GROWTH AND DEVELOPMENT DURING THE DAY. REGION 7070
(N07W54) CONTINUED AS THE LARGEST, MOST COMPLEX GROUP ON THE
DISK BUT DIDN'T PRODUCE ANY FLARES AND SHOWED SIGNS OF SLOW
DECAY.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. THE MOST LIKELY SOURCES FOR M-CLASS ACTIVITY
ARE 7067 (N03W81), 7070, AND 7083
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 29/2100Z TO 01/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD RANGED FROM QUIET TO MAJOR STORM
LEVELS. YESTERDAY'S MINOR STORM CONTINUED THROUGH 1200Z,
WITH A BRIEF QUIET LULL FROM 0000-0300Z. THE PEAK OF THE
STORM FOR LAST 24 HOURS ACTIVITY OCCURRED FROM 0600-1200Z,
WITH MINOR STORM LEVELS AT MID-LATITUDES AND MAJOR STORM
LEVELS AT HIGH LATITUDES. THE ACTIVITY DROPPED DOWN TO
QUIET TO UNSETTLED LEVELS AFTER 1200Z AND REMAINED THERE
THROUGH THE END OF THE PERIOD.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE GENERALLY UNSETTLED TOMORROW. AN INCREASE TO
MOSTLY ACTIVE IS EXPECTED, BEGINNING ON THE SECOND DAY AND
LASTING PARTWAY THROUGH THE THIRD DAY. THE INCREASE IS
EXPECTED AS A RESPONSE TO A FAVORABLY POSITIONED CORONAL
HOLE.
