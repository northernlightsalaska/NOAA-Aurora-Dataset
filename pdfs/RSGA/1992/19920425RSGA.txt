JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 116 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 25 APR 1992
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 24/2100Z
TO 25/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. SEVERAL SMALL OPTICAL
FLARES WERE OBSERVED SINCE YESTERDAY BUT FEW HAD X-RAY OUTPUT.
THE LARGEST X-RAY FLARE WAS AN OPTICALLY UNCORRELATED C1 AT
25/1530Z. REGION 7135 (S17W39) CONTINUES TO SIMPLIFY. REGION
7138 (N09W17) ALSO HAS SHOWN SIMPLIFICATION DURING THE PAST
DAY. REGION 7143 (S05E41) HAS NOT GROWN MUCH IN TOTAL SUNSPOT
AREA, BUT IT HAS DEVELOPED A DELTA CONFIGURATION. BOTH THE PLAGE
AND SUNSPOTS ARE COMPACT IN THE REGION AND ADDITIONAL GROWTH
IS POSSIBLE. NEW REGION 7146 (N08E55) WAS NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. M-CLASS ACTIVITY IS POSSIBLE IN REGIONS 7135,
7138, AND 7143.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 24/2100Z TO 25/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS QUIET TO UNSETTLED.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE QUIET TO UNSETTLED FOR THE NEXT THREE DAYS.
