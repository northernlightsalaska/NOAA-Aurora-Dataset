JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 310 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 05 NOV 1992
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 04/2100Z
TO 05/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. WEATHER INHIBITED
OPTICAL PATROLS DURING THE PERIOD, BUT SPACEBORNE X-RAY
INSTRUMENTS DETECTED AN IMPULSIVE M2 FLARE FROM AN UNKNOWN
SOURCE AT 0622Z. MORE RECENTLY, A C8 FLARE HAD ITS MAXIMUM AT
2041Z. TWO NEW REGIONS HAVE BEEN SEEN AT EAST LIMB, 7334
(N12E69) AND 7335 (S17E75). BOTH ARE BRIGHT IN H-ALPHA AND
SHOW PROMISE FOR FURTHER ACTIVITY. REGION 7330 (S08E18) HAS
GROWN SLIGHTLY AND REGION 7332 (S21W41) SEEMS TO HAVE
STABILIZED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 04/2100Z TO 05/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN PREDOMINANTLY UNSETTLED. THE
GREATER THAN 10 MEV PROTON EVENT CONTINUES. CURRENT FLUXES
ARE HOVERING NEAR THE 10 PFU THRESHOLD.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO PERSIST AT MOSTLY UNSETTLED CONDITIONS THE NEXT
THREE DAYS.
