JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 019 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 19 JAN 1992
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 18/2100Z
TO 19/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. SEVERAL C-CLASS FLARES
OCCURRED SINCE YESTERDAY, BUT MOST WERE NOT CORRELATED TO
OPTICAL FLARES. REGION 7016 (N04E63) IS THE LARGEST SUNSPOT
GROUP ON THE DISK. IT HAS ALSO BEEN THE MOST ACTIVE WITH
SUBFLARES AND FREQUENT PLAGE BRIGHTENINGS. NEW REGION 7019
(N18E14) IS EMERGING RAPIDLY AND HAS ALREADY FORMED PENUMBRA
ON BOTH LEADER AND TRAILER SPOTS. NEW REGION 7020 (S06W42) IS
EMERGING JUST TO THE WEST OF REGION 7008 (S09W32). A 15 DEGREE
SECTION OF THE LARGE FILAMENT RUNNING ALONG THEIR COMMON
INVERSION LINE DISAPPEARED OVERNIGHT.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW FOR THE NEXT 24 HOURS. M-CLASS ACTIVITY WILL BECOME MORE
PROBABLE AS REGIONS 7016 AND 7019 DEVELOP.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 18/2100Z TO 19/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS QUIET TO UNSETTLED.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE QUIET TO UNSETTLED.
