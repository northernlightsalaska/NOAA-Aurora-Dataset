JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 286 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 12 OCT 1992
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 11/2100Z
TO 12/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. ONLY TWO LOW LEVEL C-CLASS
FLARES WERE OBSERVED TODAY. REGION 7303 (S13W84) DISPLAYED SURGE
ACTIVITY AND OCCASIONAL PLAGE FLUCTUATIONS AS IT NEARED WEST
LIMB TRANSIT. REGION 7306 (S08W44) SHOWED CONSIDERABLE DECAY
AND SIMPLIFICATION TODAY, PARTICULARLY IN THE TRAILING SPOTS.
ALL OF THE OTHER SOLAR REGIONS WERE QUIET AND STABLE.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
PREDOMINANTLY LOW. THERE IS A SLIGHT CHANCE FOR AN ISOLATED M-
CLASS EVENT FROM 7306 OR 7303 DURING THE NEXT 24 HOURS. REGION
7303 IS EXPECTED TO CONTINUE TO PROVIDE A SLIGHT CHANCE FOR
AN M-FLARE DURING THE REMAINDER OF THE FORECAST PERIOD.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 11/2100Z TO 12/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD RANGED FROM QUIET TO MAJOR STORM LEVELS
WITH SOME ISOLATED SEVERE STORM LEVELS AT HIGH LATITUDES. THE
PERIOD BEGAN AT MOSTLY QUIET LEVELS, BUT A GRADUAL ONSET OF
ACTIVITY BEGAN AT 0745UT AND DEVELOPED INTO A BRIEF PERIOD OF
MAJOR STORM LEVELS AT MID-LATITUDES AND MAJOR TO SEVERE
LEVELS AT HIGH LATITUDES. THE DISTURBANCE WAS SHORT LIVED,
HOWEVER, AS CONDITIONS MARKEDLY DECLINED AND RETURNED TO
QUIET TO UNSETTLED LEVELS FROM ABOUT 1600UT THROUGH THE END
OF THE PERIOD.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE QUIET TO UNSETTLED FOR THE NEXT TWO DAYS. AN
INCREASE TO GENERALLY ACTIVE LEVELS IS EXPECTED ON THE THIRD
DAY AS A RESPONSE TO A CORONAL HOLE WHICH WILL BE ROTATING
INTO A FAVORABLE POSITION AT THAT TIME.
