JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 143 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 22 MAY 1992
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 21/2100Z
TO 22/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. SMALL FLARES FROM A
VARIETY OF SMALL REGIONS WAS THE GENERAL RULE FOR THE PAST
DAY. THE LARGEST X-RAY FLARE WAS A C1/1N IN REGION 7177
(S23W32). REGION 7172 (N22E02) REMAINS THE LARGEST REGION ON
THE DISK BUT HAS SHOWN SIGNS OF MAGNETIC SIMPLIFICATION. THE
WEAK DELTA CONFIGURATION IN THE LEADER SPOT COMPLEX NO LONGER
APPEARS TO BE PRESENT. REGION 7167 (S07W75) HAS DEVELOPED ANEW
WITH THE ERUPTION OF NEW FLUX AND THE FORMATION OF SMALL
SPOTS. THIS REGION HAS ALSO PRODUCED A FEW SUBFLARES.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW. SMALL FLARES ARE EXPECTED FROM A NUMBER OF REGIONS.
THERE IS A SMALL CHANCE OF M-CLASS ACTIVITY IN REGION 7172.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 21/2100Z TO 22/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS QUIET TO UNSETTLED UNTIL THE 22/03-
06Z PERIOD WHEN IT GRADUALLY BECAME DISTURBED. THE FIELD
WAS AT ACTIVE TO STORM LEVELS THEREAFTER. THE SOURCE OF THIS
DISTURBANCE IS UNCLEAR BUT IT MAY BE THE FILAMENT ERUPTION
NOTED ON 19 MAY.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO RETURN TO QUIET TO UNSETTLED LEVELS AT MIDDLE
LATITUDES BY TOMORROW. THE DISTURBANCE MAY PERSIST AT HIGH
LATITUDES FOR THE NEXT DAY OR SO.
