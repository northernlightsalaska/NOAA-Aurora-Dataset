IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 28/2100Z

FILAMENT (ABOUT 40 DEGREES IN EXTENT) DISAPPEARED FROM THE
SOUTHEAST QUADRANT SOMETIME BETWEEN 28/2126-29/0205Z. NEW
REGIONS 7298 (N16E61) AND 7299 (N12E75) WERE NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW. REGIONS 7293 (S07W16) AND 7296 MAY PRODUCE ISOLATED
C-CLASS FLARES.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 28/2100Z TO 29/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT UNSETTLED TO MAJOR STORM
LEVELS. ISOLATED PERIODS OF SEVERE STORMING OCCURRED AT HIGH
LATITUDES. THE STORMING BEGAN NEAR 29/0300Z AND SUBSIDED TO
MOSTLY UNSETTLED LEVELS BY 29/1800Z. THIS ACTIVITY MAY HAVE
BEEN RELATED TO THE FILAMENT DISAPPEARANCES OF 24 SEPTEMBER.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM LEVELS DUE TO CORONAL
HOLE EFFECTS.
