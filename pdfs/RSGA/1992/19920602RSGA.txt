JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 154 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 02 JUN 1992
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 01/2100Z
TO 02/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS VERY LOW. REGION 7186 (S08E72)
IS THE MOST SIGNIFICANT REGION IN WHITE LIGHT AND H-ALPHA, BUT
IS NOT MAGNETICALLY COMPLEX AND HAS PRODUCED ONLY 1 SUBFLARE
TODAY. A SURGING AREA OF COMPACT PLAGE IS ROTATING ONTO THE DISK
BEHIND IT. NEW REGION 7187 (S08E71), AN A-TYPE SPOT, CAME ONTO
THE DISK NEAR THE VICINITY OF OLD REGION 7160. SEVERAL MINOR
FILAMENT DISAPPEARANCES WERE NOTED. SURGING WAS REPORTED AT
S10W90 NEAR DEPARTED SPOTLESS REGION 7179. THERE WAS AN ACTIVE
PROMINENCE NEAR S17E90.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW. REGION 7186 CARRIES THE HIGHEST PROBABILITY FOR ERUPTIVE
ACTIVITY.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 01/2100Z TO 02/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET LEVELS FOR THE PAST
24 HOURS. AN EXPECTED DISTURBANCE DID NOT OCCUR.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO PERSIST AT QUIET LEVELS UNTIL 5 JUNE, WHEN EFFECTS
ASSOCIATED WITH A MAJOR FILAMENT DISAPPEARANCE BETWEEN 31 MAY
AND 01 JUNE ARE EXPECTED TO ARRIVE AND PRODUCE ACTIVE
CONDITIONS.
