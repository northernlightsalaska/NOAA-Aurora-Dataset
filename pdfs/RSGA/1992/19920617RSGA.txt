JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 169 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 17 JUN 1992
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 16/2100Z
TO 17/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. FREQUENT SMALL FLARES
AND FLARE BRIGHT FLUCTUATIONS HAVE OCCURRED ALONG THE INVERSION
LINE COMMON TO REGION 7194 (S11W31), 7200 (S20W07), AND NEW
REGION 7206 (S13W18). MANY OF THE FLARES ORIGINATED IN THE
NEW EMERGENCE ASSOCIATED WITH 7206. THE MOST SIGNIFICANT
ACTIVITY INCLUDED A LONG DURATION C4 FLARE BEGINNING AT 17/
1546Z AND REACHING A MAXIMUM AT 17/1847Z. SMALL OPTICAL FLARE
ERUPTIONS ALL ALONG THE INVERSION LINE MENTIONED ABOVE WERE
INVOLVED IN THIS X-RAY OUTPUT.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW. C-CLASS FLARES ARE POSSIBLE IN THE REGIONS MENTIONED ABOVE.
MODERATE ACTIVITY WILL BECOME MORE LIKELY IF THE FLUX EMERGENCE
IN 7206 CONTINUES.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 16/2100Z TO 17/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS MOSTLY QUIET.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE QUIET TO UNSETTLED.
