SDF NUMBER 227 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 14 AUG 1992
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 13/2100Z
TO 14/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. REGION 7248 (S12W84)
PRODUCED A C3/SF FLARE AT 0005Z. ANOTHER C-CLASS FLARE OCCURRED
AT 1925Z BUT WAS UNASSOCIATED WITH ANY SOURCE REGION. REGION
7260 (N18E41) REMAINED QUIET DESPITE BEING THE LARGEST REGION ON
THE DISK. REGION 7261 (S22W09) IS GROWING RAPIDLY AND PRODUCED
SEVERAL SUBFLARES. TWO NEW REGIONS WERE ASSIGNED: 7262 (N17W16)
AND 7263 (N17W05).
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. REGIONS 7260 AND 7261 HAVE MODERATE FLARE
POTENTIAL.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 13/2100Z TO 14/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO UNSETTLED
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS. ALASKA STATIONS REPORTED
ACTIVE TO SEVERE STORM LEVELS FROM 0900Z TO 1800Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED. A RECURRING CORONAL HOLE SHOULD
INCREASE MAGNETIC ACTIVITY BY 17 AUG WHEN HIGH LATITUDES SHOULD
HAVE PERIODS OF ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM.
