SDF NUMBER 204 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 22 JUL 1992
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 21/2100Z
TO 22/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. REGION 7229 (N23W68) WAS
THE MOST PRODUCTIVE REGION ON THE SUN TODAY WITH NUMEROUS SUB
FLARES. THE LARGEST OF THESE WAS A C1/SF AT 21/2345Z AND A C1/SF
AT 22/0129Z. A PROMINENCE IN THE WEST SECTION OF 7229 ERUPTED
AT 22/0523Z. THIS REGION CONTINUED TO DECAY AS IT NEARED THE
WEST LIMB. REGION 7234 (S17W65) PRODUCED A 1F FLARE WITH NO
ASSOCIATED X-RAYS AT 21/0203Z AND THREE OTHER SUB FLARES.
REGION 7236 (N18W02), 7232 (N17W90), AND 7230 (S14W46) EACH
PRODUCED A SINGLE SUB FLARE. REGION 7237 (N06W36) WAS ASSIGNED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW. C-CLASS FLARES ARE EXPECTED FROM REGIONS 7229 AND 7236.
BOTH HAVE A VERY SMALL CHANCE OF PRODUCING AN M-CLASS FLARE.

LEVELS OWING TO A FAVORABLY POSTIONED CORONAL HOLE.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE WITH ISOLATED PERIODS OF
MAJOR STORMING.
