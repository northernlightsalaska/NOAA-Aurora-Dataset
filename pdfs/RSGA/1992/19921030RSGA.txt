JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 304 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 30 OCT 1992
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 29/2100Z
TO 30/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH. REGION 7321 (S25W66)
PRODUCED AN X1/2B LONG DURATION TENFLARE AT 30/1816UT. THIS WAS
ACCOMPANIED BY AN IMPRESSIVE SURGE AND DISK LOOPS. THIS REGION
SHOWED CONTINUED GROWTH AND REMAINS IMPRESSIVE AND COMPLEX. THE
REGION ALSO PRODUCED A VARIETY OF M-CLASS AND C-CLASS FLARES
THROUGH THE PERIOD. TODAY'S 10.7 CM FLUX VALUE WAS SEVERELY
ENHANCED BY THE X1/2B FLARE.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE. REGION 7321 RETAINS GOOD M-CLASS POTENTIAL AND STILL
HAS A CHANCE OF PRODUCING AN ISOLATED X-CLASS FLARE BEFORE IT
ROTATES OFF THE WEST LIMB.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 29/2100Z TO 30/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO ACTIVE LEVELS
FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS. HIGH LATITUDE STATIONS SAW A SHORT
PERIOD OF MAJOR STORM LEVELS. A PROTON EVENT BEGAN AT 30/1920UT
IN CONJUNCTION WITH THE X1/2B FLARE. THE PROTON FLUX REACHED
460 PFU AT THE END OF THE PERIOD.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE FOR THE NEXT 24 HOURS.
MAJOR STORM LEVELS ARE EXPECTED AT THAT TIME DUE TO THE EFFECTS
OF THE X1/2B FLARE THROUGH THE END OF THE PERIOD.
