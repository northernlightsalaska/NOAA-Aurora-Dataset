JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 155 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 03 JUN 1992
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 02/2100Z
TO 03/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS VERY LOW. HOWEVER, THERE ARE
NOW 7 SPOTTED REGIONS ON THE VISIBLE DISK INCLUDING NEW REGIONS
7188 (N08W01) AND 7189 (N05E30). SIGNIFICANT GROWTH OCCURRED IN
REGION 7181 (N08W27). SPOTS BEHIND REGION 7186 (N09E55) MAY OR
MAY NOT BE ASSOCIATED WTIH IT; IDENTIFICATION IS DIFFICULT DUE
TO LIMB PROXIMITY. AGAIN TODAY, SEVERAL FILAMENTS DISAPPEARED
INCLUDING A PATCHY ONE 16 DEGREES LONG NEAR CENTER DISK. A
WEAK EPL WAS REPORTED FROM S16E90 AT APPROXIMATELY 0800Z.
SURGING NEAR N10E90, AND AN ACTIVE PROMINENCE NEAR S28E90 WERE
OBSERVED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW. REGION 7186 CARRIES THE HIGHEST PROBABILITY FOR ERUPTIVE
EVENTS.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 02/2100Z TO 03/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET LEVELS FOR THE PAST
24 HOURS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE QUIET TOMORROW, BECOMING MOSTLY ACTIVE ON THE
NEXT DAY DUE TO POSSIBLE EFFECTS FROM A FILAMENT DISAPPEARANCE
BETWEEN 31 MAY AND 01 JUNE. DIMINISHED LEVELS ARE EXPECTED ON
THE THIRD DAY, BUT ACTIVITY MAY RESUME ON THE FOLLOWING DAY AS
A RESULT OF DISTURBED SOLAR WIND ASSOCIATED WITH A NUMBER OF
FILAMENT DISAPPEARANCES OVER THE PAST SEVERAL DAYS.
