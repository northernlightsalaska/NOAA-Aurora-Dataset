JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 029 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 29 JAN 1992
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 28/2100Z
TO 29/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW, HOWEVER A MARKED INCREASE
IN FLARING WAS OBSERVED. REGIONS 7031 (S05E05), 7038 (S11W07)
AND 7039 (S15E64) EACH CONTRIBUTED SEVERAL C-CLASS FLARES. THE
LARGEST, A C9/1F EVENT AT 1544Z, WAS ATTRIBUTED TO 7039. THIS
AREA HAS SHOWN NOTABLE GROWTH DURING THE PERIOD. REGION 7038 AND
7031 ALSO DISPLAYED GROWTH. THE LATTER CONTINUES TO REIGN AS THE
LARGEST AND MOST COMPLEX ON THE DISK. NEW REGION 7041 (N07E77)
WAS ASSIGNED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. GENERALLY C-CLASS FLARING IS EXPECTED FROM
REGIONS 7031, 7038, AND 7039. THESE REGIONS ALSO POSE AN
INCREASED THREAT FOR M-FLARE PRODUCTION.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 28/2100Z TO 29/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN QUIET TO UNSETTLED WITH SOME
ISOLATED PERIODS OF MINOR STORMING AT HIGH LATITUDES.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED.
