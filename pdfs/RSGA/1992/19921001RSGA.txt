JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 275 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 01 OCT 1992
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 30/2100Z
TO 01/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. TWO C-CLASS X-RAY BURSTS
OCCURRED, ONE OF WHICH WAS ASSOCIATED WITH A C1/SF FLARE FROM
REGION 7298 (N17E35) AT 01/1633Z. THIS REGION HAS DISPLAYED
GRADUAL GROWTH FOR THE PAST TWO DAYS. NEW REGION 7300 (N12E73)
WAS ASSIGNED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW. C-CLASS SUBFLARES ARE EXPECTED FROM REGION 7298.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 30/2100Z TO 01/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT UNSETTLED TO MINOR STORM
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS. ACTIVE TO MAJOR STORM LEVELS
OCCURRED AT HIGH LATITUDES. THIS ACTIVITY WAS PROBABLY DUE TO
CORONAL HOLE EFFECTS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM LEVELS DURING THE
FIRST DAY AS CORONAL HOLE EFFECTS CONTINUE. ACTIVE TO MINOR
STORM LEVELS WILL CONTINUE DURING THE REMAINDER OF THE PERIOD
DUE TO THE EFFECTS OF A FILAMENT DISAPPEARANCE THAT OCCURRED
ON 28-29 SEPTEMBER (SEE SDF NUMBER 273).
