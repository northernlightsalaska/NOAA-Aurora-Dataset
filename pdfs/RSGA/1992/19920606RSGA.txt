JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 158 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 06 JUN 1992
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 05/2100Z
TO 06/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY CONTINUED LOW. HOWEVER, FLARES
WERE MORE FREQUENT DURING THE INTERVAL, THE MAJORITY COMING
FROM REGION 7186 (N09E13). ITS MOST NOTEWORTHY ACTIVITY WAS
A C4/1N TENFLARE AT 1120Z. THE BRUNT OF THE FLARING HAS BEEN
CONFINED TO THE NORTH CENTRAL PORTION OF THE REGION. REGION
7192 (N09E46) HAD A C-CLASS FLARE - C2/0F AT 0209Z - BUT HAS
NOT CHANGED IN OBSERVABLE PARAMETERS. LITTLE ELSE OF SIGNIFI-
CANCE WAS REPORTED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO
PERSIST AT THE LOW LEVEL. REGION 7186 WILL REMAIN THE PREMIER
FLARE SITE, BUT DOES NOT SEEM CAPABLE OF M-CLASS ACTIVITY AT
THIS TIME.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 05/2100Z TO 06/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET LEVELS FOR THE PAST
24 HOURS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE QUIET TO UNSETTLED THROUGHOUT THE FORECAST
PERIOD.
