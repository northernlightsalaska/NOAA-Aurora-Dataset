JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 031 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 31 JAN 1992
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 30/2100Z
TO 31/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 7031 (S05W21)
HAD THE LARGEST FLARE OF THE DAY, THAT AN M1/1F AT 1917Z. THIS
ACTIVITY IMMEDIATELY FOLLOWED A SIMILAR FLARE, AN M1/1N AT
1903Z, IN NEARBY REGION 7032 (N06W28). FREQUENT SUBFLARES WERE
OBSERVED THE REST OF THE DAY FROM A VARIETY OF REGIONS,
ALTHOUGH REGION 7031 WAS THE MOST PRODUCTIVE. SEVENTEEN REGIONS
ARE NOW ON THE VISIBLE DISK, WITH THE NEWEST ONE BEING REGION
7044 (S27E67). TODAY'S 10.7 CM RADIO FLUX EXCEEDED THE 300 MARK.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE. ADDITIONAL M-LEVEL ACTIVITY IS LIKELY FROM REGION
7031 AND, TO A LESSER EXTENT, FROM REGION 7039 (S15E38).
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 30/2100Z TO 31/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO UNSETTLED
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM LEVELS THE NEXT 24
HOURS, DUE TO EFFECTS FROM THE LONG DURATION M2 FLARE FROM
REGION 7031 ON 30 JANUARY. UNSETTLED TO MILDLY ACTIVE
CONDITIONS SHOULD END THE INTERVAL.
