JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 306 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 01 NOV 1992
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 31/2100Z
TO 01/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. OCCASIONAL C-CLASS X-RAY
FLARES, WITH NO OPTICAL COUNTERPART, WERE THE EXTENT OF THE
DAY'S ACTIVITY. IT IS MOST LIKELY THAT MANY WERE RELATED TO THE
SURGING AT WEST LIMB FROM REGION 7321 (S25W93). TWO NEW REGIONS,
7329 (S19E41) AND 7330 (S08E68) WERE ASSIGNED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW. THERE IS A CHANCE OF AN M-CLASS FLARE FROM NOW DEPARTED
REGION 7321.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 31/2100Z TO 01/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN PREDOMINANTLY QUIET TO
UNSETTLED THE PAST 24 HOURS. THE ANTICIPATED SHOCK FROM THE
RECENT X1/2B FLARE HAS YET TO PASS. THE GREATER THAN 10
MEV PROTON EVENT PERSISTS. A PEAK FLUX OF 2700 PFU OCCURRED
AT 0710Z ON 31 OCT. IT HAS SINCE DECREASED TO 77 PFU AT
THIS WRITING. THE POLAR CAP ABSORPTION EVENT ALSO REMAINS
IN PROGRESS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT MINOR TO MAJOR STORM CONDITIONS THE NEXT
24 HOURS. THIS PREDICTION IS ASSUMING THE SHOCK AND CME FROM
THE PROTON FLARE OF 30 OCT ARE STILL IN TRANSIT. UNSETTLED TO
ACTIVE CONDITIONS SHOULD END THE FORECAST PERIOD.
