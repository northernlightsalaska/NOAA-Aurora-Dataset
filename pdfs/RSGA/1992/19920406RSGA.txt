SDF NUMBER 097 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 06 APR 1992
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 05/2100Z
TO 06/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. A FEW C-CLASS SUBFLARES
OCCURRED. THE LARGEST EVENT OF THE PERIOD WAS AN OPTICALLY
UNCORRELATED C9 X-RAY BURST AT 06/0747Z. REGIONS 7123 (S06E16)
AND 7125 (S16E67) SHOWED MINOR GROWTH AND EACH PRODUCED ISOLATED
SUBFLARES. NO NEW REGIONS WERE ASSIGNED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW. ISOLATED C-CLASS SUBFLARES MAY OCCUR FROM REGIONS 7123 AND
7125.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 05/2100Z TO 06/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO MAJOR STORM LEVELS
FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS. MINOR TO MAJOR STORM LEVELS OCCURRED
FROM 06/0600-0900Z AND MAY HAVE BEEN DUE TO EITHER ONE OF TWO
CORONAL MASS EJECTIONS THAT OCCURRED LATE ON 4 APRIL, ONE
FROM THE NORTHWEST LIMB AND THE OTHER FROM THE SOUTHEAST
LIMB. THE FIELD SUBSIDED TO UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE LEVELS NEAR
06/1200Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT MOSTLY UNSETTLED LEVELS WITH QUIET LEVELS
POSSIBLE AT MIDDLE LATITUDES ON THE FINAL DAY.
