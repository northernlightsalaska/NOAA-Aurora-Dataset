JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 236 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 23 AUG 1992
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 22/2100Z
TO 23/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. REGION 7260 (N16W78) HAS
DECAYED OVERNIGHT. MT WILSON NO LONGER SEES A DELTA
CONFIGURATION IN IT. THE REGION FLARED TWICE AT THE C4 LEVEL
DURING THE PERIOD AND PRODUCED THE DAY'S LARGEST FLARE, A C5 AT
23/2045Z.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. REGION 7260 CONTINUES TO BE THE ONLY REGION
WITH M-CLASS FLARE POTENTIAL ON THE DISK. IT ALSO HOLDS
SLIGHT X-CLASS FLARE POTENTIAL.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 22/2100Z TO 23/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT UNSETTLED TO MAJOR STORM
LEVELS. MAJOR STORM PERIODS EXISTED FROM 2200-0900Z BEFORE
THE FIELD QUIETED TO THE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE RANGE.
A SERIES OF M-CLASS FLARES PERSISTED FOR ABOUT 12 HOURS ON
20 AUG, PERHAPS CAUSING TODAY'S STORM.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE ACTIVE THE FIRST DAY, THEN RETURN TO UNSETTLED
FOR 25 AND 26 AUG.
