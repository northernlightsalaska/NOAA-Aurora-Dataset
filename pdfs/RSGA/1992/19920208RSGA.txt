JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 039 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 08 FEB 1992
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 07/2100Z
TO 08/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. REGION 7042 (S12W42) PRO-
DUCED MOST OF TODAY'S FLARE ACTIVITY INCLUDING THE LARGEST EVENT
OF THE PERIOD: A C7/SF AT 0322Z. BOTH REGION 7042 AND 7039
(S15W71) SHOWED CLEAR SIGNS OF DECAY AND SIMPLIFICATION. SOME
GROWTH WAS NOTED IN REGIONS 7048 (S04W03) AND 7051 (N21W06)
SURGING WAS SEEN ON EAST LIMB AT S17 AND S31.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE. REGIONS 7042 AND 7039 CONTINUE TO BE THE MOST LIKELY
SOURCES FOR M-CLASS LEVEL FLARES FOR THE NEXT THREE DAYS.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 07/2100Z TO 08/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD RANGED FROM QUIET TO SEVERE STORM LEVELS.
AN INITIALLY QUIET TO ACTIVE FIELD JUMPED UP TO SEVERE STORM
LEVELS FOR ABOUT 2 HOURS, FOLLOWING A SUDDEN COMMENCEMENT AT
1429Z (MEASURING 16 GAMMA IN BOULDER). DURING THE DISTURBANCE
A MAGNETOPAUSE CROSSING WAS OBSERVED ON GOES-7. SINCE ABOUT
1630Z, HOWEVER, ACTIVITY HAS DECLINED TO GENERALLY ACTIVE
LEVELS. THE GREATER THAN 10 MEV PROTON EVENT THAT BEGAN AT
07/0645Z ENDED TODAY AT 08/1940UT.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT MINOR TO MAJOR STORM LEVELS TOMORROW AS A
CONTINUATION OF TODAY'S ACTIVITY AND AS ADDITIONAL EFFECTS
ARRIVE FROM RECENT MAJOR FLARE ACTIVITY. CONDITIONS SHOULD
SUBSIDE TO MOSTLY ACTIVE ON THE SECOND DAY, AND THEN DOWN
TO GENERALLY UNSETTLED BY DAY THREE.
