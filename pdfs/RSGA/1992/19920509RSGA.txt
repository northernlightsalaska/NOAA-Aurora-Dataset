JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 130 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 09 MAY 1992
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 08/2100Z
TO 09/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY HAS BEEN VERY LOW SINCE YESTERDAY'S
M7. THE BEST REGION 7154 (S25, W12) COULD MUSTER WAS A B6/SF
FROM A B3 X-RAY BACKGROUND. THERE WERE SEVERAL DISAPPEARING
FILAMENTS NOTED TODAY, THE LARGEST WAS 35 DEGREES CENTERED AT
S42E82.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. REGION 7154 HAS SHOWN SOME DECAY SINCE THE M7
BUT MAY STILL BE CAPABLE OF ANOTHER ISOLATED M-CLASS EVENT.
REGION 7162 (N07W25) HAS DISPLAYED SOME MODERATE GROWTH OVER
THE PERIOD AND MAY ALSO BE CAPABLE OF ISOLATED M-CLASS FLARING.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 08/2100Z TO 09/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT UNSETTLED TO MINOR STORM
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS. MOSTLY UNSETTLED CONDITIONS
PREVAILED UNTIL A SUDDEN IMPULSE OF 55 NANOTESLA WAS
MEASURED AT BOULDER AT 1601Z. AT 1955Z A SUDDEN STORM
COMMENCEMENT OF 31 NANOTESLA WAS RECORDED AT BOULDER. A
PROTON EVENT AT THE GREATER THAN 10 MEV LEVEL BEGAN AT
1005Z IN RESPONSE TO YESTERDAY'S M7. A SIGNIFICANT INCREASE
IN THE PROTON FLUX WAS NOTED IN CONCERT WITH THE SSC.
CURRENT MAXIMUM FOR THE EVENT IS 4200 PFU AT 2035Z. HIGHER
ENERGY PROTONS HAVE REMAINED BELOW EVENT THRESHOLDS THUS
FAR. A POLAR CAP ABSORPTION EVENT BEGAN AT 1405Z WITH A
CURRENT MAXIMUM OF 18 DECIBELS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT MOSTLY MINOR STORM LEVELS THROUGH DAY TWO.
ANOTHER SHOCK IS ANTICIPATED AROUND 1500Z ON 10 APRIL IN
RESPONSE TO THE M7 FROM REGION 7154 ON 08 APRIL. THE GREATER
THAN 10 MEV PROTONS SHOULD BE NEAR THEIR PEAK FLUX. A SLOW
DECLINE IN EXPECTED TO BEGIN SHORTLY. A SLIGHT INCREASE IS
POSSIBLE AGAIN WITH THE ANTICIPATED SHOCK ON 10 APRIL. BY
12 APRIL CONDITIONS ARE EXPECTED TO BE MOSTLY UNSETTLED AGAIN.
