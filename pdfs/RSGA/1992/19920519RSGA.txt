JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 140 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 19 MAY 1992
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 18/2100Z
TO 19/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. REGION 7172 (N22E41) GREW
RAPIDLY TODAY TO A 230 MILLIONTHS DAO GROUP. THE REGION PRODUCED
FREQUENT LOW LEVEL C-CLASS FLARES AND SHOWED BRIGHT PLAGE DURING
THE PERIOD. THE OTHER SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS WERE SIMPLE, STABLE
AND QUIET. A 23 DEGREE FILAMENT DISAPPEARED FROM THE NORTHEAST
QUADRANT SOMETIME BETWEEN 18/2302Z AND 19/0550Z.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MOSTLY LOW, WITH A CHANCE FOR AN ISOLATED M-CLASS EVENT. REGION
7172 IS EXPECTED TO BE THE PREDOMINANT SOURCE OF SOLAR ACTIVITY
FOR THE NEXT THREE DAYS.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 18/2100Z TO 19/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD RANGED FROM QUIET TO ACTIVE LEVELS
FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS. ACTIVE CONDITIONS PREVAILED FROM
THE BEGINNING OF THE PERIOD THROUGH ABOUT 0600Z, PRESUMABLY
DUE TO A FAVORABLY POSITIONED CORONAL HOLE. ACTIVITY LEVELS
DECLINED TO MOSTLY QUIET TO UNSETTLED LEVELS FOR THE REMAIN-
DER OF THE PERIOD, WITH SOME ISOLATED ACTIVE PERIODS AT HIGH
LATITUDES.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE GENERALLY UNSETTLED. SOME PERIODS OF ACTIVE
LEVELS ARE ANTICIPATED LATE ON THE 21ST THROUGH EARLY ON THE
22ND DUE TO POSSIBLE EFFECTS FROM ANOTHER CORONAL HOLE AND
FROM TODAY'S DISAPPEARING FILAMENT.
