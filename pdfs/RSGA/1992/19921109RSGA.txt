JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 314 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 09 NOV 1992
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 08/2100Z
TO 09/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. REGION 7330 (S08W36) PRO-
DUCED MOST OF TODAY'S LIMITED FLARE ACTIVITY INCLUDING THE LAR-
GEST EVENT: A C2/SF AT 1732Z. THIS GROUP IS BEGINNING TO SHOW
SOME DECAY AND SIMPLIFICATION. REGION 7335 (S18E21) GREW DURING
THE PERIOD AND DEVELOPED A SIMPLE DELTA IN THE TRAILING PORTION
OF THE REGION. THE GROUP NEVERTHELESS REMAINS STABLE AND ESSEN-
TIALLY QUIET. REGION 7334 (N12E19) DEVELOPED SLOWLY DURING THE
PERIOD BUT WAS STABLE. SURGING ACTIVITY AND AN ACTIVE PROMINENCE
WERE OBSERVED ON EAST LIMB AT ABOUT S04. THE REMAINDER OF THE
DISK AND LIMBS WERE QUIET AND STABLE.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. REGIONS 7330 AND 7335 POSE A FAIR THREAT FOR
AN ISOLATED M-CLASS EVENT SOME TIME OVER THE NEXT THREE DAYS.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 08/2100Z TO 09/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD RANGED FROM QUIET TO SEVERE STORM LEVELS.
AN INITIALLY QUIET TO UNSETTLED FIELD PICKED UP TO MINOR STORM
LEVELS AT MID-LATITUDES AND MAJOR TO SEVERE STORM AT HIGH LATI-
TUDES AROUND 0730Z. THIS ENHANCED PERIOD OF ACTIVITY CARRIED
THROUGH WITH A GRADUAL DECLINE FOR THE REMAINDER OF THE DAY.
AT DAY'S END MID-LATITUDES WERE MOSTLY UNSETTLED AND HIGH
LATITUDES WERE AT MOSTLY ACTIVE LEVELS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE FOR THE NEXT THREE DAYS.
ISOLATED ACTIVE PERIODS ARE POSSIBLE AT HIGH LATITUDES OVER
THE NEXT 24 HOURS AS THE CURRENT DISTURBANCE PERSISTS.
CONDITIONS ON DAYS TWO AND THREE ARE EXPECTED TO BE ENHANCED
DUE TO RECURRENT EFFECTS.
