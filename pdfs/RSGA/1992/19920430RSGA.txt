JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 121 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 30 APR 1992
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 29/2100Z
TO 30/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. REGION 7143 (S05W27)
PRODUCED ISOLATED SUBFLARES WHILE DISPLAYING GRADUAL DECAY. THE
REGION MAINTAINED A WEAK DELTA MAGNETIC STRUCTURE THROUGHOUT THE
PERIOD. REGION 7150 (S07W09) DISPLAYED MODERATE GROWTH DURING
THE FIRST HALF OF THE PERIOD, BUT BECAME STABLE DURING THE
SECOND HALF. NO NEW REGIONS WERE ASSIGNED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW. REGIONS 7143 AND 7150 ARE EXPECTED TO PRODUCE ISOLATED
C-CLASS SUBFLARES.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 29/2100Z TO 30/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO UNSETTLED LEVELS
FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE MOSTLY UNSETTLED DURING THE FIRST TWO DAYS.
ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO INCREASE TO UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE LEVELS
ON THE FINAL DAY DUE TO THE EFFECTS OF A FAVORABLY POSITIONED
CORONAL HOLE.
