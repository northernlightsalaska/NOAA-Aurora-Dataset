JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 318 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 13 NOV 1992
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 12/2100Z
TO 13/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. THE ACTIVE REGIONS
CURRENTLY VISIBLE ON THE DISK APPEAR TO BE DECLINING IN
COMPLEXITY AND HAVE BEEN QUIET TODAY. THE MOST SIGNIFICANT
X-RAY FLARE WAS AN OPTICALLY UNCORRELATED LDE PEAKING AT C1
AT ABOUT 13/1647Z. THIS EVENT MAY HAVE ORIGINATED FROM BEYOND
THE EAST LIMB. NEW REGION 7340 (S10E73) WAS NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW FOR THE NEXT DAY WITH AN INCREASING POSSIBILITY OF MAJOR
ACTIVITY AS OLD REGION 7321 (S25,L-70) APPROACHES THE EAST LIMB.
THIS REGION PRODUCED AN X9 FLARE FROM JUST BEYOND THE WEST LIMB
ON 02 NOV.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 12/2100Z TO 13/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS QUIET TO UNSETTLED FOR MOST OF THE
DAY. ISOLATED ACTIVE TO STORM LEVELS (SEVERE STORM AT SOME
HIGH LATITUDE STATIONS) WERE OBSERVED FROM 13/0900-1500Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE MOSTLY UNSETTLED FOR THE NEXT DAY, BECOMING
QUIET TO UNSETTLED.
