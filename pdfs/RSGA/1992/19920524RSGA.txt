JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 145 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 24 MAY 1992
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 23/2100Z
TO 24/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. REGION 7172 (N22W14)
PRODUCED THE LARGEST FLARE WHICH WAS ALSO OBSERVED OPTICALLY,
A C1/SF AT 24/1345Z. THIS FLARE WAS ASSOCIATED WITH A
2900 SFU RADIO BURST AT 245 MHZ. THIS REGION REMAINS THE
LARGEST SUNSPOT GROUP ON THE DISK. AN OPTICALLY UNCORRELATED
LONG-DURATION C1 FLARE BEGAN AT ABOUT 24/0430Z AND ENDED AT
24/0725Z. THE SOURCE OF THIS FLARE IS UNCERTAIN, ALTHOUGH IT
MAY BE RELATED TO ACTIVITY IN REGION 7172.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW. REGION 7172 WILL BE THE MOST LIKELY SOURCE OF FLARES.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 23/2100Z TO 24/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS QUIET TO UNSETTLED. A SATELLITE
ENHANCEMENT OF THE GREATER THAN 10 MEV PROTONS BEGAN AT ABOUT
24/0800Z. THE SOURCE OF THE ENHANCEMENT IS UNKNOWN, ALTHOUGH
IT MAY BE RELATED TO THE LONG-DURATION X-RAY FLARE MENTIONED
ABOVE. THIS ENHANCEMENT IS NOT EXPECTED TO REACH EVENT LEVELS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE QUIET TO UNSETTLED FOR THE NEXT 12 TO 24 HOURS.
ACTIVE TO STORM CONDITIONS ARE THEN POSSIBLE AS A RESULT OF THE
LONG-DURATION X-RAY FLARE.
