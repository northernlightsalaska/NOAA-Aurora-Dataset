JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 253 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 09 SEP 1992
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 08/2100Z
TO 09/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 7270 (S10W83)
PRODUCED THE DAY'S ONLY CORRELATED M-CLASS FLARE, AN M1/SF, AT
09/1803UT. AN M3 EVENT OCCURRED AT 09/0213UT, BUT NO ASSOCIATED
OPTICAL FLARE WAS REPORTED. SUBFAINT FLARES WERE REPORTED IN
REGION 7276 (N16E53) AND NEWLY ASSIGNED REGION 7279 (S06E63).
FOUR C CLASS EVENTS WITH NO CORRELATED OPTICAL FLARES WERE ALSO
RECORDED. REGION 7278 (N05E14) WAS ALSO ASSIGNED. REGION 7270
WILL ROTATE OFF THE DISK LATER TODAY. REGION 7276 HAS DECAYED IN
SIZE OVER THE PAST 24 HOURS.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. REGION 7276 IS CAPABLE OF PRODUCING M CLASS
FLARES AS IT DECAYS AND STILL HAS AN OUTSIDE CHANCE OF AN
X CLASS FLARE. REGION 7270 MAY STILL PRODUCE A MAJOR FLARE
BEFORE IT IS COMPLETELY GONE.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 08/2100Z TO 09/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT ACTIVE TO MAJOR STORM
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS BECAUSE OF FLARE ACTIVITY
OVER THE PAST FEW DAYS. A SUDDEN IMPULSE OF 52 NANOTESLAS
WAS RECORDED AT 09/0139UT. A POSSIBLE FOREBUSH DECREASE
BEGAN ABOUT 09/0130UT AND IS STILL DECREASING SLOWLY.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT MINOR TO MAJOR STORM LEVELS FOR THE NEXT 24
HOURS BECAUSE OF RECENT FLARE ACTIVITY, DECLINING TO ACTIVE
LEVELS BY DAY TWO AND UNSETTLED LEVELS BY THE END OF PERIOD.
