IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 28/2100Z
TO 29/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. A FEW SMALL FLARES FROM A
VARIETY OF ACTIVE REGIONS WERE OBSERVED DURING THE PAST DAY.
REGION 7138 (N09W71) PRODUCED THE LARGEST FLARE, A C1/SF AT
29/1935Z. THIS REGION IS A SMALL, DECAYING SUNSPOT GROUP THAT
HAS BEEN QUIET OTHERWISE. REGION 7143 (S04W13) GENERATED A FEW
SMALL OPTICAL SUBFLARES AND FLARE BRIGHT PLAGE FLUCTUATIONS.
THIS REGION STILL CONTAINS A MAGNETIC DELTA CONFIGURATION.
REGION 7150 (S07E04) IS THE LARGEST SUNSPOT GROUP ON THE DISK
AFTER RAPIDLY EMERGING DURING THE PAST DAY. THE GROWTH RATE
APPEARS TO BE SUBSIDING. NEW REGIONS NUMBERED INCLUDE 7151
(S09E63), 7152 (N13E62), AND 7153 (S40W06).

LOW. C-CLASS ACTIVITY IS POSSIBLE IN REGIONS 7143 AND 7150.
