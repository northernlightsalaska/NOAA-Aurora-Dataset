JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 320 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 16 NOV 1991
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 15/2100Z
TO 16/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH. REGION 6919 (S12W27),
AFTERS DAYS OF ISOLATED SUBFLARING, GENERATED AN X1/3B TENFLARE
AT 15/2239Z. THE EVENT BOASTED A FULL SPECTRAL BURST OF MODEST
INTENSITY WITH 1100 SFU ACHIEVED AT 2695MHZ. A STRONG TYPE 2
SWEEP BURST ALSO ACCOMPANIED THIS ACTIVITY. THE REGION HAS SINCE
PRODUCED ISOLATED C-CLASS FLARING WHILE CONTINUING TO DECAY IN
AREA. A LONE SUBFLARE FROM REGION 6921 (N10W56) WAS OBSERVED.
EVENT LEVEL BRIGHT SURGING WAS NOTED ON THE SOUTHEAST LIMB AT
0415Z, POSSIBLY HAILING THE RETURN OF OLD REGION 6891 (S11,
L-186). NEW REGION 6927 (N07E62) WAS ASSIGNED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. REGION 6919 CURRENTLY POSES THE GREATEST
THREAT FOR MODERATE ACTIVITY.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 15/2100Z TO 16/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT MOSTLY UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE MOSTLY ACTIVE. EFFECTS FROM THE MAJOR FLARING
NOTED ABOVE ARE POSSIBLE LATE ON THE 17TH, EXTENDING INTO THE
18TH. PERIODS OF MINOR STORMING AT HIGH LATITUDES MAY BE NOTED.
UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE CONDITIONS SHOULD CLOSE OUT THE PERIOD.
