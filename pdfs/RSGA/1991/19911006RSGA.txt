JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 279 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 06 OCT 1991
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 05/2100Z
TO 06/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. REGION 6861 (N08W19) PRO-
DUCED TODAY'S LARGEST FLARE: A C3/SN AT 0535Z. THIS GROUP PRO-
DUCED MOST OF TODAY'S SCANT SUBFLARE ACTIVITY AND WAS SHOWING
SOME SIGNS OF DECAY NEAR DAY'S END. REGION 6850 (S12W70) CONTIN-
UES TO BE AN IMPRESSIVE LOOKING BUT STABLE REGION. CLEAR INDICA-
TIONS OF DECAY WERE EVIDENT TODAY INCLUDING THE DISSIPATION OF
THE CENTRAL DELTA SPOT. A DELTA CONTINUES TO BE PRESENT IN THE
LARGE LEADER SPOT.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. THERE CONTINUES TO BE A FAIRLY GOOD CHANCE FOR
ISOLATED M-CLASS FLARES FROM EITHER REGION 6850 OR 6861.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 05/2100Z TO 06/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS INITIALLY QUIET BUT BECAME ACTIVE WITH
MINOR STORMING AT HIGH LATITUDES AFTER 0900Z. THE INCREASE WAS
PROBABLY CAUSED BY A FAVORABLY POSITIONED CORONAL HOLE.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT ACTIVE LEVELS TOMORROW AND FOR THE SECOND DAY
AS THE CURRENT DISTURBANCE CONTINUES. A GRADUAL DECLINE TO UN-
SETTLED TO ACTIVE LEVELS IS EXPECTED BY DAY THREE.
