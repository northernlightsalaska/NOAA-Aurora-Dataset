JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 273 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 30 SEP 1991
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 29/2100Z
TO 30/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. REGIONS 6850 (S12E07)
AND 6853 (S20E15) CONTINUED TO GROW AND RESTRUCTURE DURING THE
DAY, BUT NEITHER PRODUCED AN M-CLASS FLARE. REGION 6850 HAD A
C8/0F AT 0457Z, AND 6853 GENERATED A C7/0F AT 1501Z. BOTH
REMAIN COMPLICATED MAGNETICALLY AND IMPRESSIVE IN WHITE LIGHT
AND H-ALPHA. AN ERUPTIVE PROMINENCE WAS SEEN AT EAST LIMB AT
1420Z, ASSOCIATED WITH NEWLY NUMBERED REGION 6859 (N01E73). ONE
OTHER NEW REGION WAS BORN ON THE DISK NEAR THE 6850/6853
COMPLEX, REGION 6858 (S22E07).
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE. IT SEEMS JUST A MATTER OF TIME BEFORE EITHER REGION
6850 OR 6853 PRODUCE THE NEXT MAJOR FLARE.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 29/2100Z TO 30/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS. AN ENHANCEMENT IN THE GREATER
THAN 10 MEV PROTON FLUX BEGAN ABOUT 0700Z. YESTERDAY'S MAJOR
FLARE IS THE LIKELY SOURCE OF THE PARTICLES WHOSE FLUX HAS
HOVERED NEAR 3 PFU THE PAST 10 HOURS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE MOSTLY ACTIVE. THE LARGE DISAPPEARING FILAMENT
OF 28 SEPT IS EXPECTED TO BRING EPISODES OF MINOR STORM LEVELS
ON 02 OCT.
