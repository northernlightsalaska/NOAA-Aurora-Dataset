IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 25/2100Z
TO 26/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. NEW REGION 6853

CONTRIBUTED AN M3/SN EVENT AT 1236Z. REGION 6850 (S11E56)
CONTINUED TO GROW IN BOTH SIZE AND COMPLEXITY. ONLY SUBFLARING

BETWEEN 1451-1512Z.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE. INTERACTION BETWEEN REGIONS 6848, 6850, AND 6853 MAY
PRODUCE ISOLATED M-CLASS ACTIVITY. REGION 6850 ALSO POSES A
THREAT FOR MAJOR FLARE PRODUCTION.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 25/2100Z TO 26/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT UNSETTLED TO MAJOR STORM
LEVELS. THE MAJOR STORMING WAS MOST EVIDENT BETWEEN
0800-1200Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM LEVELS THROUGH THE
28TH IN RESPONSE TO EFFECTS FROM A WELL POSITIONED CORONAL
HOLE. GENERALLY ACTIVE CONDITIONS ARE EXPECTED BY THE END
OF THE PERIOD.
