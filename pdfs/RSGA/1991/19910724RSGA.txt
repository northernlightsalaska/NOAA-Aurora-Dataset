JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 205 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 24 JUL 1991
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 23/2100Z
TO 24/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. REGION 6734 (N08E13) PRO-
DUCED TODAY'S LARGEST FLARE: A C7/1F AT 1729Z. THE REGION PRO-
DUCED SEVERAL ADDITIONAL SMALL FLARES DURING THE DAY BUT AP-
PEARS TO HAVE PEAKED FROM ITS INITIAL GROWTH PHASE. IT CONTINUES
TO POSSESS STRONG MAGNETIC FIELDS AND GRADIENTS IN A DELTA CON-
FIGURATION. REGION 6741 (N09E29) CONTINUES TO GROW AND PRODUCED
A C5/SF AT 0918Z AS WELL AS ADDITIONAL OCCASIONAL FLARES. THE
ENTIRE NORTHEAST PART OF THE SUN IN THE VICINITY OF THESE GROUPS
SHOWS THE EMERGENCE OF SEVERAL CLOSELY CLUSTERED BIPOLES AND
GENERALLY INCREASING MAGNETIC FLUX (5 HAVE BEEN ASSIGNED SESC
REGION NUMBERS). REGION 6437 (S16E09) ALSO DISPLAYED GROWTH
AND OCCASIONAL SMALL FLARES TODAY.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE PRIMARILY DUE TO THE PRESENCE OF REGIONS 6734 AND
6741. AN ISOLATED MAJOR FLARE CONTINUES TO BE A POSSIBILITY
FROM REGION 6734.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 23/2100Z TO 24/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS QUIET TO UNSETTLED.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO ATTAIN ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM LEVELS IN THE NEXT 24
HOURS AS A RESPONSE TO THE MAJOR FLARE OF 22 JULY. CONDITIONS
ARE EXPECTED TO SUBSIDE TO MOSTLY ACTIVE CONDITIONS BY DAY 2
AND TO UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE LEVELS BY DAY 3.
