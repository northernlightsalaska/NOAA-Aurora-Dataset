JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 224 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 12 AUG 1991
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 11/2100Z
TO 12/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. JUST A FEW SUBFLARES WERE
OBSERVED, AS THE CHARACTER OF THE EIGHT ACTIVE REGIONS VISIBLE
REMAINED STABLE. ONE NEW REGION CAME INTO VIEW, 6778 (S08E70).
SURGE AND PROMINENCE ACTIVITY WAS SEEN AT VARIOUS POINTS ON THE
EAST LIMB, PERHAPS HERALDING THE ARRIVAL OF MORE SPOT GROUPS.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW THROUGHOUT THE PERIOD.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 11/2100Z TO 12/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT MINOR TO MAJOR STORM LEVELS
SINCE EARLY IN THE PERIOD. THE GOES-7 SPACECRAFT EXPERIENCED
TWO BRIEF MAGNETOPAUSE CROSSINGS AT APPROXIMATELY 1500 AND
1630Z. THIS DISTURBANCE LACKS A WELL-DEFINED SOLAR SOURCE BUT
HAS RECURRED, TO VARYING DEGREES, OVER THE LAST FIVE SOLAR
ROTATIONS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM LEVELS. PERIODS OF
MAJOR STORM CONDITIONS ARE LIKELY DURING LOCAL NIGHTTIMES.
