JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 323 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 19 NOV 1991
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 18/2100Z
TO 19/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. REGION 6919 (S11W74) HAD
THE DAY'S LARGEST EVENT, THAT A C8/1F AT 0932Z. REGION 6929
(S11E50) COULD MANAGE ONLY OCCASIONAL SMALLER C-CLASS EVENTS.
THESE TWO REGIONS ARE THE MOST IMPRESSIVE OF THE NINE NOW
VISIBLE ON AN OTHERWISE DULL SUN. FOUR NEW REGIONS WERE ASSIGNED
DURING THE PERIOD, 6930 (S08W04), 6931 (S15W45), 6932 (S10E66),
AND 6933 (S19E57).
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. AN ISOLATED M-CLASS FLARE MAY OCCUR FROM
EITHER REGION 6919 OR 6929.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 18/2100Z TO 19/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS AT ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM LEVELS AT
MIDDLE LATITUDES. HIGH LATITUDES HAD SIMILAR CONDITIONS PLUS
AN EPISODE OF MAJOR STORMING. THIS DISTURBANCE MAY BE RELATED
TO THE MAJOR FLARE OF 15 NOV.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE ACTIVE THE NEXT 24 HOURS AS THE INFLUENCE OF A
WELL-POSITIONED CORONAL HOLE PREVAILS. UNSETTLED LEVELS ARE
LIKELY TO END THE FORECAST PERIOD.
