JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 243 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 31 AUG 1991
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 30/2100Z
TO 31/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. ABUTTED REGIONS 6810
(S20E11) AND 6811 (S20E26) COMBINED TO PRODUCE AN M2/1N EVENT AT
31/0203Z. '1N' FLARES WERE CONTRIBUTED BY EACH REGION DURING
THIS EVENT. FLARING WAS OBSERVED TO HAVE STARTED IN REGION 6810,
BUT QUICKLY SPREAD INTO 6811 WHERE A LARGER MORE INTENSE FLARE
AREA WAS ACHIEVED. BOTH REGIONS SHOWED LITTLE CHANGE IN AREA
DURING THE PERIOD, HOWEVER 6811 HAS DEVELOPED ADDITIONAL SPOTS
IN ITS EXTREME TRAILER. REGION 6807 (S07W21) PRODUCED ISOLATED
C-CLASS SUBFLARING WHILE SHOWING SIGNS OF SLIGHT DECAY. REGION
6805 (N23W14) CONTINUED TO DECAY. NEW REGION 6813 (N11E06) WAS
ASSIGNED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE. ONLY ISOLATED M-CLASS FLARING IS EXPECTED.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 30/2100Z TO 31/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS RANGED FROM QUIET TO MAJOR STORM
LEVELS DURING THE PAST 24 HOURS. SEVERE STORMING WAS NOTED
AT HIGH LATITUDES BETWEEN 1200-1800Z. A FAVORABLY POSITIONED
CORONAL HOLE IS THE LIKELY SOURCE FOR THIS ACTIVITY.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM LEVELS WITH ISOLATED
PERIODS OF MAJOR STORMING AT HIGH LATITUDES POSSIBLE THROUGH
2 SEPT.
