JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 035 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 04 FEB 1991
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 03/2100Z
TO 04/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH.  REGION 6469 (S12W91)
PRODUCED AN M6/1N FLARE AT 04/1055Z.  THIS REGION ALSO PRODUCED
FOUR OTHER M-CLASS FLARES SINCE THIS TIME YESTERDAY, AN M1/1F
AT 03/2256Z, AN M2/SN AT 04/0152Z, AN M1/1F AT 04/1613Z, AND
AN M3/1N AT 04/1844Z.  DECAY WAS APPARENT IN THIS REGION AS IT
APPROACHED THE WEST LIMB.  REGION 6471 (S12W51) CONTINUES TO
DECAY SLOWLY, ALTHOUGH THE SUNSPOT AREA IS STILL GREATER THAN
1000 MILLIONTHS.  ONLY A FEW SUBFLARES HAVE OCCURRED IN THIS
REGION DURING THE PAST 24 HOURS.  A RATHER SLOW RISE AND FALL
IN THE X-RAYS REACHED A MAXIMUM OF M1 AT 04/1728Z AND COULD BE
ASSOCIATED WITH ACTIVITY TO THE SOUTHEAST OF REGION 6484 (N15
E62), ALTHOUGH THIS CONNECTION IS NOT CERTAIN.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE TO HIGH.  ACTIVITY LEVELS ARE EXPECTED TO DROP AS
REGIONS 6469 AND 6471 ROTATE BEYOND THE WEST LIMB.

IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
