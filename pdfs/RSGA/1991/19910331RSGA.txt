JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 090 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 31 MAR 1991
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 30/2100Z
TO 31/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH. REGION 6555 (S23W95)
PRODUCED TWO MAJOR FLARES WHILE CROSSING THE LIMB. THE FIRST WAS
AN X1/0F AT 31/1912Z ASSOCIATED WITH A 440 SFU TENFLARE AND AN
ERUPTIVE PROMINENCE THAT REACHED .24RV. THE SECOND WAS A LONG
DURATION M6 X-RAY BURST AT 31/2034Z ASSOCIATED WITH A BRIGHT
SURGE TO .20RV AND A TYPE II SWEEP. THIS EVENT WAS IN PROGRESS
AND GRADUALLY DECAYING AS THE PERIOD ENDED. REGION 6562 (N14E28)
PRODUCED A FEW SUBFLARES AS IT CONTINUED A PERIOD OF GRADUAL
GROWTH. NEW REGION 6565 (N09E57) WAS NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. REGION 6555 MAY PRODUCE AN ISOLATED M-CLASS
EVENT FROM BEHIND THE LIMB. OTHERWISE, OCCASIONAL C-CLASS
FLARES ARE EXPECTED FROM 6555 AND GROWING REGION 6562.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 30/2100Z TO 31/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO UNSETTLED LEVELS
FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE ACTIVE ON DAY ONE DUE TO THE EFFECTS OF AN
X2/3B FLARE THAT OCCURRED ON 29 MARCH. THE FIELD IS EXPECTED
TO SUBSIDE TO UNSETTLED LEVELS FOR THE REMAINING TWO DAYS.
