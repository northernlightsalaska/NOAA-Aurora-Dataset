JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 139 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 19 MAY 1991
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 18/2100Z
TO 19/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 6633 (S12E11)
PRODUCED A LONG DURATION M1/1B FLARE AT 19/1404Z WHICH WAS
ASSOCIATED WITH A MINOR TYPE IV SWEEP BURST. THIS REGION GREW AT
A MODEST PACE THROUGHOUT THE PERIOD. A 16 DEGREE FILAMENT, LAST
SEEN AT N08W01, DISAPPEARED BETWEEN 19/1102-1539Z. NEW REGIONS
6640 (S19W00) AND 6641 (S27E04) WERE ASSIGNED WITH THE LATTER
DISPLAYING MODERATE GROWTH LATE IN THE PERIOD.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. REGION 6633 MAY PRODUCE ISOLATED M-CLASS
ACTIVITY. OTHERWISE, OCCASIONAL C-CLASS SUBFLARES ARE EXPECTED.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 18/2100Z TO 19/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET LEVELS FOR THE PAST
24 HOURS. THE GREATER THAN 10 MEV PROTON ENHANCEMENT WHICH
BEGAN NEAR 18/1500Z REACHED A MAXIMUM FLUX OF 7.8 PFU AT
19/0310Z. THEREAFTER, THE FLUX GRADUALLY DECLINED.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM LEVELS ON DAY ONE IN
RESPONSE TO THE X2/2B FLARE OF 18 MAY. THE FIELD IS EXPECTED
TO BE AT UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE LEVELS ON THE SECOND DAY AS FLARE
EFFECTS SUBSIDE. UNSETTLED LEVELS ARE EXPECTED ON THE FINAL
DAY.
