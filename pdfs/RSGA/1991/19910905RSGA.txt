JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 248 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 05 SEP 1991
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 04/2100Z
TO 05/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 6805 (N24W74)
PRODUCED THREE ENERGETIC FLARES - AN M4/1B AT 0111Z, AN M2/0N AT
0528Z, AND AN M1/0F AT 1209Z. ALL THREE WERE IMPULSIVE AND HAD
BUT A SLIGHT RADIO RESPONSE. THE REGION DID NOT CHANGE SIGNIF-
ICANTLY THROUGHOUT THE FLARE ACTIVITY. THE OTHER EIGHT VISIBLE
REGIONS WERE DEVOID OF SIGNIFICANT ACTIVITY. TWO SIZEABLE FILA-
MENTS DISAPPEARED, AN 18 DEGREE-LONG FROM N27W15, AND A 30
DEGREE-LONG FROM N39E53. ONE NEW REGION EMERGED ON THE DISK,
6819 (S25E21).
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO
REMAIN AT THE MODERATE LEVEL. REGION 6805 SHOULD YIELD MORE
LOW-LEVEL M-CLASS FLARES BEFORE IT TRANSITS WEST LIMB.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 04/2100Z TO 05/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS AT ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM LEVELS
THE FORMER PART OF TODAY. QUIET TO UNSETTELD CONDITIONS
WERE OBSERVED BOTH BEFORE AND AFTER THIS BRIEF DISTURBANCE.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE THROUGHOUT THE PERIOD.
MORE SUBSTORMS ARE LIKELY.
