JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 200 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 19 JUL 1991
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 18/2100Z
TO 19/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE.  NEW REGION 6737
(S16E73) PRODUCED AN M2/SF FLARE THAT MAXED AT 1726 UT, AS WELL
AS SEVERAL OTHER C-CLASS FLARES.  THIS REGION ROTATED OVER THE
EAST LIMB AS E-TYPE GROUP.  REGION 6734 (N08E74) PRODUCED
C-CLASS FLARE LOOPS AT 0502Z AND OTHER LIMB SURGES THROUGH
THE DAY.  NEW REGIONS 6736(S09E52) AND 6738 (S26E53) ADDED
TO THE ALREADY INTERESTING FAR-EASTERN LONGITUDES.  ON THE
WEST LIMB, SURGING WAS ASSOCIATED WITH THE DEPARTURE OF
REGION 6718 (S22W89).
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE TO HIGH.  THE COMPLEX OF REGIONS ON THE EAST LIMB
HAVE SHOWN THE CAPABILITY FOR SIGNIFICANT FLARES.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 18/2100Z TO 19/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN MOSTLY ACTIVE AT MIDDLE
LATITUDES FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS, WHILE MINOR TO MAJOR STORM
LEVELS WERE REPORTED AT HIGH GEOMAGNETIC LATITUDES.  A SUDDEN
IMPULSE OCCURRED AT 1902Z, WHICH IS THE PROBABLE ARRIVAL OF A
SHOCK FROM THE X1/1N FLARE (REGION 6729, S23E34) THAT MAXED
JUL 17/0637Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT STORM LEVELS THROUGHOUT THE FORECAST
PERIOD IN RESPONSE TO DISTURBED SOLAR WIND FROM CORONAL
HOLES, AND CORONAL MASS EJECTIONS ASSOCIATED WITH RECENT
FLARES AND FILAMENT DISAPPEARANCES.
