IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 06/2100Z
TO 07/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 6615 (S10W31)
PRODUCED TWO M-CLASS FLARES TODAY: AN M2/1B AT 0037Z AND AN
M1/SN AT 1039Z. THE GROUP CONTINUES TO GROW IN A MAGNETIC DELTA
CONFIGURATION AND PRODUCED ADDITIONAL SMALL FLARES. REGION 6619
(N28E45) PRODUCED THE REST OF TODAY'S FLARE ACTIVITY (ALL C-
CLASS OR LESS) AND IS ALSO A LARGE DELTA GROUP, BUT IS LESS
ACTIVE AND SIMPLER THAN 6615. REGION 6621 (N08E57) SHOWED
MODEST GROWTH THROUGHOUT THE DAY. SURGING WAS REPORTED ON
EAST LIMB AT N11 AND S18.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE, WITH THE MAIN THREAT FOR M-FLARES BEING REGION 6615
ALTHOUGH REGION 6619 MIGHT ALSO CONTRIBUTE. THERE IS A SLIGHT
CHANCE FOR AN ISOLATED MAJOR FLARE.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 06/2100Z TO 07/2100Z:

IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE GENERALLY QUIET TO UNSETTLED FOR THE NEXT THREE
DAYS. A SMALL INCREASE IN ACTIVITY IS POSSIBLE ON THE SECOND
DAY IN RESPONSE TO TODAY'S M2 SOLAR FLARE.
