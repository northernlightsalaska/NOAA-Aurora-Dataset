JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 159 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 08 JUN 1991
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 07/2100Z
TO 08/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW, THE LARGEST EVENT DURING
THE PAST 24 HOURS WAS A C5/SF FROM REGION 6659 (N32E07).  GROWTH
CONTINUES IN THIS REGION, THERE IS EVIDENCE OF INCREASED SHEAR,
(ESPECIALLY ALONG ITS EASTERN MAGNETIC INVERSION LINE), AND
SEVERAL DELTAS HAVE BEEN REPORTED IN THE REGION.  HOWEVER, THE
REGION IS PRODUCING ONLY SMALL FLARES.  REGION 6666 (S16E15) IS
SLOWLY DEVELOPING AND HAS SOME AREAS OF MIXED POLARITIES.  AN
ERUPTIVE PROMINENCE WAS REPORTED ON THE EAST LIMB NEAR S28.
REGIONS 6670 (S16E55) AND 6671 (N12E44) WERE NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE TO HIGH.  MAJOR EVENTS ARE EXPECTED FROM REGION 6659.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 07/2100Z TO 08/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS.  A SUDDEN IMPULSE (88 GAMMAS IN
BOULDER) WAS OBSERVED AT 07/2225UT.  THIS IMPULSE IS THOUGHT
TO BE FROM THE X12 FLARE ON 6 JUNE, HOWEVER, THE STORM THAT
WAS EXPECTED TO FOLLOW, HAS NOT MATERIALIZED.  THE SATELLITE
PROTON EVENT AND PCA CONTINUE.  THE THULE NEUTRON MONITOR
IS SHOWING A SMALL FORBUSH DECREASE.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE. SOME PERIODS OF MINOR STORM
ARE POSSIBLE IN NIGHT TIME SECTORS.  THE PROTON AND PCA EVENT
ARE EXPECTED TO CONTINUE.
