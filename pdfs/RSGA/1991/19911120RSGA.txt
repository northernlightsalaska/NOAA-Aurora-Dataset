JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 324 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 20 NOV 1991
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 19/2100Z
TO 20/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 6929 (S11E36)
GENERATED AN M4/3B FLARE WITH TYPES II AND IV SWEEP AT 0111Z.
SMALL DISCRETE FREQUENCY RADIO BURSTS ACCOMPANIED THE EVENT. THE
REGION HAS BEEN MOSTLY QUIET SINCE THE FLARE. REGION 6919
(S12W86) HAS HAD SOME LIMB ACTIVITY ASSOCIATED WITH ITS DEPART-
URE FROM THE DISK. TWO NEW REGIONS, 6934 (S25W49) AND 6935
(N25E46), WERE ASSIGNED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. INFREQUENT M-CLASS FLARES MAY COME FROM REGION
6929 OR 6919.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 19/2100Z TO 20/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS AT ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM LEVELS THE
EARLY PART OF THE PERIOD, THEN CALMED TO MOSTLY QUIET TO
UNSETTLED CONDITIONS THEREAFTER.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE CONDITIONS 21 AND 23 NOV.
CORONAL HOLE EFFECTS ARE LIKELY MIDWAY THROUGH THE INTERVAL
WHEN ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM LEVELS ARE ANTICIPATED.
