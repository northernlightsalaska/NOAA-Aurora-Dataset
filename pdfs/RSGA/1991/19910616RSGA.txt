JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 167 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 16 JUN 1991
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 15/2100Z
TO 16/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH. AN M5/2B FLARE STARTED
OFF THE PERIOD AT 15/2101Z.  REGION 6666 (S15W87) GENERATED THIS
EVENT AS IT APPROACHED THE LIMB AND HAS SINCE BECOME STABLE
AGAIN. REGION 6659 (N32W90) CONTINUED TO SHOW SIGNS OF DECAY
AS IT ROTATED OUT OF VIEW. THE LOOP PROMINENCE SYSTEM THAT BEGAN
IN THIS REGION AFTER THE 15 JUNE X12/3B FLARE WAS STILL EVIDENT
TODAY. REGION 6681 (N09E59) PRODUCED NUMEROUS C-CLASS FLARES
WITH A C7/1F EVENT AT 16/0535Z NOTED AS ITS LARGEST. THIS AREA,
ALONG WITH REGION 6682 (S12E15), HAS DISPLAYED SIGNIFICANT
GROWTH IN THE LAST 24 HOURS WITH SOME MAGNETIC COMPLEXITY NOTED.
AN UNCORRELATED M1 X-RAY EVENT WAS OBSERVED AT 16/1648Z. NEW
REGION 6683 (S15W18) WAS ASSIGNED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE TO HIGH. REGION 6659 STILL POSES A THREAT FOR ISOLATED
MAJOR FLARING. REGIONS 6666 AND 6681 MAY ADD TO M-CLASS
ACTIVITY LEVELS.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 15/2100Z TO 16/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN MOSTLY QUIET FOR THE PAST 24
HOURS. THE SATELLITE LEVEL (10 MEV) PROTON EVENT CONTINUED.
A PEAK OF 1400 PFU WAS OBSERVED AT 15/1950Z. THE POLAR CAP
ABSORPTION (PCA) EVENT CONTINUED THROUGHOUT THE PERIOD WITH
A PEAK OF 10.8 DECIBELS OCCURRING NEAR 15/2230Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT STORM LEVELS ON 17 JUNE IN RESPONSE TO THE
X12/3B FLARE NOTED ON THE 15TH. UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE CONDITIONS
SHOULD RETURN BY THE 19TH. THE PROTON AND PCA EVENTS ARE
EXPECTED TO CONTINUE.
