IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 16/2100Z
TO 17/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 6832 (N07E17)
PRODUCED AN M1 X-RAY BURST AT 17/1904Z. AN OPTICAL
CLASSIFICATION WAS NOT AVAILABLE FOR THIS FLARE. THIS REGION HAS
INCREASED ITS MAGNETIC COMPLEXITY SOMEWHAT SINCE YESTERDAY,
ALTHOUGH REGION EVOLUTION IS NOT PARTICULARLY RAPID. REGION
6827 (S21W31) WAS THE NEXT MOST ACTIVE REGION, PRODUCING TWO
C-CLASS SUBFLARES DURING THE PAST DAY. THE LARGEST WAS A C1/SF
AT 16/2120Z. REGION 6824 (S20W47) HAS DECAYED TO A SMALL BIPOLAR
SUNSPOT GROUP AND HAS NOT PRODUCED ANY SIGNIFICANT FLARES. A
LARGE HEDGE ROW PROMINENCE VISIBLE ON THE EAST LIMB AT SE30-
SE50 YESTERDAY, IS NO LONGER VISIBLE TODAY.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW, ALTHOUGH ANOTHER ISOLATED M-CLASS FLARE IS POSSIBLE IN
REGION 6832.
