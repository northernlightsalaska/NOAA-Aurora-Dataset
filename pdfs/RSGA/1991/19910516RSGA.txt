JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 136 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 16 MAY 1991
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 15/2100Z
TO 16/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH. REGION 6619 (N30W67)
PRODUCED AN M8/2B FLARE AT 16/0703Z. THE FLARE WAS ACCOMPANIED
BY MAJOR TYPES II AND IV RADIO SWEEPS AND A 5000 SFU TENFLARE.
THE REGION STABLIZED AFTER THE FLARE. REGION 6624 (S16W38)
DISPLAYED MINOR GROWTH AND PRODUCED OCCASIONAL SUBFLARES. NEW
REGION 6634 (N13W03) EMERGED EARLY IN THE PERIOD AND GREW AT A
MODERATE PACE. NEW REGION 6635 (S24E13) WAS ALSO NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE. REGION 6619 MAY PRODUCE M-CLASS ACTIVITY AS IT BEGINS
TO APPROACH THE LIMB. THIS REGION ALSO POSES A SLIGHT THREAT
FOR ADDITIONAL MAJOR FLARE ACTIVITY.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 15/2100Z TO 16/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO UNSETTLED LEVELS
FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS. A SUDDEN IMPULSE OF 44 NANOTESLA WAS
RECORDED BY THE BOULDER MAGNETOMETER AT 16/2040Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE MOSTLY UNSETTLED TOMORROW WITH ISOLATED PERIODS
OF ACTIVE LEVELS POSSIBLE IN THE WAKE OF THE SUDDEN IMPULSE.
ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO INCREASE TO ACTIVE LEVELS DURING THE
REMAINDER OF THE PERIOD IN RESPONSE TO THE MAJOR FLARE
MENTIONED ABOVE WITH PERIODS OF MINOR STORMING POSSIBLE AT
HIGH LATITUDES.
