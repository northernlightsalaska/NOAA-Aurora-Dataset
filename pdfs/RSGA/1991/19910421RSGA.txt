IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 20/2100Z
TO 21/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. REGION 6583 (N09W75)
SHOWED SIGNS OF DECAY AS IT APPROACHED THE LIMB. IT PRODUCED A
FEW SUBFLARES EARLY IN THE PERIOD. REGION 6593 (S12W19) PRODUCED

PROMINENCE SYSTEM WAS REPORTED NEAR N30W90 AT 21/0028Z. THIS
SYSTEM PROBABLY OCCURRED FROM OLD REGION 6580 WHICH CROSSED THE
LIMB EARLY YESTERDAY. NEW REGION 6601 (S20E13) WAS NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. REGIONS 6583, 6592 (N14W50), AND 6593 APPEAR
CAPABLE OF ISOLATED M-CLASS ACTIVITY.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 20/2100Z TO 21/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET LEVELS FOR THE PAST
24 HOURS. SOME HIGH LATITUDE STATIONS REPORTED UNSETTLED
CONDITIONS LATE IN THE PERIOD.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED DURING THE FIRST DAY. MINOR STORMING
IS EXPECTED DURING THE SECOND DAY DUE TO THE X1/3N FLARE OF
20 APRIL. THE FIELD IS EXPECTED TO DECLINE TO UNSETTLED LEVELS
ON THE FINAL DAY OF THE PERIOD.
