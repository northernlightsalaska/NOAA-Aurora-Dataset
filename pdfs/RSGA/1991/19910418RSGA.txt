JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 108 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 18 APR 1991
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 17/2100Z
TO 18/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW.  SEVERAL SUBFLARES
FROM A NUMBER OF DIFFERENT REGIONS WERE OBSERVED SINCE
YESTERDAY.  REGION 6593 (S10E21) HAS DECAYED SOMEWHAT DURING
THE PAST DAY.  A SMALL DELTA CONFIGURATION MAY STILL BE PRESENT
BUT PROBABLY WILL NOT LAST UNTIL TOMORROW.  REGION 6583 (N09W32)
HAS GROWN SINCE YESTERDAY AND ALSO MAY HAVE A SMALL DELTA
CONFIGURATION.  GROWTH IN REGION 6580 (N28W73) APPEARS TO BE
SLOWING.  GROWTH WAS ALSO NOTED IN REGION 6592 (N14W06).
NEW REGION 6599 (S13W11) WAS NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE.  AN ISOLATED M-CLASS FLARE IS POSSIBLE IN
REGIONS 6580, 6583, AND 6593.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 17/2100Z TO 18/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS QUIET TO UNSETTLED.  ACTIVE CONDITIONS
WERE OBSERVED AT SOME HIGH LATITUDE STATIONS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE QUIET TO UNSETTLED.
