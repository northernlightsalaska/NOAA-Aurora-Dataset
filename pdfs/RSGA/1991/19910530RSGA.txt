JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 150 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 30 MAY 1991
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 29/2100Z
TO 30/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH. REGION 6654 (N06E27)
PRODUCED TWO IMPULSIVE MAJOR FLARES TODAY, AN X1/2B TENFLARE
WITH A SIGNIFICANT TYPE IV RADIO SWEEP AT 29/2345Z AND AN
M8/1F TENFLARE AT 0941Z. SANDWICHED BETWEEN THE TWO MAJOR
FLARES REGION 6654 PRODUCED AN M1/SF AT 0344Z. REGION 6652
(S09E18) CONTRIBUTED AN M1/1B AT 1129Z. THESE TWO REGIONS
HAVE CHANGED LITTLE THE PAST 24 HOURS AND BOTH REMAIN POTENT.
REGION 6644 (N22W35), STILL THE LARGEST REGION ON THE DISK,
WAS MOSTLY STABLE, PRODUCING ONLY ISOLATED SUBFLARES.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO
CONTINUE AT MODERATE TO HIGH LEVELS. REGIONS 6652 AND 6654
ARE BY FAR THE MOST POTENT REGIONS. REGION 6644 MAY SUMMON
AN ISOLATED LOW LEVEL M-CLASS EVENT AS IT SLOWLY RE-
STRUCTURES.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 29/2100Z TO 30/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS UNSETTLED TO SLIGHTLY ACTIVE THE
PAST 24 HOURS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO FLUCTUATE BETWEEN QUIET AND MILDLY ACTIVE LEVELS
THROUGHOUT THE FORECAST PERIOD. A FLARE-INDUCED DISTURBANCE
IS NOT ANTICIPATED FROM THE ACTIVITY OF THE PAST FEW DAYS.
