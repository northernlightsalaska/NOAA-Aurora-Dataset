JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 232 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 20 AUG 1991
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 19/2100Z
TO 20/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE.  REGION 6784 (S20W36)
PRODUCED AN M1/2N FLARE AT 20/1221Z.  THIS FLARE WAS PRIMARILY
PARALLEL RIBBON WITH MULTIPLE CENTERS AND SOME OF THESE FLARE
CENTERS WERE LOCATED IN NEARBY REGION 6781 (S17W20).  SUNSPOTS
IN 6784 HAVE DECAYED SINCE YESTERDAY WHILE THOSE IN THE 6781/
6782 (S10W18) COMPLEX HAVE GROWN.  REGION 6786 (S09W02), ONE
OF THE MORE ACTIVE REGIONS YESTERDAY, HAS BEEN QUIET TODAY.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE.  REGIONS 6781, 6782, 6784, AND 6786 COULD
PRODUCE M-CLASS ACTIVITY.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 19/2100Z TO 20/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS MOSTLY UNSETTLED TO STORM.  MINOR TO
SEVERE STORM CONDITIONS WERE EXPERIENCED IN THE SEVEN HOURS
FOLLOWING A SUDDEN IMPULSE OBSERVED AT ABOUT 20/0802Z (30
NANOTESLA AT BOULDER).
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE FOR THE NEXT TWO DAYS AND
MOSTLY UNSETTLED THEREAFTER.
