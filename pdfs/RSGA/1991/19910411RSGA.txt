JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 101 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 11 APR 1991
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 10/2100Z
TO 11/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH. AN IMPULSIVE M9 XRAY
FLARE WAS OBSERVED AT 1117Z WHICH WAS ACCOMPANIED BY MODERATE
TO STRONG RADIO BURSTS. THE EVENT WAS NOT OBSERVED OPTICALLY,
ALTHOUGH ONE SITE OBTAINED SEEING AFTER THE EVENT AND NOTED
THAT REGION 6583 (N09E60) HAD A BRIGHT SURGE IN PROGRESS,
SUGGESTING IT AS A LIKELY CANDIDATE. THE GROUP SHOWED GROWTH
DURING THE DAY AND PRODUCED ADDITIONAL M-FLARES: AN M4/1B AT
0618Z AND AN M2/1N AT 0843Z. REGION 6580 (N29E16) ALSO PRODUCED
AN M-FLARE: AN M1/SF AT 0315Z. THIS GROUP IS ALSO SHOWING
STEADY GROWTH. REGION 6566 (S21W49) IS ALSO GROWING AND
PRODUCED AN M1/SN AT 0342Z. SURGING WAS OBSERVED ON EAST
LIMB TODAY AT N13.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE. THE PRINCIPAL THREATS FOR M-CLASS FLARES ARE REGIONS
6566, 6580, AND 6583. OLD REGION 6555 (S23, L-186), WHICH PRO-
DUCED 7 X-CLASS FLARES AND 29 M-CLASS FLARES ON ITS PREVIOUS
TRANSIT IS DUE TO RETURN ON THE SECOND DAY AND MAY INCREASE
ACTIVITY AND BACKGROUND LEVELS OVER THE NEXT FEW DAYS.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 10/2100Z TO 11/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS QUIET TO UNSETTLED.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE QUIET TO UNSETTLED TOMORROW, AND IS EXPECTED
TO BE GENERALLY UNSETTLED ON THE SECOND AND THIRD DAYS.
