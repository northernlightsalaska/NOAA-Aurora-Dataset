JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 045 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 14 FEB 1991
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 13/2100Z
TO 14/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. AFTER SEVERAL DAYS
OF VERY ISOLATED SUBFLARING, REGION 6487 (N14W51) BECAME
ACTIVE AGAIN PRODUCING AN M3/2B FLARE AT 14/1237Z. NO SWEEPS
AND ONLY LIGHT RADIO BURST EMISSIONS WERE DETECTED WITH THIS
EVENT. A SMALL SUBFLARE ERUPTED IN REGION 6484 (N12W66) IN
6ONJUNCTION WITH 6487'S FLARE. REGION 6492 (S12W52) DISPLAYED
SLIGHT GROWTH WHILE EMITTING OCCASIONAL C-CLASS FLARES. EVENT-
LEVEL BRIGHT SURGING WAS NOTED AT 14/1413Z FROM REGION 6481
(S23 L-050) WHICH HAS SINCE ROUNDED THE WEST LIMB. THE LARGE
FILAMENT IN THE SOUTHWEST QUADRANT OF THE DISK IS SLOWLY
BECOMING MORE DIFFUSE IN APPEARANCE AND EXPANDING IN WIDTH
ALONG ITS EASTERN HALF. NEW REGIONS 6502 (S13W30) AND 6503
(S18W24) WERE ASSIGNED TODAY.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
GENERALLY LOW WITH ONLY AN ISOLATED M-FLARE POSSIBLE FROM THE
6484/6487 COMPLEX. ELEVATED LEVELS OF ACTIVITY ARE EXPECTED
TOWARDS THE END OF THE PERIOD AS SEVERAL HIGHLY ACTIVE REGIONS,
OLD 6466 (S09 L-198) AND 6462 (S18 L-203), ARE DUE TO RETURN
EARLY ON 17 FEBRUARY.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 13/2100Z TO 14/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT MOSTLY QUIET LEVELS
FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE QUIET TO UNSETTLED.
