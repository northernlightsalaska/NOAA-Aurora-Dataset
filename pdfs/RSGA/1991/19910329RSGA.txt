IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 28/2100Z
TO 29/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH. REGION 6555 (S22W72)
PRODUCED AN X2/3B FLARE AT 29/0651Z. THE FLARE WAS ACCOMPANIED
BY A 3000 SFU TENFLARE AND A TYPE II SWEEP BURST (EST. SHOCK
VELOCITY 1600 KM/SEC). THE REGION CONTINUED TO GRADUALLY DECAY
AND MAY HAVE LOST ITS MAGNETIC DELTA CONFIGURATION IN THE
AFTERMATH OF THE X2 FLARE. IT PRODUCED FOUR ADDITIONAL FLARES
DURING THE PERIOD THE LARGEST OF WHICH WAS A C3/SF AT 28/2144Z.
AN OPTICALLY UNCORRELATED M1 X-RAY BURST OCCURRED AT 29/1047Z.
NEW REGION 6564 (N20E22) WAS NUMBERED TODAY.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE. REGION 6555 IS LIKELY TO PRODUCE ISOLATED M-CLASS
FLARES AS IT CONTINUES TO DECAY. THERE IS A SLIGHT CHANCE FOR
AN X-CLASS FLARE FROM THIS REGION AS WELL.

ENDED AT 28/2300Z.

EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED DURING THE FIRST TWO DAYS FOLLOWED BY
