JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 118 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 28 APR 1991
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 27/2100Z
TO 28/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. THE MAJORITY OF THE
DAY'S ACTIVITY CAME FROM REGION 6603 (S29W64). NUMEROUS C-CLASS
EVENTS AND AN M3/1N FLARE AT 28/0753Z WERE ATTRIBUTED TO THIS
AREA WHICH HAS EXHIBITED SLIGHT GROWTH. OCCASIONAL SUBFLARING
WAS OBSERVED FROM NEWLY ASSIGNED REGION 6612 (S27W09) WHICH
HAS GROWN RAPIDLY SINCE ITS EMERGENCE ON THE 27TH. OTHER
ACTIVITY OF NOTE INCLUDES AN ERUPTIVE PROMINENCE AT 28/1115Z
FROM THE SOUTHEAST LIMB AND THE DISAPPEARANCE OF A 12 DEGREE
SEGMENT OF FILAMENT FROM S23E10 BETWEEN 27/2247-28/1215Z. NEW
REGIONS ASSIGNED INCLUDE 6613 (S14E13), 6614 (S16E27), AND 6612
NOTED ABOVE.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. REGION 6603 OR 6612 MAY PRODUCE AN ISOLATED
M-CLASS FLARE.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 27/2100Z TO 28/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT UNSETTLED TO MINOR STORM
LEVELS. ACTIVITY EARLY IN THE PERIOD APPEARS ASSOCIATED WITH
SUBSTORMING WHILE EFFECTS FROM A CORONAL HOLE MAY HAVE BEGAN
AFFECTING THE FIELD LATER IN THE DAY.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE ACTIVE WITH PERIODS OF STORM CONDITIONS AT HIGH
LATITUDES FOR THE NEXT TWO DAY'S. UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE LEVELS
ARE EXPECTED BY THE END OF THE PERIOD.
