JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 339 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 05 DEC 1991
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 04/2100Z
TO 05/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 6952 (N17E42)
CONTINUED TO BE THE DOMINANT REGION ON THE SUN BOTH IN TERMS OF
SIZE AND FLARE PRODUCTION. IT PRODUCED AN M2/1B AT 05/0245Z. THE
REGION GREW GRADUALLY THROUGH THE PERIOD AND RETAINED A DELTA
MAGNETIC STRUCTURE IN ITS LARGE TRAILER PORTION. REGION 6946
(S09W31) PRODUCED AN M2/SF AT 05/0741Z. SLIGHT GROWTH OCCURRED
IN THIS REGION. AN M1 X-RAY BURST OCCURRED AT 05/1155Z AND WAS
ASSOCIATED WITH AN ERUPTIVE PROMINENCE NEAR N26E90 WHICH REACHED
A VERTICAL EXTENT OF 0.36 SOLAR RADII. NEW REGIONS 6958 (S18E58)
6959 (S19W22), AND 6960 (S09E66) WERE NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE TO HIGH. REGION 6952 IS EXPECTED TO PRODUCE M-CLASS
FLARES AND MAY ALSO PRODUCE A MAJOR FLARE.

IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
