JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 347 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 13 DEC 1991
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 12/2100Z
TO 13/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 6952 (N18W63)
PRODUCED THE ONLY M-CLASS EVENT OF THE DAY, THAT AN M2/2B
TENFLARE AT 0357Z. THE REGION HAS BEEN QUIET AND STABLE SINCE.
FIVE OF THE NINE SPOTTED REGIONS NOW VISIBLE HAD SOME FLARE
ACTIVITY, BUT THE OVERALL LEVEL OF ACTIVITY DECLINED FROM THAT
OF THE PAST FEW DAYS.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE. REGIONS 6958 (S18W49) AND 6961 (N09W15) ARE BOTH
CAPABLE OF M-CLASS ACTIVITY AT SOME POINT IN THE NEXT THREE
DAYS.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 12/2100Z TO 13/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT PREDOMINANTLY QUIET TO
UNSETTLED LEVELS. EPISODES OF ACTIVE CONDITIONS, THOUGHT TO
BE DUE TO CORONAL HOLE EFFECTS, WERE OBSERVED THE LATTER
PART OF THE INTERVAL.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE. OCCASIONAL MINOR STORM
CONDITIONS MAY OCCUR ON 16 DECEMBER WHEN ANOTHER CORONAL HOLE
MOVES INTO A WELL-CONNECTED POSITION.
