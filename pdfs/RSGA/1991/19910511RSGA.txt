JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 131 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 11 MAY 1991
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 10/2100Z
TO 11/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE.  REGION 6615 (S08W85)
PRODUCED THREE SMALL M-CLASS FLARES, AN M1/SF AT 11/0814Z,
AN M3/SN AT 11/1325Z, AND AN M1/SF AT 11/1523Z.  THIS REGION
REMAINS LARGE AND APPEARS TO STILL HAVE A DELTA CONFIGURATION.
REGION 6619 (N29W03) PRODUCED A LONG DURATION M1/SN BEGINNING
AT ABOUT 11/1950Z.  THIS FLARE IS ASSOCIATED WITH A 2695 MHZ
BURST OF 20000 SFU AND REMAINS IN PROGRESS AT THIS TIME.
REGION 6621 (N11E05) PRODUCED A FEW SUBFLARES BUT NONE OF THEM
HAD SIGNIFICANT X-RAY OUTPUT.  REGION 6624 (S17E30) IS EMERGING
RAPIDLY.  NEW REGIONS NUMBERED INCLUDE 6628 (N08W73), 6629
(S16W49), AND 6630 (S11E69).
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE.  REGION 6615 MAY PRODUCE ANOTHER M-CLASS FLARE BEFORE
IT ROTATES BEYOND THE WEST LIMB.  ADDITIONAL M-CLASS FLARES
ARE EXPECTED IN REGION 6619.
