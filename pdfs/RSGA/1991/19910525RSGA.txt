JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 145 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 25 MAY 1991
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 24/2100Z
TO 25/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. REGION 6644 (N20E29)
CONTINUED TO DISPLAY SIGNIFICANT GROWTH AND INCREASED COMPLEXITY
WHILE CONTRIBUTING THE MAJORITY OF THE FLARE ACTIVITY ON THE
DISK. SEVERAL C-CLASS EVENTS WERE ATTRIBUTED TO THIS AREA TODAY.
REGIONS 6646 (S14W57) AND 6647 (N17W20) ALSO SHOWED NOTABLE
GROWTH. REGION 6648 (S13E58) HAS NOW ROTATED COMPLETELY INTO
VIEW AND HAS SINCE BEEN SPLIT INTO TWO AREAS WITH THE TRAILING
PORTION RETAINING THE 6648 DESIGNATION AND THE LEADING, SIMPLE
SPOT AREA NUMBERED AS NEW REGION 6649 (S09E50). AN ERUPTIVE
PROMINENCE WAS OBSERVED ON THE SOUTHWEST LIMB AT 1734Z. A 15
DEGREE LONG SEGMENT OF FILAMENT, PREVIOUSLY ASSOCIATED WITH
REGION 6638 (N17W62) WAS NOTED TO HAVE DISAPPEARED BETWEEN
24/1300Z-25/0526Z.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW WITH THE TREND TOWARDS MODERATE LEVELS AS REGION 6644
CONTINUES TO MATURE.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 24/2100Z TO 25/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT UNSETTLED TO MINOR STORM
LEVELS AT MIDDLE LATITUDES WITH PERIODS OF MAJOR STORMING
AT HIGH LATITUDE SITES BETWEEN 0600-1200Z IN RESPONSE TO
EFFECTS FROM A CORONAL HOLE.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE MOSTLY ACTIVE WITH ISOLATED PERIODS OF STORM
CONDITIONS.
