JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 032 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 01 FEB 1991
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 31/2100Z
TO 01/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. THREE M-CLASS FLARES
WERE OBSERVED DURING THIS REPORTING PERIOD. REGION 6469 (S13W52)
PRODUCED AN M1/SF AT 0614Z. TWO ADDITIONAL M1 FLARES OCCURRED AT
1147Z AND 1303Z BUT HAD NO OPTICAL CORRELATION. REGIONS 6462
(S17W69), 6466 (S09W67), 6469 AND 6471 (S12W11) DISPLAY BETA-
GAMMA-DELTA MAGNETIC CONFIGURATIONS. REGION 6471 ALSO SHOWS
STRONG MAGNETIC SHEAR IN ITS CENTRAL PORTION. NEW REGION 6480
(S09E71) WAS ADDED TODAY.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
HIGH. REGIONS 6462, 6466, 6469 AND 6471 HAVE THE POTENTIAL TO
PRODUCE BOTH M AND X-CLASS FLARES.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 31/2100Z TO 01/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT MOSTLY UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE
CONDITIONS WITH SEVERAL PERIODS AT MINOR STORM LEVELS. THE
PROTON EVENT THAT BEGAN ON JAN 31 AT 1130Z, MAXING AT 240 PFU
ON JAN 31 AT 1620Z ENDED ON 01 FEB AT 1540Z. PCA EVENT THAT
BEGAN ON 31 JAN AT 1300Z ENDED ON 01 FEB AT 1300Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT MAJOR TO MINOR STORM LEVELS THE FIRST DAY
WITH THE SECOND AND THIRD DAYS BEING MOSTLY AT UNSETTLED TO
ACTIVE LEVELS.
