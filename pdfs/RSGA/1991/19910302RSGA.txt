JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 061 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 02 MAR 1991
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 01/2100Z
TO 02/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. A LONG DURATION,
OPTICALLY UNCORRELATED X-RAY EVENT OCCURRED AT 02/1457Z. ITS
VERY GRADUAL RISE AND DECLINE WAS CHARACTERISTIC OF LIMB EVENTS,
BUT NO ACTIVITY WAS OBSERVED FROM EITHER LIMB. THE EVENT WAS
ACCOMPANIED BY A 850 SFU TENFLARE AND A TYPE II SWEEP. REGION
6509 (S22W82) PRODUCED THREE C-CLASS FLARES THE LARGEST OF WHICH
WAS A C7/1N AT 02/0130Z. REGION 6508 (S14W80) PRODUCED
SYMPATHETIC FLARES IN RESPONSE TO THE ACTIVITY IN 6509, BUT THIS
WAS NOT SURPRISING INASMUCH AS THEY ARE IN CLOSE PROXIMITY AND
SHARE A COMMON NEUTRAL LINE. NEW REGIONS 6528 (S11W59), 6529
(N10W14), AND 6530 (N11E73) WERE ASSIGNED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. THERE IS A SLIGHT CHANCE FOR AN M-CLASS FLARE
FROM THE 6508/6509 COMPLEX.
