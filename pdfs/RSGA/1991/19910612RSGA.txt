JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 163 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 12 JUN 1991
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 11/2100Z
TO 12/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH. REGION 6659 (N31W43)
PRODUCED A LONG DURATION M5/1B FLARE AT 11/2132Z. A LOOP
PROMINENCE SYSTEM WAS ASSOCIATED WITH THE FLARE. THIS REGION
ALSO PRODUCED AN M1/2B FLARE AT 12/1759Z ALONG WITH SEVERAL
C-CLASS FLARES. AN OPTICALLY UNCORRELATED M2 OCCURRED AT
12/0712Z. REGION 6666 (S15W40) DISPLAYED GRADUAL GROWTH AND
PRODUCED ISOLATED SUBFLARES. NEW REGIONS 6674 (S01W61), 6675
(S14W06), AND 6676 (S06E16) WERE NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
HIGH. REGION 6659 IS EXPECTED TO PRODUCE ISOLATED MAJOR FLARE
ACTIVITY. REGION 6666 MAY PRODUCE AN ISOLATED M-CLASS FLARE.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 11/2100Z TO 12/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT MOSTLY MINOR STORM LEVELS
WITH PERIODS OF MAJOR TO SEVERE STORMING NOTED AT HIGH
LATITUDES. A SUDDEN IMPULSE OF 33 NANOTESLA OCCURRED AT
BOULDER AT 12/1013Z. THE GREATER THAN 10 MEV PROTON EVENT
AND ASSOCIATED POLAR CAP ABSORPTION EVENT CONTINUED THROUGH
THE PERIOD.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT MAJOR TO SEVERE STORM LEVELS ON THE FIRST
DAY. THE FIELD IS EXPECTED TO GRADUALLY DECLINE TO ACTIVE TO
MAJOR STORM LEVELS ON THE SECOND DAY. A CONTINUING DECLINE TO
ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM LEVELS IS EXPECTED ON THE FINAL DAY. THE
PROTON EVENT IS EXPECTED TO CONTINUE.
