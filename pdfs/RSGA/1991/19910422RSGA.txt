JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 112 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 22 APR 1991
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 21/2100Z
TO 22/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. TWO OPTICALLY UNCORRELATED
C-CLASS X-RAY BURSTS OCCURRED. AN ERUPTIVE PROMINENCE REACHING
0.24 RV OCCURRED NEAR S25W90 AT 22/0712Z. REGION 6593 (S10W31)
PRODUCED FOUR SUBFLARES AS IT DISPLAYED GRADUAL DECAY. REGION
6583 (N08W89) WHICH PRODUCED THE X1/3N FLARE OF 20 APRIL WAS
QUIETLY CROSSING THE LIMB AT THE END OF THE PERIOD. NO NEW
REGIONS WERE ASSIGNED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. THERE IS A SLIGHT CHANCE FOR AN ISOLATED
M-CLASS SUBFLARE FROM REGIONS 6583 OR 6593.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 21/2100Z TO 22/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT MOSTLY QUIET LEVELS FOR THE
PAST 24 HOURS. ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM LEVELS OCCURRED AT HIGH
LATITUDES LATE IN THE PERIOD. A GREATER THAN 10 MEV PROTON
ENHANCEMENT BEGAN NEAR 22/1000Z AND MAY HAVE ORIGINATED FROM
OLD REGION 6580 WHICH CROSSED THE WEST LIMB TWO DAYS AGO.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT MINOR STORM LEVELS DURING THE FIRST DAY DUE
TO THE X1/3N FLARE OF 20 APRIL. THEREAFTER, THE FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO DECLINE TO UNSETTLED LEVELS. THE PROTON
ENHANCEMENT AT GREATER THAN 10 MEV IS NOT EXPECTED TO REACH
EVENT LEVELS.
