JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 143 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 23 MAY 1991
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 22/2100Z
TO 23/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. FIVE UNCORRELATED LOW
LEVEL C-CLASS EVENTS STARTED OFF THE PERIOD. REGION 6644
(N21E53) CONTRIBUTED SEVERAL C-CLASS SUBFLARES TODAY WHILE
SHOWING SOME GROWTH AND AN INCREASE IN COMPLEXITY. SLIGHT
GROWTH WAS ALSO NOTED IN REGION 6645 (N05E17) AND 6646 (S15W29).
WEAK SURGING WAS OBSERVED ON THE SOUTHEAST LIMB. NEW REGION
6647 (N18E07) WAS ASSIGNED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW. CONTINUED GROWTH OF REGION 6644 HOWEVER AND THE RETURN OF
OLD REGION 6615 (S08, L-350) MAY BOOST ACTIVITY LEVELS BY THE
END OF THE PERIOD.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 22/2100Z TO 23/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE WITH
PERIODS OF STORMING AT HIGH LATITUDES. SATELLITE LEVEL
PROTONS AT GREATER THAN 10MEV CONTINUED TO REMAIN ENHANCED
ABOVE BACKGROUND LEVELS THROUGHOUT THE PERIOD.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE GENERALLY ACTIVE WITH PERIODS OF MINOR
STORMING AT HIGHER LATITUDES IN RESPONSE TO EFFECTS FROM
RECURRENT CORONAL HOLES.
