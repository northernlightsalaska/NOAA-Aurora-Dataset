JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 142 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 22 MAY 1991
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 21/2100Z
TO 22/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. NEW REGION 6644 (N20E64)
CONTINUED TO PRODUCE OCCASIONAL FLARING WITH A C3/1N EVENT AT
0555Z NOTED AS ITS LARGEST. THIS REGIONS COMPLETE SPOT GROUP HAS
NOW ROTATED INTO VIEW AND APPEARS ONLY MODEST IN SIZE. REGION
6638 (N18W22) HAS SHOWN ONLY SLIGHT GROWTH WITH SEVERAL SMALL
C-CLASS EVENTS TO ITS CREDIT. A C6/2N FLARE ERUPTED IN REGION
6637 (S26W04) AT 0029Z. THE FLARE EXHIBITED PARALLEL RIBBONS
AND OCCURED IN A REGION THAT WAS VOID OF SPOTS AT THE TIME. A
SIMPLE SPOT AREA HAS SINCE FORMED IN THIS REGION. NEW REGIONS
ASSIGNED INCLUDE 6645 (N05E29), 6646 (S14W16), AND 6644 NOTED
ABOVE.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW BUT WITH AN INCREASING CHANCE FOR AN ISOLATED M-CLASS FLARE.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 21/2100Z TO 22/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS. A SUDDEN IMPULSE, MEASURING
43 NANOTESLAS AT BOULDER, WAS OBSERVED AT 22/0018Z. ISOLATED
PERIODS OF ACTIVE CONDITIONS FOLLOWED THIS EVENT WHICH MAY
HAVE BEEN DUE TO EFFECTS FROM A MODEST SIZED FILAMENT THAT
DISAPPEARED LATE ON THE 18TH. SATELLITE LEVEL PROTONS AT
GREATER THAN 10 MEV REMAINED SLIGHTLY ENHANCED ABOVE
BACKGROUND LEVELS THROUGHOUT THE PERIOD.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED WITH PERIODS OF ACTIVE CONDITIONS.
