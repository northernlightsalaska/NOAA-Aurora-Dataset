JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 107 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 17 APR 1991
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 16/2100Z
TO 17/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. THERE WERE FOUR LOW
LEVEL M-CLASS EVENTS EARLY IN THE DAY. ONE, AN M1 AT 0028Z WAS
UNCORRELATED, THE OTHER THREE EVENTS ORIGINATED IN REGION 6593
(S08E32). THE LARGEST OF THESE WAS A LONG DURATION M2/1N AT
0700Z. REGIONS 6583 (N09W18) AND 6593 HAVE SHOWN SOME DECAY THE
LAST 24 HOURS AND 6583 MAY HAVE LOST IT'S DELTA CONFIGURATION.
REGION 6580 (N29W49) HAS SHOWN SUBSTANTIAL GROWTH AND APPEARS TO
HAVE DEVELOPED A DELTA. THIS REGION PRODUCED NUMEROUS SUBFLARES
TODAY. A BRIGHT SURGE ON THE LIMB TO .22 SOLAR RADII, WAS
OBSERVED AT S22W90 BETWEEN 0311 AND 0537Z. NEW REGION 6598
(S08E16) WAS ASSIGNED TODAY.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE. REGIONS 6580, 6583 AND 6593 CONTINUE TO HAVE POTENTIAL
FOR M-CLASS ACTIVITY AND A POSSIBLE ISOLATED MAJOR FLARE.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 16/2100Z TO 17/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN QUIET TO UNSETTLED AT
MIDDLE LATITUDES. HIGH LATITUDE REPORTED ACTIVE CONDITIONS
BETWEEN 1200 - 1800Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE MOSTLY UNSETTLED. PERIODS OF ACTIVE CONDITIONS
ARE POSSIBLE THE NEXT 24 - 36 HOURS IN RESPONSE TO THE
FAVORABLY POSITIONED CORONAL HOLE.
