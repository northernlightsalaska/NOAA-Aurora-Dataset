JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 353 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 19 DEC 1991
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 18/2100Z
TO 19/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 6972 (S09E21)
PRODUCED TODAY'S ONLY M-CLASS FLARE: AN M1/1B AT 0802Z WHICH WAS
ACCOMPANIED BY A MODERATE TYPE II SWEEP AND FAIRLY STRONG DI-
SCRETE RADIO BURSTS. NO SIGNIFICANT CHANGES WERE OBSERVED IN THE
REGION DURING THE PAST 24 HOURS. NEW REGION 6982 (S14E67) RO-
TATED INTO VIEW AS A MATURE D-TYPE GROUP BUT DIDN'T PRODUCE
ANY FLARES. THE REMAINDER OF THE SPOTTED GROUPS ON THE DISK
WERE QUIET AND STABLE.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. REGION 6972 APPEARS TO BE THE MOST LIKELY
SOURCE FOR FLARES. REGIONS 6966, 6978, OR 6982 MIGHT ALSO MAKE
SMALL CONTRIBUTIONS TO ACTIVITY.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 18/2100Z TO 19/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS QUIET TO UNSETTLED AT MID-LATITUDES
AND WAS UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE AT HIGH LATITUDES.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE ACTIVE FOR THE NEXT TWO DAYS IN RESPONSE TO
A FAVORABLY POSITIONED CORONAL HOLE. LEVELS SHOULD DECLINE
TO MOSTLY UNSETTLED BY THE THIRD DAY.
