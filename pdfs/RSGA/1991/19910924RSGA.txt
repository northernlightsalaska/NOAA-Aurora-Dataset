JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 267 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 24 SEP 1991
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 23/2100Z
TO 24/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH. AN M6/1N FLARE ERUPTED
FROM REGION 6848 (S07E63) AT 24/0756Z. A WEEK TYPE II SWEEP
AND BRIGHT LIMB SURGING ACCOMPANIED THIS ACTIVITY. THE REGION
APPEARS SIMPLE AT THIS TIME. NEW REGION 6850 (S12E81), CURRENTLY
ROTATING INTO VIEW AND TRAILING 6848, MAY BE RETURNING OLD
REGION 6818 (S12, L-182), A COMPLEX AREA WHICH PRODUCED
NUMEROUS ENERGETIC EVENTS DURING ITS LAST TRANSIT. REGION 6841
(S13W76) CONTRIBUTED OCCASIONAL SUBFLARING WHILE SIMPLIFYING
IN STRUCTURE. NEW REGION 6849 (N11W12) WAS ALSO ASSIGNED TODAY.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. ISOLATED M-CLASS FLARING IS POSSIBLE FROM
REGION 6848 OR NEW REGION 6850, CURRENTLY EMERGING IN ITS WAKE.

THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET LEVELS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BECOME UNSETTLED WITH THE TREND TOWARDS ACTIVE
CONDITIONS ON THE 26TH DUE TO EFFECTS FROM A CORONAL HOLE.
