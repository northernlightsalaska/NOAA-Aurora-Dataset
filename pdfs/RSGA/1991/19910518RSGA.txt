JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 138 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 18 MAY 1991
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 17/2100Z
TO 18/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH. REGION 6619 (N30W93)
PRODUCED AN X2/2B AT 18/0545Z WHILE CROSSING THE WEST LIMB.
MINOR TYPES II AND IV SWEEPS, A 5600 SFU TENFLARE, AN ERUPTIVE
PROMINENCE, AND A LOOP PROMINENCE SYSTEM WERE ASSOCIATED WITH
THE FLARE. REGION 6624 (S16W62) PRODUCED A FEW FLARES DURING
THE FIRST HALF OF THE PERIOD INCLUDING A C6/1F AT 18/0119Z. IT
STABLIZED DURING THE SECOND HALF OF THE PERIOD. REGION 6633
(S13E24) PRODUCED SUBFLARES THROUGHOUT THE PERIOD WHILE IN A
PHASE OF GRADUAL GROWTH. NEW REGIONS 6638 (N18E33) AND 6639
(S28E66) WERE ASSIGNED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE. REGION 6619 MAY PRODUCE AN M-CLASS FLARE FROM BEHIND
THE LIMB. AN ISOLATED M-CLASS FLARE IS POSSIBLE FROM REGION
6633.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 17/2100Z TO 18/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET LEVELS FOR THE PAST
24 HOURS. A SLIGHT ENHANCEMENT IN THE GREATER THAN 10 MEV
PROTON FLUX BEGAN NEAR 18/1500Z IN THE WAKE OF THE X2/2B
FLARE.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE ACTIVE DURING THE FIRST TWO DAYS WITH PERIODS
OF MINOR STORMING POSSIBLE AT HIGH LATITUDES DUE TO RECENT
MAJOR FLARE ACTIVITY. UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE CONDITIONS ARE
EXPECTED DURING THE FINAL DAY AS FLARE EFFECTS SUBSIDE.
