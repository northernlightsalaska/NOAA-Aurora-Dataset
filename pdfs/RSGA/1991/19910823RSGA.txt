SDF NUMBER 235 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 23 AUG 1991
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 22/2100Z
TO 23/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE.  REGION 6786 (S09W45)
PRODUCED AN M2/1N FLARE AT 22/2231Z.  THIS REGION CONTINUES TO
DECAY SLOWLY.  REGION 6781 (S18W59) CONTINUES TO DEVELOP IN
WHITE LIGHT AREA AND MAGNETIC COMPLEXITY.  IT IS THE LARGEST
REGION ON THE DISK AT WELL OVER A THOUSAND MILLIONTHS BUT HAS
NOT YET PRODUCED MAJOR FLARE ACTIVITY.  REGIONS 6782 (S09W58)
AND 6784 (S20W77) ARE DECAYING AND HAVE ONLY PRODUCED A FEW
SMALL SUBFLARES SINCE YESTERDAY.  NEW REGIONS 6804 (N05W19),
6805 (N21E81), AND 6806 (N16E52) WERE NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE.  M-CLASS ACTIVITY IS POSSIBLE IN REGIONS
6782, 6784, AND 6786.  REGION 6781 MAY PRODUCE A MAJOR FLARE.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 22/2100Z TO 23/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS AT UNSETTLED TO STORM LEVELS UNTIL
ABOUT 23/1200Z.  AFTERWARDS THE FIELD WAS QUIET TO UNSETTLED.
THE DISTURBANCE OF THE PAST FEW DAYS APPEARS TO BE ENDING
ALTHOUGH ACTIVE TO STORM CONDITIONS ARE POSSIBLE DURING LOCAL
NIGHTIME.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE QUIET TO UNSETTLED AT MIDDLE LATITUDES AND
UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE AT HIGH LATITUDES.
