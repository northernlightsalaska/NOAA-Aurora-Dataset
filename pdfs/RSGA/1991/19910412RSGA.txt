JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 102 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 12 APR 1991
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 11/2100Z
TO 12/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 6566 PRODUCED
3 M-CLASS FLARES TODAY: AN M1/SF AT 0046Z, AN M1/1B AT 0421Z,
AND AN M1/2N AT 0455Z. THE GROUP SHOWED STEADY GROWTH DURING
THE PERIOD. AN ADDITIONAL M-CLASS FLARE WAS PRODUCED BY 6578
(S19W19): AN M1/2N AT 0337Z. PERHAPS MOST REMARKABLE OF TODAY'S
TRENDS WAS GROWTH ACROSS THE DISK IN SEVERAL REGIONS AND THE
EMERGENCE OF 5 NEW SPOTTED GROUPS. SPECIFIC REGIONS WHICH ARE
SHOWING MATURE CONFIGURATIONS ARE 6570 (S11W46), 6580 (N29E03),
AND 6583 (N09E46). THESE GROUPS PRODUCED ONLY SUBFLARE ACTIVITY
DURING THE PERIOD. SURGING WAS REPORTED ON THE EAST LIMB AT S13.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE. THE MAIN THREATS FOR M-CLASS EVENTS APPEAR TO BE
REGIONS 6566, 6580, AND 6583. IN ADDITION, OLD REGION 6555
(S23, L-186), IS DUE TO RETURN TOMORROW AND MAY ALSO CONTRIBUTE
TO ACTIVITY LEVELS. THERE IS A SLIGHT CHANCE FOR AN ISOLATED
MAJOR FLARE.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 11/2100Z TO 12/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS MOSTLY QUIET TO UNSETTLED DURING
THE PERIOD. SOME ISOLATED ACTIVE PERIODS WERE OBSERVED AT
HIGH LATITUDES.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE GENERALLY UNSETTLED FOR THE NEXT THREE DAYS.
