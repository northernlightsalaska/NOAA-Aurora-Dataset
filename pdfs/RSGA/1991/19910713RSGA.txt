JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 194 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 13 JUL 1991
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 12/2100Z
TO 13/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. AN M4 X-RAY EVENT
WAS OBSERVED AT 13/1851Z. A 400 SFU 10CM BURST ACCOMPANIED
THIS ACTIVITY WHICH APPEARS TO HAVE EMANATED FROM THE EAST
LIMB FROM NEW REGION 6729 (S20E90). A LOOP PROMINENCE SYSTEM
WAS OBSERVED IN THIS AREA SHORTLY AFTER THE EVENT MAXIMUM.
REGION 6718 (S24W12) CONTINUED TO GROW WHILE PRODUCING ISOLATED
C-CLASS SUBFLARES. REGION 6722 (S14E13) ALSO DISPLAYED GROWTH
WHILE SPAWNING OCASSIONAL C-CLASS EVENTS. ISOLATED SUBFLARING
WAS OBSERVED FROM DEPARTING REGION 6703 (N28W88) WHICH HAS
BEEN VOID OF SPOTS FOR SEVERAL DAYS NOW. A 10 DEGREE CIRCULAR
RIBBON OF FILAMENT LOCATED NEAR S25 E60 DISAPPEARED BETWEEN
12/1734Z-13/0111Z. NEW REGION 6729 WAS ASSIGNED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE. AN ISOLATED M-CLASS FLARE IS POSSIBLE FROM REGION
6718, DEPARTING REGION 6703, OR NEWLY EMERGING REGION 6729.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 12/2100Z TO 13/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT UNSETTLED TO MAJOR STORM
LEVELS WITH PERIODS OF SEVERE STORMING OBSERVED AT HIGH
LATITUDES. STORM CONDITIONS BEGAN NEAR 13/0600Z AND ARE
LIKELY DUE TO EFFECTS FROM THE M7/3B FLARE NOTED ON THE 11TH.
MAGNETOPAUSE CROSSINGS WERE OBSERVED ON THE GOES 7 MAGNETOMETER
BETWEEN 13/1621-1638Z AND 1730-1740Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT STORM LEVELS FOR THE NEXT 24 HOURS WITH THE
FIELD BECOMING UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE BY THE 15TH AS FLARE EFFECTS
SUBSIDE.
