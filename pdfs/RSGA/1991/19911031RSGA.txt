JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 304 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 31 OCT 1991
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 30/2100Z
TO 31/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. AN UNCORRELATED M1
FLARE WAS OBSERVED AT 0912Z. THE REMAINDER OF TODAY'S ACTIVITY
CONSISTED OF A HANDFUL OF MID TO UPPER LEVEL C-CLASS EVENTS,
MOSTLY FROM REGION 6891 (S12W43), ALTHOUGH REGIONS 6892 (S19W44)
AND 6893 (N17W28) ALSO MADE SOME CONTRIBUTIONS. REGION 6891
CONTINUES TO BE THE MOST IMPRESSIVE GROUP ON THE DISK BUT
IT SHOWED CONTINUING DECAY AND FRAGMENTATION DURING THE PERIOD.
REGION 6893 HAS SHOWN STEADY GROWTH OVER THE LAST TWO DAYS.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MOSTLY MODERATE. REGIONS 6891 AND 6892 ARE EXPECTED TO BE THE
MAIN CENTERS FOR FLARE ACTIVITY, ALTHOUGH REGION 6893 MAY ALSO
MAKE A CONTRIBUTION. THERE CONTINUES TO BE A THREAT FOR AN
ISOLATED MAJOR FLARE FROM REGION 6891.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 30/2100Z TO 31/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM LEVELS
WITH AN ISOLATED PERIOD OF MAJOR TO SEVERE STORM LEVELS AT
HIGH LATITUDES BETWEEN 0600-1200UT. A SUDDEN IMPULSE WAS
OBSERVED IN BOULDER AT 1655UT OF 15 GAMMA. THE MOST PROBABLE
SOURCE FOR THE IMPULSE WAS YESTERDAY'S X2 SOLAR FLARE. THE
PROTON EVENT WHICH BEGAN AT 30/0745Z SHOWED STEADY DECAY TO-
DAY. A BRIEF ENHANCEMENT IN PROTON FLUX LEVELS WAS OBSERVED
AT THE TIME OF THE IMPULSE, BUT LEVELS PROMPTLY DROPPED BELOW
THRESHOLD THEREAFTER AT 1715UT.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT MINOR STORM LEVELS TOMORROW DUE TO THE X2
FLARE OF 30 OCTOBER. CONDITIONS ARE EXPECTED TO GRADUALLY
SUBSIDE TO ACTIVE WITH MINOR STORM PERIODS AT HIGH LATITUDES
FOR THE SECOND AND THIRD DAYS.
