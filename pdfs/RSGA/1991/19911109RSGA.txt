SDF NUMBER 313 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 09 NOV 1991
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 08/2100Z
TO 09/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH. REGION 6906 (S16W67)
PRODUCED AN X1/1B PARALLEL RIBBON FLARE AT 09/1544Z. A 540 SFU
TENFLARE, AND WEAK TYPES II AND IV SWEEPS WERE ASSOCIATED WITH
THE FLARE. THE ONLY REMAINING SPOT IN OLD REGION 6914 MERGED
WITH THE TRAILER SPOTS IN REGION 6906 AND IS NOW CONSIDERED PART
OF THAT REGION. REGION 6906 WAS GENERALLY STABLE IN TERMS OF
GROWTH. REGION 6918 (N22W08) PRODUCED AN M1/SN FLARE AT
09/0320Z, THEN DECAYED DURING THE REMAINDER OF THE PERIOD.
REGION 6915 (N20E17) PRODUCED FOUR C-CLASS SUBFLARES WHILE
DISPLAYING SIGNIFICANT GROWTH. MINOR GROWTH WAS NOTED IN REGION
6919 (S12E61). NEW REGION 6920 (S20W31) WAS NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE TO HIGH. REGION 6906 IS CAPABALE OF PRODUCING AN
ISOLATED MAJOR FLARE. REGION 6915 MAY PRODUCE ISOLATED M-CLASS
ACTIVITY.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 08/2100Z TO 09/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT MINOR TO SEVERE STORM
LEVELS. SEVERE STORM LEVELS OCCURRED AT MIDDLE LATITUDES
UNTIL 09/0900Z, THEN DECLINED TO MINOR STORM LEVELS. HIGH
LATITUDE SEVERE STORMING PERSISTED THROUGH THE PERIOD.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM LEVELS TOMORROW WITH
A DECREASING TREND AS STORMING SUBSIDES. THE MAJOR FLARE
MENTIONED ABOVE SHOULD CAUSE MOSTLY ACTIVE LEVELS DURING THE
REMAINING TWO DAYS.
