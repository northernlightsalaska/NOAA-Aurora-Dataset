JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 178 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 27 JUN 1991
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 26/2100Z
TO 27/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 6692 (S11E06),
A SPOTLESS PLAGE AREA, PRODUCED TODAY'S ONLY M-CLASS EVENT: AN
M1/2N AT 1405Z. ADDITIONAL SUBFLARES WERE OBSERVED FROM A VAR-
IETY OF REGIONS. A LONG DURATION C8 FLARE WAS OBSERVED FROM
26/2255Z TO 27/0319Z AND WAS NOT CORRELATED WITH ANY OPTICAL
ACTIVITY. REGION 6694 (N06E08) CONTINUED TO GROW STEADILY
BUT ONLY PRODUCED A COUPLE SUBFLARES. EXTREMELY INTENSE
CALCIUM EMISSION WAS REPORTED ON EAST LIMB AT N40-N10. THIS,
ALONG WITH THE UNCORRELATED LDE EVENT MAY BE THE FIRST IN-
DICATIONS OF THE RETURN OF OLD REGION 6659.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. AN UPWARD TREND IN BACKGROUND AND ACTIVITY
LEVELS IS EXPECTED WITH THE RETURN OF OLD REGION 6659 (N31,
L-244) WHICH PRODUCED 6 X-CLASS EVENTS AND 25 M-CLASS FLARES
ON ITS PREVIOUS DISK TRANSIT.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 26/2100Z TO 27/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS AT QUIET TO ACTIVE LEVELS DURING
THE DAY.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE MOSTLY ACTIVE OVER THE NEXT 24 HOURS, AND IS
EXPECTED TO GRADUALLY DECLINE TO MOSTLY UNSETTLED LEVELS BY
THE THIRD DAY.
