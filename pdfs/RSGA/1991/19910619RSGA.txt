JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 170 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 19 JUN 1991
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 18/2100Z
TO 19/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. REGION 6681 (N08E19)
PRODUCED SEVERAL C-CLASS FLARES TODAY TO INCLUDE A C4/SN
EVENT AT 18/2244Z. THIS REGION APPEARS TO HAVE REACHED
MATURITY AND IS NOW SHOWING SOME DECAY. REGION 6682 (S13W23)
ALSO PRODUCED SOME C-CLASS FLARES INCLUDING A C6/SF EVENT
AT 19/0017Z. THIS REGION CONTINUES TO GROW IN SIZE BUT REMAINS
FAIRLY SIMPLE. SLIGHT ROTATION OF THE REGION'S LARGEST SPOT WAS
NOTED. ISOLATED SUBFLARING WAS OBSERVED IN REGIONS 6686 (S16E52)
AND 6680 (S15W36). NEW REGIONS 6687 (N16E25) AND 6688 (S27E43)
WERE ASSIGNED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW. REGIONS 6681 AND 6682 POSE ONLY A SLIGHT THREAT FOR
M-CLASS FLARE PRODUCTION. ACTIVITY LEVELS MAY INCREASE NEAR
THE END OF THE PERIOD AS PREVIOUSLY ACTIVE REGIONS 6652 (S10,
L-356) AND 6654 (N06, L-352) ARE DUE TO RETURN.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 18/2100Z TO 19/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS. PERIODS OF MINOR STORMING
WERE NOTED AT HIGH LATITUDES. THE PROTON EVENT (10 MEV)
THAT BEGAN 14/2340Z AND REACHED ITS PEAK OF 1400 PFU ON
15/1950Z, ENDED AT 19/0015Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE GENERALLY ACTIVE IN RESPONSE TO EFFECTS FROM
RECURRENT CORONAL HOLES. ISOLATED MINOR STORMING MAY OCCUR AT
HIGHER LATITUDES.
