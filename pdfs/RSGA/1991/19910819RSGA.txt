JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 231 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 19 AUG 1991
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 18/2100Z
TO 19/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW.  SUBFLARES FROM A NUMBER
OF REGIONS WERE OBSERVED DURING THE PAST 24 HOURS BUT NOTHING
WITH SIGNIFICANT X-RAY OUTPUT.  REGION 6786 (S09E10) WAS ONE OF
THE MORE ACTIVE REGIONS WITH SEVERAL SUBFLARES.  THIS IS THE
LARGEST SUNSPOT GROUP PRESENTLY ON THE DISK AND HAS CONTINUED TO
DEVELOP, ESPECIALLY IN THE INTERMEDIATE SUNSPOTS.  NEW REGIONS
6798 (S09E56) AND 6799 (N13E74) WERE NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE.  M-CLASS ACTIVITY IS POSSIBLE IN REGIONS
6781 (S17W08), 6782 (S11W05), 6784 (S20W21), AND 6786.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 18/2100Z TO 19/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS AT ACTIVE TO MAJOR STORM  LEVELS.
SEVER STORM CONDITIONS WERE OBSERVED AT SOME STATIONS FROM
19/06-09Z.  THE SOURCE OF THIS DISTURBANCE IS NOT CLEARLY KNOWN
BUT ERUPTIVE FILAMENT ACTIVITY IS SUSPECTED.  A FORBUSH DECREASE
IS ASSOCIATED WITH THIS STORM AND CONTINUES IN PROGRESS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT ACTIVE TO STORM LEVELS FOR THE NEXT 24 HOURS.
THE FIELD SHOULD RETURN TO MOSTLY UNSETTLED CONDITIONS FOR
THE REMAINDER OF THE FORECAST PERIOD.
