JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 189 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 08 JUL 1991
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 07/2100Z

QUIET DURING THE PERIOD. NEW REGIONS 6721 (S23E05), 6722
(S10E78), AND 6723 (N19E62) WERE NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE TO HIGH. REGION 6703 MAY PRODUCE A MAJOR FLARE WITHIN
THE NEXT 24 HOURS.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 07/2100Z TO 08/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT UNSETTLED TO MAJOR STORM
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS. PERIODS OF SEVERE STORMING
OCCURRED AT HIGH LATITUDES. THE STORMING FOLLOWED A SUDDEN
IMPULSE OF 24 NANOTESLA AT 08/1635Z (AS MEASURED BY THE
BOULDER USGS MAGNETOMETER). THE STORMING IS THE RESULT OF THE
X1/2B FLARE PRODUCED BY REGION 6703 AT 07/0223Z. THE GREATER
THAN 10 MEV PROTON EVENT THAT BEGAN AT 07/0455Z (ALSO A
RESULT OF THE X1/2B FLARE) CONTINUED WITH A PRELIMINARY
MAXIMUM OF 2300 PFU AT 08/1645Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT MINOR TO MAJOR STORM LEVELS DURING THE FIRST
DAY AS FLARE EFFECTS PERSIST. PERIODS OF SEVERE STORMING MAY
ALSO OCCUR UNTIL 09/1200Z. CONDITIONS SHOULD DECLINE TO MOSTLY
ACTIVE LEVELS ON THE SECOND DAY WITH A FURTHER DECREASE TO
UNSETTLED LEVELS ON THE FINAL DAY AS FLARE EFFECTS SUBSIDE.
THE PROTON EVENT IS EXPECTED TO CONTINUE.
