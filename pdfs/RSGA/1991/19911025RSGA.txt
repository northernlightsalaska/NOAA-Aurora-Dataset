JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 298 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 25 OCT 1991
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 24/2100Z
TO 25/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH. REGION 6891 (S11E34)
PRODUCED ANOTHER MAJOR FLARE, THIS ONE AN M9/1N AT 24/2241Z.
IT ALSO HAD AN M1/1B AT 1124Z AND AN M1/0F AT 1736Z. THE REGION
CONTINUES TO DOMINATE THE VISIBLE DISK, WITH ITS LARGE NAKED-
EYE SUNSPOT SURROUNDED BY A FIELD OF BRIGHT PLAGE. REGION 6892
(S20E35), POSITIONED JUST TO THE SOUTH, GREW SUBSTANTIALLY IN
ALL OBSERVABLES AND YIELDED AN M1/0F OF ITS OWN AT 0755Z. ONE
NEW REGION WAS BORN ON THE DISK, 6894 (N11E26).
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE TO HIGH. LOOK FOR MORE M-CLASS FLARES FROM REGION 6891
AND PERHAPS AN ISOLATED X-CLASS EVENT. REGION 6892 MAY ALSO
CONTRIBUTE AN OCCASIONAL M-CLASS FLARE.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 24/2100Z TO 25/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE AT MID-
LATITUDES. AN EPISODE OF MINOR STORM CONDITIONS OCCURRED AT
HIGH LATITUDES.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE THE NEXT THREE DAYS.
EFFECTS OF A SMALL CORONAL HOLE ARE LIKELY 27-28 OCT. FLARE
ACTIVITY OF THE PAST TWO DAYS IS EXPECTED TO HAVE NO EFFECT
ON THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD.
