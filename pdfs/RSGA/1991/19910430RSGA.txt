JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 120 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 30 APR 1991
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 29/2100Z
TO 30/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. A NUMBER OF LOW LEVEL
C-CLASS FLARES WERE REPORTED TODAY. MOST OF THESE EMANATED
FROM REGION 6603 (S29W85) AS IT NEARED THE WEST LIMB. THE TWO
OTHER REGIONS OF NOTE, 6605 (N08E15), AND 6612 (S27W35) WERE
MOSTLY STABLE TODAY. NEW REGION 6615 (S12E66) WAS ASSIGNED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW. ISOLATED M-CLASS FLARES ARE POSSIBLE FROM FOUR REGIONS,
6603, 6605, 6612 AND NEW REGION 6615. ALL OF THE REMAINING
REGIONS APPEAR STABLE.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 29/2100Z TO 30/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS RANGED FROM UNSETTLED TO MAJOR
STORM LEVELS. THIS ACTIVITY IS MOST LIKELY STILL IN RESPONSE
TO THE CORONAL HOLE.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE. PERIODS OF STORM CONDITIONS
ARE STILL POSSIBLE BUT THE EFFECTS FROM THE CORONAL HOLE ARE
EXPECTED TO DIMINISH TO MOSTLY UNSETTLED CONDITIONS DURING THE
NEXT 24 HOURS.
