JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 137 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 17 MAY 1991
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 16/2100Z
TO 17/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 6619 (N31W79)
PRODUCED AN M3/SF AT 17/0909Z. AN ERUPTIVE PROMINENCE REACHING
0.20 RV WAS ASSOCIATED WITH THE FLARE, BUT DISCRETE RADIO
EMISSIONS WERE MODEST. THIS REGION PRODUCED SUBFLARES THROUGHOUT
THE PERIOD AND DISPLAYED NO SIGNIFICANT CHANGE IN SIZE OR
COMPLEXITY. REGION 6624 (S15W49) SHOWED MINOR GROWTH AND
PRODUCED A C7/1B FLARE AT 17/1344Z. REGION 6633 (S12E37) ALSO
DISPLAYED MINOR GROWTH AND PRODUCED SEVERAL SUBFLARES DURING THE
SECOND HALF OF THE PERIOD. A 16 DEGREE FILAMENT, FORMERLY
POSITIONED AT N27W12, DISAPPEARED DURING THE SECOND HALF OF THE
PERIOD. NEW REGION 6636 (N18E22) AND PLAGE REGION 6637 (S26E64)
WERE NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE. REGION 6619 MAY PRODUCE M-CLASS ACTIVITY WHILE
BEGINNING ITS PASSAGE ACROSS THE WEST LIMB. THERE IS ALSO A
SLIGHT CHANCE FOR AN X-CLASS FLARE FROM THIS REGION. C-CLASS
FLARES ARE EXPECTED FROM REGIONS 6624 AND 6633.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 16/2100Z TO 17/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO MINOR STORM LEVELS
AT MIDDLE LATITUDES. THE HIGH LATITUDE FIELD RANGED FROM
UNSETTLED TO MAJOR STORM LEVELS. THE STORMING OCCURRED FROM
17/0300-1200Z FOLLOWING A SUDDEN IMPULSE (44 NANOTESLA AT
16/2040Z AS MEASURED BY THE BOULDER USGS MAGNETOMETER).
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM LEVELS TOMORROW IN
RESPONSE TO THE M8/2B FLARE THAT OCCURRED AT 16/0703Z. THE
SHOCK IS EXPECTED TO ARRIVE NEAR MIDDAY. THE FIELD IS EXPECTED
TO DECLINE TO MOSTLY ACTIVE LEVELS ON THE SECOND DAY AS FLARE
EFFECTS SUBSIDE. UNSETTLED CONDITIONS ARE EXPECTED ON THE
FINAL DAY OF THE PERIOD.
