JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 203 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 22 JUL 1991
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 21/2100Z
TO 22/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH. REGION 6734 (N08E37) PRO-
DUCED AN X1/2B FLARE AT 1003Z WHICH WAS ACCOMPANIED BY MODERATE
TO STRONG RADIO BURSTS, A STRONG TYPE II AND A STRONG TYPE IV
SWEEP. THE EVENT WAS FOLLOWED BY A PROLONGED LOOP PROMINENCE
SYSTEM BETWEEN 6734 AND NEIGHBORING 6741 (N10E53) DURING WHICH
TIME THE X-RAY FLUX WAS ENHANCED (AROUND M-LEVEL) AND THE TYPE
IV WAS IN PROGRESS. THIS REGION CONTINUES TO DOMINATE THE DISK
WITH CONTINUING GROWTH AND A MAGNETIC DELTA CONFIGURATION.
THE REST OF TODAY'S ACTIVITY CONSISTED OF A FEW SMALL C-CLASS
FLARES FROM A VARIETY OF REGIONS.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MOSTLY MODERATE WITH A CONTINUED FAIR CHANCE FOR ISOLATED MAJOR
FLARES. REGION 6734 IS EXPECTED TO BE THE MAIN SOURCE FOR MAJOR
ACTIVITY GIVEN ITS CONTINUED GROWTH AND COMPLEXITY. REGION 6741
MIGHT ALSO CONTRIBUTE AN M-CLASS EVENT.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 21/2100Z TO 22/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE MOSTLY ACTIVE TOMORROW AND PARTWAY THROUGH THE
SECOND DAY. MINOR STORM CONDITIONS ARE EXPECTED TO BEGIN LATE
ON THE SECOND DAY AND LASTING THROUGH THE THIRD DAY IN
RESPONSE TO TODAY'S SOLAR FLARE.
