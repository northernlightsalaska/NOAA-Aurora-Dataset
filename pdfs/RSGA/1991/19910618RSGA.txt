JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 169 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 18 JUN 1991
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 17/2100Z
TO 18/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. REGION 6671 (N11W86)
WAS THE MOST ACTIVE AREA DURING THE PERIOD PRODUCING A HOST
OF SUBFLARES AND ISOLATED LOW-LEVEL C-CLASS EVENTS. REGIONS
6681 (N09E33) AND 6682 (S12W10) DISPLAYED ONLY MINOR GROWTH
WHILE REMAINING GENERALLY QUIET. BRIGHT SURGING WAS OBSERVED
ON THE NORTHWEST LIMB WHERE REGION 6659 (N32, L-239) DEPARTED
YESTERDAY. NEW REGIONS 6685 (N23E70) AND 6686 (S15E65) WERE
ASSIGNED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW. REGIONS 6681 AND 6682 ARE CAPABLE OF C-CLASS ACTIVITY.
ONLY A SLIGHT CHANCE FOR ISOLATED M-CLASS FLARING EXISTS.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 17/2100Z TO 18/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT UNSETTLED TO MAJOR STORM
LEVELS. THE MAJOR GEOMAGNETIC STORM BEGAN AT 17/1018Z WITH
MINOR TO MAJOR STORMING OBSERVED AT ALL LATITUDES UP THROUGH
THE BEGINNING OF THE 18TH. THE PROTON EVENT (10 MEV) THAT
BEGAN 14/2340Z CONTINUED THROUGH THE END OF THE PERIOD WITH
FLUX LEVELS NEAR THE 10 PFU THRESHOLD. THE PCA EVENT ENDED
AT 17/2300Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE DUE TO CORONAL HOLE EFFECTS
TOWARDS THE END OF THE PERIOD. THE PROTON EVENT SHOULD END
WITHIN THE NEXT 24 HOURS.
