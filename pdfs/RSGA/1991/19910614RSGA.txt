JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 165 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 14 JUN 1991
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 13/2100Z
TO 14/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH. REGION 6659 (N31W67)
PRODUCED AN M7/2N FLARE AT 14/0142Z ASSOCIATED WITH MINOR RADIO
EMISSIONS. THE REGION SHOWED DECAY IN THE FORM OF PENUMBRAL
FRAGMENTATION, BUT REMAINED LARGE AND STRUCTURALLY COMPLEX. IT
ALSO PRODUCED SIX OTHER M-CLASS FLARES: AN M2/2F AT 13/2205Z, AN
M1/1F AT 13/2336Z, AN M4/2N AT 14/0031Z, AN M1/1F AT 14/0423Z,
AN M1/1N AT 14/0547Z, AND AN M1/SF AT 14/1915Z. AN OPTICALLY
UNCORRELATED M1 X-RAY BURST OCCURRED AT 14/0003Z. NEW REGIONS
6677 (S13E24), 6678 (S28E57), AND 6679 (N10E40) WERE NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE TO HIGH. REGION 6659 REMAINS CAPABLE OF GENERATING
ISOLATED MAJOR FLARES AS IT APPROACHES THE WEST LIMB.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 13/2100Z TO 14/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO ACTIVE LEVELS FOR
THE PAST 24 HOURS. ISOLATED PERIODS OF MINOR STORMING WERE
NOTED AT HIGH LATITUDES. THE GREATER THAN 10 MEV PROTON
EVENT ENDED AT 14/0820Z AND THE ASSOCIATED POLAR CAP
ABSORPTION EVENT ENDED AT 14/0450Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE MOSTLY UNSETTLED DURING THE FIRST DAY WITH
PERIODS OF ACTIVE LEVELS POSSIBLE AT HIGH LATITUDES. UNSETTLED
LEVELS ARE EXPECTED FOR THE REMAINDER OF THE PERIOD.
