JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 325 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 21 NOV 1991
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 20/2100Z
TO 21/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 6919, NOW A
DAY PAST WEST LIMB NEAR S12, WAS RESPONSIBLE FOR AN M1 X-RAY
EVENT WITH TYPE II SWEEP AT 1903Z. SURGING AND LOOPS WERE SEEN
IN THIS LOCATION AT THE TIME OF THE X-RAY BURST. OTHERWISE,
VERY LITTLE OF CONSEQUENCE OCCURRED FROM THE VISIBLE DISK AS THE
EIGHT SPOTTED REGIONS WERE VERY ORDINARY. ONE NEW REGION WAS
ASSIGNED, 6936 (N15E73).
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW THE NEXT THREE DAYS.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 20/2100Z TO 21/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT MOSTLY ACTIVE TO MINOR
STORM LEVELS. THIS DISTURBANCE, THOUGHT TO BE RELATED TO
A CORONAL HOLE NOW WEST OF CENTRAL MERIDIAN, BEGAN EARLIER
THAN ANTICIPATED. BOTH GOES 6 AND 7 SPACECRAFT HAD MAGNETO-
PAUSE CROSSINGS DURING THE DAY.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO PERSIST AT ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM LEVELS FOR
ANOTHER 24 HOURS. GENERALLY UNSETTLED LEVELS ARE LIKELY TO END
THE INTERVAL.
