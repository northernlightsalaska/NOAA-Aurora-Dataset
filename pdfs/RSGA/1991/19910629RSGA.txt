SDF NUMBER 180 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 29 JUN 1991
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 28/2100Z
TO 29/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE DUE TO A LONG DURATION
M1/SF FLARE FROM REGION 6703 (BEHIND EAST LIMB AT N31) AT
29/1602Z.  MINOR RADIO BURSTS ACCOMPANIED THIS EVENT. AT THIS
TIME, NOT MUCH IS KNOWN ABOUT THIS AREA OTHER THAN THE ACTIVITY
IT PRODUCES. THE RAPID GROWTH IN REGION 6693 (S06W16) NOTED
YESTERDAY CEASED TODAY AND SEVERAL CLASS C SUBFLARES OCCURRED.
REGION 6694 (N07W17) MAY BE DEVELOPING A WEAK DELTA BUT WAS
PREDOMINANTLY QUIET. A LARGE SECTION OF A FILAMENT WHICH WOULD
HAVE BEEN LOCATED NEAR S24E25-S40E60 AT THIS TIME, FADED BETWEEN
29/0526Z-1204Z.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY SHOULD CONTINUE
AT GENERALLY A MODERATE LEVEL. REGION 6703 IS EXPECTED TO
PRODUCE CLASS M OR LARGER EVENTS. REGIONS 6693 AND 6694
SHOULD PRODUCE ISOLATED CLASS M FLARES.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 28/2100Z TO 29/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS PREDOMINANTLY QUIET AT MID
LATITUDES AND HIGH LATITUDES WERE PRIMARILY UNSETTLED.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD
SHOULD BECOME UNSETTLED TO SLIGHTLY ACTIVE FOR THE DURATION
OF THE FORECAST PERIOD.
