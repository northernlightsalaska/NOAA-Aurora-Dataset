JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 272 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 29 SEP 1991
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 28/2100Z
TO 29/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH. REGION 6853 (S19E28)
PRODUCED AN M7/4B TENFLARE WITH A LONG-LIVED TYPE IV SWEEP AT
1533Z. POINT BRIGHTENINGS WERE SEEN IN NEARBY REGION 6850
(S11E19) DURING THE EVENT BUT WERE SECONDARY TO THE CENTER OF
ACTIVITY WITHIN 6853. THAT REGION EXHIBITED GROWTH IN WHITE
LIGHT AND H-ALPHA IN THE PAST 24 HOURS AND IS STILL BRIGHT.
REGION 6850 IS OSTENSIBLY THE DOMINANT SPOT GROUP IN VIEW, BUT
ITS FLARE OUTPUT TODAY WAS MODEST. REGION 6855 (N18E46)
CONTINUED TO GROW, BUT WAS QUIET.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE. REGIONS 6853 AND 6850 ARE POTENTIAL M-CLASS FLARE
SITES, AND REGION 6855 MAY MUSTER A SIGNIFICANT C-CLASS EVENT.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 28/2100Z TO 29/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS QUIET TO UNSETTLED AT MID LATITUDES.
HIGH LATITUDES EXPERIENCED UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE CONDITIONS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM LEVELS THE NEXT
THREE DAYS WITH THE MOST INTENSE ACTIVITY LIKELY AT THE END OF
THE INTERVAL. CORONAL HOLE AND DISAPPEARING FILAMENT EFFECTS
ARE ANTICIPATED.
