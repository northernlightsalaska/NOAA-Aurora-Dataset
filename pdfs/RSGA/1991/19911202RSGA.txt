JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 336 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 02 DEC 1991
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 01/2100Z
TO 02/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE.  THE LARGEST EVENT OF
THE PERIOD WAS A M3/SF AT 0501UT FROM REGION 6952 (N18E78).
THIS REGION IS IN THE AREA OF RETURNING REGION 6915 (N19 L002)
AND IS A SIMPLE H-TYPE SPOT AT THIS TIME.  AN OPTICALLY
UNCORRELATED M1 EVENT OCCURRED AT 0116UT.  A WEAK DELTA MAY
BE FORMING IN THE TRAILER OF REGION 6946 (S09E11), HOWEVER,
THIS REGION ONLY PRODUCED SMALL FLARES DURING THE PERIOD.
REGION 6943 (S06W41) APPEARS TO BE DECAYING.  REGION 6953
(S19E57) WAS ASSIGNED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE.  REGIONS 6952 AND 6946 ARE THE MOST LIKELY
SOURCES OF ENERGETIC ACTIVITY.  OLD REGION 6919 (S11 L317),
WHICH PRODUCED SEVEN M FLARES AND ONE X FLARE DURING ITS
PREVIOUS ROTATION, IS DUE BACK ON 5 DEC AND MAY PRODUCE
ADDITIONAL ACTIVITY.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 01/2100Z TO 02/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO UNSETTLED
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE GENERALLY UNSETTLED.
