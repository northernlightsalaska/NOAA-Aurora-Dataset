JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 175 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 24 JUN 1991
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 23/2100Z
TO 24/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. FREQUENT SUBFLARES WERE
PRODUCED BY A HOST OF ACTIVE REGIONS WITH REGION 6682 (S12W85)
THE MOST PROLIFIC. TWO NEW REGIONS WERE ASSIGNED, 6694 (N06E48)
AND 6695 (N02E71). A FILAMENT OF APPROXIMATELY 20 DEGREES IN
LENGTH DISAPPEARED FROM N40E50 OVER THE PAST 6 HOURS.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 23/2100Z TO 24/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN MOSTLY ACTIVE AT MIDDLE
LATITUDES. HIGH LATITUDES EXPERIENCED ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM
CONDITIONS. THIS ACTIVITY IS THOUGHT TO BE RELATED TO THE
LINGERING EFFECTS OF A WESTERN HEMISPHERE CORONAL HOLE.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE LEVELS THROUGHOUT THE
FORECAST PERIOD.
