SDF NUMBER 262 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 19 SEP 1991
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 18/2100Z
TO 19/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. REGION 6832 (N08W12)
REMAINED MODERATELY COMPLEX IN MAGNETIC STRUCTURE, BUT MANAGED
TO PRODUCE ONLY TWO C-CLASS SUBFLARES. THERE WERE SIGNS THAT
THE REGION MAY BE STARTING TO DECAY. NEW REGIONS 6840 (N08E01)
AND 6841 (S14W08) WERE NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW. REGION 6832 IS EXPECTED TO PRODUCE OCCASIONAL C-CLASS
FLARES AND ALSO POSES A SLIGHT THREAT FOR AN ISOLATED M-CLASS
FLARE.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 18/2100Z TO 19/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO UNSETTLED LEVELS
FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS. SOME HIGH LATITUDE STATIONS REPORTED
ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM LEVELS FROM 19/1200-1800Z. THERE WAS NO
APPARENT SOURCE FOR THE STORMING.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT MOSTLY UNSETTLED LEVELS.
