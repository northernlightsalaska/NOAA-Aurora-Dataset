JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 192 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 11 JUL 1991
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 10/2100Z
TO 11/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH. A LONG DURATION M7/3B
TENFLARE ERUPTED IN REGION 6703 (N28W62) AT 11/1037Z. A 320
SFU 10CM BURST AND SIGNIFICANT TYPE II SWEEP WERE ASSOCIATED
WITH THIS PARALLEL RIBBON FLARE. THE REGION WAS VOID OF SPOTS
AT THE TIME OF THE FLARE BUT MAINTAINS A SLIGHTLY COMPLEX
MAGNETIC STRUCTURE. A LOOP PROMINENCE SYSTEM WAS ALSO NOTED
WITH THE MAJOR FLARE. REGION 6713 (N36W44) WHICH HAS DISPLAYED
RAPID GROWTH FOR THE LAST 48 HOURS, PRODUCED SEVERAL C-CLASS
SUBFLARES. REGION 6718 (S25E14) REMAINED GENERALLY STABLE WHILE
CONTRIBUTING ONLY ISOLATED SUBFLARING.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE. M-CLASS ACTIVITY IS POSSIBLE FROM REGION 6718 OR
6703.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 10/2100Z TO 11/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO ACTIVE LEVELS
FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS WITH PERIODS OF MINOR STORMING AT HIGH
LATITUDE SITES SINCE 0900Z. A SATELLITE LEVEL PROTON EVENT AT
GREATER THAN 10 MEV BEGAN AT 11/0240Z. THE EVENT PEAKED AT
30 PFU AT 11/0450Z AND HAS TENTATIVELY ENDED AS OF 11/1155Z.
THE M3/2N FLARE THAT ERUPTED AT 10/1228Z IS A POSSIBLE SOURCE
FOR THIS ACTIVITY.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE ACTIVE WITH ISOLATED MINOR STORMING POSSIBLE BY
THE 13TH IN RESPONSE TO THE MAJOR FLARING NOTED IN PART 1A.
