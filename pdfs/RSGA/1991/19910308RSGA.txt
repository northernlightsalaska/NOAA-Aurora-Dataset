SDF NUMBER 067 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 08 MAR 1991
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 07/2100Z
TO 08/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH. REGION 6538 (S25E47)
DISPLAYED SIGNIFICANT SPOT DEVELOPMENT DURING THE PERIOD AND
WAS THE SOLE SOURCE FOR THE DAY'S MAJOR FLARING. AN X2/2B
TENFLARE ERUPTED FROM THIS AREA AT 07/2318Z. THREE M-CLASS
EVENTS WERE ALSO ATTRIBUTED TO THIS AREA, THE LARGEST OF
WHICH WAS AN M1/1B FLARE WITH ASSOCIATED LOOP PROMINENCE
SYSTEM OBSERVED AT 08/0233Z. AN IMPRESSIVE X1/2B TENFLARE
FROM THIS AREA AT 08/2033Z CLOSED OUT THE PERIOD'S ACTIVITY.
REGION 6537 (S08E28) REMAINED GENERALLY STABLE WITH ONLY
ISOLATED SUBFLARING NOTED. AN ERUPTIVE PROMINENCE WAS
OBSERVED ON THE SOUTHEAST LIMB AT 1937Z. NEW REGION 6543
(N12W03) WAS ASSIGNED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE TO HIGH. REGION 6538 REMAINS THE PRIME SOURCE FOR
M-CLASS FLARES AND ISOLATED MAJOR EVENTS.

FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS IN RESPONSE TO EFFECTS FROM A WELL
POSITIONED CORONAL HOLE.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE ACTIVE WITH A CHANCE OF MINOR STORMING
POSSIBLE AT HIGH LATITUDES IN RESPONSE TO EFFECTS FROM THE
MAJOR FLARING THAT OCCURRED ON THE 7TH.
