SDF NUMBER 196 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 15 JUL 1991
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 14/2100Z
TO 15/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. AN M2 X-RAY EVENT
THAT MAXED AT 1333 UT WAS ASSOCIATED WITH FLARE LOOPS NEAR
DEPARTING REGION 6713 (N35W90).  C-CLASS X-RAY FLARES OCCURRED
IN REGIONS 6716 (N11W85), 6718 (S23W38), 6728 (S26E38), AND
6729 (S22E60).  NEW REGION 6730 (N21E74) WAS NUMBERED; IT IS
THE APPARENT RETURN OF OLD REGION 6685.  SURGING AT N08W90
NEAR 1442 UT MARKED THE DEPARTURE OF 6716. FILAMENT
SEGMENTS DISAPPEARED NEAR N22W08 AND S20E57 BEWEEN
14/1717 AND 15/0320 UT.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE.  ONLY ISOLATED SIGNIFICANT FLARE ACTIVITY
APPEARS LIKELY FROM REGIONS PRESENTLY ON THE VISIBLE DISK.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 14/2100Z TO 15/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT ACTIVE TO QUIET LEVELS
FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS, FOLLOWING THE STORM CONDITIONS OF
THE PREVIOUS TWO DAYS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE MOSTLY UNSETTLED FOR THE NEXT TWO DAYS,
INCREASING TOWARD ACTIVE TO OCCASIONAL MINOR STORM LEVELS
ON THE THIRD DAY.  INDICATORS FOR INCREASED ACTIVITY INCLUDE
RECURRENCE, A WELL-POSITIONED CORONAL HOLE, AND ELEVATED
SOLAR WIND SPEED AS MEASURED BY THE PIONEER VENUS ORBITER.
