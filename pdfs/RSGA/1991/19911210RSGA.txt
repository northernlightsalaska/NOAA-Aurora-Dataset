JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 344 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 10 DEC 1991
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 09/2100Z
TO 10/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH. NUMEROUS ENERGETIC EVENTS
WERE OBSERVED DURING THE PERIOD. REGION 6961 (N09E25) APPEARS TO
HAVE REACHED ITS PEAK IN GROWTH. TWO LOW-LEVEL M-CLASS FLARES
WERE ATTRIBUTED TO THIS REGION WHICH IS NOW STARTING TO SHOW
SOME FRAGMENTATION. REGION 6966 (S04E63) ALSO PRODUCED TWO
M-CLASS FLARES. SLOW GROWTH WAS NOTED IN THIS REGION AS WELL AS
IN REGION 6958 (S17W10) WHICH CONTRIBUTED AN ISOLATED M-FLARE. A
C9 X-RAY EVENT WAS NOTED AT 0355Z AND CORRELATED WELL WITH EVENT
LEVEL SURGING OBSERVED ON THE SOUTHWEST LIMB. REGION 6952
(N18W24) CONTINUED ITS TREND OF SLOW DECAY WHILE PRODUCING
ISOLATED SUBFLARES. NEW REGION 6968 (S15E74) WAS ASSIGNED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE TO HIGH. REGIONS 6961 AND 6963 ARE FAVORABLE LOCATIONS
FOR CONTINUED M-CLASS ACTIVITY.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 09/2100Z TO 10/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO UNSETTLED LEVELS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE GENERALLY UNSETTLED DUE TO EFFECTS FROM A WELL
POSITIONED CORONAL HOLE.
