JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 162 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 11 JUN 1991
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 10/2100Z
TO 11/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH. REGION 6659 (N31W30)
PRODUCED AN X12+/1B FLARE AT 11/0209Z. THE FLARE WAS ACCOMPANIED
BY A 10000 SFU TENFLARE, MAJOR TYPES II AND IV SWEEPS, AND A
LOOP PROMINENCE SYSTEM. THIS REGION ALSO PRODUCED AN M4 LONG
DURATION X-RAY BURST WHICH WAS IN PROGRESS AT THE END OF THE
PERIOD. A 260 SFU TENFLARE AND A TYPE IV SWEEP WERE ASSOCIATED
WITH THIS FLARE. THE REGION ALSO PRODUCED AN M1/SF FLARE AT
11/1734Z. REGION 6666 (S15W30) PRODUCED SEVERAL FLARES INCLUDING
AN M1/SF AT 11/1200Z. NEW REGION 6673 (S23E51) WAS ASSIGNED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
HIGH. REGION 6659 IS LIKELY TO PRODUCE ADDITIONAL MAJOR FLARES.
REGION 6666 MAY PRODUCE ISOLATED M-CLASS FLARES.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 10/2100Z TO 11/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT MINOR TO SEVERE STORM
LEVELS. THE SEVERE STORMING OCCURRED DURING THE FIRST
HALF OF THE PERIOD. THE GREATER THAN 10 MEV PROTON EVENT WAS
FURTHER ENHANCED NEAR 11/0245Z IN THE WAKE OF THE X12 FLARE.
A PEAK FLUX OF 3000 PFU WAS OBSERVED AT 11/1420Z. THE PROTON
EVENT REMAINED IN PROGRESS AT THE END OF THE PERIOD.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE ACTIVE UNTIL NEAR 12/1500Z WHEN THE SHOCK FROM
TODAY'S X12 IS EXPECTED TO ARRIVE. THEREAFTER, THE FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT MAJOR TO SEVERE STORM LEVELS. THE FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO DECLINE TO MINOR STORM LEVELS ON THE FINAL DAY
WITH PERIODS OF MAJOR STORMING POSSIBLE AT HIGH LATITUDES. THE
PROTON EVENT IS EXPECTED TO CONTINUE.
