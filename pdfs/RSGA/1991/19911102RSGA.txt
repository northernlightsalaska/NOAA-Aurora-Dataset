JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 306 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 02 NOV 1991
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 01/2100Z
TO 02/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH. REGION 6891 (S12W72)
PRODUCED AN M9/1B FLARE AT 02/0652Z. THIS FLARE WAS ACCOMPANIED
BY A DISCRETE RADIO BURST OF 1400 SFU AT 2695 MHZ, BUT NO EVENT
LEVEL SWEEP ACTIVITY WAS OBSERVED. THIS REGION ALSO PRODUCED
A LONG DURATION M4/1B FLARE AT 02/1702Z. FURTHER DECAY IS
APPARENT SINCE YESTERDAY, CHARACTERIZED BY ADDITIONAL
FRAGMENTATION OF THE LARGE SPOT COMPLEX AND A SLIGHTLY REDUCED
SPOT COUNT. REGION 6893 (N15W51) IS THE NEXT MOST SIGNIFICANT
REGION ON THE DISK. IT IS ALSO DECAYING SLOWLY AND PRODUCED
AN M3/1B AT 02/1152Z. NEW REGION 6907 (S21E13) WAS NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE TO HIGH. ANOTHER MAJOR FLARE IS EXPECTED IN REGION
6891 BEFORE IT ROTATES AROUND THE WEST LIMB. M-CLASS ACTIVITY
IS POSSIBLE IN REGION 6893.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 01/2100Z TO 02/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD REMAINED AT STORM LEVELS AT MOST
STATIONS UNTIL 02/0600Z. AFTERWARD THE MIDDLE LATITUDE FIELD
WAS QUIET TO UNSETTLED AND THE HIGH LATITUDE FIELD WAS QUIET
TO ACTIVE.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE MOSTLY UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE. ISOLATED STORM
CONDITIONS ARE POSSIBLE DURING LOCAL NIGHTTIME.
