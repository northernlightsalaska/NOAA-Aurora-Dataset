JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 190 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 09 JUL 1991
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 08/2100Z
TO 09/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 6718 (S23E39)
DISPLAYED GRADUAL GROWTH AND PRODUCED AN M1/1N FLARE AT
09/1505Z. A TYPE IV RADIO SWEEP ACCOMPANIED THE FLARE. REGION
6703 (N27W37) CONTINUED TO GRADUALLY DECAY AND PRODUCED FOUR
SMALL FLARES WITH THE LARGEST BEING A C2/1F AT 09/0206Z. AN
ERUPTIVE PROMINENCE OCCURRED IN REGION 6699 (N08W86) AS IT BEGAN
TO CROSS THE WEST LIMB. THE ERUPTION REACHED A VERTICAL EXTENT
OF 0.13 RV. NO NEW REGIONS WERE ASSIGNED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE. REGIONS 6703 AND 6718 MAY PRODUCE M-CLASS FLARES.
REGION 6703 STILL PROVIDES A SLIGHT CHANCE FOR MAJOR FLARE
ACTIVITY, GIVEN ITS HISTORY.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 08/2100Z TO 09/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT MINOR TO MAJOR STORM LEVELS
WITH PERIODS OF SEVERE STORMING RECORDED AT HIGH LATITUDES.
HOWEVER, ACTIVITY BEGAN TO GRADUALLY DECLINE NEAR 09/1800Z.
THE PROTON EVENT AT GREATER THAN 10 MEV CONTINUED. THE FLUX
WAS HOVERING NEAR 30 PFU AT THE END OF THE PERIOD.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE ACTIVE DURING THE FIRST DAY, THOUGH GRADUALLY
DECLINING, AS FLARE EFFECTS SUBSIDE. THE FIELD IS EXPECTED TO
BE AT UNSETTLED LEVELS FOR THE REMAINDER OF THE PERIOD. THE
PROTON EVENT IS EXPECTED TO CONTINUE THROUGH 11 JULY.
