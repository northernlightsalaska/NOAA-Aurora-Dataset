JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 059 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 28 FEB 1991
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 27/2100Z
TO 28/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW.  SEVERAL SMALL C-CLASS
FLARES WERE OBSERVED SINCE YESTERDAY.  MOST OF THEM ORIGINATED
IN REGION 6508 (S14W53).  THIS REGION IS APPARENTLY STILL THE
MOST COMPLEX REGION ON THE DISK.  REGION 6509 (S21W58) IS THE
LARGEST REGION, BUT REMAINS RLATIVELY QUIET.  NEW REGIONS 6525
(S20W04) AND 6526 (S16W18) WERE NUMBERED SINCE YESTERDAY.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE.  AN ISOLATED M-CLASS FLARE IS POSSIBLE IN
REGION 6508.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 27/2100Z TO 28/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD GRADUALLY BECAME MORE DISTURBED SINCE
YESTERDAY.  THE MIDDLE LATITUDE FIELD WAS MOSTLY UNSETTLED
AND THE HIGH LATITUDE FIELD WAS UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE MOSTLY UNSETTLED FOR THE NEXT 24 HOURS AND
QUIET TO UNSETTLED THEREAFTER.
