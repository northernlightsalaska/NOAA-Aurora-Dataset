JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 226 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 14 AUG 1991
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 13/2100Z
TO 14/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. ONLY A FEW SMALL SUBFLARES
WERE OBSERVED TODAY FROM A VARIETY OF REGIONS. GROWTH CONTINUES
IN REGION 6768 (S12W46) BUT IT PRODUCED NO FLARES. NEW FLUX IS
ROTATING INTO VIEW ON THE SOUTHEAST PART OF THE DISK ACCOM-
PANIED BY EAST LIMB SURGING AT S23 AND S11, ACCOUNTING FOR
TODAY'S SHARP INCREASE IN 10.7 CM FLUX.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW. A GENERAL UPWARD TREND IS EXPECTED WITH THE RETURN OF
SEVERAL OLD REGIONS OVER THE NEXT FEW DAYS. IN PARTICULAR,
OLD REGION 6734 (N10, L-353) WHICH PRODUCED 2 X-CLASS
FLARES ON ITS PREVIOUS TRANSIT IS DUE BACK LATE TOMORROW.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 13/2100Z TO 14/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS QUIET TO UNSETTLED FROM THE BEGIN-
NING OF THE PERIOD UNTIL AROUND 1500Z. SINCE THEN THE FIELD
HAS BEEN AT UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE LEVELS WITH SOME MINOR STORM
PERIODS AT HIGH LATITUDES.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE FOR THE NEXT THREE DAYS DUE
TO RECURRENT EFFECTS.
