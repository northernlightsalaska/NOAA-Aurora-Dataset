JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 147 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 27 MAY 1991
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 26/2100Z
TO 27/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 6644 (N21E03)
PRODUCED AN M1/0F AT 0147Z, AS WELL AS A FEW C-CLASS SUBFLARES.
THE REGION GREW SLIGHTLY DURING THE PAST 24 HOURS, BUT IS STILL
A SIMPLE BIPOLE MAGNETICALLY. DURING THE PAST EIGHT HOURS, THE
EAST LIMB HAS DOMINATED ACTIVITY AS EVENT LEVEL SURGING AND
FREQUENT C-CLASS FLARES WERE OBSERVED IN REGIONS 6652 (S09E56)
AND 6654 (N07E66). NEWLY ASSIGNED REGION 6655 (S07E73) IS ALSO
IN THE VICINITY.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE. M-CLASS ACTIVITY MAY OCCUR IN REGION 6644, 6652, OR
6654. RISING X-RAY AND 10.7 CM BACKGROUND INDICES ARE ALSO
SUPPORTIVE OF LARGER AND MORE FREQUENT FLARE OCCURRENCES.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 26/2100Z TO 27/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN MOSTLY ACTIVE THE PAST 24
HOURS. HIGH LATITUDES EXPERIENCED AN EPISODE OF MINOR STORM
CONDITIONS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE THROUGH 29 MAY, THEN
STRICTLY UNSETTLED AT THE END OF THE INTERVAL.
