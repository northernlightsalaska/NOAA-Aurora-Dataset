JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 351 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 17 DEC 1991
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 16/2100Z
TO 17/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE.  A SINGLE M CLASS
FLARE OCCURRED IN THE PAST 24 HOURS, AN M2/1B FROM REGION
6961 (N08W66) AT 0629UT.  THIS REGION APPEARS TO BE DECLINING.
REGION 6972 (S10E44) PRODUCED SEVERAL C FLARES, THE LARGEST
A C7/SF AT 16/2207UT.  AN AFS WAS REPORTED IN THE TRAILER
PORTION OF THIS REGION.  THE SHARP INCREASE IN 10.7CM RADIO
FLUX OBSERVED YESTERDAY, DID NOT CONTINUE TODAY.  THE FLUX
DROPPED 35 SFU TO 198.  THE SOURCE OF THE LARGE INCREASE ON
16 DEC IS NOT KNOWN.  NO ACTIVE REGIONS HAVE ROTATED OVER THE
EAST LIMB, HOWEVER, REGION 6977 (S33W72) EMERGED ON THE DISK.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE.  REGIONS 6961 AND 6972 CONTINUE TO BE THE
MOST LIKELY SOURCES OF ENERGETIC EVENTS.  REGION 6961 WILL
BEGIN WEST LIMB TRANSIT ON 19 DEC AND CHANCES FOR ENERGETIC
SHOULD DECREASE ACCORDINGLY.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 16/2100Z TO 17/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT UNSETTLED TO MINOR STORM
LEVELS, MOST LIKELY RELATED TO THE LARGE NORTHERN CORONAL
HOLE NEAR W40.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM LEVELS FOR THE NEXT
THREE DAYS.
