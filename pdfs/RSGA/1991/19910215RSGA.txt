JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 046 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 15 FEB 1991
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 14/2100Z
TO 15/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. ONLY C-CLASS ACTIVITY
OCCURRED IN THE PAST 24 HOURS. THE MOST NOTEWORTHY EVENT WAS
THE DISAPPEARANCE OF THE VERY LARGE - APPROXIMATELY 50 DEGREES
IN LENGTH - FILAMENT FROM THE SOUTHWEST QUADRANT. THIS EVENT
ACTUALLY OCCURRED FROM 1600-1900Z ON 14 FEB., AND WAS ACCOMPAN-
IED BY VERY A VERY MINOR - IF ANY - X RAY ENHANCEMENT AND A
SIMILARLY SMALL RESPONSE IN RADIO. HELIUM 10830 SUGGESTS A
CORONAL MASS EJECTION MAY HAVE OCCURRED, BUT THE EVIDENCE IS
NOT COMPELLING. A NEW REGION, 6504 (S17E76), IS VISIBLE
NEAR THE LOCATION OF REPORTED CALCIUM XV EMISSION SEEN
YESTERDAY.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW. ISOLATED M-CLASS FLARES, THOUGH NOT SEEN TO BE LIKELY AT
THIS TIME, MAY OCCUR IN REGION 6484 (N12W81), 6487 (N15W64), 0R
NEW REGION 6504.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 14/2100Z TO 15/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN MOSTLY QUIET.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE QUIET TO UNSETTLED EARLY ON. A WEAK RESPONSE TO
THE FILAMENT DISAPPEARANCE OF 14 FEB. IS ANTICIPATED LATE IN
