JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 294 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 21 OCT 1991
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 20/2100Z
TO 21/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. THE MOST ENERGETIC
ACTIVITY OF THE PERIOD ERUPTED IN THE FORM OF A C6/SN FLARE
FROM REGION 6884 (S12W37) AT 21/0100Z. THIS AREA HAS GROWN
CONSIDERABLY IN THE LAST 24 HOURS WITH SOME INCREASE IN
COMPLEXITY NOTED. SEVERAL UNCORRELATED C-CLASS X-RAY EVENTS
WERE OBSERVED. NEW REGIONS 6888 (N20E71), 6889 (S16E36),
AND 6890 (N09W35) WERE ASSIGNED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW WITH ISOLATED C-CLASS FLARING POSSIBLE.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 20/2100Z TO 21/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS RANGED FROM QUIET TO ACTIVE LEVELS
DURING THE PAST 24 HOURS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE AT THE START OF THE PERIOD
WITH THE TREND TOWARDS ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM CONDITIONS BY
THE 24TH IN RESPONSE TO EFFECTS FROM A WELL POSITIONED
CORONAL HOLE.
