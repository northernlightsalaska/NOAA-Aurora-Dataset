JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 171 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 20 JUN 1991
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 19/2100Z
TO 20/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGIONS 6687 (N16E12)
AND 6688 (S27E30) PRODUCED SIMULTANEOUS SUBFLARES WITH THE NET
EFFECT BEING AN M1 X-RAY EVENT AT 0233Z. NEITHER REGION
IMPRESSES IN OBSERVABLE PARAMETERS AND 6688 IS NOW A SPOTLESS
PLAGE. SUBFLARE ACTIVITY WAS REPORTED FROM FOUR OTHER SPOTTED
REGIONS AS WELL.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW THE NEXT THREE DAYS.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 19/2100Z TO 20/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN MOSTLY UNSETTLED AT MID
LATITUDES. HIGH LATITUDE SITES WERE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE AT MID LATITUDES. HIGH
LATITUDES SHOULD BE SOMEWHAT MORE DISTURBED. A RECURRENT
CORONAL HOLE SHOULD BRING THIS HEIGHTENED LEVEL OF ACTIVITY.
