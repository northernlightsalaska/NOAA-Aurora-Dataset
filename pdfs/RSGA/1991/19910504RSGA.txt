JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 124 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 04 MAY 1991
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 03/2100Z
TO 04/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. REGION 6615 (S11E10) WAS
AGAIN THE MOST PRODUCTIVE OF THE NINE SPOTTED REGIONS. IT HAD
INTERMITTENT C-CLASS FLARES, THE LARGEST BEING A C5/1N AT
03/2238Z. IT HAS GROWN SUBSTANTIALLY IN WHITE LIGHT AND THE
LARGE EMBEDDED FILAMENTS WITHIN THE REGION HAVE BEEN ACTIVE AT
TIMES. TWO NEW REGIONS WERE ASSIGNED, 6617 (N18W10) AND 6618
(N17E69).
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW. WATCH REGION 6615 FOR CONTINUED DEVELOPMENT WHICH, IF
IT OCCURS, MAY HERALD AN ISOLATED M-CLASS EVENT.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 03/2100Z TO 04/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT MOSTLY QUIET LEVELS
FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE QUIET TO MILDLY UNSETTLED THE NEXT THREE DAYS.
