JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 322 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 18 NOV 1991
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 17/2100Z
TO 18/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 6919 (S12W58)
GENERATED THE ONLY M-CLASS EVENT OF THE DAY, AN M1/1B AT 1058Z.
REGION 6929 (S11E62), THE SITE OF TWO M-CLASS FLARES YESTERDAY,
COULD MUSTER BUT TWO C-CLASS SUBFLARES IN THE PAST 24 HOURS.
NEITHER OF THESE REGIONS IS PARTICULARLY IMPRESSIVE IN WHITE
LIGHT OR H-ALPHA. REGION 6929, NEE 6981, SEEMS TO HAVE DECAYED
SUBSTANTIALLY SINCE IT WAS LAST SEEN AT WEST LIMB PASSAGE TWO
WEEKS AGO. ELSEWHERE, THE OTHER 4 SPOTTED REGIONS WERE QUIET AND
UNREMARKABLE.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE. REGIONS 6919 AND 6929 SHOULD PRODUCE OCCASIONAL
M-CLASS FLARES.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 17/2100Z TO 18/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS MOSTLY ACTIVE.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE GENERALLY UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE THE NEXT THREE
DAYS. MINOR STORM LEVELS MAY BE REACHED MIDWAY THROUGH THE
INTERVAL WHEN A TRANS-EQUATORIAL CORONAL HOLE ROTATES TO A
FAVORABLE LOCATION.
