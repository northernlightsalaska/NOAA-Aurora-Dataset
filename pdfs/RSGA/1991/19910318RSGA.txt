JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 077 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 18 MAR 1991
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 17/2100Z
TO 18/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH. REGION 6545 (S09W28) PRO-
DUCED TWO MAJOR FLARES TODAY. THE FIRST ONE WAS AN X1/2B AT
2126Z WHICH WAS ACCOMPANIED BY VERY STRONG RADIO BURSTS. THE
EVENT WAS NONETHELESS FAIRLY IMPULSIVE. THE SECOND WAS AN
M5/1B AT 1734Z, ALSO FAIRLY IMPULSIVE AND ACCOMPANIED BY
MODEST RADIO BURSTS. THE REGION CONTINUES TO SHOW SLIGHT
DECAY BUT IS STILL MAGNETICALLY COMPLEX AND FLARE POTENT.
REGION 6555 (S22E67) HAS ROTATED MORE FULLY INTO VIEW AS
A VERY LARGE FKI GROUP. THE REGION PRODUCED FREQUENT SMALL
FLARES, THE LARGEST OF WHICH WAS A C8/1N AT 17/2330Z. AN
ADDITIONAL ENERGETIC M1 FLARE OCCURRED AT 1301Z BUT WAS NOT
OPTICALLY CORRELATED. REGION 6538 (S24W73) CONTINUED TO
DECAY WITH OCCASIONAL SUBFLARES. REGION 6551 (N21W02)
SHOWED SOME GROWTH TODAY AND PRODUCED A FEW SUBFLARES.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE TO HIGH. ADDITIONAL M-CLASS FLARES ARE EXPECTED CER-
TAINLY FROM REGION 6545 AND PROBABLY FROM REGION 6555. REGION
6545 IS EXPECTED TO PRODUCE AN ADDITIONAL ISOLATED MAJOR FLARE
DURING THE PERIOD.

IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
