JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 149 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 29 MAY 1991
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 28/2100Z
TO 29/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS HIGH. REGION 6654 (N07E40)
CONTINUED TO PRODUCE M-CLASS EVENTS. ITS LARGEST WAS AN M4/0F
TENFLARE AT 1211Z, WHILE OTHER SIGNIFICANT ACTIVITY INCLUDED
AN M1/1B AT 28/2211Z, AN M1/2B AT 0304Z, AND AN M1/1N AT 0450Z.
STRUCTURALLY THE REGION HAS CHANGED LITTLE IN THE LAST 24 HOURS
AND REMAINS POTENT. REGION 6652 (S09E31) CONTRIBUTED A LONG-
DURATION M-LEVEL EVENT OF ITS OWN, AN M2/1B AT 1602Z. THIS FLARE
WAS CHARACTERIZED BY A "Y" SHAPED RIBBON OF BRIGHT PLAGE THAT
EXTENDED TO REGION 6654 ON THE OPPOSITE SIDE OF THE SOLAR
EQUATOR. THE REGION ALSO HAD A FEW SMALL SUBFLARES DURING THE
PERIOD. REGION 6644 (N23W22), STILL THE LARGEST IN SPOT AREA,
WAS STABLE.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO
PERSIST AT MODERATE TO HIGH LEVELS. WATCH REGIONS 6654 AND
6652 FOR MORE ENERGETIC FLARES.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 28/2100Z TO 29/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS GENERALLY ACTIVE. THE CURRENT
DISTURBANCE, WHICH BEGAN 22 MAY, SEEMS TO BE WEAKENING.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO MILDLY ACTIVE THE MAJORITY OF
THE PERIOD. A FLARE-INDUCED DISTURBANCE IS NOT ANTICIPATED.
