JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 068 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 09 MAR 1991
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 08/2100Z
TO 09/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. THE SPOT AREA
ASSOCIATED WITH REGION 6538 (S23E32) HAS BECOME MORE COMPACT
WITH A SIGNIFICANT INCREASE IN INTERIOR SPOTS AND CONTINUES TO
POSSESS A SLIGHTLY COMPLEX MAGNETIC STRUCTURE. EVENTS NOTED
FROM THIS REGION INCLUDE AN M1/SF FLARE AT 1245Z AND AN
M1/SN FLARE AT 1422Z. REGION 6537 (S08E16) REMAINS MAGNETICALLY
SIMPLE AND IS SHOWING SIGNS OF GRADUAL DECAY. AN M1/2F FLARE
WAS ATTRIBUTED TO THIS AREA AT 0655Z. OTHER ACTIVITY OF NOTE
INCLUDES ISOLATED SUBFLARING FR0M REGIONS 6539 (N19W01) AND
6542 (S11E41). AN ERUPTIVE PROMINENCE WAS OBSERVED ON THE
SOUTHEAST LIMB AT 0200Z, POSSIBLY HAILING THE RETURN OF ACTIVITY
ASSOCIATED WITH OLD REGION 6497 (S12, L-265) DUE BACK ON 11
MARCH. A DENSE 14 DEGREE LONG FILAMENT, LOCATED NEAR S29W22,
DISAPPEARED BETWEEN 08/2355Z AND 09/1344Z. NEW REGION 6544
(S32W06) WAS ASSIGNED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE TO HIGH. M-CLASS FLARE PRODUCTION AND ISOLATED MAJOR
EVENTS ARE POSSIBLE FROM REGION 6538.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 08/2100Z TO 09/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE
LEVELS WITH OCCASIONAL PERIODS OF MINOR STORMING. THIS
ACTIVITY MAY BE IN RESPONSE TO EFFECTS FROM THE NUMEROUS
MAJOR FLARES NOTED ON 7 MARCH.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT ACTIVE TO OCCASIONAL MINOR STORM LEVELS
THE NEXT TWO DAY'S WITH UNSETTLED CONDITIONS RETURNING BY
THE END OF THE PERIOD.
