JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 341 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 07 DEC 1991
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 06/2100Z
TO 07/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. REGION 6952 (N21E15)
PRODUCED ISOLATED SUBFLARING WHILE SHOWING SOME SIGNS OF DECAY.
SIGNIFICANT GROWTH WAS NOTED IN REGION 6958 (S19E31) WHICH
PRODUCED A C5/SF FLARE AT 1824Z. REGION 6961 (N09E63) HAS BEEN
THE MAIN POINT OF INTEREST TODAY. THIS AREA PRODUCED NUMEROUS
FLARES TO INCLUDE A C6/1F EVENT AT 1232Z WHICH WAS ACCOMPANIED
BY A SMALL LOOP PROMINENCE SYSTEM. EVENT LEVEL BRIGHT SURGING
WAS OBSERVED FROM REGION 6946 (S08W59) NEAR THE END OF THE
PERIOD. NO NEW REGIONS WERE ASSIGNED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE. M-CLASS FLARING IS POSSIBLE FROM REGIONS 6961 AND
6952. REGION 6952 ALSO POSES A THREAT FOR MAJOR FLARING.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 06/2100Z TO 07/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET LEVELS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE GENERALLY QUIET WITH UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE LEVELS
EXPECTED BY THE 10TH IN RESPONSE TO CORONAL HOLE EFFECTS.
