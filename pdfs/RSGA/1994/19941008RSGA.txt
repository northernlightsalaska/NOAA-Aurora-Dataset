JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 281 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 08 OCT 1994
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 07/2100Z
TO 08/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS VERY LOW. MOST DISK REGIONS
SHOWED SLIGHT DECAY. REGION 7787 (N09E30) GREW SLIGHTLY. YOHKOH
IMAGERY SHOWED A NEW REGION APPEARING AT THE EAST LIMB NEAR
NE10. IT IS NEW FROM LAST ROTATION AND FOR MOST OF THE PERIOD
WAS THE BRIGHTEST REGION IN X-RAYS.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY SHOULD RETURN TO
LOW LEVELS. REGION 7787 AND THE NEW REGION NEAR THE NE LIMB
APPEAR CAPABLE OF C-CLASS FLARES.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 07/2100Z TO 08/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD RANGED FROM QUIET TO ACTIVE LEVELS.
ENERGETIC ELECTRON FLUXES REMAINED AT A HIGH LEVEL.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD
SHOULD BE AT MOSTLY UNSETTLED TO SLIGHTLY ACTIVE LEVELS FOR
09 OCT. QUIET TO UNSETTLED CONDITIONS ARE FORECAST FOR 10-
11 OCT. ISOLATED ACTIVE PERIODS ARE POSSIBLE DURING THAT
PERIOD. ENERGETIC ELECTRON FLUXES SHOULD REMAIN AT A HIGH
LEVEL FOR THE NEXT THREE DAYS.
