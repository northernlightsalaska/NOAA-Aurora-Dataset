JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 332 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 28 NOV 1994
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 27/2100Z
TO 28/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS VERY LOW.  THE LARGEST EVENT
OBSERVED THIS PERIOD WAS A B7/SF AT 28/0620UT FROM REGION 7811
(S17W38).  THIS REGION HAS SHOWN SOME WHITE LIGHT DECAY AS WELL
AS A SLIGHT RELAXATION OF THE MAGNETIC GRADIENT.  NO OTHER
SIGNIFICANT ACTIVITY NOTICED ON THE DISK OR LIMB.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW.  REGION 7811 STILL HAS THE POTENTIAL TO PRODUCE C-CLASS
ACTIVITY.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 27/2100Z TO 28/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO ACTIVE LEVELS
FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS WITH SOME PERIODS OF HIGH LATITUDE
NIGHTTIME MINOR STORM CONDITIONS.  THE GREATER THAN 2 MEV
ENERGETIC ELECTRON FLUX AT GEOSYNCHRONOUS ALTITUDES WAS
MODERATE TO HIGH.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE QUIET TO UNSETTLED FOR THE FIRST TWO DAYS OF
THE PERIOD.  ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO PICK UP TO UNSETTLED TO
ACTIVE ON DAY THREE IN RESPONSE TO A FAVORABLY POSITIONED
CORONAL HOLE FEATURE.
