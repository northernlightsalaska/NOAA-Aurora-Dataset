JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 292 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 19 OCT 1994
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 18/2100Z
TO 19/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW.  AN M1 X-RAY EVENT WAS
STILL IN PROGRESS AFTER FORECAST TIME.  THE EVENT FIRST HIT
M1 AT 19/2102Z.  THIS EVENT WAS OPTICALLY CORRELATED WITH A
LARGE RIBBON FLARE FROM RGN 7790 (N11W26).  THIS FLARE WAS SEEN
BRIEFLY IN A BREAK IN THE CLOUDS FROM CULGOORA, AUSTRALIA.
ASSOCIATED WITH THIS EVENT WAS A TENFLARE BURST OF 93 SFU WHICH
MAXED AT 19/2103Z AND REPORTS OF A TYPE II SWEEP WITH A RANGE OF
25-85MHZ AND AN ESTIMATED SHOCK VELOCITY OF 1000 KM/SEC.  NO
OTHER SIGNIFICANT ACTIVITY OCCURRED DURING THE PERIOD.  REGION
7790 CONTINUES ITS SLOW DECAY WHILE REGION 7792 (S07E57)
CONTINUES TO ROTATE INTO FULL VIEW ONTO THE DISK.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW.  REGION 7790 IS EXPECTED TO PRODUCE MORE C-CLASS ACTIVITY
WITH AN ISOLATED CHANCE OF M-CLASS ACTIVITY.

IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
