JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 350 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 16 DEC 1994
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 15/2100Z
TO 16/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS VERY LOW.  NUMEROUS B-CLASS
X-RAY EVENTS WERE OBSERVED THIS PERIOD.  THE LARGEST WAS A B7
OPTICALLY UNCORRELATED EVENT WHICH MAXED AT 16/1720UT.  A B6/SF
WAS OBSERVED AT 16/1816UT FROM A NEW EMERGING REGION ON THE EAST
LIMB AT N01.  REGION 7815 (S11W41) PRODUCED A B4/SF AT
16/1940UT.  THIS REGION HAS SHOWN LITTLE CHANGE OVER THE PAST 24
HOURS.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 15/2100Z TO 16/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT MOSTLY QUIET TO ACTIVE
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS.  ISOLATED PERIODS OF MINOR
STORM CONDITIONS WERE OBSERVED AT HIGH LATITUDE STATIONS
DURING LOCAL NIGHTTIME.  A FAVORABLY POSITIONED RECURRENT
CORONAL HOLE IS BELIEVED BE TO RESPONSIBLE FOR THE ELEVATED
LEVELS.  THE GREATER THAN 2 MEV ELECTRON FLUX WAS AT NORMAL
TO MODERATE BACKGROUND LEVELS UNTIL 16/1700UT WHEN THE
LEVELS REACHED HIGH.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE MOSTLY QUIET TO UNSETTLED, AT THE MIDDLE
LATITUDES, ON DAY ONE AND MOSTLY QUIET FOR THE REMAINDER OF
THE PERIOD.  HIGH LATITUDES WILL EXPERIENCE UNSETTLED TO
ACTIVE CONDITIONS FOR THE FIRST DAY AND MOSTLY QUIET TO
UNSETTLED THEREAFTER.
