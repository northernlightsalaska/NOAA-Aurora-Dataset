IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 21/2100Z

OF NOTE. NEW REGION 7793 (N04E12) WAS NUMBERED TODAY.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
VERY LOW TO LOW. REGION 7790 HAS THE BEST CHANCE OF PRODUCING
C-CLASS ACTIVITY AND AN OUTSIDE CHANCE OF AN ISOLATED M-CLASS
FLARE.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 21/2100Z TO 22/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO ACTIVE LEVELS
FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS. A SUDDEN IMPULSE OF 24 NT OCCURRED AT
22/1213Z, PROBABLY THE RESULT OF THE ANTICIPATED SHOCK FRONT
RESULTING FROM THE M3/1F PROTON PRODUCING TENFLARE ON 19/2127Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO MINOR STORM FOR THE NEXT 24 TO 36
HOURS. ACTIVITY SHOULD GRADUALLY DIMINISH DURING THE LAST TWO
DAYS OF THE FORECAST PERIOD. HIGH LATITUDE STATIONS SHOULD SEE
MINOR TO MAJOR STORMING FOR THE NEXT 48 HOURS, ESPECIALLY
DURING LOCAL NIGHTTIME.
