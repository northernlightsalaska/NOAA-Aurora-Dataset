JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 253 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 10 SEP 1994
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 09/2100Z
TO 10/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS VERY LOW. OCCASIONAL B-CLASS
X-RAY BURSTS OCCURRED. THE LARGEST WAS A B7/SF AT 10/0950Z FROM
REGION 7774 (N11W67). ALL REGIONS WERE STABLE THROUGHOUT THE
PERIOD. NO NEW REGIONS WERE ASSIGNED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
VERY LOW.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 09/2100Z TO 10/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO UNSETTLED LEVELS
FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS AT MIDDLE LATITUDES. A BRIEF EPISODE OF
MINOR STORMING OCCURRED NEAR 10/0300Z. HIGH LATITUDE ACTIVITY
RANGED FROM UNSETTLED TO MAJOR STORM LEVELS WITH MOST
STORMING OBSERVED BETWEEN 10/0900-1500Z. THE STORMING WAS
PROBABLY DUE TO CORONAL HOLE EFFECTS. THE GT 2 MEV ENERGETIC
ELECTRON FLUX WAS IN THE HIGH RANGE.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE DURING THE FIRST DAY WITH
BRIEF PERIODS OF MINOR TO MAJOR STORMING POSSIBLE DURING LOCAL
NIGHTTIMES. UNSETTLED CONDITIONS ARE EXPECTED FOR THE BALANCE
OF THE PERIOD.
