JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 164 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 13 JUN 1994
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 12/2100Z
TO 13/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY CONTINUED AT A VERY LOW LEVEL.
REGIONS 7730 (S09W04) AND 7731 (N08E02) DISPLAYED SOME MINOR
MIXED POLARITIES.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY SHOULD CONTINUE
AT A VERY LOW LEVEL. AN ISOLATED LOW C-CLASS BURST IS
POSSIBLE FROM SEVERAL OF THE SMALL REGIONS ON THE DISK.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 12/2100Z TO 13/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS MOSTLY QUIET TO UNSETTLED. A FEW
HIGH LATITUDE SITES EXPERIENCED ACTIVE CONDITIONS NEAR
MIDDAY. ENERGETIC ELECTRON FLUXES WERE AT MODERATE TO HIGH
LEVELS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD
SHOULD BE PREDOMINANTLY QUIET TO UNSETTLED FOR 14 JUN AND
FOR THE FIRST HALF OF 15 JUN. MINOR TO MAJOR STORMING SHOULD
BEGIN NEAR THAT TIME AND CONTINUE THROUGH 16 JUN. THIS
FORECAST IS BASED UPON A FAVORABLY POSITIONED DISAPPEARING
FILAMENT AND PROBABLE CORONAL MASS EJECTION OBSERVED ON 12
JUN.
