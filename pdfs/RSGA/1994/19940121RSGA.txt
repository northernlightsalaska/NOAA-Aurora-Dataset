SDF NUMBER 021 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 21 JAN 1994
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 20/2100Z
TO 21/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS VERY LOW. REGION 7654 (N08E09)
PRODUCED SEVERAL SUBFLARES AND APPEARS TO BE ROTATING
COUNTERCLOCKWISE, BUT HAS REMAINED MOSTLY STABLE IN WHITE
LIGHT AND H-ALPHA.  NEW REGION 7657 (N12W20) EMERGED
RAPIDLY ON THE DISK.  IT IS MAGNETICALLY SIMPLE BUT WITH AN
EAST-WEST INVERSION LINE. A DARK FILAMENT NEAR S23W03
DISAPPEARED BETWEEN 1747 AND 2015 UT.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW.  ERUPTIVE EVENTS ARE EXPECTED FROM REGION 7654 AND POSSIBLY
FROM 7657, IF RAPID GROWTH CONTINUES IN THAT REGION.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 20/2100Z TO 21/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT MOSTLY QUIET LEVELS
FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS. ACTIVITY THAT WAS EXPECTED FROM AN
ISOLATED, EQUATORIAL CORONAL HOLE HAS NOT OCCURRED.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO PERSIST AT QUIET TO OCCASIONALLY UNSETTLED LEVELS
THROUGHOUT THE FORECAST PERIOD. TODAY'S FILAMENT DISAPPEARANCE
WILL BE EVALUATED FOR POSSIBLE EFFECTS ON 25-26 JANUARY.
