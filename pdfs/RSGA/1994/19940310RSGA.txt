JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 069 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 10 MAR 1994
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 09/2100Z
TO 10/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS VERY LOW WITH FIVE OPTICALLY
UNCORRELATED B-CLASS X-RAY EVENTS OBSERVED THIS PERIOD.  ALL
REGIONS ARE EXHIBITING SLOW DECAY.  NEW REGION 7688 (N18E75) WAS
NUMBERED THIS PERIOD.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
AT VERY LOW TO LOW LEVELS.  REGION 7685 (S08W40) APPEARS TO
HAVE THE GREATEST POTENTIAL OF PRODUCING C-CLASS ACTIVITY.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 09/2100Z TO 10/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT MOSTLY ACTIVE TO MINOR
STORM LEVELS THE PAST 24 HOURS.  PERIODS OF MAJOR STORM
LEVELS WERE OBSERVED AT HIGH LATITUDES.  STORM CONDITIONS
ARE BELEIVED TO BE THE RESULT OF A FAVORABLY POSITIONED
CORONAL HOLE.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT MOSTLY ACTIVE LEVELS FOR THE NEXT TWO DAYS.
PERIODS OF MINOR STORM CONDITIONS CAN BE EXPECTED DURING
HIGH LATITUDE LOCAL NIGHTTIME.  THE FIELD IS EXPECTED TO BE
MOSTLY UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE ON DAY THREE.
