JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 251 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 08 SEP 1994
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 07/2100Z
TO 08/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW.  AN OPTICALLY UNCORRELATED
C1 X-RAY EVENT OCCURRED AT 08/0129Z.  OTHER ACTIVITY INCLUDED A
B8/SF X-RAY FLARE FROM REGION 7775 (N16W04) AT 07/2057Z.  A WEAK
TYPE II SWEEP WAS ASSOCIATED WITH THIS EVENT.  ESTIMATED SHOCK
VELOCITY WAS 400 KM/SEC.  ALL REGIONS ON THE DISK ARE IN SLOW
DECAY.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
VERY LOW.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 07/2100Z TO 08/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT UNSETTLED TO MINOR STORM
LEVELS.  A FAVORABLY POSITIONED CORONAL HOLE IS MOST LIKELY
THE CAUSE OF THE ACTIVITY.  THE GT 2 MEV ENERGETIC ELECTRON
FLUX WAS IN THE HIGH RANGE.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE MOSTLY UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE WITH ISOLATED
PERIODS OF MINOR TO MAJOR STORM CONDITIONS PERSISTING FOR THE
NEXT TWO DAYS.  CONDITIONS ARE EXPECTED TO RELAX TO MOSTLY
UNSETTLED ON DAY THREE.
