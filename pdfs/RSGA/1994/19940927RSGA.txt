JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 270 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 27 SEP 1994
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 26/2100Z
TO 27/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS VERY LOW.  FOUR WEAK B-BLASS
X-RAY EVENTS OCCURRED THIS PERIOD -- 2 OPTICALLY UNCORRELATED
AND 2 OPTICALLY CORRELATED WITH REGION 7781 (S06W61).  THE
LARGEST OF THESE FLARES WAS A B2/SF WHICH MAXED AT 27/1515UT.
ALL REGIONS EXHIBIT SLOW DECAY.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
VERY LOW.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 26/2100Z TO 27/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO ACTIVE LEVELS
FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS.  A BRIEF PERIOD OF MAJOR STORM
LEVELS WAS REACHED AT HIGH LATITUDES FROM 27/0900 - 1200UT.
THE GREATER THAN 2 MEV ENERGETIC ELECTRON FLUX WAS MOSTLY
MODERATE REACHING HIGH LEVELS AFTER 27/1800UT.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE MOSTLY QUIET TO UNSETTLED FOR THE NEXT THREE
DAYS.  OCCASIONAL ACTIVE CONDITIONS COULD EXIST AT HIGH
LATITUDES ON DAY ONE OF THE FORECAST PERIOD.
