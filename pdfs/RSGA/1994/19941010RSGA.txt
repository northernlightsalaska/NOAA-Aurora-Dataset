JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 283 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 10 OCT 1994
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 09/2100Z
TO 10/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS VERY LOW. REGION 7785 (S08W72)
STABILIZED AND BURSTS FROM THAT REGION DIMINISHED. REGION 7789
(N08E60) IS AN E CLASS SUNSPOT GROUP WITH MINOR MIXED POLARITIES
IN THE CENTRAL PORTION OF THIS EXTENDED REGION.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY SHOULD CONTINUE
AT MOSTLY A VERY LOW LEVEL. ISOLATED C-CLASS FLARES ARE
POSSIBLE FROM REGIONS 7785 AND 7789.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 09/2100Z TO 10/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS QUIET TO UNSETTLED. ENERGETIC
ELECTRON FLUXES WERE MOSTLY IN THE HIGH RANGE.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD
SHOULD BE QUIET TO UNSETTLED FOR MOST OF THE FORECAST
PERIOD. A SLIGHT DISTURBANCE IS EXPECTED TO BEGIN DURING
THE LATTER HALF OF 13 OCT WHICH MAY RESULT IN ISOLATED
ACTIVE PERIODS. THIS DISTURBANCE IS FORECAST DUE TO A
SMALL CORONAL HOLE NOW LOCATED IN THE SOUTHWEST QUADRANT.
