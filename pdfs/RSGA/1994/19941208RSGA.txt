JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 342 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 08 DEC 1994
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 07/2100Z
TO 08/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS VERY LOW. A NEW REGION 7815
(S09E63) WAS ASSIGNED TODAY AND PRODUCED SEVERAL B-CLASS SUB-
FLARES. THE REGION APPEARS TO BE A SIMPLE B-TYPE SPOT GROUP
IN WHITE LIGHT. REGIONS 7814 (S14E38) AND 7813 (S08E10) WERE
ESSENTIALLY STABLE AND DECREASED IN AREA DURING THE PERIOD.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
VERY LOW TO LOW. THERE IS A FAIR CHANCE FOR AN ISOLATED C-CLASS
FLARE FROM REGION 7815.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 07/2100Z TO 08/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS PREDOMINANTLY QUIET TO UNSETTLED
FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS. ISOLATED ACTIVE CONDITIONS WERE
OBSERVED BETWEEN 0000-0300UT AT SOME LOCATIONS. THE
GREATER THAN 2 MEV ELECTRON FLUXES WERE AT MODERATE TO
HIGH LEVELS DURING THE PERIOD.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE QUIET TO UNSETTLED FOR THE NEXT THREE DAYS.
