JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 349 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 15 DEC 1994
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 14/2100Z
TO 15/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS VERY LOW.  REGION 7815 (S10W28)
PRODUCED A B3/SF FLARE AT 15/0952UT.  NO OTHER ACTIVITY OF
CONSEQUENCE HAS BEEN OBSERVED.  REGION 7815 HAS EXHIBITED SOME
DECAY, PARTICULARILY IN ITS AREAL COVERAGE.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW.  REGION 7815 STILL HAS THE POTENTIAL TO PRODUCE C-CLASS
ACTIVITY.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 14/2100Z TO 15/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT MOSTLY QUIET TO ACTIVE
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS.  PERIODS OF MINOR TO MAJOR
LEVELS WERE RECORDED AT HIGH LATITUDE STATIONS DURING THEIR
LOCAL NIGHTTIME.  A FAVORABLY POSITIONED RECURRENT CORONAL
HOLE FEATURE IS THOUGHT TO BE RESPONSIBLE FOR THE ELEVATED
LEVELS.  THE GREATER THAN 2 MEV ELECTRON FLUX WAS NORMAL TO
MODERATE.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE MOSTLY UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE FOR THE NEXT TWO
DAYS.  MINOR TO MAJOR STORM CONDITIONS CAN BE EXPECTED AT HIGH
LATITUDE STATIONS DURING LOCAL NIGHTTIME.  CONDITIONS ARE
EXPECTED TO RELAX TO MOSTLY QUIET BY DAY THREE OF THE PERIOD.
