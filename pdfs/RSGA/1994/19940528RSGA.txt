JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 148 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 28 MAY 1994
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 27/2100Z
TO 28/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS VERY LOW. THE SOLAR DISK IS
SPOTLESS. NO EVENTS WERE OBSERVED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
VERY LOW.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 27/2100Z TO 28/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS QUIET UNTIL ABOUT 28/1000Z WHEN
THE FIELD GRADUALLY BECAME DISTURBED. UNSETTLED TO STORM
CONDITIONS WERE OBSERVED THEREAFTER. MAJOR STORM CONDITIONS
OCCURRED AT MOST STATIONS FROM 28/1500-1800Z. THIS IS THOUGHT
TO BE THE BEGINNING OF A RECURRENT DISTURBANCE ASSOCIATED WITH
A CORONAL HOLE.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT ACTIVE TO STORM CONDITIONS FOR THE THREE
DAY FORECAST PERIOD. MAJOR TO SEVERE STORM PERIODS ARE POSSIBLE
DURING LOCAL NIGHTTIME.
