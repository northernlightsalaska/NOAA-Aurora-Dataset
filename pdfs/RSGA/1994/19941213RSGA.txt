SDF NUMBER 347 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 13 DEC 1994
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 12/2100Z
TO 13/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. REGION 7815 (S10W03)
PRODUCED THE ONLY C-CLASS FLARE SINCE YESTERDAY, A C6 WHICH
BEGAN AT 13/2055Z AND REACHED A MAXIMUM AT 13/2101Z. AN
OPTICAL FLARE REPORT WAS NOT AVAILABLE AT THIS WRITING. GROWTH
IN THIS REGION HAS SLOWED AND SO HAS ITS PRODUCTION OF SMALL
SUBFLARES.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. AN M-FLARE IS STILL POSSIBLE IN REGION 7815.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 12/2100Z TO 13/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS QUIET TO UNSETTLED DURING MOST OF
THE PAST DAY. MOST STATIONS EXPERIENCED ACTIVE CONDITIONS FROM
13/0600-0900Z. THE GREATER THAN 2 MEV ELECTRON FLUENCE AT
GEOSYNCHRONOUS ALTITUDES WAS MODERATE.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE QUIET TO UNSETTLED FOR THE NEXT DAY OR SO. IT
MAY BECOME ACTIVE BY 16 DECEMBER DUE TO A RECURRING DISTURBANCE.
