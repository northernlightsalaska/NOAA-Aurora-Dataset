JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 188 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 07 JUL 1994
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 06/2100Z
TO 07/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE DUE TO AN M1/1N
FLARE IN REGION 7746 (N11E10) AT 07/1002Z. THE FLARE WAS
ASSOCIATED WITH TYPE II/IV SWEEPS AND MINOR DISCRETE RADIO
BURSTS ACROSS THE SPECTRUM. THIS SUNSPOT REGION HAS DECREASED
IN AREA SINCE YESTERDAY. REGION 7747 (S15E21) IS GROWING,
INCREASING SPOT AREA AND DEVELOPING PENUMBRA AT BOTH POLES.
NEW REGIONS 7748 (S03W23) AND 7749 (S09E04) EMERGED ON THE
DISK.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW. FLARES ARE EXPECTED TO CONTINUE IN REGION 7746. ANOTHER
SMALL M-CLASS FLARE IS POSSIBLE BUT UNLIKELY.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 06/2100Z TO 07/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD GRADUALLY BECAME SOMEWHAT DISTURBED
AFTER 06/2100Z. UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE CONDITIONS OCCURRED AT MOST
LOCATIONS THEREAFTER. STORM CONDITIONS WERE RECORDED AT A FEW
STATIONS. THE GREATER THAN 2 MEV ELECTRON FLUX AT GEOSYNCHRONOUS
ALTITUDE WAS MODERATE FOR MOST OF THE DAY.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE WITH LOCAL NIGHTTIME
SUBSTORMS POSSIBLE.
