JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 041 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 10 FEB 1994
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 09/2100Z
TO 10/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW.  RGN 7665 (N03W80)
PRODUCED AN EPL/BSL LIMB EVENT WHICH BEGAN AT 10/1825Z.
MATERIAL FROM THIS SURGE REACHED A MAXIMUM OF 0.38 RV.  ALONG
WITH THIS LIMB EVENT WAS A C1 X-RAY EVENT WHICH MAXED AT
10/1834Z.  RGN 7668 (N09E25) CONTINUES TO GROW BUT STILL
EXHIBITS A WEAK MAGNETIC CONFIGURATION.  THE REMAINDER OF THE
DISK AND LIMB IS STABLE.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
VERY LOW TO LOW.  WE'LL WATCH THE DEVELOPMENT OF RGN 7668 AND
THE RETURN OF OLD REGION 7654.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 09/2100Z TO 10/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS.  ISOLATED MAJOR STORM COND-
ITIONS WERE REPORTED AT HIGH LATITUDES.  A FAVORABLY POSI-
TIONED CORONAL HOLE IS BELIEVED TO BE RESPONSIBLE FOR THE
STORM CONDITIONS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO REMAIN AT MOSTLY ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM LEVELS FOR
THE DURATION OF THE FORECAST PERIOD.  OCCASIONAL PERIODS OF
MAJOR STORM CONDITIONS ARE STILL LIKELY.  DISTURBED GEOMAG-
NETIC CONDITIONS SHOULD BE EXPECTED FOR THE NEXT FIVE DAYS.
