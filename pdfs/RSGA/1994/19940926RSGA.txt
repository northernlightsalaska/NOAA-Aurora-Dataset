JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 269 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 26 SEP 1994
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 25/2100Z
TO 26/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS VERY LOW.  NO FLARES WERE
REPORTED THIS PERIOD.  REGION 7781 (S07W47) INDICATED SLIGHT
AREAL GROWTH IN THE LEADER SPOT GROUP WHILE LOSING MOST OF ITS
TRAILER SPOTS.  REGION 7782 (N03W48) GREW SLIGHTLY IN BOTH AREA
AND SPOT NUMBER.  NEW REGION 7783 (S07E74) WAS NUMBERED THIS
PERIOD AS AN HSX ALPHA GROUP.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
VERY LOW.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 25/2100Z TO 26/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT MOSTLY UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS.  A PERIOD OF MINOR TO MAJOR
STORM CONDITIONS EXISTED AT ALL LATITUDES FROM 26/09-15Z.
THIS ACTIVITY MAY BE ASSOCIATED WITH A SMALL CORONAL HOLE.
THE GREATER THAN 2 MEV ENERGETIC ELECTRON FLUX WAS MOSTLY
NORMAL TO MODERATE BUT BECAME HIGH AFTER 26/1800Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE MOSTLY QUIET TO UNSETTLED FOR THE NEXT THREE
DAYS.  OCCASIONAL ACTIVE CONDITIONS COULD EXIST AT HIGH
LATITUDES.
