JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 289 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 16 OCT 1994
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 15/2100Z
TO 16/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS VERY LOW.  ONLY ONE OPTICALLY
UNCORRELATED XRAY EVENT WAS RECORDED THIS PERIOD--A B2 WHICH
MAXED AT 16/0123Z.  OTHER ACTIVITY CONSISTED OF WEAK LOW
FREQUENCY RADIO EMISSIONS FROM 245 MHZ.  REGION 7790 (N11E16)
INDICATED SLIGHT GROWTH IN THE LEADER PENUMBRA WITH LITTLE
CHANGE NOTED ELSEWHERE IN THE REGION.  REGION 7787 (N08W80)
LOST ITS TWO SPOT CONFIGURATION, DECAYING TO JUST AN AREA OF
PLAGE.  NO OTHER SIGNIFICANT CHANGE NOTED IN REGIONS 7788
(S02W68) AND REGION 7789 (N10W24).
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW.  C-CLASS FLARING IS EXPECTED FROM REGION 7790 WITH
ISOLATED M-CLASS ACTIVITY POSSIBLE.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 15/2100Z TO 16/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO UNSETTLED LEVELS
FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS.  THE GREATER THAN 2 MEV ENERGETIC
ELECTRON FLUX WAS AT MOSTLY MODERATE LEVELS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE PREDOMINATELY QUIET WITH SOME ISOLATED
UNSETTLED PERIODS.
