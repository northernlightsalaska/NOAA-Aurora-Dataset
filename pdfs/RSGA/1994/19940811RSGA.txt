JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 223 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 11 AUG 1994
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 10/2100Z
TO 11/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY CONTINUED VERY LOW. REGION 7762
(N04W51) SHOWED TRAILER DECAY. A SMALL H CLASS SPOT ROTATED
AROUND THE EAST LIMB AND WAS NUMBERED AS NEW REGION 7764
(S05E68).
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY SHOULD CONTINUE
AT THE VERY LOW LEVEL. THERE IS A SLIGHT PROBABILITY OF A
C-CLASS FLARE FROM EITHER REGION 7762 OR 7764.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 10/2100Z TO 11/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS MOSTLY UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE. SOME
HIGH LATITUDE SITES EXPERIENCED BRIEF PERIODS OF MINOR
STORMING IN THE 1200-1500Z REPORTING PERIOD. ENERGETIC
ELECTRON FLUXES BEGAN TO INCREASE NEAR 1300Z AND REACHED
MODERATE LEVELS AT THE END OF THE PERIOD.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD
SHOULD BE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE ON 12 AUG. MOSTLY UNSETTLED
CONDITIONS ARE FORECAST FOR 13-14 AUG BUT ISOLATED ACTIVE
INTERVALS ARE LIKELY DURING THAT TIME. ENERGETIC ELECTRON
FLUXES AT GEOSYNCHRONOUS ORBIT COULD REACH HIGH LEVELS ON
12 AUG.
