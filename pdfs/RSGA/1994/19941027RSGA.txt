JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 300 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 27 OCT 1994
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 26/2100Z
TO 27/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS VERY LOW. X-RAY FLUXES BARELY
BUDGED FROM BACKGROUND LEVELS ALL DAY. THERE ARE STILL FIVE
SPOTTED REGIONS VISIBLE, BUT ALL ARE SIMPLE AND QUIET AT THIS
TIME.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW. ISOLATED C-CLASS ACTIVITY IS POSSIBLE FROM REGION 7794
(N16E48).
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 26/2100Z TO 27/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS QUIET TO UNSETTLED. THE SHOCK
RELATED TO ACTIVITY ON 25 OCTOBER HAS YET TO PASS. THE
GREATER THAN 2 MEV ELECTRON FLUX PERSISTS AT HIGH LEVELS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE AT ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM LEVELS BOTH EARLY AND
LATE IN THE PERIOD. A SHOCK SHOULD DISTURB THE FIELD SOON, THE
DISTURBANCE WEAKEN ON DAY TWO, AND THEN CORONAL HOLE EFFECTS
DOMINATE ON DAY THREE.
