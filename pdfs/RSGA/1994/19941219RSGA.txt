IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 18/2100Z

RESPONSIBLE FOR A C4/1N FLARE WITH AN ASSOCIATED TYPE II SWEEP
AT 19/0440Z. THIS REGION ALSO PRODUCED A B2/SF FLARE AT
19/0912Z. IT HAS SHOWN SLOW GROWTH OVER THE PAST 24 HOURS.
REGION 7815 (S12W82) IS SLOWLY DECAYING AS IT ROTATES OFF THE
WEST LIMB. IT HAS BEEN QUIET, ONLY PRODUCING A SMALL SUBFAINT
FLARE. NO NEW REGIONS WERE NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
VERY LOW TO LOW. REGION 7817 HAS A GOOD CHANCE OF PRODUCING
C-CLASS FLARES AND AN OUTSIDE CHANCE OF AN ISOLATED M-CLASS
FLARE. REGION 7815 HAS A DIMINSHED CHANCE OF C-CLASS FLARING AS
IT LEAVES THE DISK.

IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE QUIET TO UNSETTLED FOR THE ENTIRE FORECAST
PERIOD.
