JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 042 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 11 FEB 1994
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 10/2100Z
TO 11/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. TWO OPTICALLY UNCORRELATED
C1 X-RAY EVENTS WERE RECORDED DURING THIS PERIOD.  REGION 7669
(N06E72) WAS NUMBERED THIS PERIOD.  THIS HERALDS THE RETURN OF
OLD REGION 7654 WHICH WAS QUITE ACTIVE ITS LAST PASS WITH 4 M-
CLASS AND 27 C-CLASS FLARES.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
VERY LOW TO LOW.  REGION 7669 WILL BE CLOSELY WATCHED AS IT
ROTATES MORE FULLY ONTO THE DISK.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 10/2100Z TO 11/2100Z:
AT MIDDLE LATITUDES, THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT ACTIVE
TO MINOR STORM LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS.  HIGH LATITUDES
HAVE EXPERIENCED MOSTLY MINOR TO SEVERE STORM CONDITIONS FOR
THE SAME PERIOD.  A FAVORABLY POSITIONED CORONAL HOLE IS
BELIEVED TO BE RESPOPNSIBLE FOR THIS LENGTHY STORM.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO REMAIN AT ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM LEVELS FOR THE
FIRST TWO DAYS OF THE FORECAST PERIOD WITH OCCASIONAL PERIODS
OF MAJOR TO SEVERE STORM CONDITIONS AT HIGH LATITUDES.
ACTIVITY SHOULD BEGIN TO MODERATE ON DAY THREE TO MOSTLY
UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE WITH SOME NIGHTTIME MINOR STORM PERIODS
AT HIGH LATITUDES.
