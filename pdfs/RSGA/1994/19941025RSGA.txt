JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 298 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 25 OCT 1994
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 24/2100Z
TO 25/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. REGION 7792 (S06W23)
PRODUCED A C4/0F WITH STRONG TYPE II AT 1009Z. MODEST DISCRETE
BURSTS ACROSS THE RADIO SPECTRUM ACCOMPANIED THE EVENT. THE
REGION HAS BEEN QUIET SINCE. A NEW REGION ROTATED FULLY INTO
VIEW, 7794 (N15E69).
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
VERY LOW TO LOW. AN ISOLATED C-CLASS FLARE IS POSSIBLE FROM
EITHER OF THE TWO SPOTTED REGIONS.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 24/2100Z TO 25/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD VARIED FROM QUIET TO MINOR STORM
LEVELS, WITH THE MOST DISTURBED TIMES OCCURRING DURING LOCAL
NIGHTTIMES. AN ENHANCEMENT IN THE GREATER THAN 10 MEV PROTON
FLUX BEGAN AROUND 1100Z, JUST AFTER THE C4 FLARE. THE PEAK
FLUX SEEN WAS NEAR 1 PFU. THE GREATER THAN 2 MEV ELECTRON
FLUX WAS AT HIGH LEVELS ALL DAY.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE MOSTLY UNSETTLED FOR THE NEXT 36 HOURS. A
SHOCK RELATED TO TODAY'S ACTIVITY FROM REGION 7792 IS EXPECTED
TO BRING ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM CONDITIONS THE LATTER HALF OF
THE PERIOD.
