JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 172 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 21 JUN 1994
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 20/2100Z
TO 21/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS VERY LOW.  NO SIGNIFICANT
ACTIVITY WAS RECORDED OVER THE PAST 24 HOURS.  REGION 7736
(S16W06) RE-EMERGED AS A 2 SPOT BXO BETA.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
VERY LOW.  HOWEVER, DUE TO THE CLOSE PROXIMITY OF REGION 7737
(S11E20) AND 7739 (S11E16), THESE REGIONS BEAR WATCHING AS A
POTENTIAL AREA FOR ISOLATED C-CLASS ACIVITY.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 20/2100Z TO 21/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO UNSETTLED LEVELS
FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS.  ISOLATED PERIODS OF ACTIVE CONDITIONS
WERE OBSERVED AT ALL LATITUDES DURING LOCAL NIGHTTIME.  THE
GT 2 MEV ELECTRON FLUX WAS IN THE MODERATE TO HIGH RANGE.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE MOSTLY QUIET TO UNSETTLED FOR THE NEXT THREE
DAYS WITH SOME ISLOATED PERIODS OF ACTIVE CONDITIONS EXPECTED
ON DAY THREE OF THE FORECAST PERIOD.
