JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 293 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 20 OCT 1994
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 19/2100Z
TO 20/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 7790 (N11W39)
PRODUCED AN M3/1F TENFLARE AT THE END OF THE FORECAST PERIOD
YESTERDAY. THIS TURNED OUT TO BE A PROTON PRODUCING FLARE THAT
MAXED AT 35 PFU GREATER THAN 10 MEV AT 20/0340Z. THERE WAS NO
OTHER SIGNIFICANT ACTIVITY NOTED. NO NEW REGIONS WERE NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
VERY LOW TO LOW. REGION 7790 HAS A GOOD CHANCE OF PRODUCING
C-CLASS FLARES AND AN OUTSIDE CHANCE OF PRODUCING ANOTHER
ISOLATED M-CLASS FLARE. REGION 7792 (S08E47) ALSO APPEARS TO
HAVE C-CLASS FLARE POTENTIAL.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 19/2100Z TO 20/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO UNSETTLED
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE FOR THE NEXT 24 HOURS, THEN
ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM LEVELS FOR THE REMAINDER OF THE FORECAST
PERIOD. HIGH LATITUDE STATIONS ARE EXPECTED TO SEE MINOR TO
SEVERE STORMING DURING THE LAST 2 DAY'S OF THE FORECAST PERIOD.
ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED AS AN EFFECT OF THE APPARENT CORONAL MASS
EJECTION ASSOCIATED WITH THE M3/1F FLARE IN REGION 7790.
