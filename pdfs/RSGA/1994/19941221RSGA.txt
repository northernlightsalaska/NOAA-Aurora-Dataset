JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 355 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 21 DEC 1994
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 20/2100Z
TO 21/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS VERY LOW. THERE WERE NO EVENTS
OF NOTE. REGION 7817 (S01E18) MANAGED TO PRODUCE ONLY TWO SMALL
B-CLASS FLARES. THIS REGION HAS LOST PENUMBRA SIZE BUT IS NOW
A LITTLE MORE COMPLEX. NO NEW REGIONS WERE NUMBERED.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
VERY LOW TO LOW. REGION 7817 STILL HAS A FAIR CHANCE OF C-CLASS
FLARING AND AN OUTSIDE CHANCE OF AN ISOLATED M-CLASS FLARE.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 20/2100Z TO 21/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO UNSETTLED LEVELS
FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE QUIET TO UNSETTLED FOR THE NEXT 24 HOURS, THEN
UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE FOR THE REMAINDER OF THE FORECAST PERIOD.
HIGH LATITUDE STATIONS CAN EXPECT MINOR TO MAJOR STORM LEVELS,
ESPECIALLY AT LOCAL NIGHTTIME, BY THE THIRD DAY OF THE PERIOD.
ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED BECAUSE OF A FAVORABLY POSITIONED CORONAL
HOLE.
