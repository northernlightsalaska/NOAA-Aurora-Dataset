JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 029 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 29 JAN 1994
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 28/2100Z
TO 29/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. TWO UNASSOCIATED
M-2 X-RAY FLARES OCCURRED, AT 0413Z AND AT 1129Z. THE LIKELY
SOURCE OF THIS ACTIVITY IS NOW-DEPARTED REGION 7654, A DAY PAST
WEST LIMB AT N09. SURGING WAS SEEN AT THIS LATITUDE MOST OF
THE DAY. APART FROM THIS, LITTLE OF SIGNIFICANCE OCCURRED ON
THE VISIBLE DISK. REGION 7658 (N08W76) DID MUSTER A C6/0F FLARE
AT 0905Z. THE REGION IS NOW REPORTED TO BE IN DECAY.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW. REGION 7654 MAY STILL RECORD A SMALL M-CLASS EVENT IN
THE NEAR TERM.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 28/2100Z TO 29/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS QUIET TO UNSETTLED.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE MOSTLY UNSETTLED. EPISODES OF ACTIVE CONDITIONS
ARE POSSIBLE ON 01 FEB IF EFFECTS OF YESTERDAY'S SMALL
DISAPPEARING FILAMENT MATERIALIZE.
