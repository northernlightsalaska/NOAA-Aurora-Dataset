JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 016 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 16 JAN 1994
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 15/2100Z
TO 16/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW.  ACTIVITY THIS PERIOD
CONSISTED OF TWO OPTICALLY UNCORRELATED C-CLASS EVENTS (C1.1 AND
C8.5), A C1.4 SUBFLARE FROM NEWLY NUMBERED REGION 7654 (N08E74),
AND LIMB SURGING, AGAIN FROM 7654.  WHITE LIGHT REPORTS INDICATE
THIS REGION HAS DEVELOPED INTO A BETA-GAMMA AS IT SLOWLY ROTATES
ONTO THE DISK.  REGION 7655 (S07W24) WAS ALSO NUMBERED LAST
NIGHT.  REGIONS 7650 AND 7653 ROTATED OFF THE DISK.  ALL OTHER
REGIONS ARE STABLE.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO
REMAIN LOW.  REGION 7654 CONTINUES TO GROW AS IT ROTATES
ONTO THE DISK AND OFFERS THE GREATEST PROBABILITY FOR C-
CLASS AND POSSIBLE M-CLASS ACTIVITY.

THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT MOSTLY UNSETTLED LEVELS
AT MIDDLE LATITUDES AND AT MOSTLY ACTIVE LEVELS IN THE HIGHER
LATITUDES.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO REMAIN AT MOSTLY UNSETTLED LEVELS FOR THE NEXT
THREE DAYS.
