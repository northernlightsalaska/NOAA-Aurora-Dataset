SDF NUMBER 170
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 18 JUN 1988
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 17/2100Z
TO 18/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY CONTINUES AT A LOW LEVEL.  A C5/0F
FLARE FROM REGION 5047 (S17E23) AT 0015Z WAS THE MOST ENERGETIC
EVENT OF THE PERIOD.  THAT REGION EXHIBITS A MODERATE DEGREE
OF MAGNETIC COMPLEXITY AND HAS UNDERGONE RESTRUCTURING AND
GROWTH IN WHITE LIGHT.  REGION 5041 (N14W66), PRODUCER OF A FEW
SUBFLARES, ALSO IS SLIGHTLY COMPLEX IN STRUCTURE.  NEWLY
NUMBERED REGION 5053 (S21E76) ROTATED INTO VIEW AS A SINGLE
MATURE SPOT WITH PENUMBRA AND SOME PLAGE.  IT IS PROBABLY THE
RETURN OF OLD REGION 5027 WHICH GENERATED 6 M-CLASS FLARES
IN ITS LIFETIME.  SURGING HERALDED ITS ARRIVAL ON THE DISK.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW.  REGION 5047 MAY PRODUCE AN ISOLATED M-CLASS EVENT IN
THE NEXT THREE DAYS.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 17/2100Z TO 18/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE AT MID-
LATITUDES.  AT HIGH LATITUDES GENERALLY ACTIVE CONDITIONS
GAVE WAY TO MINOR STORM LEVELS THE PAST 6 HOURS.  THIS
ACTIVITY IS THOUGHT TO BE RELATED TO THE FLARE/CME ACTIVITY
OF JUNE 15.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD
SHOULD PERSIST AT ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM LEVELS THE NEXT 24
HOURS.  THE LAST TWO DAYS OF THE INTERVAL ARE LIKELY TO BE
UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE.
