FROM SPACE ENVIRONMENT SERVICES CENTER, BOULDER, COLORADO
SDF NUMBER 215
JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY.
ISSUED 2200Z 02 AUG 1988
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 01/2100Z
TO 02/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY HAS BEEN MODERATE.  REGION 5092
(N28W01) PRODUCED AN M1/1B EVENT THAT MAXED AT 1724 UT. IT WAS A
COMPACT FLARE, ASSOCIATED WITH AN IMPRESSIVE DARK SURGE ON THE
DISK AND A MAJOR TYPE II RADIO SWEEP.  REGION 5092 HAS SIMPLI-
FIED SOMEWHAT TODAY BUT REMAINS A THREAT FOR FURTHER ACTIVITY.
FIVE OF THE SIX SPOTTED REGIONS ON THE DISK PRODUCED SUBFLARE
ACTIVITY. NONE OF THE REGIONS SHOWED DRAMATIC GROWTH OR DECAY.
SEVERAL SEGMENTS OF A LONG INVERSION LINE FILAMENT NEAR S25E10
DISAPPEARED BETWEEN 01/1512 AND 02/0037. SURGING WAS REPORTED
ON THE EAST LIMB NEAR N22 AND N31.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
MODERATE.  REGION 5092 RETAINS THE POTENTIAL FOR CONTINUED
SIGNIFICANT ACTIVITY, WITH A SLIGHT POTENTIAL FOR MAJOR FLARE
ACTIVITY.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 01/2100Z TO 02/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT MOSTLY QUIET LEVELS
AT MIDDLE LATITUDES FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS. AT HIGH
LATITUDES, THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS PREDOMINATELY UNSETTLED.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE QUIET TO UNSETTLED TOMORROW, BUT COULD BECOME
UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE ON THE NEXT DAY DUE TO SOLAR EVENTS (FLARE
AND FILAMENT DISAPPEARANCE) ON 1 AUGUST. THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD
SHOULD RETURN TO GENERALLY QUIET LEVELS BY THE END OF THE
FORECAST PERIOD. THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD AT HIGH LATITUDES IS
EXPECTED TO REMAIN GENERALLY ACTIVE.
