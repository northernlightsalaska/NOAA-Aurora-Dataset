IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 26/2100Z
TO 27/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS MODERATE. REGION 5254 (S19E42)
PRODUCED AN M1/SF FLARE AT 26/2342Z AND REGION 5241 (N29W94)
PRODUCED AN M1/1N FLARE AT 27/0829Z. THERE WERE NO SIGNIFICANT
RADIO EMISSIONS ASSOCIATED WITH EITHER OF THESE FLARES. A
TYPE II RADIO SWEEP BURST WAS DETECTED AT 27/0219Z IN
CONJUNCTION WITH A C-CLASS SUBFLARE FROM REGION 5251 (N19E01).
THREE NEW REGIONS WERE ASSIGNED TODAY: 5256 (N23E02), A SIMPLE
BIPOLE WHICH WAS FORMERLY CONSIDERED AS THE TRAILER PORTION
OF 5251, 5257 (S17W08), A SMALL UNIPOLE, AND 5258 (N13E81), AN
H-TYPE GROUP WHICH MAY BE THE RETURN OF OLD REGION 5218.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW TO MODERATE. REGION 5254 RETAINS A MODERATE LEVEL OF
MAGNETIC COMPLEXITY PROVIDING A SLIGHT CHANCE FOR M-CLASS
ACTIVITY.
