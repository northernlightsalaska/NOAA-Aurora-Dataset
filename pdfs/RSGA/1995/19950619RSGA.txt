SDF NUMBER 170 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 19 JUN 1995
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 18/2100Z
TO 19/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS VERY LOW. THERE WAS NO FLARE
ACTIVITY OF SIGNIFICANCE. NO NEW REGIONS WERE NUMBERED. BOTH
SPOTTED REGIONS, REGION 7881 (N02W08) AND REGION 7882 (N07E60)
REMAINED STABLE.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
VERY LOW. REGION 7882 HAS A CHANCE OF ISOLATED C-CLASS ACTIVITY.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 18/2100Z TO 19/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO ACTIVE LEVELS
FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS. HIGH LATITUDE STATIONS EXPERIENCED
PERIODS OF MINOR TO MAJOR STORMING. ACTIVITY IS MOSTLY DUE TO
A WELL POSITIONED CORONAL HOLE. SOLAR WIND DATA INDICATED A
LOW DENSITY, HIGH VELOCITY WIND AT SATELLITE ALTITUDE.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE FOR THE NEXT 36 TO 48 HOURS
IN RESPONSE TO THE CORONAL HOLE. HIGH LATITUDE STATIONS WILL
CONTINUE TO SEE PERIODS OF MINOR TO MAJOR STORMING. ACTIVITY
WILL TAPER OFF TO MOSTLY UNSETTLED BY DAY THREE OF THE FORECAST
PERIOD.
