JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 237 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 25 AUG 1995
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 24/2100Z
TO 25/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS VERY LOW.  A B7/SF WAS OBSERVED
FROM REGION 7901 (N04W40) AT 25/0638UT.  WEAK RADIO EMISSION IN
THE 410-8800 MHZ RANGE WAS ALSO RECORDED WITH THIS EVENT.  BOTH
REGION 7901 AND 7902 (S10W35) HAVE SHOWN SOME WHITE LIGHT GROWTH
IN THE PAST 24 HOURS.  TWO NEW REGIONS WERE NUMBERED THIS
PERIOD.  REGION 7903 (N06E78) ROTATED ON THE DISK AS AN HSX
ALPHA SPOT AND REGION 7904 (N11E52) EMERGED AS A BXO BETA GROUP.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW.  REGIONS 7901 AND 7902 EXHIBIT THE BEST POTENTIAL FOR
C-CLASS ACTIVITY.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 24/2100Z TO 25/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT QUIET TO UNSETTLED LEVELS
UNTIL 24/2230UT WHEN A WEAK SUDDEN IMPULSE WAS OBSERVED.
AFTER THE ONSET OF THE SI, THE FIELD BECAME MOSTLY ACTIVE
WITH PERIODS OF MINOR TO SEVERE STORM CONDITIONS OBSERVED AT
HIGH LATITUDES.  SINCE 25/1500UT, THE FIELD CALMED DOWN TO
QUIET.  THE GREATER THAN 2 MEV ELECTRON FLUX WAS AT
BACKGROUND LEVELS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE QUIET TO UNSETTLED FOR THE NEXT THREE DAYS.
