JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 032 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 01 FEB 1995
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 31/2100Z
TO 01/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY BECAME MODERATE. REGION 7832
(S21W82) PRODUCED M1 FLARES AT 01/0906Z AND 01/0932Z. MINOR
RADIO BURSTS ACCOMPANIED THESE EVENTS. A TYPE II WAS OBSERVED
BETWEEN 01/0844-0847Z (BEFORE THE BEGIN TIME OF THE FIRST M-
CLASS FLARE). THERE WAS A POSSIBLE DELTA IN REGION 7832 ON
31 JAN. REGION 7834 (S13E49) PRODUCED A C7/1N FLARE AT
01/0826Z. THIS REGION IS GROWING AND MIXED POLARITIES WERE
OBSERVED HERE DURING THE PERIOD. AN EIGHT DEGREE LONG FILAMENT
LOCATED NEAR S23E33 FADED BETWEEN 01/1620-1650Z. A SPOTLESS
PLAGE NEAR S09E70 PRODUCED SEVERAL SUBFLARES AND WAS NUMBERED
AS NEW REGION 7835.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY SHOULD BE
GENERALLY LOW. ISOLATED M-CLASS FLARES ARE POSSIBLE FROM
REGIONS 7832 AND 7834. REGION 7832 WILL ROTATE FROM VIEW ON
02 FEB AND BECOME LESS OF A THREAT AFTER THAT TIME.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 31/2100Z TO 01/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS PREDOMINANTLY QUIET TO UNSETTLED.
SOME HIGH LATITUDE SITES EXPERIENCED ACTIVE CONDITIONS. THE
GT 2 MEV FLUXES WERE IN THE MODERATE TO HIGH RANGE.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD
SHOULD BE MOSTLY UNSETTLED FOR THE NEXT THREE DAYS. THERE
IS A POSSIBILITY OF BRIEF ACTIVE CONDITIONS ON 04 FEB IN
RESPONSE TO THE FLARE ACTIVITY AND TYPE II ON 01 FEB.
