JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 136 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 16 MAY 1995
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 15/2100Z
TO 16/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. REGION 7870 (N09W37) AND
7871 (S15W07) BOTH GREW RAPIDLY TODAY AND PRODUCED FREQUENT B
AND C-CLASS SUBFLARES. REGION 7870 IS THE LARGEST GROUP AT 220
MILLIONTHS AND PRODUCED TODAY'S LARGEST EVENT: A C4/SN AT 1129Z.
REGION 7871 IS A 130 MILLIONTHS D GROUP AND SHOWS SOME MAGNETIC
COMPLEXITY NEAR THE CENTER OF THE REGION. THE REMAINDER OF THE
SOLAR DISK WAS QUIET AND STABLE.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW. THERE IS A SLIGHT CHANCE FOR AN ISOLATED M-CLASS FLARE
FROM REGION 7870.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 15/2100Z TO 16/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD RANGED FROM QUIET TO SEVERE STORM LEVELS.
AN INITIALLY QUIET FIELD BECAME UNSETTLED TO SLIGHTLY ACTIVE
FROM ABOUT 0300Z TO 0800Z WITH SOME ISOLATED HIGH LATITUDE
MINOR STORM LEVELS BETWEEN 0500-0700Z. CONDITIONS QUIETED
BRIEFLY BUT BECAME MOSTLY ACTIVE FROM 1000Z THROUGH 1800Z.
DURING THE MOST DISTURBED PART OF THE LATTER PERIOD (1200-
1500Z), MID-LATITUDES ATTAINED MINOR STORM LEVELS AND HIGH
LATITUDES WERE AT MAJOR TO SEVERE STORM LEVELS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO SLIGHTLY ACTIVE DUE TO PERSISTENCE.
CONDITIONS SHOULD BE PREDOMINANTLY UNSETTLED FOR THE SECOND AND
THIRD DAYS.
