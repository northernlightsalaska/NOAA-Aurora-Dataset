JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 017 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 17 JAN 1995
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 16/2100Z
TO 17/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS VERY LOW. REGION 7827 (S07E11)
HAS CONTINUED TO GROW, PARTICULARLY IN THE TRAILER SPOTS. THE
REGION DID NOT PRODUCE ANY SIGNIFICANT FLARE ACTIVITY. NEW
REGION 7828 (S13W10) EMERGED ON THE DISK TODAY AND IS CURRENTLY
A SMALL C-TYPE SPOT GROUP. ANOTHER NEW REGION (7829 AT S14E80)
ROTATED INTO VIEW TODAY. THIS GROUP CORRESPONDS TO THE LIMB
ACTIVITY REPORTED YESTERDAY.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 16/2100Z TO 17/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD RANGED FROM QUIET TO ACTIVE LEVELS AT
MID-LATITUDES AND FROM QUIET TO SEVERE STORM AT HIGH LAT-
ITUDES. AN INITIALLY QUIET FIELD BECAME DISTURBED SHORTLY
AFTER 0600Z. PERIODS OF SUBSTORM ACTIVITY PERSISTED UNTIL
AROUND 1500Z, WITH THE HIGHER LATITUDE STATIONS SHOWING
PARTICULARLY STRONG ACTIVITY. THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS
PREDOMINANTLY QUIET TO UNSETTLED FOR THE REMAINDER OF THE
DAY, ALTHOUGH SOME ISOLATED ACTIVE PERIODS WERE OBSERVED IN
SOME LOCATIONS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE GENERALLY UNSETTLED FOR THE NEXT TWO DAYS.
LOCAL NIGHTTIME SUBSTORMING IS EXPECTED TO PERSIST. AN
INCREASE TO ACTIVE CONDITIONS IS EXPECTED ON THE THIRD DAY
(20 JANUARY) AS A RESPONSE TO A FAVORABLY POSITIONED CORONAL
HOLE.
