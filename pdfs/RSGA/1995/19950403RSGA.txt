JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 093 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 03 APR 1995
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 02/2100Z
TO 03/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY REMAINED VERY LOW. REGIONS 7858
(S15W74) AND 7860 (S02W61) WERE MOSTLY STABLE. THE FILAMENT
NEAR REGION 7860 WAS GENERALLY STABLE DURING THE PERIOD.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY SHOULD CONTINUE
AT VERY LOW LEVELS. THERE IS A SLIGHT CHANCE FOR A SMALL
C-CLASS FLARE FROM REGION 7858 OR 7860 BEFORE THEIR WEST LIMB
TRANSIT.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 02/2100Z TO 03/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS QUIET AT ALL LATITUDES DURING THE
PERIOD. ENERGETIC ELECTRON FLUXES WERE IN THE NORMAL TO
MODERATE RANGE.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD
SHOULD BE QUIET TO UNSETTLED FOR 04-05 APR. LATE ON 06 APR
A CORONAL HOLE RELATED DISTURBANCE IS EXPECTED TO BEGIN.
THE SOURCE HOLE IS THE SOUTHERN POLAR HOLE WHICH HAS A
LOBE EXTENDING TO LOWER LATITUDES. THIS WILL BE THE FOURTH
RECURRENCE OF THIS DISTURBANCE WHICH HAS INCREASED IN
DURATION AND INTENSITY EACH ROTATION. ACTIVE TO
ISOLATED MAJOR STORM CONDITIONS SHOULD BE EXPERIENCED WITH
THIS DISTURBANCE.
