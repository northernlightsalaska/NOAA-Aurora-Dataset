JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 183 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 02 JUL 1995
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 01/2100Z
TO 02/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. REGION 7887 (N10E52)
PRODUCED A C9/1B FLARE AT 02/0001Z. THIS FLARE WAS VERY
IMPULSIVE AT THE BEGINNING AND WAS FOLLOWED BY A LONG DURATION
TAIL WITH A LOW X-RAY OUTPUT. THE FLARE WAS ASSOCIATED WITH A
TYPE II RADIO SWEEP AND A DISCRETE RADIO BURST OF 350 SFU
AT 245 MHZ. OTHER REGIONS WERE QUIET.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
VERY LOW TO LOW. ANOTHER C-CLASS FLARE IS POSSIBLE IN REGION
7887.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 01/2100Z TO 02/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS QUIET. THE GREATER THAN 2 MEV ELECTRON
FLUX INCREASED FROM NORMAL TO MODERATE LEVELS AFTER 02/0900Z.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE QUIET TO UNSETTLED. INCREASED ACTIVITY MAY
OCCUR ON JULY 4 AND 5 DUE TO THE (POSSIBLE) CME ASSOCIATED
WITH THE C9 FLARE MENTIONED IN PART IA.
