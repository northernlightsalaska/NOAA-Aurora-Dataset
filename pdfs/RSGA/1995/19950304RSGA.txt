JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 063 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 04 MAR 1995
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 03/2100Z
TO 04/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. AN OPTICALLY UNCORRELATED
C1 FLARE OCCURRED AT 04/1600Z. REGION 7846 (S16W19) REMAINS THE
LARGEST REGION ON THE DISK BUT HAS NOT BEEN PARTICULARLY ACTIVE.
REGION 7848 (S13W38) IS MATURING AS A SMALL C-TYPE GROUP. NEW
REGION 7849 (S08W25) EMERGED TO THE NORTHWEST OF REGION 7846.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW. C-CLASS FLARES ARE POSSIBLE IN REGIONS 7846 AND 7848.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 03/2100Z TO 04/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS QUIET UNTIL ABOUT 04/0900Z. SINCE THEN
IT GRADUALLY BECAME UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE AT MIDDLE LATITUDES
AND ACTIVE TO MINOR STORM AT HIGH LATITUDES. THE SOURCE OF
THIS DISTURBANCE IS NOT CLEARLY KNOWN. THE GREATER THAN 2 MEV
ELECTRON FLUX WAS HIGH.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE. A RECURRENT DISTURBANCE
ASSOCIATED WITH A CORONAL HOLE IS EXPECTED TO CONTRIBUTE TO
THE ACTIVITY BEGINNING ON MARCH 6.
