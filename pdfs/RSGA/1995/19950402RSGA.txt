JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 092 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 02 APR 1995
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 01/2100Z
TO 02/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY REMAINED VERY LOW. REGION 7858
(S15W61) CONTINUED TO DECAY. REGION 7860 (S02W47) GREW AND A
NEARBY FILAMENT WAS QUITE ACTIVE AT TIMES.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY SHOULD CONTINUE
AT A VERY LOW LEVEL. THERE IS THE POSSIBILITY OF A FILAMENT
ERUPTION IN EVOLVING REGION 7860. THIS COULD PRODUCE A LONG
DURATION C-CLASS FLARE AND CORONAL MASS EJECTION FROM THAT
LOCATION.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 01/2100Z TO 02/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD RANGED BETWEEN QUIET AND MINOR STORM
LEVELS. SOME HIGH LATITUDE SITES EXPERIENCED MAJOR STORMING
NEAR THE MIDDLE OF THE PERIOD. ENERGETIC ELECTRON FLUXES WERE
HIGH AT THE BEGINNING OF THE PERIOD BUT DROPPED QUICKLY TO
NORMAL LEVELS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD
SHOULD BE QUIET TO ACTIVE ON 03 APR WITH ISOLATED MINOR
STORM PERIODS POSSIBLE. THE FIELD SHOULD BE PREDOMINANTLY
QUIET TO UNSETTLED ON 04-05 APR.
