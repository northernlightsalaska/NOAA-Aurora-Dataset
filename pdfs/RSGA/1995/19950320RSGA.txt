SDF NUMBER 079 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 20 MAR 1995
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 19/2100Z
TO 20/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS LOW. REGION 7854 (S16W02) WAS
MORE ACTIVE TODAY WITH FREQUENT SUBFLARE ACTIVITY. THE GROUP
MANAGED TO PRODUCE A C1/SF AT 1737Z. THIS EVENT WAS PROMPTLY
FOLLOWED BY THE ERUPTION OF A 12 DEGREE FILAMENT AND A LONG
DURATION B5 X-RAY EVENT FROM 1857Z TO 2043Z. THE SPOTS OF
7854 SHOWED TRANSITION TODAY FROM A SIMPLE BETA MAGNETIC
CONFIGURATION TO BETA-GAMMA WITH THE APPEARENCE OF SOME SMALL
NEGATIVE POLARITY SPOTS IN FRONT OF THE LARGE LEADER SPOT
COMPLEX. REGIONS 7856 (N12E10) AND 7857 (N15E38) WERE BOTH
STABLE AND QUIET D-TYPE GROUPS.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW. REGION 7854 IS LIKELY TO PRODUCE ADDITIONAL ISOLATED C-
CLASS FLARES OVER THE NEXT THREE DAYS.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 19/2100Z TO 20/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS PREDOMINANTLY QUIET FOR THE PAST
24 HOURS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO ACTIVE FOR THE NEXT 24 HOURS. AN
INCREASE IS POSSIBLE IN RESPONSE TO THE FILAMENT ERUPTION OF

DAY. AN ADDITIONAL INCREASE TO UNSETTLED TO SLIGHTLY ACTIVE
LEVELS IS ALSO POSSIBLE ON THE THIRD DAY IN RESPONSE TO TODAY'S
