JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 229 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 17 AUG 1995
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 16/2100Z
TO 17/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS VERY LOW.  NO SIGNIFICANT
ACTIVITY WAS OBSERVED AS ALL REGIONS EXHIBITED SLOW DECAY.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
VERY LOW FOR THE NEXT THREE DAYS.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 16/2100Z TO 17/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT MOSTLY QUIET TO UNSETTLED
LEVELS FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS.  THE FIELD INCREASED IN
ACTIVITY TO ACTIVE TO SEVERE STORM LEVELS AT APPROXIMATELY
17/1100UT, BUT HAS QUIETED DOWN TO MOSTLY QUIET TO UNSETTLED
LEVELS SINCE 17/1800UT.  THE BRIEF PERIOD OF ACTIVITY IS
THOUGHT TO HAVE BEEN CAUSED BY THE LARGE FILAMENT WHICH
DISAPPEARED SOMETIME BETWEEN 12/1943UT AND 13/0013UT.  THE
GREATER THAN 2 MEV ELECTRON FLUX FROM THE GOES-8 SATELLITE
INDICATED BORDERLINE HIGH TO VERY HIGH LEVELS FROM 16/2100UT
TO 17/0300UT.  SINCE THEN, THE FLUX STEADILY DECLINED TO
MODERATE LEVELS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE MOSTLY UNSETTLED FOR THE NEXT THREE DAYS.
