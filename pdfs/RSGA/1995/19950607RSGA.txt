JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 158 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 07 JUN 1995
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 06/2100Z
TO 07/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS VERY LOW. REGION 7877 (N09E11)
CONTINUED GROWTH AND DEVELOPED SMALL PENUMBRA ON THE LEADER AND
TRAILER SPOTS. THE GROUP HAS A SIMPLE BETA MAGNETIC CONFIGURA-
TION. AN 8 DEGREE FILAMENT NEAR S13E07 ERUPTED BETWEEN 0721Z AND
0757Z. PARALLEL H-ALPHA RIBBONS AND A WEAK GOES X-RAY EVENT
(B4.1) WERE ASSOCIATED WITH THIS EVENT. THE GOES SIGNATURE HAD
A GRADUAL RISE AND FALL CHARACTER. AN ADDITIONAL FILAMENT NEAR
S03W60 DISAPPEARED BETWEEN 06/2322Z AND 07/0026Z.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
VERY LOW TO LOW.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 06/2100Z TO 07/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS MOSTLY QUIET. THE GREATER THAN
2 MEV ELECTRON FLUXES CONTINUE TO BE AT HIGH LEVELS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE QUIET TO UNSETTLED FOR THE NEXT THREE DAYS.
THERE IS A ENHANCED PROBABILITY, HOWEVER, FOR ACTIVE CON-
DITIONS ON THE SECOND AND THIRD DAYS DUE TO THE POSSIBLE
EFFECTS FROM TODAY'S DISAPPEARING FILAMENT.
