JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 225 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 13 AUG 1995
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 12/2100Z
TO 13/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY REMAINED VERY LOW. REGION 7899
(S18E44) WAS STABLE AND EXHIBITED REVERSE POLARITY. A SEGMENTED
FILAMENT BETWEEN S24E23-S36E53 FADED BETWEEN 12/1943-13/0013Z.
SOME SEGMENTS DISAPPEARED RAPIDLY WHILE OTHERS FADED SLOWLY.
NO YOHKOH DATA ARE AVAILABLE FOR THIS TIME PERIOD BUT IT IS
LIKELY A CORONAL MASS EJECTION OCCURRED FROM THIS AREA.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY SHOULD CONTINUE
VERY LOW FOR THE NEXT THREE DAYS.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 12/2100Z TO 13/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS QUIET TO UNSETTLED WITH THE
EXCEPTION OF THE 0000-0600Z PERIOD WHEN MANY SITES
EXPERIENCED ACTIVE CONDITIONS. ENERGETIC ELECTRON FLUXES
BEGAN THE PERIOD AT HIGH LEVELS BUT DROPPED QUICKLY TO
LOW-MODERATE LEVELS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD
SHOULD BE PREDOMINANTLY QUIET TO UNSETTLED FOR 14-15 AUG.
ISOLATED ACTIVE CONDITIONS ARE POSSIBLE ON 14 AUG. A
DISTURBANCE RELATED TO THE DISAPPEARING FILAMENT MENTIONED
ABOVE IS FORECAST TO BEGIN LATE ON 16 AUG. ACTIVE TO MINOR
STORM PERIODS ARE POSSIBLE. THE TIMING OF THE ARRIVAL OF
THIS DISTURBANCE IS DIFFICULT TO FORECAST.
