JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 160 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 09 JUN 1995
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 08/2100Z
TO 09/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS VERY LOW. A FEW B-CLASS X-RAY
FLARES OCCURRED BUT NONE WERE CORRELATED TO OPTICAL FLARES.
REGION 7877 (N08W15) IS THE ONLY SPOTTED REGION ON THE SUN.
IT HAS GROWN SLIGHTLY IN SUNSPOT AREA BUT IT REMAINS
MAGNETICALLY SIMPLE.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
VERY LOW. C-CLASS FLARES ARE NOT LIKELY UNLESS REGION 7877
CONTINUES TO DEVELOP.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 08/2100Z TO 09/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS QUIET. THE GREATER THAN 2 MEV
ELECTRON FLUX MEASURED AT GOES 8 WAS MODERATE TO HIGH.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE QUIET TO UNSETTLED FOR MOST OF THE PERIOD.
ACTIVE CONDITIONS ARE POSSIBLE ON JUNE 11 IN RESPONSE TO A CME
ASSOCIATED WITH A DSF ON JUNE 7.
