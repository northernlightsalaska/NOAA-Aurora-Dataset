JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 152 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 01 JUN 1995
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 31/2100Z
TO 01/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY REMAINED VERY LOW. ONLY ONE MINOR
SPOTTED REGION WAS ON THE DISK. FAINT X-RAY EMISSION WAS VISIBLE
OVER THE NORTHEAST AND SOUTHEAST LIMBS.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY SHOULD CONTINUE
AT A VERY LOW LEVEL FOR THE NEXT THREE DAYS. A REGION THAT
PRODUCED 8 C-CLASS FLARES LAST ROTATION IS DUE BACK ON 04
JUN. THIS REGION WAS DECAYING PRIOR TO WEST LIMB TRANSIT AND
IS NOT EXPECTED TO RETURN AS ACTIVE.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 31/2100Z TO 01/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD WAS QUITE VARIABLE. UNSETTLED TO MINOR
STORM CONDITIONS WERE EXPERIENCED AT MOST LATITUDES. SOME
ISOLATED MAJOR TO SEVERE STORM LEVELS WERE OBSERVED AT A
FEW HIGH LATITUDE SITES. ENERGETIC ELECTRON FLUXES WERE
AT PREDOMINANTLY MODERATE TO HIGH LEVELS.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD
SHOULD BE AT UNSETTLED TO MINOR STORM LEVELS FOR 02-03 JUN.
ISOLATED PERIODS OF MAJOR STORMING AT HIGH LATITUDES ARE
POSSIBLE DURING THAT TIME. UNSETTLED TO SLIGHTLY ACTIVE
CONDITIONS ARE FORECAST FOR 04 JUN AS THIS CORONAL HOLE
RELATED DISTURBANCE BEGINS TO SUBSIDE.
