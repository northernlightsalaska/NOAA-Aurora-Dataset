IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 29/2100Z

ASSOCIATED TYPE IV SWEEP. THE LARGEST OF THE C-CLASS FLARES WAS
A C4.2/SF AT 30/1907Z. REGION 7834 (S13E72) WAS RESPONSIBLE FOR
A C2/SF FLARE AT 30/0117Z. THERE WERE TWO UNCORRELATED C-CLASS
FLARES AS WELL. REGIONS 7834 AND 7833 (S18E36) ALSO

PLAGE REGION YESTERDAY BUT QUICKLY REFORMED INTO A CSO SPOT
GROUP. THIS REGION HAS PROVEN TO BE FAIRLY PROLIFIC OVER THE
PAST 24 HOURS. REGION 7834 WAS NUMBERED TODAY.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW. REGIONS 7832 AND 7834 HAVE A GOOD CHANCE FOR CONTINUED
C-CLASS FLARING AND AN OUTSIDE CHANCE FOR AN ISOLATED M-CLASS
FLARE. REGION 7832 HAS A SLIM CHANCE FOR OCCASIONAL C-CLASS
FLARING.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 29/2100Z TO 30/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN AT UNSETTLED TO MINOR STORM
FOR THE PAST 24 HOURS. HIGH LATITUDE STATIONS HAVE BEEN AT
UNSETTLED TO SEVERE STORM LEVELS. ACTIVITY IS BECAUSE OF A
WELL POSTIONED RECURRENT CORONAL HOLE. GREATER THAN 2 MEV
ELCTRON FLUX RANGED FROM MODERATE TO HIGH DURING THE LAST HALF
OF THE PAST 24 HOUR PERIOD.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE UNSETTLED TO MINOR STORM AT MIDDLE LATITUDES AND
UNSETTLED TO MAJOR STORM AT HIGH LATITUDES FOR THE NEXT 12-18
HOURS. ACTIVITY IS THEN EXPECTED TO DECLINE TO QUIET TO
UNSETTLED FOR THE REMAINDER OF THE FORECAST PERIOD.
