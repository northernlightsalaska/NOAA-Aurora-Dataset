JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 179 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 28 JUN 1995
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 27/2100Z
TO 28/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS VERY LOW. ONLY TWO B-CLASS X-
RAY FLARES WERE OBSERVED TODAY. THE LARGEST WAS A B4.5 AT 0436Z
WHICH WAS NOT SEEN OPTICALLY. THE EVENT HAD A LONG DECAY TIME
OF 36 MINUTES FROM X-RAY MAXIMUM TO HALF POWER. REGION 7882
(N03W64) WAS RESPONSIBLE FOR THE OTHER B1.9 EVENT AT 1237Z.
REGION 7882 CONTINUED TO DECAY AND WAS STABLE. EVIDENCE FOR
THE RETURN OF OLD REGION 7877 COULD BE SEEN ON YOHKOH IMAGES
TODAY WITH THE APPEARANCE OF BRIGHT LOOPS ON EAST LIMB AT
ABOUT N05.
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
VERY LOW. THERE SHOULD BE A STEADY INCREASE IN BACKGROUND
LEVELS AS THE RETURN OF OLD REGION 7877 ROTATES INTO VIEW.

IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
