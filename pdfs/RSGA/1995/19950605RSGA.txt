JOINT USAF/NOAA REPORT OF SOLAR AND GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY
SDF NUMBER 156 ISSUED AT 2200Z ON 05 JUN 1995
IA.  ANALYSIS OF SOLAR ACTIVE REGIONS AND ACTIVITY FROM 04/2100Z
TO 05/2100Z:  SOLAR ACTIVITY WAS VERY LOW. REGION 7874 (N01W42)
GREW SIGNIFICANTLY TO AN AREA OF APPROXIMATELY 180 MILLIONTHS
WITH FREQUENT B-CLASS X-RAY FLARES. THE GROUP SHOWS SOME MAG-
NETIC COMPLEXITY IN THE SMALL SPOTS NEAR REGION CENTER. TWO NEW
A-TYPE GROUPS WERE ASSIGNED TODAY: 7876 (N12W59) AND 7877
(N10E38).
IB.  SOLAR ACTIVITY FORECAST:  SOLAR ACTIVITY IS EXPECTED TO BE
LOW. REGION 7874 APPEARS TO HAVE THE POTENTIAL FOR A C-CLASS
FLARE. THERE IS A VERY SLIGHT CHANCE FOR AN ISOLATED M-CLASS
EVENT.
IIA.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY SUMMARY FROM 04/2100Z TO 05/2100Z:
THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD HAS BEEN MOSTLY QUIET FOR THE PAST
24 HOURS. THE GREATER THAN 2 MEV ELECTRON FLUXES OBSERVED
BY GOES WERE AT HIGH LEVELS THROUGHOUT THE DAY.
IIB.  GEOPHYSICAL ACTIVITY FORECAST:  THE GEOMAGNETIC FIELD IS
EXPECTED TO BE QUIET TO UNSETTLED FOR THE NEXT THREE DAYS.
